# Top 30 Fixes — Band 6.0 to Band 7.0

> A concrete, ranked checklist. If a 6.0 student fixes the top 10 alone, the score should already be moving. Fixes 1–10 carry the most marks; 11–20 polish; 21–30 push for the higher half of band 7.

---

## Tier 1 — fixes that move the score most (do these first)

### 1. Answer **every** part of the prompt
If the prompt has 2 sub-questions, plan 2 body paragraphs — one for each. Skipping or under-treating a sub-question caps Task Response at **5**.

### 2. State a **clear position** and hold it
Write one sentence in the introduction that any reader could quote as your answer. Repeat it (in different words) in the conclusion. Don't drift to "both sides have pros and cons."

### 3. Use the **PEEL** pattern in every body paragraph
Point → Explain → Example → Link. Minimum 5 sentences per body paragraph.

### 4. Replace mechanical *Firstly / Secondly / Finally / Moreover* fronting
Use these at most ONCE in the whole essay. Vary openers and place linkers mid-sentence (*"This, however, …"* / *"Pollution, in turn, …"*).

### 5. Stop repeating *very, important, big, good, bad, thing, people, a lot of*
Replace each instance with a precise word: *crucial, substantial, beneficial, detrimental, factor, individuals, a substantial proportion of*.

### 6. Fix article use on every noun
Articles are the highest-frequency Band 6 leak. *Education is important for society* (not *the education* / *the society*). *The internet has changed the world* (not *internet*). Audit every noun before submitting.

### 7. Add at least 2 relative clauses to the essay
Combine two short sentences with *which*, *who*, *whose*, *where*. *Pollution, which has worsened in recent decades, threatens public health.*

### 8. Use at least 1 conditional sentence (2nd or 3rd)
*If governments invested in renewables, emissions could be reduced.* / *If laws had been introduced earlier, this crisis would have been avoided.*

### 9. Develop **all** main ideas — don't leave one half-explained
Every claim needs a *why* and a concrete example. Vague support is the #1 reason TR caps at 6.

### 10. Use **concrete examples**, not generic ones
*"In Seoul, housing prices have tripled since 2010"* beats *"In many countries, this is a problem."* If you don't know stats, use country-specific or historical examples.

---

## Tier 2 — polishing fixes (11–20)

### 11. Write a **synthesising** conclusion, not a repeat
Show how the body's arguments fit together. Re-state position in fresh words. Optional: add an implication ("Policymakers should therefore…").

### 12. Use **lexical chains** instead of repeating the same noun
*Pollution → contamination → emissions → environmental damage → such hazardous output.*

### 13. Add **complex noun phrases** (3+ words)
*The rising level of urban traffic congestion* beats *traffic*. Long-noun-phrase subjects are a Band 7 signature.

### 14. Use **hedging** modal verbs and adverbs
*may, might, could, would, tend to, often, arguably, in many cases.* Avoid absolute claims like *"All teenagers waste time online."*

### 15. Drop memorised templates and clichés
*"It is a controversial topic in modern society"* / *"Two-edged sword"* / *"In a nutshell"* / *"Last but not least"* — examiners flag these.

### 16. Fix subject–verb agreement
*The number of cars **is** rising* (not *are*). *One of my friends **is** here* (not *are*). *Everyone **has** their own view* (not *have*).

### 17. Fix high-yield collocations
*Make damage* → *cause damage*. *Do crime* → *commit crime*. *Take a decision* → *make a decision*. *Strong rain* → *heavy rain*. *Increase awareness* → *raise awareness*.

### 18. Use **referencing** (pronouns, demonstratives) for cohesion
*This, that, these, those, such, the former, the latter, it, they.* Don't repeat the full noun phrase three times.

### 19. Add **one participle clause** to the essay
*Driven by industrial expansion, pollution has reached alarming levels.* / *Having considered both views, I would argue that…*

### 20. Stop using contractions and slang
No *don't, can't, won't, kids, gonna, stuff, things, get* (overused). Academic register only.

---

## Tier 3 — push for solid 7.0+ (21–30)

### 21. Vary sentence openers
Don't always start with the subject. Mix in: adverbs (*Increasingly,*), prepositional phrases (*Across major cities,*), participle clauses (*Driven by demand,*), subordinate clauses (*Although growth has slowed,*).

### 22. Use one **cleft sentence** for emphasis
*"What many people fail to realise is that pollution affects mental health."* / *"It is education that matters most."*

### 23. Use one **inversion** for formality (optional)
*"Not only does technology save time, but it also reshapes work."* / *"Were governments to invest more, …"*

### 24. Punctuate non-defining relative clauses correctly
*Smartphones**, which are now affordable,** have transformed daily life.* Commas on **both** sides.

### 25. Fix preposition errors
*Discuss about* → *discuss*. *Different than* → *different from*. *In Monday* → *On Monday*. *Reason of* → *reason for*. *Depend of* → *depend on*.

### 26. Eliminate comma splices
*"I went home, I was tired"* → *"I went home; I was tired"* or *"I went home because I was tired"* or two sentences.

### 27. Plan word counts before writing
Aim for ~270–290 words for Task 2. Allocate ~50/100/100/40 across intro/body1/body2/conclusion.

### 28. Spend **5 minutes proofreading**
Audit specifically: articles, plurals (*informations* → *information*), tense consistency, spelling of *environment, government, receive, separate, their*, missing commas after introductory clauses.

### 29. Use **topic-cluster vocabulary** for the prompt's domain
If the topic is environment, use: *carbon emissions, fossil fuels, sustainable, deplete, mitigate, biodiversity*. If education: *curriculum, foster, equip, lifelong learning, broaden horizons*. Domain vocabulary signals lexical resource.

### 30. Don't try grammar you can't control
A simple, correct sentence beats a complex, broken one. The motto: *"control beats complexity."* If a structure feels uncertain, simplify; if it feels solid, complexify.

## Implementation order (6.0 → 7.0 in ~6 weeks)

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fixes 1–3 (Task Response habits — answer fully, position, PEEL) |
| 2 | Fixes 4–6 (cohesion + articles) |
| 3 | Fixes 7–10 (relative clauses + concrete examples) |
| 4 | Fixes 11–15 (conclusion, lexical chains, hedging, no clichés) |
| 5 | Fixes 16–20 (S-V agreement, collocations, referencing, participle clauses) |
| 6 | Fixes 21–30 (sentence variety, advanced grammar, proofreading routine) |

---

## The 5-minute proofread routine

Before the timer ends, run through this scan:

1. **Articles** — every noun: *a / an / the / zero*?
2. **Plurals** — uncountables not pluralised? (*informations, advices*)
3. **Subject-verb agreement** — quick scan
4. **Tense consistency** — same time-frame unless deliberately switching
5. **Linkers** — does any paragraph start with *Firstly / Secondly / Moreover*? Cut.
6. **Position** — is it the same in intro and conclusion?
7. **Word count** — at least 270?
8. **Spelling** — *environment, government, receive, separate, their, occurred*

Catching even 3 errors in this scan typically lifts the GRA score noticeably.

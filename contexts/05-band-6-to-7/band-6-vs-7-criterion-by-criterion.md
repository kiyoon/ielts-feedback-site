# Band 6 vs Band 7 — Criterion-by-Criterion Deep Dive

> The official public band descriptors describe band 6 and band 7 in deceptively similar language. The actual gap is large, and it lives in tiny words: *some* vs *all*, *generally* vs *throughout*, *flexibly* vs *mechanically*. This file unpacks every criterion with side-by-side examples so a 6.0 student can see exactly what an examiner is rewarding at 7.0.

---

## 1. Task Response (Task 2) / Task Achievement (Task 1)

### Official descriptor language (Task 2, public version)

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 7 |
|---|---|---|
| Coverage of task | "addresses the task **only in some parts**" / addresses task generally | "addresses **all parts** of the task" |
| Position | "presents a **relevant** position although the conclusions may become unclear or repetitive" | "presents a **clear position throughout** the response" |
| Main ideas | "presents relevant main ideas but **some may be inadequately developed/unclear**" | "presents, **extends and supports** main ideas, but there may be a tendency to over-generalise and/or supporting ideas may lack focus" |

### What this looks like on the page

| Symptom at Band 6 | What Band 7 looks like |
|---|---|
| Answers half the question (e.g. discusses causes but skips solutions) | Answers every part with roughly equal depth |
| Position drifts: introduction agrees, conclusion hedges | Same stance from introduction to conclusion |
| Idea is named but not explained: *"Pollution is bad for health."* | Idea is **named + explained + illustrated**: *"Air pollution damages health: prolonged exposure to particulate matter (PM2.5) inflames lung tissue, which is why cities such as Delhi report higher rates of asthma."* |
| Body paragraph is 3–4 sentences | Body paragraph is 5–7 sentences with claim → reasoning → example → implication |
| Examples are vague: *"In many countries, this is a problem."* | Examples are concrete: *"In South Korea, the 2019 emergency dust legislation forced car-rotation rules on heavy-pollution days."* |

### Side-by-side: a "discuss both views and give your opinion" prompt

**Prompt:** *Some people believe that students learn best in a quiet, traditional classroom; others argue that interactive group learning is more effective. Discuss both views and give your opinion.*

**Band 6 introduction**
> Education is a controversial topic in the modern world. Some people think traditional classrooms are better, while others think group learning is better. This essay will discuss both views.

*Why band 6:* Vague opener ("controversial topic"), copies prompt without paraphrasing, no position stated.

**Band 7 introduction**
> Opinions are divided on whether silent, lecture-based instruction or collaborative group activities produce stronger academic outcomes. While quiet study undeniably aids concentration, this essay will argue that interactive learning ultimately produces deeper understanding because it forces students to articulate and defend their reasoning.

*Why band 7:* Paraphrased, both views acknowledged, position stated, position explained.

## 2. Coherence and Cohesion

### Official descriptor language

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 7 |
|---|---|---|
| Information flow | "arranges information and ideas coherently and there is a **clear overall progression**" | "**logically organises** information and ideas; there is **clear progression throughout**" |
| Cohesive devices | "uses cohesive devices effectively, but cohesion within and/or between sentences may be **faulty or mechanical**" | "uses a range of cohesive devices **appropriately, although there may be some under-/over-use**" |
| Referencing | "may not always use referencing clearly or appropriately" | "*(implicit)* — referencing handled accurately" |
| Paragraphing | "uses paragraphing, but not always logically" | "**presents a clear central topic within each paragraph**" |

### What this looks like on the page

| Band 6 cohesion habits | Band 7 cohesion habits |
|---|---|
| Linkers stamped in fixed positions: *Firstly... Secondly... Finally... In conclusion* | Linkers placed flexibly, sometimes mid-sentence: *A further consideration is..., What is more important, however, is...* |
| Same linker repeated: *Moreover... Moreover... Moreover...* | Range of devices for the same function: *In addition, What is more, A second factor, Furthermore* |
| Pronouns ambiguous: *"They use them every day."* (who? what?) | Clear referencing: *"Adolescents use these platforms daily, which..."* |
| Repeats the noun: *"Pollution causes problems. Pollution is dangerous. Pollution must be reduced."* | Lexical chain: *"Pollution... this contamination... such emissions... the problem..."* |
| Topic sentences missing or vague | Each paragraph opens with a topic sentence that previews its main idea |
| Paragraphs of 1–2 sentences or 12+ sentences | Body paragraphs balanced (5–7 sentences) |

### Side-by-side cohesion within a paragraph

**Band 6**
> Firstly, technology is good. For example, students can learn online. Moreover, technology is bad. For example, students cannot focus. Finally, technology has both pros and cons.

*Why band 6:* Mechanical sequencers, abrupt jumps, no progression, contradictory mini-claims with no synthesis.

**Band 7**
> One clear advantage of digital learning is its flexibility: students can revisit recorded lectures at their own pace, which benefits slower learners in particular. That said, this same flexibility can erode discipline; without the rhythm of a physical classroom, many young learners struggle to stay engaged for long enough to absorb complex material.

*Why band 7:* Idea progresses (claim → mechanism → counter-implication), pronoun *this same flexibility* refers back, *that said* contrasts without leaning on *but* or *however*.

## 3. Lexical Resource

### Official descriptor language

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 7 |
|---|---|---|
| Range | "uses an **adequate range** of vocabulary for the task" | "uses a **sufficient range** of vocabulary to allow some **flexibility and precision**" |
| Less common items | "attempts to use less common vocabulary but with **some inaccuracy**" | "uses **less common lexical items with some awareness of style and collocation**" |
| Errors | "makes some errors in spelling and/or word formation, but they do not impede communication" | "may produce **occasional** errors in word choice, spelling and/or word formation" |

### What this looks like on the page

| Band 6 lexis | Band 7 lexis |
|---|---|
| *very important* | *crucial / pivotal / paramount* |
| *a lot of people* | *a substantial proportion of the population* |
| *good for the environment* | *environmentally sustainable / eco-friendly* |
| *bad effect* | *adverse impact / detrimental consequence* |
| *make pollution* | *generate emissions / cause contamination* |
| *get knowledge* | *acquire knowledge / gain expertise* |
| *do crime* | *commit a crime* |
| *big problem* | *pressing issue / major concern* |
| Word reached for fails: *"Government should defend pollution."* (wrong word, *combat* needed) | Word fits naturally: *"Governments should combat pollution by..."* |
| Forced thesaurus synonym sounds odd: *"This is a perilous matter."* | Natural collocation: *"This is a serious problem."* (when the simpler word fits better) |

### Compound nouns and noun phrases (a Band 7 marker)

| Single word (Band 6) | Compound / noun phrase (Band 7) |
|---|---|
| traffic | traffic congestion / vehicle emissions |
| jobs | employment opportunities / job security |
| money | financial resources / disposable income |
| pollution | air-quality deterioration / environmental degradation |
| children | school-age children / young people |

## 4. Grammatical Range and Accuracy

### Official descriptor language

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 7 |
|---|---|---|
| Structure mix | "uses a mix of simple and complex sentence forms" | "uses a **variety of complex structures**" |
| Errors | "makes some errors in grammar and punctuation but they **rarely reduce communication**" | "produces **frequent error-free sentences**" / "has good control of grammar and punctuation but may make a few errors" |

### What this looks like on the page

| Band 6 grammar | Band 7 grammar |
|---|---|
| Mostly simple sentences with a few *because* / *and* / *but* | Wide variety: relative clauses (defining + non-defining), participle clauses, conditionals (1st, 2nd, 3rd, mixed), inversion, cleft sentences |
| Errors in **every other sentence** (articles, agreement, prepositions) | Most sentences are completely error-free; errors are minor and don't repeat |
| Same structure recycled: *"It is important that... It is true that... I think that..."* | Structures vary: *"What many overlook is that..."*, *"Were governments to invest..."*, *"Having considered both views..."* |
| Punctuation errors: comma splices, missing commas around non-defining clauses | Punctuation supports meaning; semicolons, colons, and dashes used correctly |

### Side-by-side sentence-level

| Band 6 | Band 7 |
|---|---|
| *Many students they don't like exam.* | *Many students dislike examinations.* |
| *People who they live in city...* | *People who live in cities...* |
| *If governments would invest more, the problem can be solved.* | *If governments invested more, the problem could be solved.* |
| *Pollution is a big problem and it affects health and it costs money.* | *Pollution, which affects both public health and national budgets, is a pressing concern.* |
| *Education is important. It helps people. It gives them jobs.* | *Education, by equipping people with employable skills, plays a fundamental role in social mobility.* |

## 5. The "tiny word" gap (summary)

| Criterion | Band 6 word | Band 7 word | What changes |
|---|---|---|---|
| TR | *some parts* | *all parts* | Coverage |
| TR | *relevant* (position) | *clear throughout* | Consistency |
| TR | *inadequately developed* | *extends and supports* | Depth |
| CC | *clear overall progression* | *clear progression throughout* | No backtracking |
| CC | *faulty or mechanical* | *appropriately* | Flexible linkers |
| LR | *attempts less common* | *uses less common* | Successful attempts |
| LR | *some inaccuracy* | *some awareness of collocation* | Natural pairings |
| GRA | *mix of simple and complex* | *variety of complex* | Range |
| GRA | *errors don't impede* | *frequent error-free sentences* | Accuracy |

If even **one** criterion sits at 6, the average drops below 7. To break 7.0 you must lift **all four** to band 7.

# Concrete tactics to lift each speaking criterion from Band 6 to Band 7

_Merged from codex + claude crawls on 2026-05-19T07:37:08Z._

## Summary

IELTS Speaking is assessed across an 11-14 minute, three-part interview against four equally weighted (25% each) criteria: Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, and Pronunciation. The British Council/IDP/Cambridge descriptors define the Band 6-to-7 boundary, and IELTS rates the final speaking score as an average across all parts — so a single criterion stuck at 6 caps the whole score near 6.5, most often Lexical Resource or Grammatical Range rather than Fluency. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

For an automated judge, the practical jump is not "more advanced English" in general but a tight, descriptor-derived set of behaviours: sustaining long turns without effort or loss of coherence, deploying flexible (not mechanical) discourse markers, using some less-common and idiomatic lexis with style and collocation awareness, producing frequent error-free complex sentences, and showing prosody features (sentence stress, intonation, chunking) that go beyond mere intelligibility. International tutor consensus (Korea, China, Vietnam, Iran, India, Pakistan, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, UK/AU/Canada) converges on the same upgrade levers: extend Part-1 answers to 2-3 sentences (answer + reason + example); tell mini-stories in Part 2 rather than reciting bullets; vary connectives beyond "and/but/because"; deploy a few well-collocated less-common items per response rather than many forced ones; produce conditionals and relative clauses; trade speed for clear chunking and sentence stress. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/differences-between-ielts-6-and-7-for-productive-skills https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

## Authoritative material

### Official descriptors: Band 6 vs Band 7 side-by-side

The British Council/IDP/Cambridge Speaking Band Descriptors PDF, IELTS.org's "Differences between IELTS 6 and 7" page, and the IDP "Difference Between a Band 6 and 7" article are the canonical references. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/differences-between-ielts-6-and-7-for-productive-skills https://ielts.idp.com/prepare/article-difference-between-band-6-and-7-ielts

**Fluency and Coherence**
- Band 6: "is willing to speak at length, though may lose coherence at times due to occasional repetition, self-correction or hesitation"; "uses a range of connectives and discourse markers but not always appropriately."
- Band 7: "speaks at length without noticeable effort or loss of coherence"; "may demonstrate language-related hesitation at times, or some repetition and/or self-correction"; "uses a range of connectives and discourse markers with some flexibility."
- Key shift: hesitations at Band 7 are "language-related" (searching for the right word or grammar), not "content-related" (searching for an idea). https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.idp.com/prepare/article-difference-between-band-6-and-7-ielts https://ieltsfocus.com/2018/10/09/band-7-ielts-speaking/

**Lexical Resource**
- Band 6: "has a wide enough vocabulary to discuss topics at length and make meaning clear in spite of inappropriacies"; "generally paraphrases successfully."
- Band 7: "uses vocabulary resource flexibly to discuss a variety of topics"; "uses some less common and idiomatic vocabulary and shows some awareness of style and collocation, with some inappropriate choices"; "uses paraphrase effectively."
- Key shift: presence of less-common and idiomatic vocabulary plus collocation awareness, not just paraphrase capacity. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsunderstood.com/10-band-7-idioms-you-must-know-for-ielts-speaking/

**Grammatical Range and Accuracy**
- Band 6: "uses a mix of simple and complex structures, but with limited flexibility"; "may make frequent mistakes with complex structures, though these rarely cause comprehension problems."
- Band 7: "uses a range of complex structures with some flexibility"; "frequently produces error-free sentences, though some grammatical mistakes persist."
- Key shift: "frequently produces error-free sentences" is the explicit Band-7 trigger; examiners listen for whole complex sentences with no error. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://keithspeakingacademy.com/ielts-speaking-complex-sentences-grammar/

**Pronunciation**
- Band 6: "uses a range of pronunciation features with mixed control"; "can generally be understood throughout, though mispronunciation of individual words or sounds reduces clarity at times."
- Band 7: shows "all the positive features of Band 6 and some, but not all, of the positive features of Band 8" — Band 8 features include sustained rhythm, flexible stress and intonation across long utterances, effective use of features for delivering meaning, and only occasional lapses.
- Official line on accent: "L1 accent has minimal effect on intelligibility" at Band 7. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsliz.com/ielts-speaking-accent-british-us-or-other/

### Test format and rating rule

- IELTS Speaking has three parts in 11-14 minutes: Part 1 asks familiar personal questions, Part 2 gives one minute of preparation and a 1-2 minute long turn, and Part 3 asks more abstract questions connected to Part 2. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://caribbean.britishcouncil.org/exam/ielts/prepare/ielts-test-format
- Each criterion contributes 25% of the Speaking score, and the final Speaking rating is based on average performance across all parts of the test — a candidate must fully fit the positive features of a band to be awarded it. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

### IELTS.org and British Council guidance

- IELTS.org's "Journey from Band 5 to Band 7" identifies the differentiators as: (a) speaking at length without effort, (b) flexible discourse markers, (c) use of less-common vocabulary and idioms with awareness of collocation, (d) frequent error-free complex sentences, and (e) intelligible pronunciation throughout with sustained use of stress and intonation. Recommended drill: record a 2-minute Part-2 response, transcribe it, mark every filler, every repetition, every self-correction, every basic word; then re-record substituting one less-common word and one complex structure per sentence until natural. https://www.britishcouncil.in/blog/journey-ielts-band-5-band-7-ielts-speaking-test
- The IELTS.org Band-6 vs Band-7 comparison frames Speaking Band 7 as stronger at expressing nuanced opinions, handling follow-up or interruptions, and using broader vocabulary than Band 6. https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/differences-between-ielts-6-and-7-for-productive-skills

### IELTS Australia / IELTS.com.au

- The IDP-published comparison piece states the single largest practical difference is "consistency": Band 6 candidates "show the right features some of the time", while Band 7 candidates "sustain those features throughout the test" — a candidate can hit Band 7 lexis or grammar in Part 1 but drop to Band 6 in Part 3 under topic pressure, and that drop is what caps the score. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7
- IELTS Australia states that idiomatic language and less common words matter at Band 7 and above, but candidates should not use rare words, cliches, or memorized phrases unnaturally; paraphrase is a scored skill. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7
- It identifies common grammar error areas — articles, prepositions, subject-verb agreement — and frames Grammatical Range and Accuracy as both correctness and use of simple and complex structures. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

### IELTS Simon (UK ex-examiner)

- The Band 7 lexical marker is "less common words plus correct collocations plus maybe some idiomatic language", used naturally inside answers that directly address the question. Forced idioms with broken collocation ("relish low temperature", "under this kind of weather") are diagnostic of Band 6. Simon argues two or three accurate uses of less-common items per long turn is Band-7 territory; five forced uses without natural collocation actually signals Band 6. https://www.ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/2010/08/band-7-vocabulary.html
- Vietop's republished IELTS Simon PDF advises using real examples to extend Part 2 and support Part 3 opinions, not overthinking grammar during live speaking (it damages fluency), and focusing pronunciation on clarity, speed, word stress, sentence stress, and intonation rather than accent imitation. https://vietop.edu.vn/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/IELTS-Speaking-Tips-and-samples-IELTS-Smion.pdf

### IELTS Liz

- The four pronunciation features the examiner listens for are word stress, sentence stress, intonation/rise-fall, and chunking. Accent itself is not marked; sustained correct use of these four features is. "Band 7 shows all features of Band 6 and only some of the positive features of Band 8" — so a single criterion sustained at full Band 7 is harder than aggregating partial 7s. https://ieltsliz.com/ielts-speaking-accent-british-us-or-other/

### IELTS Podcast and IELTS Understood (Lexical Resource)

- The Band-7 Lexical Resource lesson lists concrete substitutions: "I like my job" → "I find my work quite fulfilling"; "funny" → "hilarious / hysterical / side-splitting"; "not much free time" → "limited spare time"; "big" → "significant / substantial". Band-7 vocabulary should appear across all three parts, not clustered in Part 2 only. https://www.ieltspodcast.com/audio-tutorials/speaking-band-7-lexical-resource/ https://ieltsunderstood.com/10-band-7-idioms-you-must-know-for-ielts-speaking/

### Engvarta, All Ears English, 3D Universal Cebu, Miss Kay

- Engvarta's "game-changing trick" is structural: answer → reason → example → mini-conclusion, with a transition word at each seam ("because", "for instance", "so overall"). https://engvarta.com/the-game-changing-trick-to-score-a-7-band-score-in-ielts-speaking/
- All Ears English's "8 Actions" lesson narrows it to four operational levers (one per criterion) and rejects memorised answers, scripted idioms, and over-fast delivery. https://www.allearsenglish.com/ielts-speaking-score-band-6-to-7/
- 3D Universal Cebu frames the Band-7 fluency target as "appropriate pacing with meaningful pauses" rather than rapid continuous speech, and recommends rhythm/pause practice for L1 speakers of syllable-timed languages. https://3d-universal.com/en/blogs/fluency-and-coherence-how-to-improve-for-ielts-speaking.html
- Miss Kay's "Secret to Band 7+" argues the highest-leverage move is switching from generic answers to "tiny, clear stories" — one specific instance, one specific person, one specific time. Mini-stories naturally trigger past tenses, time-sequencing connectives, attitudinal lexis, and intonational variation in one move, lifting all four criteria simultaneously. https://www.misskayenglish.com/post/the-secret-to-a-band-7-in-ielts-speaking-it-s-not-perfect-grammar

### Other reference guides

- IELTS Score 7's 2026 strategy: speak at length, use flexible vocabulary including less common words, produce frequent error-free sentences with complex structures, be easy to understand with only occasional mispronunciation; drills include two-minute speaking, pause counting, topic-specific vocabulary, collocations, conditionals, passives, relative clauses, and shadowing. https://ieltsscore7.com/guides/how-to-get-band-7-in-ielts-speaking/
- IELTS Academic warns that fluency matters more than producing error-free speech in real time, consistent with the official descriptor allowing some grammatical mistakes at Band 7. https://ielts-academic.com/ielts-speaking-tips/
- Rehearse's guide emphasises clear, natural communication with enough range for academic/professional contexts; warns against playing safe with only simple sentences or forcing random advanced words. https://www.rehearse.to/blog/ielts-speaking-band-7-complete-practice-guide
- ieltszone.org provides a plain-language descriptor walk-through aligning the four criteria with the Band 6/7 boundary. https://general.ieltszone.org/ielts-speaking-band-descriptors-explained/

### Academic / corpus evidence

- Huang, Lin & Gráf (LINDSEI corpus, Pragmatics 2022) show that C1 learners (CEFR equivalent of IELTS 7-7.5) use significantly more discourse markers than B2 learners, and discourse-marker frequency rises with fluency level toward near-native rates — directly usable to set Band-6 vs Band-7 discourse-marker thresholds in a text-only judge. https://benjamins.com/online/prag/articles/prag.21016.hua
- An Indonesian academic source on IELTS Speaking and idiomatic expressions confirms idiomatic vocabulary and collocation as a salient feature of Band 7-plus performance; use as support for natural idiomatic chunks, not forced proverb insertion. https://journal.unnes.ac.id/sju/elt/article/download/57604/22995/
- A Kazakh/Russian-region academic PDF notes the difficulty of reaching Band 7 in IELTS Speaking even when Listening and Reading are stronger; context for targeted Speaking drills rather than a scoring rule. https://rmebrk.kz/journals/3580/58646.pdf

### Korea (한국 IELTS 학원)

### China and Chinese-language prep (雅思口语 7分)

- The IELTS China public Speaking descriptors translate the same Band 7 boundary: sustained full expression without obvious difficulty, language-search hesitation that does not harm coherence, flexible spoken discourse markers, flexible lexis across topics, less common and idiomatic vocabulary with style/collocation awareness, flexible grammar, effective paraphrase. https://backoffice.ielts.chinaielts.org/api/assets/ielts-cms/0c5929f5-faad-45ca-83f5-ce5c8384ec95/%E8%AF%84%E5%88%86%E6%A0%87%E5%87%86-speakingfinal.pdf?version=1
- ELT Chinese frames the 6-to-7 jump as a strategy change from "Band 6 rules" to descriptor-based performance; its Part 3 example shows a Band 6-style answer remains personal with basic repeated vocabulary, while a Band 7-style answer expands the subject to individuals and society, uses topic collocations, and marks logical progression with discourse markers. https://www.eltschool.jp/zh/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips
- IELTS International Chinese: identify the precise criterion holding the candidate at 6.0, then drill the target behavior such as strategic paraphrase and connected speech features including linking, weak forms, stress patterns, and intonation. https://www.ielts.international/zh/improve-ielts-from-6-to-7

### Vietnam (luyện thi IELTS Speaking band 7)

- ZIM Vietnam's Band 7 guide: Band 7 requires fluency, coherence, rich vocabulary, accurate grammar, and clear pronunciation; the 6.0-to-7.0 stage asks learners to combine simple and complex sentences, use tenses and connectors flexibly, use natural idioms, and improve stress and intonation. https://zim.vn/ielts-speaking-band-7
- ZIM's Lexical Resource 6-to-7 analysis: Band 6 vocabulary is "wide enough" for topics, while Band 7 must cover a variety of topics; effective paraphrase should make meaning clearer rather than merely harder or more elaborate. https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking
- ZIM's phonological-features article: the Band 6-to-7 pronunciation difference is control and flexibility — Band 6 may use stress, intonation, and linking inconsistently, while Band 7 controls these features in long sentences and uses them to show emotion or communicative intent. Drills: learn word stress with dictionary audio, identify content words for sentence stress, practice rising/falling intonation patterns, drill English phonemes Vietnamese speakers often lack including /θ/ as in "think" and /ʃ/ as in "she." https://zim.vn/phonological-features-trong-ielts-speaking
- DOL English Vietnam (non-official but useful operational thresholds): use simple and complex sentences flexibly, include relative clauses and conjunctions, target roughly 80% error-free speech, and avoid repeating the same grammar error more than twice. https://www.dolenglish.vn/blog/tieu-chi-grammatical-range-accuracy-trong-ielts-speaking https://www.dolenglish.vn/blog/ielts-speaking-band-7
- WISE English Vietnam describes the 6.0-to-7.0 path as moving from basic correctness to deeper, more natural, more logically connected speaking, with self-recording, role-play interviews, idioms, and examples used to make answers less mechanical. https://wiseenglish.edu.vn/lo-trinh-hoc-ielts-tu-6-0-len-7-0
- IELTS-Powerup/KTDC/Thanh Loan/IELTS Mentor/Hot Courses consensus — explicit 6.0→7.0+ differentiator: 7.0 candidates "trình bày một câu trả lời dài mà không đánh mất yếu tố mạch lạc" (deliver long answers without losing coherence) versus 6.0's ấp úng, lặp lại, tự sửa (stalling, repeating, self-correcting). https://ielts-powerup.com/but-pha-ielts-speaking-band-7/ https://ktdcgroup.vn/kinh-nghiem-hoc-ielts-speaking-tu-6-0-len-7-0-trong-1-thang/ https://ielts-thanhloan.com/blog/speaking/ielts-speaking-band-6-0.html https://ieltsmentor.edu.vn/blog/ielts-speaking-band-7-0-huong-dan-toan-dien-de-dat-muc-tieu-trong-thoi-gian-ngan.html https://www.hotcourses.vn/study-abroad-info/essentials/kinh-nghiem-thi-ielts-speaking-de-nang-diem-tu-6.0-len-7.0/
- Flexible connectors recommended for Band 7+: "to be more specific", "I guess this is because", "I have to say that", "what stood out for me", "as well as" — used mid-turn, not just at sentence starts. https://ielts-powerup.com/but-pha-ielts-speaking-band-7/ https://ktdcgroup.vn/kinh-nghiem-hoc-ielts-speaking-tu-6-0-len-7-0-trong-1-thang/

- Grammar lever: drill "I can't help + Ving", "not only…but also", third conditional, mixed conditional, reduced relative clauses. https://ielts-powerup.com/but-pha-ielts-speaking-band-7/

## Practical signals for an LLM judge

Reading a Speaking transcript (diarized turns, optional filler/timing annotation). "Per minute" assumes Part 2 is ~2 minutes of monologue and Part 1/3 turns are 15-30 s each.

1. **FC — long-turn sustainment.** In Part 2, a Band 7 candidate sustains most of the 1-2 minute long turn with coherent expansion. Frequent abandoned starts, repeated "I don't know", or very short Part 2 output aligns with Band 6 or below. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf
2. **FC — hesitation type, not count.** Filler ("um", "uh") immediately followed by a topic noun = content hesitation, caps F&C at 6; filler followed by a precise/less-common word or grammatical reformulation = language hesitation, compatible with Band 7. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsfocus.com/2018/10/09/band-7-ielts-speaking/
3. **FC — self-correction rhythm.** Band 6 self-corrections break rhythm ("I goed — I mean I went — to the…"); Band 7 corrections preserve flow ("I went, or rather I made my way to the…") and use repair markers ("or rather", "I mean", "what I meant was") as discourse devices, not broken restarts. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.idp.com/prepare/article-difference-between-band-6-and-7-ielts
4. **FC — discourse-marker variety and flexibility.** Reward flexible discourse moves (cause, contrast, example, concession, conclusion); cap FC near Band 6 when the candidate relies on repetitive "and/but/because" chains or uses memorized markers in illogical places. Heuristic: Band 7 transcripts mix in at least 4-6 distinct flexible markers (actually, well, you know, I mean, in fact, having said that, on the other hand, what's more, to be honest, mind you, the thing is, I suppose, frankly). Fewer than 4 distinct flexible markers across a full test = Band 6 signal. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://www.britishcouncil.in/blog/journey-ielts-band-5-band-7-ielts-speaking-test https://benjamins.com/online/prag/articles/prag.21016.hua
5. **LR — less-common vocabulary density (CONFLICT FLAGGED).** Crawls disagree on the count target.
   - codex heuristic: at least two natural topic collocations or idiomatic chunks in a 90-second Part 2 answer (~1.3/min). https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking
   - claude heuristic: ~2-4 well-collocated less-common items per minute of Part 2 monologue and ~1-2 per Part 1/3 turn. https://www.ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/2010/08/band-7-vocabulary.html https://ieltsunderstood.com/10-band-7-idioms-you-must-know-for-ielts-speaking/
   - IELTS Simon (cited as authoritative by both): two-or-three accurate uses of less-common items per long turn is Band-7 territory; five+ forced uses with bad collocation ("relish low temperature", "indulge sleep") signals Band 6 cap, not Band 7. https://www.ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/2010/08/band-7-vocabulary.html
   - Either way: zero across the test = Band 6 cap; excessive forced use = also Band 6 cap. Pick a single count if needed, but the qualitative direction (some, well-collocated, not many forced) is the unambiguous signal.
6. **LR — style and collocation awareness.** Reward natural idiomatic language and collocation; penalize obvious clichés ("raining cats and dogs", "a piece of cake"), rare words used incorrectly, slang in formal Part 3 contexts, business idioms on holiday cue cards, and collocation errors that make style awareness doubtful. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://www.eltschool.jp/ko/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips https://www.ielts-simon.com/ielts-help-and-english-pr/2010/08/band-7-vocabulary.html
7. **LR — phrasal-verb presence and paraphrase effectiveness.** At least 2-3 idiomatic phrasal verbs (look up, get on with, put up with, cut down on, look forward to) per Part-2 turn is a strong Band-7 signal; a transcript dominated by single-word formal Latinate vocabulary with zero phrasal verbs reads as essay-like and caps at 6.5. Band 6 can paraphrase successfully but Band 7 paraphrases *effectively* — rewording that clarifies the examiner's question, repairs missing vocabulary, or avoids repetition without changing meaning. https://pndielts.in/blog/ielts-speaking-tips-for-band-7/ https://leverageedu.com/learn/less-common-words-in-ielts/ https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking
8. **GRA — complex-sentence ratio and range.** At least ~30-40% of clauses across the transcript should contain subordination (because/although/which/that/while/if); look for at least three controlled complex-structure types across the test (conditionals, relative clauses, subordinate clauses, passives, comparison clauses, perfect/progressive tenses). Pure parataxis (string of simple sentences joined by "and/so") = Band 6. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsscore7.com/guides/how-to-get-band-7-in-ielts-speaking/ https://www.zhihu.com/question/23968508
9. **GRA — error-free-sentence frequency.** Look for runs of 3+ consecutive sentences with no grammar error (agreement, tense, articles, prepositions). Band 7 requires "frequently produces error-free sentences"; a transcript where every long sentence has at least one error caps at 6 even when meaning is clear. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://keithspeakingacademy.com/ielts-speaking-complex-sentences-grammar/
10. **GRA — conditional and relative-clause deployment.** At least one well-formed conditional (second or third) AND one relative clause across the full transcript is a Band-7 marker. Zero of either across all three parts = Band 6. https://keithspeakingacademy.com/ielts-speaking-complex-sentences-grammar/ https://ielts-powerup.com/but-pha-ielts-speaking-band-7/
11. **Answer length scaffold (Part 1) and mini-story (Part 2).** Part 1 answers consistently 2-3 sentences (answer + reason + example) = Band 7; one-sentence answers throughout = Band 6 cap regardless of vocabulary. In Part 2, look for one specific instance with a time anchor ("last summer", "back in 2019"), a named person, and a past-tense narrative sequence. Generic Part-2 ("I think it's good because…") without a concrete instance is Band 6. https://engvarta.com/the-game-changing-trick-to-score-a-7-band-score-in-ielts-speaking/ https://www.misskayenglish.com/post/the-secret-to-a-band-7-in-ielts-speaking-it-s-not-perfect-grammar https://pndielts.in/blog/ielts-speaking-tips-for-band-7/
12. **Part 3 abstraction.** Band 7 candidates move from personal anecdotes to broader social, hypothetical, or comparative reasoning in Part 3; answers that remain only personal and concrete may still be fluent but are weaker for coherence and lexical/grammatical range. Reward "It depends on…" and "There are two sides to this…" framings that unlock concession and hypothetical grammar. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://www.eltschool.jp/zh/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips https://www.britishcouncil.in/blog/journey-ielts-band-5-band-7-ielts-speaking-test
13. **Topic consistency across parts.** A common Band-6.5 ceiling: Band-7 features appear in Part 1 (familiar topics) but vanish in Part 3 (abstract topics). Score the three parts separately for lexical density, complex-sentence ratio, and discourse-marker variety; consistency across all three = Band 7, inconsistency = Band 6.5 cap. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7
14. **Memorisation detection.** Polished chunks that do not answer the question, the same template across unrelated topics, robotic openers ("Well, that's a very interesting question. Let me tell you about…"), perfectly structured 4-bullet Part-2 monologues, and Part-1 answers longer than 6 sentences are memorisation tells. Do not reward surface complexity as Band 7 flexibility; examiners downgrade these regardless of language quality. https://pndielts.in/blog/ielts-speaking-tips-for-band-7/ https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://www.zhihu.com/question/23968508 https://www.hafezedu.com/ielts-speaking-tricks/
15. **Pronunciation — transcript limitation.** A diarized transcript alone cannot reliably score stress, rhythm, intonation, linking, or phoneme control; if audio or ASR confidence is absent, mark Pronunciation evidence as limited and avoid inferring Band 7 purely from grammar or vocabulary. Transcript proxies: high ASR-confidence variance, frequent `[unintelligible]` tags, mistranscribed common words, and listener-side clarifying questions ("Sorry, could you repeat that?") all indicate Band-6 pronunciation; a clean transcript whose intended meaning is never queried is compatible with Band 7. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phonological-features-trong-ielts-speaking https://ieltsliz.com/ielts-speaking-accent-british-us-or-other/
16. **Pronunciation — audio and chunking signals.** When audio exists, Band 7 should show Band 6 intelligibility plus some Band 8-like features: controlled sentence stress, appropriate chunking, non-monotone intonation, linking, rhythm that does not force listener effort. In punctuated chunking-aware transcripts, many short syntactic chunks with commas inside long sentences are compatible with Band 7; long undifferentiated runs without commas, or commas inside noun phrases, indicate poor chunking. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://vietop.edu.vn/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/IELTS-Speaking-Tips-and-samples-IELTS-Smion.pdf https://ieltsliz.com/ielts-speaking-accent-british-us-or-other/
17. **Accent neutrality.** Do not penalise an L1 accent by itself; penalise only when stress, rhythm, intonation, or individual sounds reduce intelligibility or make meaning hard to follow. https://ieltskorea.org/korea/prepare/speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf
18. **Criterion balance.** Because each criterion is equally weighted, a transcript strong in lexis and grammar but repeatedly losing coherence — or coherent but grammatically limited — should not be pushed to Band 7 overall without evidence the weaker criterion also meets Band 7. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

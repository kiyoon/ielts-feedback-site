# Concrete tactics to lift each speaking criterion from Band 6 to Band 7

_Crawled by codex on 2026-05-19T07:32:43Z. Sources cited inline._

## Summary

This file turns the official Band 6 and Band 7 IELTS Speaking descriptors into scoring-time checks for Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, and Pronunciation; IELTS states that Speaking is assessed across an 11-14 minute, three-part interview using these criteria, and British Council's public descriptors define the Band 6-to-7 boundary for each criterion. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

For an automated judge, the practical jump is not "more advanced English" in general: Band 7 requires sustained long turns, flexible cohesion, flexible and topic-aware lexis, frequent error-free complex grammar, and pronunciation features that go beyond merely being understandable. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

## Authoritative material

- IELTS Speaking has three parts in 11-14 minutes: Part 1 asks familiar personal questions, Part 2 gives one minute of preparation and a 1-2 minute long turn, and Part 3 asks more abstract questions connected to Part 2. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://caribbean.britishcouncil.org/exam/ielts/prepare/ielts-test-format

- The four Speaking criteria are Fluency and Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy, and Pronunciation, and IELTS Australia states each criterion contributes 25% of the Speaking score. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Official descriptor note for judging: a candidate must fully fit the positive features of a band, and the final Speaking rating is based on average performance across all parts of the test. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- Fluency and Coherence at Band 6: the candidate can keep going and is willing to produce long turns, but coherence may be lost because of hesitation, repetition, or self-correction; discourse markers and connectives are present but not always appropriate. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsidpindia.com/information/ielts-band-scores/speaking

- Fluency and Coherence at Band 7: the candidate readily produces long turns without noticeable effort, hesitation/repetition/self-correction may appear but does not damage coherence, and spoken discourse markers, connectives, and cohesive features are used flexibly. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Lexical Resource at Band 6: vocabulary is sufficient for extended discussion and meaning is clear despite inappropriate choices; paraphrase is generally successful. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsidpindia.com/information/ielts-band-scores/speaking

- Lexical Resource at Band 7: vocabulary is used flexibly across varied topics, some less common and idiomatic items are used with awareness of style and collocation, inappropriate choices can still occur, and paraphrase is effective when needed. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Grammatical Range and Accuracy at Band 6: the speaker mixes simple and complex sentence forms, but flexibility is limited and complex structures often contain errors that rarely block communication. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsidpindia.com/information/ielts-band-scores/speaking

- Grammatical Range and Accuracy at Band 7: a range of structures is used flexibly, error-free sentences are frequent, and simple and complex sentences are effective even though some errors and a few basic errors may persist. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- Pronunciation at Band 6: a range of phonological features is used with variable control; chunking is generally appropriate, but rhythm can be affected by lack of stress timing or rapid rate, and individual sounds may occasionally reduce clarity while the candidate remains generally understandable. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- Pronunciation at Band 7: the candidate shows all positive Band 6 features plus some positive Band 8 features; the Band 8 features include sustained rhythm, flexible stress and intonation across long utterances, easy understanding throughout, and minimal effect of accent on intelligibility. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- IELTS.org's comparison of Band 6 and Band 7 for productive skills frames Speaking Band 7 as stronger at expressing nuanced opinions, handling follow-up or interruptions, and using broader vocabulary than Band 6. https://ielts.org/news-and-insights/differences-between-ielts-6-and-7-for-productive-skills

## Country-specific prep material

### Korea

- IDP Korea explains the same 11-14 minute, three-part Speaking format, confirms Part 2 has one minute of note preparation and a two-minute talk, and advises that test takers do not need to change accent but should speak clearly at a natural speed. https://ieltskorea.org/korea/prepare/speaking

- Skybel Korea's 6.0-to-7.0 self-study routine tells candidates to self-score each recording against the four official criteria, routine the timing as Part 1 20-30 seconds, Part 2 one-minute prep plus up to two minutes, and Part 3 30-60 seconds, then record, review, and answer the same question again. https://blog.skybel.co.kr/blog/%EC%95%84%EC%9D%B4%EC%97%98%EC%B8%A0%20%EC%8A%A4%ED%94%BC%ED%82%B9/%EC%95%84%EC%9D%B4%EC%97%98%EC%B8%A0-%EC%8A%A4%ED%94%BC%ED%82%B9-%EC%A4%80%EB%B9%84-%EB%8F%85%ED%95%99-%EB%A3%A8%ED%8B%B4%EC%9C%BC%EB%A1%9C-60%EC%97%90%EC%84%9C-70-%EC%8B%A4%EC%A0%84-%EC%A0%90%EC%88%98-%ED%96%A5%EC%83%81-%EC%A0%84%EB%9E%B5-%EA%B3%B5%EA%B0%9C

- Skybel operationalizes Lexical Resource by building topic-collocation cards and replacing vague phrases such as "very big problem" with more precise alternatives such as "a considerable problem"; it operationalizes Pronunciation through sentence-level shadowing, reading aloud, and two-minute mini-presentations. https://blog.skybel.co.kr/blog/%EC%95%84%EC%9D%B4%EC%97%98%EC%B8%A0%20%EC%8A%A4%ED%94%BC%ED%82%B9/%EC%95%84%EC%9D%B4%EC%97%98%EC%B8%A0-%EC%8A%A4%ED%94%BC%ED%82%B9-%EC%A4%80%EB%B9%84-%EB%8F%85%ED%95%99-%EB%A3%A8%ED%8B%B4%EC%9C%BC%EB%A1%9C-60%EC%97%90%EC%84%9C-70-%EC%8B%A4%EC%A0%84-%EC%A0%90%EC%88%98-%ED%96%A5%EC%83%81-%EC%A0%84%EB%9E%B5-%EA%B3%B5%EA%B0%9C

- ELT Korea emphasizes that Part 3 Band 7 answers should generalize beyond the candidate's personal story, use topic collocations such as "mental well-being" and "blurred boundaries," and use discourse markers such as "consequently" and "on the other hand" to organize an argument. https://www.eltschool.jp/ko/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

- ELT Korea warns against overused cliche idioms such as "raining cats and dogs" and "a piece of cake," recommending more natural expressions or topic-relevant collocations instead; for an LLM judge this supports penalizing forced idiom display under style/collocation awareness. https://www.eltschool.jp/ko/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

### China and Chinese-language prep

- The IELTS China public Speaking descriptors translate the same Band 7 boundary: sustained full expression without obvious difficulty, language-search hesitation that does not harm coherence, flexible spoken discourse markers, flexible lexis across topics, less common and idiomatic vocabulary with style/collocation awareness, flexible grammar, and effective paraphrase. https://backoffice.ielts.chinaielts.org/api/assets/ielts-cms/0c5929f5-faad-45ca-83f5-ce5c8384ec95/%E8%AF%84%E5%88%86%E6%A0%87%E5%87%86-speakingfinal.pdf?version=1

- ELT Chinese frames the 6-to-7 jump as a strategy change from "Band 6 rules" to descriptor-based performance: long turns must feel effortless, connectives must be flexible rather than mechanical, lexis must include idiom/collocation with style awareness, and grammar must frequently be error-free. https://www.eltschool.jp/zh/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

- ELT Chinese's Part 3 example highlights a useful scoring signal: a Band 6-style answer remains personal and uses basic repeated vocabulary, while a Band 7-style answer expands the subject to individuals and society, uses topic collocations, and marks logical progression with discourse markers. https://www.eltschool.jp/zh/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

- IELTS International Chinese describes the 6-to-7 speaking move as identifying the precise criterion holding the candidate at 6.0, then drilling the target behavior such as strategic paraphrase and connected speech features including linking, weak forms, stress patterns, and intonation. https://www.ielts.international/zh/improve-ielts-from-6-to-7

### Vietnam

- ZIM Vietnam's Band 7 guide states that Band 7 requires fluency, coherence, rich vocabulary, accurate grammar, and clear pronunciation, and its 6.0-to-7.0 stage asks learners to combine simple and complex sentences, use tenses and connectors flexibly, use natural idioms, and improve stress and intonation. https://zim.vn/ielts-speaking-band-7

- ZIM Vietnam's Lexical Resource 6-to-7 analysis says Band 6 vocabulary is "wide enough" for topics, while Band 7 must cover a variety of topics; it also stresses that effective paraphrase should make the meaning clearer rather than merely harder or more elaborate. https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking

- ZIM Vietnam's phonological-features article says the Band 6-to-7 pronunciation difference is control and flexibility: Band 6 may use stress, intonation, and linking but inconsistently, while Band 7 controls these features better in long sentences and uses them to show emotion or communicative intent. https://zim.vn/phonological-features-trong-ielts-speaking

- ZIM Vietnam gives specific pronunciation drills for the 6-to-7 move: learn word stress with dictionary audio, identify content words for sentence stress, practice rising/falling intonation patterns, and drill English phonemes that Vietnamese speakers often lack, including /theta/ as in "think" and /sh/ as in "she." https://zim.vn/phonological-features-trong-ielts-speaking

- DOL English Vietnam gives non-official but useful operational grammar thresholds for Band 7 practice: use simple and complex sentences flexibly, include relative clauses and conjunctions, target roughly 80% error-free speech, and avoid repeating the same grammar error more than twice. https://www.dolenglish.vn/blog/tieu-chi-grammatical-range-accuracy-trong-ielts-speaking

- Vietop's republished IELTS Simon speaking PDF advises using real examples to extend Part 2 and support Part 3 opinions, not overthinking grammar during live speaking because that damages fluency, and focusing pronunciation on clarity, speed, word stress, sentence stress, and intonation rather than accent imitation. https://vietop.edu.vn/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/IELTS-Speaking-Tips-and-samples-IELTS-Smion.pdf

- WISE English Vietnam describes the 6.0-to-7.0 path as moving from basic correctness to deeper, more natural, more logically connected speaking, with self-recording, role-play interviews, idioms, and examples used to make answers less mechanical. https://wiseenglish.edu.vn/lo-trinh-hoc-ielts-tu-6-0-len-7-0

### Iran and Persian-language prep

- Iran Cambridge lists the four Speaking scoring dimensions as vocabulary, grammatical structure, pronunciation/intonation, and fluency, and advises Band 7 candidates to speak well about any topic, use a range of words appropriately, connect ideas, use idiomatic/non-basic language, produce generally error-free sentences, and control tone and pronunciation. https://www.ircambridge.com/%D9%86%D9%85%D8%B1%D9%87-7-%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%BE%DB%8C%DA%A9%DB%8C%D9%86%DA%AF-%D8%A2%DB%8C%D9%84%D8%AA%D8%B3/

- Iran Cambridge states the Speaking interview has three parts, including a cue-card task with one minute of preparation and two minutes of speaking, and treats Part 3 as a demanding discussion stage; use this as a preparation signal, not as evidence that IELTS weights Part 3 more heavily than the official average-performance rule. https://www.ircambridge.com/%D9%86%D9%85%D8%B1%D9%87-7-%D8%A7%D8%B3%D9%BE%DB%8C%DA%A9%DB%8C%D9%86%DA%AF-%D8%A2%DB%8C%D9%84%D8%AA%D8%B3/ https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- 24Talk Iran reminds learners that Pronunciation and accent are distinct, that IELTS does not award points for having a particular accent, and that intonation and expressive tone matter because they help convey meaning. https://24talk.ir/five-easy-ways-to-increase-your-ielts-speak/

- 24Talk Iran recommends reading the official Speaking band-descriptor PDF, answering the exact question rather than drifting, using stage-appropriate vocabulary, and using linking words to improve coherence; its advice to use slang should be filtered by the official requirement for awareness of style and collocation. https://24talk.ir/five-easy-ways-to-increase-your-ielts-speak/ https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- IELTS2 Persian explains the four descriptors, advises avoiding long pauses and unnecessary repetitions, using connectors such as "for example" and "in contrast," expanding vocabulary with synonyms, using compound/complex sentences, and practicing word stress and intonation with shadowing. https://ielts2.com/understanding-the-ielts-speaking-band-descriptors/

### UK, Australia, and Canada native-perspective material

- IELTS Australia states that idiomatic language and less common words matter at Band 7 and above, but candidates should not use rare words, cliches, or memorized phrases unnaturally; it also highlights paraphrase as a scored skill. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- IELTS Australia identifies common grammar error areas for candidates to become aware of, including articles, prepositions, and subject-verb agreement, and frames Grammatical Range and Accuracy as both correctness and use of simple and complex structures. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- British Council Canada describes the Speaking test as a real-life-style conversation, recommends finding a study partner, and says Part 3 is a deeper two-way discussion based on Part 2, which supports judging Part 3 for abstraction, explanation, and responsiveness rather than prepared monologue only. https://www.ielts.ca/take-ielts/ielts-test-format/ielts-speaking-test/

- IELTS Score 7's 2026 strategy says Band 7 candidates should speak at length, use flexible vocabulary including less common words, produce frequent error-free sentences with complex structures, and be easy to understand with only occasional mispronunciation; its drills include two-minute speaking, pause counting, topic-specific vocabulary, collocations, conditionals, passives, relative clauses, and shadowing. https://ieltsscore7.com/guides/how-to-get-band-7-in-ielts-speaking/

- IELTS Academic's Band 7 tips warn that fluency is more important than producing error-free speech in real time, which is consistent with the official descriptor allowing some grammatical mistakes at Band 7 while requiring sustained coherent speech. https://ielts-academic.com/ielts-speaking-tips/ https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- Rehearse's Band 7 practice guide emphasizes that IELTS Speaking is not testing perfect English but clear, natural communication with enough range for academic/professional contexts; it also warns against playing safe with only simple sentences or forcing random advanced words. https://www.rehearse.to/blog/ielts-speaking-band-7-complete-practice-guide

## Open-source tools and code

- SpeechSuper API Samples is a GitHub sample repository for speech and pronunciation assessment that reports phoneme, syllable, word, sentence, speech-quality, fluency, and IELTS-speaking assessment modes; it is not an official IELTS scoring source, and license/last-updated metadata should be verified in GitHub before reuse. https://github.com/speechsuper/SpeechSuper-API-Samples

- speechocean762 is an open-source non-native English speech corpus for pronunciation assessment with 5,000 utterances from 250 non-native speakers; it is relevant to Pronunciation feature extraction but does not itself encode IELTS Band 6-to-7 decisions. https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.01378

- MultiPA proposes a multi-task pronunciation-assessment model for open responses with sentence-level accuracy, fluency, prosody, and word-level accuracy assessment; it can inspire Pronunciation/fluency features but is not an IELTS official rubric. https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.12490

- GOPT, a transformer-based multi-aspect, multi-granularity non-native pronunciation assessment model, evaluates pronunciation aspects including accuracy, fluency, completeness, and prosody on speechocean762; those categories overlap with IELTS Pronunciation but not with all four Speaking criteria. https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.03432

- "Spoken Grammar Assessment Using LLM" discusses spoken-language assessment systems and ASR-supported grammar assessment, which is relevant to Grammatical Range and Accuracy from transcripts, but the IELTS judge should still anchor final band decisions in official descriptors. https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.01579

## Practical signals for an LLM judge

- FC long-turn signal: in Part 2, a Band 7 candidate should normally sustain most of the 1-2 minute long turn with coherent expansion; if a transcript shows frequent abandoned starts, repeated "I don't know," or very short Part 2 output, that aligns more with Band 6 or below because Band 7 requires long turns without noticeable effort. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- FC repair signal: occasional self-correction that preserves the line of thought is compatible with Band 7, but repeated mid-sentence repair that causes topic drift or loss of coherence is a Band 6 marker. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- FC cohesion signal: reward flexible discourse moves such as cause, contrast, example, concession, and conclusion; cap FC near Band 6 when the candidate relies on repetitive "and/but/because" chains or uses memorized markers in illogical places. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Part 3 abstraction signal: Band 7 candidates should often move from personal anecdotes to broader social, hypothetical, or comparative reasoning in Part 3; answers that remain only personal and concrete may still be fluent but are weaker for coherence and lexical/grammatical range. https://ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-academic-test/ielts-academic-format-speaking https://www.eltschool.jp/zh/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

- LR topic-range signal: Band 7 lexis should include topic-specific collocations and less common lexical items across multiple topics, not only one memorized theme; a useful internal heuristic is at least two natural topic collocations or idiomatic chunks in a 90-second Part 2 answer, but this is a judge heuristic, not an official IELTS count. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking

- LR style signal: reward natural idiomatic language and collocation; penalize obvious cliches, rare words used incorrectly, slang in formal Part 3 contexts, and collocation errors that make style awareness doubtful. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://www.eltschool.jp/ko/column/ielts-speaking-band-7-tips

- LR paraphrase signal: Band 6 can usually paraphrase successfully, but Band 7 paraphrases effectively; in transcripts, look for rewording that clarifies the examiner's question, repairs missing vocabulary, or avoids repetition without changing meaning. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phan-tich-tieu-chi-va-cach-hoc-lexical-resource-cho-band-6-7-ielts-speaking

- GRA range signal: Band 7 requires flexible complex structures and frequent error-free sentences; as an internal transcript heuristic, look for at least three controlled complex-structure types across the test, such as conditionals, relative clauses, subordinate clauses, passives, comparison clauses, or perfect/progressive tenses. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ieltsscore7.com/guides/how-to-get-band-7-in-ielts-speaking/

- GRA accuracy signal: frequent tense, agreement, article, preposition, or word-order errors in nearly every answer point toward Band 6 even when meaning is clear; Band 7 allows persistent mistakes but should still show frequent error-free sentences. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Memorization signal: if the candidate produces polished chunks that do not answer the question, repeats the same template across unrelated topics, or shifts into unnatural scripted style, do not reward the surface complexity as Band 7 flexibility. https://pndielts.in/blog/ielts-speaking-tips-for-band-7/ https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7

- Pronunciation transcript limitation: a diarized transcript alone cannot reliably score stress, rhythm, intonation, linking, or phoneme control; if audio or ASR confidence is absent, mark Pronunciation evidence as limited and avoid inferring Band 7 purely from grammar or vocabulary. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://zim.vn/phonological-features-trong-ielts-speaking

- Pronunciation audio signal: when audio evidence exists, Band 7 should show Band 6 intelligibility plus some Band 8-like features such as controlled sentence stress, appropriate chunking, non-monotone intonation, linking, and rhythm that does not force listener effort. https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf https://vietop.edu.vn/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/IELTS-Speaking-Tips-and-samples-IELTS-Smion.pdf

- Accent signal: do not penalize an L1 accent by itself; penalize only when stress, rhythm, intonation, or individual sounds reduce intelligibility or make meaning hard to follow. https://ieltskorea.org/korea/prepare/speaking https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

- Criterion-balance signal: because each criterion is equally weighted, a transcript that is strong in lexis and grammar but repeatedly loses coherence, or is coherent but too grammatically limited, should not be pushed to Band 7 overall without evidence that the weaker criterion also meets Band 7. https://ielts.com.au/australia/about/news-and-articles/article-speaking-band-6-vs-band-7 https://takeielts.britishcouncil.org/sites/default/files/ielts_speaking_band_descriptors.pdf

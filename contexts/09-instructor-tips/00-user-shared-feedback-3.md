## 1. Verbs that express a figure / proportion (수치를 나타내다)

Use these as the core toolkit for Task 1 chart-description sentences. They are interchangeable in many slots, so rotating them within a single answer demonstrates **lexical range** (a band-7 LR criterion).

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **account for** | X accounts for [percentage / proportion / share] of Y | *Renewable sources accounted for 18 % of total energy production in 2020.* |
| **consist of** | X consists of [components] | *The workforce consisted of three roughly equal groups.* |
| **make up** | X makes up [proportion] of Y | *Women made up just over half of the graduate intake.* |
| **take up** | X takes up [proportion / space] of Y | *Public housing took up the largest share of the city budget.* |
| **constitute** | X constitutes [proportion] of Y | *Imports constituted almost a third of total trade.* |

### Passive forms (instructor's earlier point — see `00-user-shared-feedback.md`)

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| São Paulo took up the largest population. | The largest population was **taken up by** São Paulo. |
| Imports made up 30 % of trade. | 30 % of trade was **made up of** imports. |
| Salaries account for 60 % of costs. | 60 % of costs is **accounted for by** salaries. |
| Three departments constitute the firm. | The firm is **constituted of** three departments. |

### Adjacent verbs worth adding to the same toolkit

These weren't in the user-shared list but pair naturally with the five above when describing figures:

| Verb | Pattern | Note |
|---|---|---|
| represent | X represents [proportion] of Y | very common in academic register |
| comprise | X comprises [components] / X is comprised of [components] | comprise = consist of |
| amount to | X amounts to [figure] | for totals/sums |
| be allocated to | [proportion] is allocated to X | for budget/resource charts |
| be dominated by | Y is dominated by X | when one item is overwhelming |
| be characterised by | Y is characterised by X | for trend/feature description |

### Drop-in patterns

```
- ___ accounted for the [largest / smallest / most significant] proportion of ___
- The figure was made up of ___ , ___ , and ___
- ___ constituted just over [a third / a half / two-thirds] of the total
- The largest share was taken up by ___ , followed by ___
- ___ comprised ___ , ___ , and ___ in roughly equal proportions
```

## 2. "Loads of people" → variety-style quantifier

| Don't (band 5–6 / informal) | Do (band 7) |
|---|---|
| loads of people | **a wide variety of people** |
| loads of people | **an extensive range of people** |

**Why:** *loads of* is conversational. The upgrades signal **diversity**, not just quantity — useful when Task 2 wants you to refer to a heterogeneous group (e.g., "students from different backgrounds", "members of the public").

### Extended bank — quantifying people / groups at band 7

| Common (band 6) | Upgrade (band 7) |
|---|---|
| many people | a significant number of individuals / a sizeable proportion of citizens |
| lots of / loads of people | a wide variety of people / an extensive range of individuals / a broad cross-section of society |
| some people | a segment of the population / a portion of citizens |
| most people | the majority of individuals / the bulk of the population |
| all people | every member of society / the population at large |
| young people | the younger generation / adolescents / youths |
| old people | the elderly / senior citizens / older adults |
| rich people | the affluent / high-income earners / the well-off |
| poor people | low-income groups / the disadvantaged / those in financial hardship |

### When to choose `variety` vs `number`

- Use **a wide variety / an extensive range / a broad spectrum** when emphasising *diversity* (different types of people).
- Use **a significant number / a sizeable proportion / the majority** when emphasising *quantity*.
- Mixing both in one essay shows lexical range:
  > *A significant number of citizens — drawn from a wide variety of socioeconomic backgrounds — have voiced concerns about ___ .*

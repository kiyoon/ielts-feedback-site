## 1. 시원스쿨 로라김 — Paraphrasing 비법 (YouTube)

🔗 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rtd-L-1IofQ

**Original:** "패러프레이징(Paraphrasing)은 기존 문장의 뜻은 같지만 표현을 다르게 하는 것을 의미합니다. Body 문단의 끝부분에서 Topic Sentence를 다시 paraphrase하여 쓰는 Concluding sentence를 작성할 때, 메시지(내용, 주장, 논조)는 같고 표현(문장구조)이 달라야 합니다."

**Translation:** "Paraphrasing means keeping the same meaning but changing the expression. When you re-paraphrase the topic sentence at the end of a body paragraph as a concluding sentence, the message (content, argument, tone) must stay the same while the expression (sentence structure) must differ."

**Takeaway:** Body paragraphs need an internal echo — topic sentence at the start, paraphrased echo at the end. This is **independent of the introduction-paraphrase**. Cram-school standard structure.

## 2. ScoreFactory — Task 1 변화를 나타내는 표현들 (full guide)

🔗 https://scorefactory.io/ielts_board_1/1399035

**Quoted phrase set (Korean instructor's full bank):**

```
- showed an upward trend / showed a downward trend
- hit the highest point / hit the lowest point
- reached a peak / showed some fluctuation / fluctuated wildly
- remained stable / remained static / remained unchanged / stayed constant
- reached a plateau / levelled off / flattened out
```

**Sample sentences (drop-in level):**

```
- "Sales of ice cream flattened out toward the end of the period."
- "Sales of ice cream showed some fluctuation between 2000 and 2008."
- "Car export figures hit the lowest point in 2014."
- "Coal exports showed a downward trend throughout the 10-year period."
- "The number of cars sold levelled off in the last part of the 20-year period."
- "There was a substantial increase in the value of stocks on March 15th."
- "House prices rose dramatically in July."
- "The number of tourists visiting New York fell sharply in October."
- "The number of students studying Economics rose gradually from 2001 to 2010."
- "Interest in environmental issues has risen steadily over the last 10 years."
```

**Takeaway:** This is the canonical Korean cram-school Task 1 verb bank. Every band-7 candidate should know all 15+ phrases by heart.

## 3. ScoreFactory — 7.0을 받을 수 있는 간단한 문법 팁 5가지

🔗 https://scorefactory.io/ielts_board_1/1272238

**Korean instructor's 5-point rule (synthesised):**

1. **이유 절을 쓸 때** `because` 만 반복하지 말고 `as`, `since` 를 섞어 써라.
   *Translation:* When writing reason clauses, don't repeat `because` — mix in `as` and `since`.
2. **5형식 동사, 관계대명사, 분사구문**을 의도적으로 한 문장에 한 개씩 끼워 넣어라.
   *Translation:* Deliberately insert one of: 5-pattern verb / relative pronoun / participle clause per sentence.
3. **수량 표현**은 % / 배수 / hedging adverb 세 가지를 함께 사용하라.
   *Translation:* For quantity expressions, combine percentage / multiplication / hedging adverb.
4. 한국식 사고로 영작하지 말고 **주어/서술어/목적어 순서**로 끊어서 영어 식으로 사고하라.
   *Translation:* Don't translate from Korean syntax — think in English in S-V-O order.
5. 문장을 길게 쓸 때 **분사구 + 콤마**를 쓴 뒤 주절을 이어라.
   *Translation:* For long sentences, use participle phrase + comma, then main clause.

**Takeaway:** The participle-phrase opener is a Korean cram-school favourite. Example: `Considering the rate of urbanisation, governments must …`.

## 4. ScoreFactory — 라이팅에서 쓰지 말아야할 표현 10선

🔗 https://scorefactory.io/ielts_board_1/1161580 (anchor — content paywalled but title and consensus confirmed across cross-references)

**The 10 expressions to AVOID** (cross-referenced with cohesion file 08):

1. "Nowadays" / "In modern society"
2. "I think" / "I believe" (when overused)
3. "Every coin has two sides"
4. "Last but not least"
5. "All in all" (informal)
6. "From my point of view" (acceptable but cliché-overused)
7. "In a nutshell" (informal)
8. "It is a controversial topic"
9. "Generally speaking" (acceptable in moderation)
10. "Many people think" (vague — use "It is widely held that")

**Takeaway:** All ten are markers of band-6 templated writing. Replace every one with the upgrades from file 08.

## 5. 해커스 고숭이 — 라이팅 Task 2 표현 모음

🔗 https://www.gohackers.com/?c=ielts/ielts_info2/ielts_method&type=url&uid=588122

**Korean instructor's Task 2 framework (synthesised):**

### Topic Sentence Templates

**Agree/Disagree:**
- "As [명사] has emerged, some experts/individuals believe that ~ because"
- "However/Therefore I firmly believe that ~"

**Discussion:**
- "Discussing this issue is important because ~"
- "Some people argue that ~"

**Cause & Solutions:**
- "Many experts have become concerned because + [problem]"
- "In response to this problem, ~ minimize"

### Body Paragraph Introducers

```
- "To begin with," (first point)
- "On top of that," (second point)
- "It is commonly known that ~"
- "For example/For instance/In this regard"
- "According to recent research"
```

### Conclusion Patterns

```
- "To sum up, In conclusion"
- "Therefore/Overall, It is clear/evident/apparent that ~"
- "It is important/necessary to ~"
```

### Korean instructor's recommended C1 vocabulary (used as drop-ins)

```
significant · multiple · mandatory · enhance · foster · address · manage · detrimental to
```

**Takeaway:** 고숭이's emphasis: **don't memorise full essays. Build modular templates per essay type.** This is the pan-Korean cram-school philosophy — different from Chinese template-heavy approach.

## 6. 해커스 고우석사 — 독학러를 위한 라이팅 공부법

🔗 https://www.gohackers.com/?c=ielts/ielts_info2/ielts_method&type=url&uid=578236

**Excerpt (synthesised from cross-search summaries):** "여러 종류의 템플릿을 전부 외우기보다 외우기 쉬우면서 고급 단어를 사용할 수 있는 템플릿을 유형별로 하나씩 정해두고, 해당 템플릿에 맞는 문제가 나오면 거의 그대로 사용하는 방식이 단기간에 시험 점수를 얻으려는 목적일 때 효율적입니다."

**Translation:** "Rather than memorising every template, decide on **one easy-to-remember template per essay type that uses advanced vocabulary**, and use it nearly verbatim when a matching question appears. This is efficient if your goal is short-term score improvement."

**Takeaway:** Korean instructor consensus: **one template per question type**. For 4 question types (agree/disagree, discuss, advantage/disadvantage, problem/solution), commit four templates to memory — not 20.

## 7. 파랑땡땡이 — Task 2 작성 노하우

🔗 https://ielts.gohackers.com/?m=bbs&bid=ielts_method&iframe=Y&print=Y&uid=497701

**Korean instructor's structural rule:**

- **Brainstorming time:** 5–10 minutes out of the 40-minute Task 2 block
- **Body structure:** 2 main arguments × 2 supporting points each
- **Sentences per paragraph:** 5–6 (any more risks repetition)
- **Conclusion length:** 30–40 words (concise, restate thesis)

**Takeaway:** Korean cram-school standard time allocation: **5–10 min planning / 25 min writing / 5 min review**. Most candidates spend too long on the introduction.

## 8. 해커스 Jen — 라이팅 고득점 받기 좋은 표현 (YouTube)

🔗 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-zjnzoCwpno (channel-confirmed via search)

**Synthesised key tips (Korean instructor consensus):**

- **Concession sentences are free GRA points** — every essay should have at least one `Convenient/Beneficial as X is, …`
- **Vary sentence length** — short, punchy sentence after a long complex one shows control.
- **Don't open every paragraph with the same word** — Firstly / Secondly / Thirdly = band 6.5 ceiling.

**Sample Jen drop-in (paraphrased):** "House prices rose dramatically in July." → upgrade to "There was a dramatic increase in house prices in July." (passive nominalisation showcases GRA).

## 9. brunch.co.kr — 다사다난했던 나의 아이엘츠 정복 과정 (Chap. 10)

🔗 https://brunch.co.kr/@striveforward/27

**Personal account excerpt (synthesised):** A Korean test-taker's reflection on Writing 7.0 — the breakthrough was switching from "translating Korean ideas" to "thinking in English templates first, then filling content".

**Korean writer's self-flag:** "한국식 사고로 영작하면 관사와 단복수가 무너진다."
*Translation:* "When you write English from Korean thinking, articles and singular/plural collapse."

**Takeaway:** L1 Korean writers should pre-write the **English skeleton sentence** (with articles and plurals) before adding content — not the other way around.

## 10. 해커스 Champ — Jen 박 IELTS 라이팅 스타강사 7.0 비법

🔗 https://champ.hackers.com/?r=champstudy&c=teacher/tc_ielts&tci_id=52

**Star-instructor's three breakthrough tips (synthesised):**

1. **Show your grammar range deliberately** — don't write 12 SVO sentences. Insert one inversion, one cleft, one conditional Type 2 per essay.
2. **Avoid Korean-syntax patterns** — `~를 위해서` ≠ "for to"; use "to / in order to".
3. **Body paragraph "PEEL" structure** — Point / Evidence / Explanation / Link. Strict structure beats vague flair.

## Common Korean test-taker self-reported breakthrough moves

From brunch / 해커스 게시판 / Daum 카페 testimonials:

1. **Stop translating Korean → English mid-sentence.** Pre-write the skeleton, then fill.
2. **Memorise four templates** (one per question type) and adapt rather than improvise.
3. **Always paraphrase the prompt verb + subject + time** in the introduction. Three changes minimum.
4. **Drop one inversion + one cleft per essay.** That's the GRA boost.
5. **Use `as / since` to vary `because`.** This is a one-line fix that markers reward.

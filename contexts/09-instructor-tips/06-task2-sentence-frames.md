# Task 2 Sentence Frames — Drop-in Templates

> **Compiled from:** Korean (해커스 고숭이, scorefactory, 시원스쿨), Chinese (老烤鸭 29 句型, 新东方, 学为贵, zhihu), Vietnamese (IELTS Fighter, Vietop, ZIM), Japanese (solo-ielts).
>
> Drop-in templates for: introduction, thesis, topic sentence, supporting sentence, example, concession, conclusion. Cross-checked across 3+ language regions.

---

## 1. Introduction templates

🇨🇳 zhihu canon: the introduction is a **funnel** — Background → Paraphrase topic → Outline/Thesis.

### Funnel-shaped intro (CN canon)

```
[Background sentence — general context]
[Paraphrase the prompt in your own words]
[Thesis / outline of what the essay will argue]
```

### Universal openers (avoid `Nowadays / In modern society` — band 6)

| Common (band 6) | Upgrade (band 7+) |
|---|---|
| Nowadays | In recent decades, … / In contemporary society, … / Over the past few decades, … |
| In modern society | In today's increasingly interconnected world, … / In an era of rapid X, … |
| Many people think | A common perception is that … / It is widely held that … / There is a prevailing view that … |
| It is a controversial topic | This issue has sparked widespread debate / X has become a contentious topic in recent years |
| There are advantages and disadvantages | This phenomenon brings with it both merits and drawbacks / X is a double-edged sword |

### Type-specific intro frames (KR 고숭이)

#### Agree/Disagree
```
- "It is widely contended that [paraphrased prompt]. While there are valid arguments on both sides, I firmly believe that …"
- "As [issue] has emerged, some hold the view that [opposing position]. However, I am of the opinion that …"
```

#### Discuss both views
```
- "[Background]. While some maintain that [view 1], others contend that [view 2]. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that …"
- "There is an ongoing debate over whether [topic]. This essay will discuss both viewpoints …"
```

#### Advantages/Disadvantages
```
- "[Phenomenon] has become increasingly prevalent in recent years. While this trend brings clear benefits, it also presents notable drawbacks. This essay will explore both sides."
- "The proliferation of [X] has transformed [Y]. The advantages and disadvantages of this development merit careful consideration."
```

#### Problem/Solution / Cause/Solution
```
- "In recent years, [problem] has emerged as a pressing concern. This essay will examine the underlying causes before proposing viable solutions."
- "[Problem] is one of the most acute challenges facing modern society. The roots of this issue can be traced to [cause], and addressing it requires a multi-pronged approach."
```

## 2. Thesis statement templates

🇰🇷 (해커스 Jen): the thesis must **commit clearly**. "I will discuss" is band 6; "I will argue that" is band 7.

| Common | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| I will discuss this | I will argue that … / I will contend that … / This essay will demonstrate that … |
| I think it is good | I am of the opinion that the merits of [X] eclipse its drawbacks |
| I agree | I subscribe to the view that … / I am inclined to agree with the proposition that … |
| I disagree | I take issue with the view that … / I am of a contrary opinion / I beg to differ with this proposition |
| Both are important | Both perspectives carry weight, though [X] holds greater significance |
| This essay will talk about | This essay will examine / explore / scrutinise / interrogate / weigh up |

### Drop-in thesis frames

```
- "This essay will argue that, while [counter-position] has some merit, [main thesis] is the more compelling stance."
- "Although [opposing view] is not without basis, I firmly believe that [thesis]."
- "On balance, I subscribe to the view that the benefits of [X] outweigh the costs, primarily because [reason]."
- "While both [view 1] and [view 2] have valid grounds, this essay will demonstrate that [thesis] offers the more pragmatic approach."
```

## 3. Topic sentence templates

🇨🇳 zhihu p/143568166: the topic sentence must be **general enough** to cover the whole paragraph but **specific enough** to commit to one idea.

### Generic topic-sentence frames

```
- "One compelling reason in favour of [X] is that …"
- "A primary advantage of [X] lies in its capacity to …"
- "Perhaps the most significant argument for [X] concerns …"
- "It is undeniable that [X] plays a pivotal role in …"
- "There is no denying that [X] has profoundly reshaped …"
- "What lends weight to this position is the fact that …"
- "A cornerstone of this argument is …"
- "Central to this debate is the recognition that …"
```

### Topic-sentence by essay type

#### For "advantages" body paragraph
```
- "The most compelling benefit of [X] is that …"
- "Among the merits offered by [X], [specific benefit] stands out."
- "Foremost among the advantages is …"
```

#### For "disadvantages" body paragraph
```
- "The most pressing concern surrounding [X] relates to …"
- "Despite its appeal, [X] carries with it significant drawbacks, the most notable being …"
- "A serious shortcoming of [X] lies in …"
```

#### For "cause" body paragraph
```
- "The principal driver of [problem] is …"
- "At the root of this issue lies …"
- "[Problem] can be traced primarily to …"
- "The most fundamental cause stems from …"
```

#### For "solution" body paragraph
```
- "A viable response to this issue would be to …"
- "One promising remedy lies in …"
- "Tackling [problem] requires a coordinated effort to …"
- "An effective countermeasure would involve …"
```

## 4. Supporting / explanation sentences

🇰🇷 (고숭이): after the topic sentence, two layers of support.

### Layer 1 — Explanation
```
- "This is because …"
- "The reason for this is that …"
- "What underpins this is …"
- "This stems from the fact that …"
- "The rationale behind this is …"
- "Underlying this is the principle that …"
```

### Layer 2 — Consequence / elaboration
```
- "As a consequence, …"
- "This, in turn, leads to …"
- "The knock-on effect is that …"
- "What follows from this is …"
- "By extension, …"
- "The corollary is that …"
- "It thus comes as no surprise that …"
```

```
Topic:        "A primary advantage of public transport lies in its capacity to reduce urban congestion."
Explanation:  "This is because each bus or train can replace dozens of private vehicles, freeing up
              road space and reducing emissions."
Consequence:  "As a consequence, cities with well-developed public transport networks consistently
              report lower commute times and improved air quality."
```

## 5. Example introducer frames

🇨🇳 / 🇰🇷 / 🇻🇳: never start an example with "for example" twice in the same essay.

| Common | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| For example | For instance / To illustrate / A case in point is / By way of example / As an illustration |
| For instance | A telling example is / A clear illustration of this is |
| Such as | including / namely / particularly · for one |
| In China | In countries such as China, … / China, for one, … / Take China as an example: … |
| A study showed | A 2020 study by [X] found that … / Research conducted by [X] revealed that … / Empirical evidence suggests that … |

### Drop-in frames

```
- "A clear illustration of this can be seen in …"
- "Take, for instance, the case of …"
- "This is exemplified by …"
- "A telling example is provided by …"
- "Consider, for example, …"
- "One need only look at … to see this in practice."
- "The case of [X] is particularly instructive in this regard."
```

## 6. Concession / counter-argument frames (the band-7 move)

🇨🇳 (老烤鸭): a band-7 essay **must acknowledge the opposing view** at least once. The concession is a free GRA + TR upgrade.

### Concession openers
```
- "Admittedly, …"
- "Granted, …"
- "It is true that … However, …"
- "While it is the case that … this does not negate the fact that …"
- "Although there is some merit in the argument that …, it nonetheless overlooks …"
- "Critics of this view might argue that … Yet such a position fails to consider …"
- "One could counter that … However, this objection is undermined by …"
- "There are, of course, those who maintain that … Their position, however, is weakened by the fact that …"
```

### Inversion / "Convenient as X is" pattern (CN cram-school favourite)
```
- "Convenient as online shopping is, it cannot fully replace the social experience of in-store browsing."
- "Beneficial as technology may be, its overuse risks eroding face-to-face interaction."
- "Reasonable as this view sounds, it cannot bear closer scrutiny."
```

🇨🇳 source: 朗阁 / 新航道 / 老烤鸭 — this `[Adjective] + as + S + V` inversion is one of the most-recommended showcase patterns. Worth one drop in every essay.

## 7. Linking arguments together

| Function | Drop-in |
|---|---|
| add a reason | What is more / Furthermore / Moreover / In addition / On top of this / Beyond this / Adding to this |
| add a stronger reason | More importantly / Crucially / What is more telling is that / A more compelling argument is that |
| concede then pivot | Granted, … However, … / While …, … nonetheless … / Although …, …none the less … |
| introduce a contrast | By contrast / Conversely / On the other hand / In contradistinction / That said |
| reinforce | Indeed / In fact / What is more / Tellingly |
| limit | To some extent / Within limits / Up to a point |

## 8. Conclusion templates

🇨🇳 / 🇰🇷 (cross-source consensus): the conclusion must **restate the thesis** but never copy the introduction's wording. Two functions: (1) summarise, (2) re-commit to the thesis.

### Conclusion openers
| Common | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| In conclusion | In summary / To recapitulate / On balance / All things considered / To draw the threads together / Taking everything into consideration |
| To sum up | Weighing up the evidence / Bringing the various strands together / On reflection |
| In my opinion | My own view, then, is that … / The position I would defend is that … / I therefore submit that … |

### Drop-in conclusion frames

```
- "In conclusion, while [counter-view] is not without merit, the weight of evidence suggests that [thesis]."
- "On balance, then, the merits of [X] outweigh its drawbacks, particularly when one considers …"
- "To draw the threads together, [restate thesis]. While challenges remain, [solution / outlook]."
- "Weighing up both sides, I am inclined to conclude that …"
- "Taking everything into consideration, [thesis]. It is therefore incumbent upon [actor] to …"
- "Ultimately, the case for [X] rests on [key reason]; until that is addressed, [counter-position] will continue to hold sway."
```

### Closing prediction / call-to-action
```
- "Looking ahead, …"
- "It is to be hoped that …"
- "Should this trend continue, [outcome]."
- "Unless [actor] takes decisive action, [consequence]."
- "Going forward, the priority must be to …"
```

## 9. The 29 high-band sentence patterns (CN 老烤鸭)

🇨🇳 source: laokaoya.com/14609 — the canonical Chinese cram-school list. Drop **at least 3** of these into any Task 2.

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `So + adj + be + S + that …` | "So precious is time that we cannot afford to waste it." |
| 2 | `Adj + as + S + be, S + V` | "Rich as our country is, the quality of life is by no means satisfactory." |
| 3 | `The + comparative + S + V, the + comparative + S + V` | "The harder you work, the more progress you make." |
| 4 | `It is time + S + past tense` | "It is time the authorities took proper steps." |
| 5 | `An advantage of … is that + clause` | "An advantage of solar energy is that it produces no pollution." |
| 6 | `It was not until X that …` | "It was not until recently that the problem was solved." |
| 7 | `S + will be successful + as long as + S + V` | "We will succeed as long as we persist." |
| 8 | `No matter + wh- + clause, …` | "No matter how difficult English may be, you should do your best." |
| 9 | `It's no use / no good + V-ing` | "It's no use crying over spilt milk." |
| 10 | `The reason why … is that …` | "The reason why we plant trees is that they supply fresh air." |
| 11 | `The + superlative + N + that + S + have ever + V` | "Lack of interpersonal skills is one of the most serious defects we have ever seen." |
| 12 | `Nothing is + more + adj + than to + V` | "For parents, nothing is more important than to educate children properly." |
| 13 | `Cannot emphasize the importance of … too much` | "We cannot emphasize the importance of protecting privacy too much." |
| 14 | `There is no denying that + S + V` | "There is no denying that education has declined in practical relevance." |
| 15 | `It is universally acknowledged that …` | "It is universally acknowledged that healing the wounded is a doctor's duty." |
| 16 | `There is no doubt that + clause` | "There is no doubt that the Internet has changed how we communicate." |
| 17 | `By + V-ing, … can …` | "By delegating responsibility to local government, citizens gain better services." |
| 18 | `Enable + O + to + V` | "Community programmes enable government to ease the welfare burden." |
| 19 | `On no account can we + V` | "On no account can we ignore the significance of education." |
| 20 | `It goes without saying that …` | "It goes without saying that family is the cornerstone of society." |
| 21 | `Only by + V-ing/N + can + S + V` (inversion) | "Only by addressing the root causes can we make lasting progress." |
| 22 | `Not only + aux + S + V, but also + S + V` | "Not only does exercise improve health, but it also boosts mental wellbeing." |
| 23 | `Were it not for + N, S + would + V` (inverted conditional) | "Were it not for technology, modern life would be unrecognisable." |
| 24 | `Provided that + clause, …` | "Provided that adequate funding is in place, the project can proceed." |
| 25 | `What + S + V + is + clause` | "What this debate ultimately comes down to is whether …" |
| 26 | `It is a fact that …` | "It is a fact that climate change disproportionately affects developing nations." |
| 27 | `Considering + N, …` | "Considering the rate of innovation, traditional industries face profound disruption." |
| 28 | `Despite + N, S + V` | "Despite considerable investment, the problem persists." |
| 29 | `Given that + clause, …` | "Given that resources are finite, sustainable consumption is non-negotiable." |

## 10. The "skeleton" — putting it all together

🇰🇷 (해커스 Jen 7-sentence framework adapted):

```
[Para 1 — Intro]
  Background sentence (Pattern 15: It is universally acknowledged that …)
  Paraphrased prompt
  Thesis (with concession: While X, I firmly believe Y)

[Para 2 — Body 1, supporting main view]
  Topic sentence (Pattern 11: One of the most compelling reasons is …)
  Explanation (This is because …)
  Example (A clear illustration of this is …)
  Consequence (As a consequence, …)

[Para 3 — Body 2, second support OR concession then pivot]
  Concession opener (Admittedly, … / Reasonable as it may sound, …)
  Pivot (However, this view fails to consider …)
  Stronger reason (More importantly, …)
  Example + consequence

[Para 4 — Conclusion]
  Conclusion opener (On balance, …)
  Restated thesis (paraphrased)
  Closing prediction (Looking ahead, …)
```

## 11. Phrases to AVOID (KR 해커스 + JP solo-ielts)

⚠ These phrases are flagged by every region's instructors as band-6 markers:

- "Nowadays" / "In modern society" (cliché openers)
- "I think", "I believe" (when overused without variation)
- "Every coin has two sides" (cliché in opinion essays)
- "Last but not least" (cliché transition)
- "All in all" (informal closing)
- "From my point of view" (acceptable but overused)
- "In a nutshell" (informal)
- "Generally speaking" (acceptable in moderation, but cliché if every paragraph starts with it)
- "It is a controversial topic that …" (vague opener)

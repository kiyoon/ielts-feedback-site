# Passive-Voice Patterns — Task 1

> **Anchor:** the user-shared `00-user-shared-feedback.md` taught the passive `the largest population was taken up by São Paulo` pattern. This file extends that into a full bank of passive constructions used by Korean, Chinese, and Vietnamese cram schools.

---

## Why passive in Task 1?

🇰🇷 / 🇨🇳 / 🇻🇳 instructor consensus:

1. **Grammar Range and Accuracy (GRA)** explicitly rewards variety. Twelve active sentences in a row caps GRA at 6.0 even with perfect accuracy.
2. Passive **shifts focus to the figure**, which is the actual content of Task 1 — making the writing feel academic rather than narrative.
3. Passive lets you use the **proportion verbs** (`make up / take up / account for / be allocated`) idiomatically.

🇨🇳 (xhd 新航道) note: passive is **most natural** when the subject of an active sentence is a vague "people" or "the chart" — in those cases, passive removes the noise and foregrounds the data.

## 1. The proportion-verb passive family (extends user-shared)

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| X had the largest population | The largest population **was taken up by** X |
| X had a significant share | A significant share **was held by** X |
| X made up 30% | 30% **was made up by** X / X **was made up of** Y % |
| X accounted for 40% | 40% **was accounted for by** X |
| X consisted of three categories | X **was comprised of** three categories |
| X took up 25% of the total | 25% of the total **was taken up by** X |
| X dominated the market | The market **was dominated by** X |
| X was characterised by Y | X **was characterised by** Y (already passive) |
| X was allocated to Y | A budget **was allocated to** Y |
| X represented 50% | 50% **was represented by** X |

```
- The largest share was accounted for by São Paulo, at approximately 35%.
- 40% of household income was taken up by housing costs.
- The transportation budget was largely dominated by road infrastructure.
- The chart was characterised by a sharp upturn in 2010.
```

## 2. The "X is/was + V-ed by Y" frame for charts

The frame the Korean instructor in `00-user-shared-feedback.md` uses:

```
the [superlative] [noun] was [verb-past-participle] by [subject]
the [adjective] [trend noun] was [verb-past-participle] by [subject]
```

Slot fillers:

| Slot | Examples |
|---|---|
| `[superlative]` | largest · smallest · most prominent · most dominant · most marginal · most negligible |
| `[noun]` | population · share · proportion · figure · percentage · amount |
| `[verb-past-participle]` | taken up · made up · accounted for · held · represented · dominated · occupied |
| `[subject]` | the city / the country / the group / São Paulo / the male population |

```
- The most dominant share was accounted for by India among the four nations.
- The most marginal proportion was held by manual labour, at just 5%.
- The largest figure was represented by China in the year 2010.
- The most overwhelming portion was taken up by housing among household expenses.
- The most negligible percentage was accounted for by entertainment expenditure.
```

## 3. Passive of trend verbs — observation/recording

🇨🇳 (gedu.org) Pattern 5:

```
A + [adj] + [trend noun] + was + observed / recorded / witnessed / seen + in + [data]
```

| Active subject | Passive |
|---|---|
| The figure rose sharply | A sharp rise was observed in the figure |
| The number declined steadily | A steady decline was recorded in the number |
| Prices fluctuated | Considerable fluctuation was witnessed in prices |
| Energy consumption surged | A pronounced surge was seen in energy consumption |

```
- A pronounced upward trend was observed in the proportion of female workers.
- A substantial decline was recorded in the consumption of fossil fuels.
- Considerable fluctuation was witnessed in commodity prices throughout the period.
- A modest increase was seen in the number of immigrants between 1990 and 2000.
```

## 4. Passive with "estimate / forecast / project" (extends user-shared point #5)

🇰🇷 source: original `00-user-shared-feedback.md`. Drop-ins for forecasted-vs-actual:

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| They predicted X | X was predicted / forecast(ed) / projected / anticipated |
| They overestimated X | X was overestimated |
| They underestimated X | X was underestimated |
| They expected the figure to reach 50 | The figure was expected / anticipated / projected to reach 50 |
| Actual was lower than forecast | The actual figure fell short of the forecast / undershot the projection |
| Actual was higher than forecast | The actual figure exceeded the forecast / outstripped the projection |

```
- The figure for 2000 was overestimated by approximately 10 million.
- The number of subscribers was projected to reach 5 million but actually fell short of the forecast.
- The actual energy consumption exceeded the projection by 20%.
- The trend was anticipated to plateau but in fact continued to climb.
```

## 5. Passive of "show / illustrate" (intro restructure)

🇻🇳 (vietop / zim) introduction-paraphrase technique:

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| The graph shows the population | The population is shown by the graph / Population figures are illustrated in the graph |
| The chart compares spending | Spending is compared in the chart |
| The diagram explains the process | The process is illustrated in the diagram |
| People in Japan spent time reading | Time was spent on reading by Japanese citizens |

```
- The proportion of energy consumed across four sectors is illustrated in the supplied bar chart.
- Demographic figures for India and China are presented in the given line graph.
- The amount of waste generated per household is set out in the table provided.
```

## 6. Passive of "use / consume" (process diagrams)

For process diagrams (cement-making, water cycle, etc.), passive is **mandatory**. Active forms here sound elementary.

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| Workers mix sand and cement | Sand and cement are mixed |
| The factory grinds the limestone | The limestone is ground / crushed |
| Workers heat the mixture | The mixture is heated |
| Then they pour the liquid into moulds | The liquid is then poured into moulds |
| The product is packaged for shipping | The product is then packaged for shipping |

🇨🇳 instructor note (老烤鸭): in process passages, ~80% of verbs should be passive. Common process verbs in past participle:

```
mixed · ground · crushed · heated · cooled · filtered · refined · packaged ·
distilled · boiled · stirred · combined · separated · transferred · stored ·
distributed · allocated · processed · transformed · converted · purified
```

## 7. The "it" + passive frame

🇻🇳 (vietop): "It is/was + V-ed + that" frame is a free-money grammar upgrade.

| Active | Passive (with "it") |
|---|---|
| People believe that X | It is believed that X |
| People say that X | It is said that X |
| People claim that X | It is claimed that X |
| Studies show that X | It has been shown that X / It is widely shown that X |
| The chart suggests that X | It is suggested by the chart that X |
| The data confirms that X | It is confirmed by the data that X |

```
- It is widely believed that urbanisation will continue to accelerate.
- It has been shown by recent data that household income has stagnated.
- It is forecast that the figure will reach 80 million by 2030.
```

## 8. The "be" + adjective + passive participle (chart description hybrid)

🇰🇷 (해커스 Jen) style — embedded passives that don't sound robotic:

| Construction | Example |
|---|---|
| be characterised by | "The chart is characterised by a steep rise from 2005 onwards." |
| be marked by | "The period is marked by considerable volatility." |
| be dominated by | "The market is dominated by three major firms." |
| be defined by | "The trend is defined by a steady upward trajectory." |
| be punctuated by | "The decline is punctuated by occasional brief recoveries." |
| be accompanied by | "The rise was accompanied by a parallel growth in employment." |

```
- The 1990s are characterised by an unprecedented surge in technology investment.
- The energy mix in 2020 is dominated by fossil fuels, accounting for 70%.
- The consumer trend is marked by a steady shift towards online purchases.
```

## 9. Passive subject = abstract noun (the formal-register move)

🇨🇳 (zhihu p/83528343) advanced move: convert active sentences with vague subjects (`the chart`, `we`, `people`) into passives with **abstract data** as the subject.

| Vague active | Abstract-noun passive |
|---|---|
| The chart shows the change | A change is illustrated / A clear shift is observed |
| We can see growth | Growth is observable / Growth can be seen |
| People bought more cars | An increase in car purchases was recorded |
| Companies invested more | A surge in corporate investment was witnessed |

## 10. ⚠ Common passive errors (CN/KR-flagged)

🇨🇳 (xhd 新航道) instructor list of errors students make when forcing passives:

### Error 1 — passivising intransitive verbs
| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| The figure was risen | The figure rose / increased |
| Pollution was occurred | Pollution occurred |
| The trend was happened | The trend happened / emerged |
| A change was taken place | A change took place |

⚠ `rise / occur / happen / take place / emerge / appear` are **intransitive** — they have no passive form.

### Error 2 — wrong agent
| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| 30% was made up by housing | 30% was accounted for by housing / Housing made up 30% |
| The largest was occupied by China | The largest figure was held by China / China occupied the largest figure |

🇨🇳 instructor: `make up` and `occupy` in passive sound forced unless paired with `by + clear actor`. Prefer `account for / was held by`.

### Error 3 — over-passivising
🇰🇷 (해커스 Jen): never write more than **two passives per paragraph**. Excessive passive sounds robotic and harms Coherence & Cohesion. Rule of thumb: 1 passive per 3 sentences max.

## 12. Pattern stacking — combining passive + comparative + hedge

The band-7 power move: stack passive + comparative + hedging adverb in one clause. Example progression:

| Level | Sentence |
|---|---|
| Band 5 | "São Paulo had a higher population." |
| Band 6 | "São Paulo had a higher population than Mumbai." |
| Band 7 | "The population in São Paulo was **considerably higher** than that in Mumbai." |
| Band 7+ | "The largest population was **taken up by** São Paulo, which exceeded Mumbai's by **a considerable margin**." |
| Band 8 | "The most dominant demographic figure was held by São Paulo, surpassing Mumbai's by approximately 4 million — a margin that **was forecast to narrow** by 2020 but in fact **was overestimated**." |

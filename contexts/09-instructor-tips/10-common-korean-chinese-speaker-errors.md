# Common Korean / Chinese Speaker Errors — Instructor-Flagged

> **Compiled from:** Chinese (新航道, mypitaya, Spiiker, Chinadaily 新东方, earthedu), Korean (scorefactory, brunch, 해커스), and cross-source consensus.
>
> Errors that local instructors flag as **band-7 blockers** for L1-Korean and L1-Chinese writers. Most stem from L1 transfer (Chinese has no articles; Korean has no plural marking; both lack S-V agreement).

---

## A. Article errors (the / a / no article)

🇨🇳 cross-source #1 most-flagged error category. Chinese has **no articles**, so writers either omit or randomly insert.

### A1. Missing article before countable singular noun

⚠ Most common:

| Wrong | Right | Note |
|---|---|---|
| "Teacher asked me to work alone." | "**The/My** teacher asked me to work alone." | countable singular needs determiner |
| "Part-time job offers chance to demonstrate ability." | "**A** part-time job offers **a** chance to demonstrate **one's** ability." | both nouns need articles |
| "Student should focus on study." | "**A** student should focus on **his/her** studies." | + plural |
| "Government should reduce pollution." | "**The** government should reduce pollution." | "the government" = institutional reference |
| "He has chance to study abroad." | "He has **a/the** chance to study abroad." | "chance" is countable here |

🇰🇷 instructor note (brunch.co.kr): Korean writers tend to omit articles **at sentence beginnings** because Korean has no syntactic equivalent. Audit the first word of every sentence.

### A2. Wrong "the" usage with abstract / general nouns

⚠ Inserting "the" where general meaning is intended:

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "**The** education is important." | "Education is important." (general) |
| "**The** technology has changed our lives." | "Technology has changed our lives." (general) |
| "**The** society is changing." | "Society is changing." (general) |
| "I love **the** music." | "I love music." (in general) |

⚠ Conversely, omitting "the" where specific meaning is required:

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Education provided by government is free." | "**The** education provided by **the** government is free." (specific, with modifier) |
| "Technology of 21st century is advanced." | "**The** technology of **the** 21st century is advanced." |

🇨🇳 instructor rule: if the noun is followed by a defining phrase (`of …`, `in …`, `that …`), use **the**. If the noun stands alone in a general sense, omit.

### A3. Articles with "only / main / same / top / first / last"

🇨🇳 (xhd 新航道) explicit rule:

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "**Only** way to solve this is …" | "**The only** way to solve this is …" |
| "**Main** reason for this is …" | "**The main** reason for this is …" |
| "**Same** thing happened in 2010." | "**The same** thing happened in 2010." |
| "**Best** solution is …" | "**The best** solution is …" |
| "**First** point worth making is …" | "**The first** point worth making is …" |

These adjectives **always trigger "the"** because they presuppose uniqueness.

## B. Plural / countable-uncountable errors

🇨🇳 (xhd) and 🇰🇷-flagged (Korean has no plural -s):

### B1. Missing plural -s

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Many student go abroad." | "Many student**s** go abroad." |
| "There are many problem in society." | "There are many problem**s** in society." |
| "Children spend too much time on social media." (✓ already plural) | — |
| "Different country have different culture." | "Different countries have different cultures." |

### B2. Plural marking on uncountable nouns (the L1 Chinese trap)

⚠ These nouns are uncountable. **No** -s.

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| informations | information |
| advices | advice |
| equipments | equipment |
| furnitures | furniture |
| evidences | evidence |
| researches (when meaning "research generally") | research |
| knowledges | knowledge |
| homeworks | homework |
| feedbacks | feedback |
| accommodations (when general) | accommodation |
| traffics | traffic |
| pollutions | pollution |
| vocabularies (when meaning "general vocabulary") | vocabulary |

To quantify uncountables: `a piece of advice / two pieces of furniture / much information`.

### B3. Number/percentage agreement

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "The number of student**s** **are** increasing." | "The number of students **is** increasing." |
| "A number of student**s is** absent." | "A number of students **are** absent." |

⚠ Rule: `the number of X` = singular; `a number of X` = plural.

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "30% of students **is** female." | "30% of students **are** female." |
| "30% of the population **are** female." | "30% of the population **is** female." (collective, treated as singular) |

⚠ Rule: % of plural noun → plural verb; % of mass/collective → singular.

## C. Subject-verb agreement

🇨🇳 (Spiiker, Pitaya):

### C1. Compound / gerund subjects

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Buying things **make** me happy." | "Buying things **makes** me happy." (gerund subject = singular) |
| "Reading books **are** beneficial." | "Reading books **is** beneficial." |
| "To learn a foreign language **require** effort." | "To learn a foreign language **requires** effort." |

### C2. There be + plural/singular

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "There **is** many problems." | "There **are** many problems." |
| "There **are** a number of countries that …" | ✓ correct |

### C3. Distance subjects

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "The role of teachers in modern schools **are** crucial." | "The role of teachers in modern schools **is** crucial." (subject = role, singular) |
| "The number of children who attend private schools **have** risen." | "The number … **has** risen." |

## D. Tense errors

🇰🇷 / 🇨🇳: time markers in source language often dropped.

### D1. Past + present mix in same context

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "In 2010, the population **is** 50 million and **rose** by 10%." | "In 2010, the population **was** 50 million and **rose** by 10%." |
| "Last year, the policy **is** introduced." | "Last year, the policy **was** introduced." |

### D2. Present perfect vs past simple

| Wrong (KR/CN tendency) | Right |
|---|---|
| "Since 2000, the figure increased." | "Since 2000, the figure **has increased**." (present perfect for `since`) |
| "Over the past decade, the trend rose." | "Over the past decade, the trend **has risen**." |

⚠ Rule: time markers `since / so far / over the past X / in recent years / yet / already / just / ever` trigger present perfect.

### D3. Future for chart projections

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "In 2030, the figure rises to 80." | "In 2030, the figure **is projected / forecast / expected to rise** to 80." |
| "By 2050, the population doubles." | "By 2050, the population **will have doubled**." |

## E. Preposition errors

🇨🇳 (earthedu / xhd): cross-source list of high-frequency errors.

### E1. Verb + wrong preposition

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| provide sb sth | provide sb **with** sth / provide sth **to** sb |
| provide sb for sth | provide sb **with** sth |
| compare A to B (when contrasting) | contrast A **with** B |
| be satisfied **for** | be satisfied **with** |
| listen the music | listen **to** the music |
| discuss **about** | discuss (no preposition) |
| enter **into** the room | enter the room |
| return **back** | return |
| explain **to** sb sth → explain sth **to** sb | explain sth **to** sb |
| suggest sb to do | suggest **that sb (should) do** / suggest **doing** |
| afraid for sth | afraid **of** sth |
| different than | different **from** |

### E2. "Such as" vs "for example" confusion

⚠ 🇨🇳 instructor flag (earthedu):

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "I like languages, such as in French and Italian." | "I like languages, such as French and Italian." |
| "I like subjects, for example physics" (mid-sentence) | "I like subjects such as physics" / "For example, I like physics." (sentence-initial) |

Rule: `such as` is followed by **examples of the noun**; not by complete clauses.

### E3. "Despite / In spite of / Although"

⚠ Single most-flagged confusion (CN):

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Despite **he is rich**, he is unhappy." | "**Although** he is rich, he is unhappy." OR "Despite **his wealth**, he is unhappy." |
| "Despite **of** the rain" | "**Despite** the rain" / "**In spite of** the rain" |
| "Although the rain, …" | "Although **it rained**, …" |
| "Because of **he was late**" | "Because **he was late**" / "Because of **his lateness**" |

🇨🇳 rule: `despite / in spite of / because of / due to` → **noun / -ing**, NOT clause. `although / because / since` → **clause**.

## F. "There be" / existential errors

🇨🇳 most-flagged double-verb error (Pitaya 100505):

### F1. Double predicate after "there be"

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "There are many ways **can improve** education." | "There are many ways **which can improve** education." / "**that can improve**" |
| "For those under 26, there were 80% students study for career." | "… 80% students **who studied** for career." |
| "There is a man **lives** here." | "There is a man **who lives** here." / "There is a man **living** here." |

⚠ Rule: a finite clause can have only one finite verb (predicate). Add a relative pronoun or convert the second verb to participle.

### F2. "It is + adj + for sb to V" misuse

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "We are impossible to make any progress." | "**It is impossible for us** to make any progress." |
| "I am necessary to study harder." | "**It is necessary for me** to study harder." |
| "He is convenient to come." | "**It is convenient for him** to come." |

⚠ Rule: adjectives like `impossible / necessary / convenient / important` describe the **action**, not the person. Use `It is + adj + for sb + to V`.

## G. Word-form / part-of-speech errors

🇨🇳 (Spiiker):

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "using new energy to **instead of** the traditional energy" | "using new energy to **replace** traditional energy" |
| "He **succeed** in passing the exam." | "He **succeeded** in passing the exam." (succeed is verb; success is noun) |
| "**Effective** of medicine is high." | "The **effectiveness** of medicine is high." (use noun) |
| "We need to **environment** protection." | "We need to **protect** the environment." / "**Environmental** protection is needed." |
| "He is **interesting** in music." | "He is **interested** in music." |
| "The **influent** of media" | "The **influence** of media" |

## H. Modal-verb errors

🇨🇳 (xhd):

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "We **can to** improve …" | "We **can** improve …" (no "to" after modals) |
| "He **must to** study harder." | "He **must** study harder." |
| "Government should **to** invest more." | "Government should invest more." |
| "He may **goes** there." | "He may **go** there." (modal + base form) |

## I. Comma splice / sentence-fragment errors

🇨🇳 (Pitaya, Spiiker):

### I1. Comma splice (linking two independent clauses with only a comma)

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Education is important, it shapes our future." | "Education is important. It shapes our future." OR "Education is important; it shapes our future." OR "Education is important **because** it shapes our future." |
| "The graph shows a rise, then a fall, then a rise." | (✓ if items are parallel; otherwise repunctuate) |

### I2. Fragment after subordinating conjunction

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "I support online learning. **Because** it is flexible." | "I support online learning **because** it is flexible." (or "I support online learning. **This is because** it is flexible.") |

🇨🇳 rule: `because / although / since / when / if` introduce **dependent clauses**; never let a `because`-clause stand alone as a sentence.

## K. Korean-specific patterns

🇰🇷 brunch / scorefactory / 해커스 + cross-source:

| Korean L1 transfer | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| `~를 위해` (for the sake of) | "for help students" | "**to help** students" / "**in order to help** students" |
| `~함으로써` (by V-ing) | "by **to study**" | "**by studying**" |
| `많은 + uncountable` | "many information" | "**much** information" / "**a lot of** information" |
| Topic-drop (subject omission) | "Is important." | "**It** is important." |
| Discourse markers untranslated | "actually, …" / "like, …" | omit or replace with "in fact" |
| `그리고` overuse | every sentence starts with "And" | use furthermore / moreover / additionally |

## L. Chinese-specific patterns

🇨🇳:

| Chinese L1 transfer | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| `把字句` (object fronting) | "students should the subject choose" | "students should **choose the subject**" |
| `没有 + N + V` (negative existential) | "no people can solve this" | "**no one** can solve this" |
| `因为…所以…` double | "Because A, **so** B." | "Because A, B." OR "A, **so** B." (only one) |
| `虽然…但是…` double | "Although A, **but** B." | "Although A, B." OR "A, **but** B." (only one) |
| `~多/少` adverb-as-adjective | "people earn **more money**" mid-clause | "people earn **more**" / "people earn **more** money" |

## M. False friends / misused "academic" words

🇨🇳 (chinadaily):

| Wrong word | Better |
|---|---|
| "good thing" | "a positive development / a benefit" |
| "thing" / "things" (vague) | "factor / aspect / element / consideration" |
| "stuff" | omit or use "material / content / items" |
| "bad" | "detrimental / harmful / adverse / negative" |
| "people" (overused) | "individuals / citizens / residents" |
| "society" (overused) | "the community / the public / the population" |
| "guys" | informal, never use in writing |
| "kids" | "children / adolescents / young people" |
| "a lot of" | "numerous / a multitude of / a great deal of" |
| "very + adj" | use a stronger adjective: "very good" → "excellent" |

## N. The "audit pass" — top 10 errors to scan for

When marking, scan for these in order — they are the highest-yield fixes:

1. ⚠ **Article omission/insertion** (esp. with abstract nouns or "the only/main/best")
2. ⚠ **Missing plural -s** on countable nouns
3. ⚠ **Plural -s on uncountables** (informations, advices, equipments)
4. ⚠ **Subject-verb agreement** with `the number of / a number of / 30% of`
5. ⚠ **`Despite / Although / Because` confusion** (clause vs noun)
6. ⚠ **Double predicate** after `there be` / no relative pronoun
7. ⚠ **Comma splice** between independent clauses
8. ⚠ **Tense backshift** in past contexts ("Last year, … is …")
9. ⚠ **Present perfect missing** with `since / over the past / in recent years`
10. ⚠ **Wrong preposition** with `provide / compare / discuss / suggest / different / satisfied`

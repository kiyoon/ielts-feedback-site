# Superlative & Comparative Upgrades — Synonym Banks + Hedging-Adverb Rule

> **Anchor:** the user-shared `00-user-shared-feedback.md` taught two key rules:
> 1. Don't say "the largest" — say "the most dominant / overwhelming / prominent / significant".
> 2. Always pair every comparative with a hedging adverb: `slightly / considerably / relatively / significantly higher`.
>
> This file is the full bank of synonyms and the calibration table for adverbs.

## 3. The hedging-adverb master table

🇰🇷 user-shared rule: **every comparative needs a calibration adverb**. Bare "higher / lower / more / less" reads as band 5–6.

### Calibration ranges

| Magnitude | Adverbs | Use when… |
|---|---|---|
| Tiny (under 5%) | marginally · slightly · minimally · negligibly · barely | the difference is small enough that you must hedge — otherwise you over-claim |
| Small (5–15%) | somewhat · moderately · relatively · slightly | a clear gap but not striking |
| Medium (15–30%) | considerably · noticeably · appreciably · substantially | a clearly visible gap |
| Large (30–60%) | significantly · markedly · sharply · dramatically | a striking gap |
| Extreme (>60%) | dramatically · drastically · overwhelmingly · vastly · enormously | when one figure is multiples of the other |

### The pairing rule (drop-in)

```
- "São Paulo's population was [marginally / slightly / considerably / substantially / dramatically] higher than Mumbai's."
- "The figure for renewable energy was [appreciably / noticeably / substantially] greater in 2020 than in 2000."
- "Spending on healthcare was [marginally / slightly / moderately] lower than spending on education."
```

⚠ **Anti-pattern (band 6):** "The population was higher than Mumbai's." — vague, uncalibrated.
✓ **Band 7:** "The population was **considerably higher** than that in Mumbai, exceeding it by approximately 4 million."

## 4. Comparative upgrades

🇨🇳 (新东方 50组) the basic forms have many academic upgrades:

| Common | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| more than | greater than · in excess of · above · over · surpassing |
| less than | fewer than · below · under · short of · falling below |
| higher than | exceeding · surpassing · outstripping · outweighing · outpacing |
| lower than | trailing · falling short of · lagging behind · falling below |
| about the same as | comparable to · on a par with · in line with · roughly equivalent to · level with |
| the same | identical · equivalent · comparable · uniform · consistent |
| different | divergent · disparate · distinct · contrasting |
| twice as much | double · twice the figure of · two-fold the amount of |
| three times as much | triple · threefold · three times the figure of |
| half as much | half · half the figure of · 50% of |

```
- Energy consumption in China exceeded that of India by approximately 30%.
- Spending on housing was on a par with spending on transport in 2010.
- Solar capacity in 2020 was triple the figure recorded a decade earlier.
- The figure for tourism falls short of the projection by some 20%.
```

## 5. Comparative-of-comparison frames

For when you want to compare two things in a single clause:

| Frame | Example |
|---|---|
| `The X was higher in A than in B.` | "The figure was higher in São Paulo than in Mumbai." |
| `A had a [adj]-er [noun] than B.` | "São Paulo had a larger population than Mumbai." |
| `[Subject] in A [verb-ed] [adverb] more/less than [subject] in B.` | "Citizens in São Paulo numbered considerably more than those in Mumbai." |
| `A surpassed B by [margin].` | "São Paulo's population surpassed Mumbai's by 4 million." |
| `In contrast to B, A [verb-ed] [adverb].` | "In contrast to Mumbai, São Paulo experienced sharp demographic growth." |
| `Whereas B [verb-ed], A [verb-ed].` | "Whereas Mumbai grew gradually, São Paulo expanded dramatically." |
| `A [verb-ed], compared with B which [verb-ed].` | "São Paulo grew rapidly, compared with Mumbai which plateaued." |

## 6. The "double comparative" — band-7 grammar showcase

🇨🇳 (老烤鸭 pattern 25): `The + comparative + …, the + comparative + …`

```
- The more children are exposed to digital media, the more dependent they become on it.
- The harder you study, the greater your chance of success.
- The higher the income, the more discretionary spending households tend to allocate.
- The longer the time spent on social media, the more pronounced the effect on attention span.
```

## 7. "Twice as / three times as" — quantified comparison

| Concept | Drop-in |
|---|---|
| ×2 | twice / double / two-fold / 200% / a twofold increase |
| ×3 | three times / triple / threefold / a threefold increase |
| ×4 | four times / quadruple / fourfold |
| ×5 | five times / fivefold |
| ÷2 | half / halved / 50% lower |
| ÷3 | a third / a third of the figure |

Frame:
```
A is/was + [n] times + as + [adjective] + as + B
A is/was + [n] times + the + [noun] + of + B
A had + [n] times + more + [noun] + than + B
A is/was + [n]-fold + [comparative] + than + B
```

```
- The population of São Paulo was almost twice as large as that of Mumbai in 2010.
- Energy consumption in 2020 was three times the figure of 1990.
- China had four times more solar capacity than the United States by 2018.
- The number of subscribers grew tenfold over the decade.
```

## 8. The "X exceeds / falls short of Y by Z" frame

A drop-in academic move that combines magnitude + direction:

```
[A] + exceeded / surpassed / outpaced + [B] + by + [magnitude / margin]
[A] + fell short of / undershot + [B] + by + [margin]
[A] + was eclipsed by / was outpaced by + [B] + by + [margin]
```

```
- China's emissions exceeded those of the US by approximately 25% in 2018.
- Female participation in the workforce fell short of male participation by 12 percentage points.
- The actual figure was outpaced by the projection by some 5 million.
```

## 9. "Compared to" alternatives — full bank

Single-clause:
- compared to / compared with
- in comparison with
- relative to
- in contrast to / in contrast with
- as opposed to
- as against
- vis-à-vis
- versus / vs

Multi-clause:
- whereas
- while
- by contrast
- on the other hand
- conversely
- by comparison

```
- Relative to the previous decade, the figures show marked growth.
- Whereas the male participation rate stagnated, the female rate climbed steadily.
- Vis-à-vis other industrialised nations, Japan's birth rate is exceptionally low.
```

## 11. Composite move — "the most + adj + passive + by + actor + with + figure"

The band-7 power-stack derived from the user-shared file:

```
The + [most + adjective] + [noun] + was + [past participle] + by + [actor] + with + [figure]
```

| Slot | Examples |
|---|---|
| `[most + adj]` | most dominant · most overwhelming · most marginal · most negligible |
| `[noun]` | share · proportion · figure · percentage · portion · slice |
| `[past participle]` | held · taken up · accounted for · represented · occupied · made up |
| `[actor]` | São Paulo · the male population · housing · the 18–25 age group |
| `[figure]` | 35% · approximately 2.5 million · roughly a third |

```
- The most dominant share was held by São Paulo, with approximately 35%.
- The most overwhelming portion was taken up by housing, with around 40% of household income.
- The most marginal proportion was accounted for by entertainment, with a mere 5%.
- The most negligible figure was represented by manual labour, with just 3% of the workforce.
```

## 12. Quick-reference cheat sheet

When marking, look for these three things:

1. ⚠ **Bare "higher / lower / more / less"** → suggest a hedging adverb (`considerably / slightly / dramatically`).
2. ⚠ **"the largest / smallest"** repeated → suggest one of `most dominant / most overwhelming / most marginal / most negligible`.
3. ⚠ **"more than X"** with no quantification → suggest `exceeding X by [margin]`.

# IELTS GT Task 1 — Band 7.0 Letter Samples

Band 7.0 letters fully address every bullet, sustain a single appropriate register throughout, use varied vocabulary and cohesive devices, and contain only minor errors. They feel "complete" — the recipient could act on the letter without needing clarification.

---

## Sample 1: Formal — Faulty work-from-home equipment complaint

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69f810fb7bb8dd0012bc9d3e)
**Letter Type:** Formal (complaint)
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 9 | GRA: 9

### Prompt
You bought a piece of equipment from a company online and it does not work. Write a letter to the company. In your letter:
- Describe the problem with the equipment
- Explain how this problem is affecting your work
- Say what you want the shop or company to do

### Answer
The candidate describes a Magnus Pro adjustable desk with a broken height-adjustment feature stuck between sitting and standing positions. They explain the postural issues and back pain caused, then request a replacement.

### Word Count
~147 words.

### Examiner Comments
**Strengths:**
- Clear structure with greeting and closing.
- Formal, appropriate tone for business correspondence.
- Each paragraph maintains a single main idea.
- Strong vocabulary (LR: 9) and grammar (GRA: 9).

**Improvement Areas:**
- Could elaborate on **when** the desk was purchased and any troubleshooting attempts.
- Could use more polite phrasing for the replacement request.
- Could use stronger linking words (First, Also, As a result, Therefore).

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (7):** All bullets covered but slightly under-developed.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Logical; some linkers thin.
- **Lexical Resource (9):** Excellent.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (9):** Excellent.

### Why this scored 7
Lexical resource and grammar at 9; task achievement and coherence at 7 averages to 7.0. This is a typical Band 7 profile: strong language but task development thin enough to limit TA/CC.

## Sample 2: Semi-formal — Late rent payment to landlord (Band 8.5 — used as Band 7+ target)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69f2c6bc75fa8600118da316)
**Letter Type:** Semi-formal (request)
**Overall Band:** 8.0–8.5 (used as target)

### Prompt
You are unable to pay your rent on time this month. Write a letter to your landlord. In your letter:
- Explain the reason for writing
- Explain why you cannot pay on time
- Say when you will be able to pay

### Answer (key extract)
Marie writes to apologise for being unable to pay rent on time due to unexpected medical expenses involving her grandmother. She commits to paying the full amount by the 15th of next month and apologises for the inconvenience.

### Word Count
154 words.

### Examiner Comments
**Strengths:**
- Answers all three parts clearly.
- Polite tone appropriate for landlord.
- Clear start, middle, and end.

**Areas for Improvement:**
- State dates more explicitly than "the 15th of next month".
- Make the request more direct: "Could I pay on the 15th?"
- Add linking words like "Because of this" for smoother transitions.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (8):** Comprehensive, polite, complete.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (8):** Strong logical flow.
- **Lexical Resource (9):** Sophisticated yet appropriate.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (9):** Excellent.

### Why this is a strong Band 7+ model
Specific reason given (grandmother's medical bills), specific date for payment, polite-but-direct request, sustained semi-formal register, no errors, varied sentence structures.

## Sample 7: Formal — Job application (formal letter)

**Source:** ieltsadvantage.com formal letter samples (Band 8/9 model)
**Letter Type:** Formal (application)
**Overall Band:** 8 (model — Band 7 target)

### Prompt (typical)
You have seen an advertisement for an internship position at a magazine. Write a formal letter to the editor applying for the internship. In your letter:
- Explain why you are writing
- Describe your relevant qualifications and experience
- Say when you would be available

### Answer (Band 7+ features)
"Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to apply for the editorial internship advertised in this month's edition of *The Westside Review*. I am currently studying English Literature at the University of Manchester and have a strong interest in pursuing a career in print journalism.

In terms of relevant experience, I have spent the past two years as features editor of my university magazine, *The Mancunion*, where I am responsible for commissioning and editing articles, liaising with contributors, and overseeing the layout for our weekly print edition. Prior to this role, I completed a two-week work placement at *The Manchester Evening News*, gaining valuable insight into the daily operations of a regional newsroom.

I am available to begin the internship from 15 June and could commit to the full twelve-week placement. I would be delighted to attend an interview at your convenience.

Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to hearing from you.

Yours faithfully,
[Full Name]"

### Word Count
~175 words.

### Why this works at Band 7+
- "Yours faithfully" matches "Dear Sir or Madam" (both unknown recipient).
- Specific details: magazine name, university, dates.
- Active verbs in CV style: "commissioning", "editing", "liaising", "overseeing".
- Polite but assertive ("I would be delighted").
- No contractions, no informal vocabulary.
- Consistent formal register throughout.

## Sample 8: Informal — Asking a friend to look after a dog

**Source:** ieltsadvantage.com (Band 8/9 model — Band 7 target)
**Letter Type:** Informal (request)

### Prompt
You are going on holiday next month and need a friend to look after your dog. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Explain why you are writing
- Describe what your dog needs
- Suggest something to repay the favour

### Answer (Band 7+ features)
"Hi Sarah,

Hope you and the family are doing brilliantly! I'm writing because I need to ask you a massive favour. As you know, Tom and I are heading off to Italy from the 12th to the 26th of June for a much-needed break, and we're stuck for someone to look after Charlie. I immediately thought of you — he absolutely adores you, and you've always said he's no trouble at all.

He's pretty low-maintenance, to be honest. He just needs two walks a day (one in the morning and one before bed), his dinner around 6 pm, and a bit of company in the evenings — a cuddle on the sofa is usually all he asks for. There's a bag of his food in the cupboard, more than enough for two weeks.

To say thank you, I'd love to bring you back a really nice bottle of Italian wine, or maybe even take you and Mark out for dinner once we're back. Just say the word!

Hope this isn't too cheeky to ask. Let me know whenever suits you.

Lots of love,
Anna"

### Word Count
~205 words.

### Why this works at Band 7+
- Sustained informal tone with contractions throughout ("I'm", "we're", "he's", "don't").
- Specific dates ("12th to 26th of June").
- Personal warmth ("he absolutely adores you").
- Idiomatic expressions ("a much-needed break", "stuck for someone", "Just say the word!").
- Concrete care details (walks, dinner time, food location).
- Appropriate offer of repayment.
- "Lots of love" sign-off matches the friendship.

## Sample 9: Semi-formal — Complaint about noise to landlord (Band 7+ model)

**Source:** Composite Band 7+ pattern (writing9 / ieltsbuddy)
**Letter Type:** Formal/semi-formal
**Overall Band:** 7.0 target

### Prompt
The flat above yours is causing serious noise problems. Write a letter to your landlord. In your letter:
- Describe the noise problem
- Explain how it is affecting your quality of life
- Suggest what should be done

### Answer (Band 7 model)
"Dear Mr Thompson,

I am writing to bring to your attention a persistent noise problem from the flat directly above mine, number 4B, which has now been ongoing for over three weeks.

The disturbance consists of loud music, often accompanied by shouting and what sounds like furniture being dragged across the floor, typically beginning around 11 pm and continuing well past 1 am. This is happening on most weeknights, not only on weekends as one might tolerate. As a result, I have been struggling to sleep and my work has begun to suffer — last week I had to take two days of sick leave because of exhaustion. I have raised the matter politely with the tenants in 4B on two occasions, but the situation has not improved.

I would be grateful if you could speak to the tenants formally and remind them of the building's quiet-hours policy, which I understand runs from 10 pm to 7 am. If the disturbance persists for another week, I would request that more formal action be considered.

Thank you for your attention to this matter. I look forward to your prompt response.

Yours sincerely,
James Wright"

### Word Count
~205 words.

### Why this works at Band 7+
- **Specific details:** flat number, timing, duration, sleep impact, sick leave, prior conversations.
- **Sustained polite-but-firm tone** — "I would be grateful", "I would request", "I look forward to your prompt response".
- **Implicit deadline** — "if the disturbance persists for another week".
- **Appropriate sign-off** — "Yours sincerely" because Mr Thompson is named.
- **Linking variety** — "as a result", "however" (implicit through structure), "if".
- **No contractions, no informal vocabulary.**

## Sample 10: Informal — Apology to a friend for missed event (Band 7+ model)

**Source:** Composite Band 7+ pattern
**Letter Type:** Informal (apology)
**Overall Band:** 7.0

### Prompt
You missed your best friend's wedding last weekend. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Apologise for missing the wedding
- Explain why you couldn't attend
- Suggest a way to make it up

### Answer (Band 7+ model)
"Hi Emma,

I'm so, so sorry I couldn't make it to your big day on Saturday — I've been gutted about it all week. I can only imagine how special the day must have been, and I'm absolutely kicking myself for missing it.

The reason I had to bail at the last minute is that my mum was rushed into hospital on Friday evening with a suspected heart attack. Thankfully, it turned out to be a less serious arrhythmia, but the doctors kept her in overnight for observation and I couldn't bear to leave her side. I tried to call you on Saturday morning to explain, but I knew you'd be in the middle of getting ready and didn't want to add to the stress.

To make it up to you and James, how about I treat the two of you to a really nice dinner next Friday — your favourite Italian place in town? I've heard the wedding photos are stunning and I'd love to see them properly with a glass of wine in hand.

Once again, I'm truly sorry. Hope you'll forgive me!

Lots of love,
Lucy"

### Word Count
~210 words.

### Why this works at Band 7+
- Genuine, sustained informal tone.
- Strong emotional vocabulary ("gutted", "kicking myself", "couldn't bear").
- Concrete reason with detail (Friday evening, suspected heart attack, arrhythmia, overnight observation).
- Specific repayment offer (dinner, place, day).
- Idioms used naturally ("bail at the last minute", "kicking myself", "couldn't bear to leave her side").
- Closing personalised — uses partner's name (James) showing relationship knowledge.
- Sign-off "Lots of love" matches the close friendship.

## Common Band 7.0 Profile (letters)

Band 7 letter writers consistently:

1. **Write 170–210 words** — comfortably above 150, allowing real development.
2. **Sustain a single register** — formal letters are formal throughout, informal letters informal throughout. **No tone slips.**
3. **Address each bullet with concrete specifics** — dates, names, places, amounts, durations.
4. **Use register-appropriate vocabulary** — formal: "request, inform, advise, appreciate, kindly, at your earliest convenience"; informal: "can't wait, give me a shout, drop by, lots of love".
5. **Vary cohesive devices** — formal: "furthermore, in addition, consequently"; informal: "anyway, plus, by the way".
6. **Are mostly grammatically accurate** — minor errors only.
7. **Use the correct sign-off** for the register.
8. **Include polite-but-clear closings** — "I look forward to your prompt response" (formal) / "Can't wait to hear from you" (informal).

**The single biggest 6-to-7 jump:** **specific concrete detail + sustained register**. Band 6 candidates write generically and slip register; Band 7 candidates write as if the recipient is a real person with a real role and the letter has a real outcome.

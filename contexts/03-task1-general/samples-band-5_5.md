# IELTS GT Task 1 — Band 5.5 Letter Samples

Band 5.5 letters typically have major issues with tone, organisation, vocabulary or grammar that prevent them from reaching Band 6. These samples are useful as **negative training examples** — what *not* to award higher than 6.

---

## Sample 1: Formal — Faulty rubbish collection complaint

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com (Letter sample 3217 — Band 5.5)
**Letter Type:** Formal (complaint to local department)
**Overall Band:** 5.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 6 | CC: 6 | LR: 5 | GRA: 5

### Prompt
You have noticed a problem with rubbish collection in your neighbourhood. Write a letter to the local council. In your letter:
- Describe the problem
- Explain how it is affecting residents
- Suggest what should be done

### Original Answer Issues
- "which, used in residential area" — fragment with rogue comma.
- "which has an automatic system" — relative clause fragment.
- "neeighbourhoods" — spelling.
- "remnisent" — spelling (probably "reminiscent" but used incorrectly).
- "apartment" used instead of "department" — major word-choice error.
- Inconsistent capitalisation, sentence fragments.

### Examiner Comments
"There are major errors in vocabulary and grammar which damage the reader's comprehension." Task achievement and coherence both rated 6 (the candidate does cover bullets in a logical order). Lexical resource and grammar both 5. The combination averages to 5.5.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (6):** Bullets covered.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (6):** Logical structure but fragmented sentences.
- **Lexical Resource (5):** Spelling errors, wrong word ("apartment" for "department").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (5):** Sentence fragments, punctuation, verb forms.

### Improved Version (provided)
The 7.0+ rewrite eliminates errors and demonstrates that **clarity beats complexity**. Same content, cleaner sentences, no spelling errors.

## Sample 2: Informal — Letter to friend (Band 5.5 typical)

**Source:** Composite (typical Band 5.5 informal submissions)
**Letter Type:** Informal
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
A friend has invited you to visit them next month. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Accept the invitation
- Suggest activities you can do together
- Ask what you should bring

### Typical Band-5.5 Answer
"Dear my friend, Thank you for your letter. I am happy that you invited me. I will come to your house next month. I want do many things with you. We can go shopping. We can eat food. I don't know what to bring. Please tell me. I am looking forward to seeing you. Yours faithfully, Maria."

### Word Count
~70 words (severely under-length).

### Examiner Comments
- "Dear my friend" — wrong opening; should use a name.
- "Yours faithfully" — wrong sign-off for a friend (should be "Take care" / "Lots of love").
- "I want do" — missing "to".
- Word count under 150 — Task Achievement capped.
- No specific suggestions — fails the "specifics" requirement of bullet 2.
- Tone confused — "Thank you for your letter" is fine, but "Yours faithfully" is too formal.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Severely under-length; tone confused.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy.
- **Lexical Resource:** Very basic.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Multiple basic errors.

### Improvement Notes
Real friend register: "Hi Sarah! I was thrilled to get your invitation — count me in! I can't wait to come over next month. We absolutely have to try the new food market you mentioned — I've heard amazing things. Should I bring anything? Are you well-stocked on wine, or shall I pick some up at the airport? Let me know!"

## Sample 3: Formal — Job application (Band 5.5 typical)

**Source:** Composite Band 5.5 pattern
**Letter Type:** Formal (application)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
You have seen a job advertisement for a customer service position. Write a letter to the manager. In your letter:
- Explain why you are writing
- Describe your experience
- Say when you can start

### Typical Band-5.5 Answer
"Dear sir, I am writting to apply for the job of customer service. I saw your advertise in newspaper. I have experience for 2 year in shop. I can speak english and arabic. I can start work next month. I hope you give me chance. Thank you. Best wishes, Ahmed."

### Word Count
~55 words.

### Examiner Comments
- **Spelling:** "writting" → "writing"; "advertise" → "advertisement".
- **Articles:** "in newspaper" → "in the newspaper"; "experience for 2 year" → "two years of experience".
- **Tone slip:** "give me chance" too informal/begging.
- **Sign-off:** "Best wishes" is too informal for a job application — should be "Yours faithfully" (Sir/Madam) or "Yours sincerely".
- **Vocabulary:** "Job" / "shop" / "customer service" repeated; needs "position", "retail outlet", "client-facing role".
- **Word count:** drastically under 150.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Severely under-length; weak development.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy short sentences.
- **Lexical Resource:** Spelling errors; basic vocabulary.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Multiple errors; no complex sentences.

### Improvement Notes
"Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to apply for the position of Customer Service Representative advertised in *The Daily News* on 5 May. I believe my background and skills make me a strong candidate for this role. Over the past two years, I have worked as a sales assistant at Bright Retail, where I have developed strong interpersonal skills and experience handling customer queries and complaints in both English and Arabic. I am confident that these skills would transfer effectively to a customer-service role. I am available to begin work from 1 June and would be delighted to attend an interview at your convenience. Thank you for considering my application. Yours faithfully, Ahmed Al-Jamil."

## Sample 4: Formal — Complaint without proper structure

**Source:** Composite Band 5.5 pattern
**Letter Type:** Formal (complaint)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
You bought a TV but it doesn't work. Write a letter to the company. In your letter:
- Give details about the purchase
- Describe the problem
- Say what you want them to do

### Typical Band-5.5 Answer
"Dear Sir, I am writing about TV. I bought it last week. It is not working. The screen is black. I want you fix it or give me money back. This is very bad. I am very angry. Please solve this problem fast. Thank you. Yours faithfully, Carlos."

### Word Count
~50 words.

### Examiner Comments
- **Tone slip:** "I am very angry" / "This is very bad" — emotional language is too informal/aggressive for formal complaint. Should use "I am extremely disappointed" / "this is unacceptable".
- "I want you fix it" — missing "to".
- "Give me money back" → "issue a full refund".
- No purchase details (model, date, price, store, order number).
- Word count drastically under 150.
- "Very" 2× — should be intensified with stronger adjectives.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length; lacks specifics.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** No real paragraphs.
- **Lexical Resource:** Emotional and basic vocabulary.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Missing infinitive markers, articles.

### Improvement Notes
Use formal complaint vocabulary: "I am writing to lodge a formal complaint regarding…", "the unit is clearly unfit for purpose…", "I would expect either a full refund or a replacement unit within seven working days." Add purchase reference, date, price.

## Sample 5: Informal — Apology with wrong register

**Source:** Composite Band 5.5 pattern
**Letter Type:** Informal (apology — but written too formally)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
You missed your friend's wedding. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Apologise
- Explain why you missed the event
- Suggest how to make it up

### Typical Band-5.5 Answer
"Dear Sarah, I am writing this letter to apologise for not attending your wedding. I am very sorry. The reason for my absence was that I had a very important meeting at work. I could not cancel it. To compensate, I would like to invite you to dinner. Please inform me of your availability. I look forward to your reply. Yours sincerely, John."

### Word Count
~75 words.

### Examiner Comments
- **Tone catastrophic:** This is to a friend's wedding. The formal register ("I am writing this letter to apologise", "the reason for my absence", "Please inform me of your availability", "Yours sincerely") is completely wrong.
- **No emotional warmth** — sounds like a business communication.
- **Reason is weak** — "an important meeting" is rarely a valid reason to miss a wedding; needs more compelling reason.
- **Word count under 150.**

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Tone is fundamentally wrong for the recipient — TA capped at 5.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical but cold.
- **Lexical Resource:** Inappropriate register vocabulary.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Accurate, but accuracy doesn't save the wrong register.

### Improvement Notes
"Hi Sarah, I'm absolutely gutted about missing your big day — I haven't stopped thinking about it. I'm so, so sorry. The reason I couldn't make it is that my dad ended up in hospital on Friday evening… [etc.]" Sign off "Lots of love, John".

**This sample illustrates the most important GT Task 1 lesson: register match. A perfectly grammatical letter in the wrong register scores below a less polished but correctly-toned letter.**

## Sample 6: Formal — Late rent (Band 5.5 typical)

**Source:** Composite Band 5.5 pattern
**Letter Type:** Semi-formal (request to landlord)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt
You can't pay the rent on time this month. Write a letter to your landlord.

### Typical Band-5.5 Answer
"Dear Mr. Smith, How are you? I write this letter to tell you that I can't pay rent this month. I have problem with money because my brother loose his job. I will pay you next month. Sorry for problem. Thanks. Bye, Marco."

### Word Count
~50 words.

### Examiner Comments
- **Opening "How are you?"** is too casual for a landlord letter — slips toward informal.
- **Contraction "can't"** — semi-formal context discourages contractions.
- **Spelling/grammar:** "loose" → "lost"; "have problem" → "have a problem".
- **Sign-off "Bye"** — informal — wrong register.
- **No specific date** for repayment.
- **Word count drastically under 150.**

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length; tone slips.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy.
- **Lexical Resource:** Basic.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Spelling, articles, missing "to", verb form.

### Improvement Notes
"Dear Mr Smith, I am writing to inform you of an unforeseen difficulty with this month's rent payment. Unfortunately, my brother lost his job last week, and I have temporarily taken on some of his living expenses while he searches for new employment. I would be grateful if you could allow me until the 20th of this month to settle the full amount. I will of course pay any late fee that may apply. Thank you for your understanding. Yours sincerely, Marco."

## Common Band 5.5 Profile (letters)

Band 5.5 letter writers typically show:

1. **Severe under-length** — many responses are 50–80 words instead of 160–190.
2. **Wrong register** — formal sign-offs to friends, informal openings to managers.
3. **Major spelling errors** — including high-frequency words.
4. **Frequent grammar errors** — articles, prepositions, verb forms, agreement.
5. **Missing or weak development of bullet points** — each bullet is one short sentence.
6. **No specific details** — generic statements with no dates, names, places, or amounts.
7. **Emotional or basic vocabulary** — "very angry", "very sad", "very happy".
8. **Mixed tones** — slipping between casual and formal in the same letter.

**Path to Band 6:**
1. **First, hit 150 words.** This alone unlocks Band 6 ceiling on Task Achievement.
2. **Pick a register and stick to it.** Match opening + closing + sign-off.
3. **Add one specific detail per bullet** — date, name, place, number.
4. **Proofread spelling** of high-frequency words ("writing", "advertisement", "experience", "received").
5. **Use the correct opening** (Dear Sir/Madam vs Dear Mr X vs Hi John) and the matching sign-off.

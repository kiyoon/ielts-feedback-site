# IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 — Band 7.0 Samples

Band 7.0 is "good user". The response is clear, well-organised, lexically varied and grammatically accurate, with only minor errors. This file collects samples and patterns at this level — the target for students currently sitting at Band 6.

---

## Sample 1: Line graph — Iceland population age trends (1990–2020)

**Source:** ieltschamp.com (Band 7.0 sample)
**Task Type:** Line graph (5 age brackets, 4 decades)
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
The graph gives information about the age of the population of Iceland between 1990 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

(Five age brackets: 0–14, 15–24, 25–54, 55–64, 65+.)

### Answer (key features)
- 25–54 age group grew from over 30% to 49%.
- 0–14 group fell from 27% to 17%.
- 15–24 group fell from 21% to 7%.
- 55–64 fluctuated between 10–13%.
- 65+ rose from 10% to 15%.

### Word Count
~175 words.

### Examiner Comments
**Strengths:** Clear identification of dominant trends with specific data. Good vocabulary range ("incremental trend", "fluctuating"). Appropriate structure and logical organisation.

**Areas for Improvement:**
- Preposition error: "started at 1990" should be "started in 1990".
- Awkward phrasing: "reaching the climax of 15%" should use "peaked at 15%".
- Subject–verb agreement in one place: "all other age groups show downward trends" (not "are evident to have").
- Smoother transitions between age groups needed.
- Avoid subjective language ("climax") for data description.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Identifies dominant trend (working-age population grew while young populations shrank). Good.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical; transitions could be tighter.
- **Lexical Resource:** Mostly precise; one or two awkward word choices ("climax").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mostly accurate; minor preposition errors.

### Improved Version (suggested fix)
"The 25–54 age bracket expanded substantially, climbing from just over 30% in 1990 to 49% by 2020. By contrast, both younger groups contracted: the 0–14 share fell by 10 percentage points, while the 15–24 share dropped even more steeply, from 21% to 7%."

## Sample 2: Bar chart — Brisk Café food sales (Band 7 version)

**Source:** ieltschamp.com (the Band 6.5 version's Band 7 model answer)
**Task Type:** Bar chart, seasonal
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Same as Sample 1 in the Band 6.5 file — daily sales of food items at Brisk Café by season.

### Answer (Band 7 model — paraphrase)
"The bar chart compares the average daily sales of five food items at Brisk Café across summer and winter. Overall, hot drinks and soup were considerably more popular in winter, whereas cold items dominated summer demand.

Hot coffee was the best-selling item overall, with daily sales reaching 70 servings in winter — almost twice its summer figure of 38. Soup followed a similar seasonal pattern, with winter sales (50 servings) markedly higher than those in summer (15).

By contrast, iced coffee and ice cream peaked in the warmer months. Iced coffee sales rose from just 10 servings in winter to 40 in summer, a fourfold increase. Ice cream showed an even sharper seasonal swing, jumping from 5 to 35 servings. Salad sales also rose modestly in summer, although the gap between the two seasons was smaller than for any other item."

### Word Count
~165 words.

### Examiner Comments
Demonstrates Band 7 features: explicit comparators ("almost twice", "markedly higher", "a fourfold increase", "an even sharper swing"); grouped data (winter-favoured vs. summer-favoured); accurate grammar throughout; varied vocabulary (best-selling, peaked, dominated, rose, jumping).

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** All five items covered with clear grouping; explicit overview.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Each paragraph has a clear topic.
- **Lexical Resource:** Strong precision verbs ("dominated", "peaked", "swung").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Complex comparatives accurate.

## Sample 3: Line graph — Rainfall in Great Britain (ex-examiner reviewed)

**Source:** ieltspodcast.com / ex-IELTS examiner review
**Task Type:** Line graph (rainfall over time)
**Overall Band:** 6.5–7.0 (review version)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 6 | LR: 7 | GR: 7

### Prompt
The line graph shows the average monthly rainfall in Great Britain in 2018, with comparison to a previous benchmark year.

### Answer Issues (from review)
- "The line graph reveals the information of rainfall" — unnatural; should be "illustrates the information about rainfall" or "shows monthly rainfall".
- Inconsistent past-tense usage when describing 2018 data.
- Linking phrases need better connection ("dropped unexpectedly" needs a clearer transition).
- Overview paragraph too short for a Band 7 score.

### Examiner Comments
The candidate is close to Band 7. To cross over, they need:
1. A two-sentence overview (not one).
2. Consistent past tense throughout 2018 data.
3. Smoother linkers between trend descriptions.
4. Replace "the information of" with "information about" / "data on".

### Improved Version
"The line graph illustrates monthly rainfall in Great Britain in 2018, measured in millimetres. Overall, rainfall was concentrated in the autumn and winter months, while spring and early summer were notably drier; the wettest month was October at 110 mm and the driest was May at 35 mm."

## Sample 4: Bar chart — UK visits to/from foreign countries (Band 9-style baseline)

**Source:** ieltsliz.com (Band 9 model — useful as a Band 7+ target)
**Task Type:** Line graph + bar chart
**Overall Band:** 9 (used here as target language for Band 7 students)

### Prompt
The line graph shows visits to and from the UK from 1979 to 1999. The bar chart shows the most popular countries visited by UK residents in 1999.

### Answer (model)
"The line graph illustrates the number of UK residents going abroad and overseas residents visiting the UK between 1979 and 1999, while the bar chart shows the five most popular destinations for UK travellers in 1999.

Overall, both inbound and outbound travel grew over the two decades, but UK residents consistently took more trips abroad than the country received from overseas. Spain was by far the most-visited destination in 1999.

Outbound visits rose steadily, climbing from approximately 15 million in 1979 to over 50 million by 1999. Inbound visits also grew, although less steeply, from around 10 million to just under 30 million. The gap between the two figures widened progressively over the period.

In 1999, UK residents made approximately 9 million trips to Spain, the leading destination. France was second at around 6 million, followed by the USA at just over 3 million. Greece and Turkey trailed at about 2 million and 1.5 million respectively."

### Why this works at Band 7+
- Two-sentence overview captures both the trend and the cross-chart link.
- Vocabulary varies: "rose steadily", "climbing", "grew", "trailed".
- Approximations are appropriate: "approximately", "just under", "around".
- Comparative structures: "less steeply than", "more trips than", "by far the most-visited".

## Sample 5: Process diagram — Silk production (Band 7+ model)

**Source:** ieltsadvantage.com (Band 9 model — used as Band 7 target)
**Task Type:** Process diagram (life cycle + manufacturing)
**Overall Band:** 9 (model)

### Prompt
The diagrams below show the life cycle of the silkworm and the stages in the production of silk cloth.

### Answer (key extract)
"The first diagram illustrates the four stages in the life cycle of the silkworm, while the second shows the eight stages in producing silk cloth from cocoons. Overall, both processes involve a series of clear, sequential stages, with the natural cycle taking around six weeks and the manufacturing process beginning where the silkworm cycle ends.

The life cycle begins when the moth lays its eggs, which then hatch into larvae after roughly ten days. The larvae feed on mulberry leaves for around six weeks before spinning a silk cocoon. From this cocoon, an adult moth eventually emerges, completing the cycle.

In the manufacturing process, cocoons are first selected and then immersed in boiling water to loosen the silk threads. The threads are subsequently unwound and twisted together to form stronger fibres. These fibres are then dyed, dried, and woven into silk cloth on industrial looms before the finished fabric is rolled and despatched."

### Why this works
- **Active voice** for the natural cycle ("the moth lays", "the larvae feed", "an adult moth emerges").
- **Passive voice** for manufacturing ("cocoons are selected", "the threads are unwound", "the fabric is rolled").
- Sequencing varied: "first", "then", "subsequently", "before", "eventually", "completing the cycle".
- Overview links the two diagrams.

## Sample 6: Map — Town development over time (Band 7 model pattern)

**Source:** ieltsliz.com map model (Band 9 — Band 7+ target)
**Task Type:** Two-map / three-map comparison
**Overall Band:** 9 (model)

### Prompt
The maps illustrate the development of Meadowside village and its neighbouring town, Fonton, over a 64-year period (from 1962 to the present).

### Answer (key extract)
"The maps illustrate how Meadowside village and the neighbouring town of Fonton developed over a 64-year period. Overall, while Meadowside has remained largely residential, Fonton has expanded considerably, with new infrastructure and services connecting the two settlements.

In 1962, Meadowside consisted of a small cluster of houses, a church, and farmland to the north. By 1985, the farmland had been replaced by a school and additional housing, and a new road linked the village to Fonton, which itself had gained a railway station and a small commercial centre.

In the present day, Meadowside has grown only marginally, with a few more houses and a community hall, while Fonton has been transformed: the railway station has been expanded, a hospital and shopping mall have been added on the eastern side, and a ring road now encircles the town. The two settlements, once relatively isolated, are now closely connected by both road and rail."

### Why this works
- Past simple ("had been replaced") for past changes; present perfect ("has grown") for changes up to now.
- Compass directions ("to the north", "on the eastern side") not "left/right".
- Change verbs: "had been replaced", "gained", "has been transformed", "have been added", "now encircles".
- Overview captures the asymmetry: village stayed small, town expanded.

## Sample 7: Pie charts — Energy production France 1995 vs 2005

**Source:** ieltsliz.com (Band 9 model — Band 7+ target)
**Task Type:** Two pie charts
**Overall Band:** 9 (model)

### Prompt
The pie charts below compare energy production in France in 1995 and 2005.

### Answer (key extract)
"The two pie charts compare the proportion of energy produced from five different sources in France in 1995 and 2005. Overall, although coal and gas remained the dominant sources in both years, France's energy mix shifted notably towards nuclear power, while reliance on petrol declined.

In 1995, coal and gas together accounted for over half of total production at 29% and 30% respectively. By 2005, gas had grown to 33%, but coal had fallen to 25%. Nuclear power saw the most significant rise, almost doubling from 9% to 20% over the decade.

Meanwhile, petrol was the only source whose share decreased noticeably, falling from 29% to 18%, while 'other' sources also declined slightly from 3% to 4% — remaining a minor contributor in both years."

### Why this works
- Overview names the dominant sources AND the main change.
- Proportion language: "accounted for", "share", "rose", "fell", "remained".
- Specific figures support every claim.
- Grouping by direction of change (rose / fell / stayed) rather than describing each pie separately.

## Sample 8: Mixed charts — Bar + pie example

**Source:** Composite Band 7 pattern (writing9 / ieltsliz)
**Task Type:** Bar + pie
**Overall Band:** 7.0

### Prompt (typical)
The bar chart shows the number of Indian and Chinese restaurants in a country between 1960 and 2015, and the pie chart shows takeaway food preferences in the country.

### Answer (Band-7 features)
"The bar chart illustrates how the number of Indian and Chinese restaurants in the country grew between 1960 and 2015, while the pie chart shows the breakdown of takeaway preferences. Overall, both restaurant types expanded over the 55-year period, with Chinese restaurants consistently outnumbering Indian ones, and Chinese cuisine also being the most popular takeaway choice.

In 1960, there were just 200 Chinese and 100 Indian restaurants. By 2015, both figures had increased substantially, with Chinese restaurants reaching approximately 12,000 and Indian restaurants 8,000. Growth was steady throughout, although Indian restaurants saw a particularly sharp rise after 1990.

The pie chart confirms this preference: Chinese food accounts for 40% of takeaway orders, followed by Indian at 30%. Italian and Thai food make up 15% and 10% respectively, while other cuisines account for the remaining 5%."

### Why this works
- Cross-chart overview integrating both data sets.
- Specific figures in every body sentence.
- Comparative language ("consistently outnumbering", "more popular", "particularly sharp rise").

## Sample 9: Table — Five-country employment statistics

**Source:** Composite Band 7 pattern (writing9 / ielts-buddy table samples)
**Task Type:** Table
**Overall Band:** 7.0

### Prompt (typical)
The table shows employment rates by sector (agriculture, manufacturing, services) in five countries in 2010 and 2020.

### Answer (Band-7 features)
"The table compares the percentage of workers employed in agriculture, manufacturing and services across five countries in 2010 and 2020. Overall, the service sector dominated employment in all five countries in both years, while agriculture's share generally declined, especially in less developed economies.

In the developed economies of the UK and Germany, services already employed over 75% of workers in 2010 and rose marginally further by 2020. Agriculture, by contrast, accounted for less than 3% of employment and continued to shrink. Manufacturing in both countries fell modestly, by around 2 to 4 percentage points.

The picture was more dramatic in India and Vietnam, where agriculture still employed 47% and 41% of workers respectively in 2010, but had fallen to 36% and 32% by 2020. The lost share in agriculture was absorbed primarily by services, which expanded by around 8 percentage points in each country. Brazil sat between these patterns, with a moderate decline in agriculture and a corresponding rise in services."

### Why this works
- Strategic data selection — does not list every cell.
- Logical grouping (developed vs. developing).
- Quantifies change: "by around 8 percentage points", "rose marginally", "fell modestly".

## Sample 10: Map — Hospital road access changes (improved version)

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com (the Band 5.5 sample's "upgraded 7.0+" version)
**Task Type:** Map (two time periods, 2007 vs 2010)
**Overall Band:** 7.0+

### Prompt
The maps illustrate the changes in road access to a city hospital between 2007 and 2010.

### Answer (Band-7 model)
"The two maps illustrate the changes in road access to the city hospital between 2007 and 2010. Overall, the area surrounding the hospital underwent substantial development, with the addition of a ring road, a new car park and a redesigned junction that significantly improved access for both staff and patients.

In 2007, the hospital was accessible only via a single two-lane road approaching from the south, with a small car park located directly in front of the main entrance. To the east of the hospital lay open fields, and to the north was a residential area connected by a narrow side street.

By 2010, the open fields had been replaced by a multi-storey car park linked to the hospital by a covered walkway. A new ring road had been constructed around the hospital, with two roundabouts replacing the original junction, allowing traffic to flow in both directions. The original front car park had been retained but reduced in size, with the saved space converted into a pedestrian plaza."

### Why this works
- Past simple for the 2007 state.
- Past perfect ("had been replaced", "had been constructed") for changes complete by 2010.
- Compass directions throughout.
- Change verbs varied: "replaced by", "constructed", "retained", "converted into".
- Clear overview at the start.

## Common Band 7.0 Profile (synthesis)

What separates Band 7 from Band 6:

1. **A real overview** — 2 sentences, signposted with "Overall", giving 2 grouped key features without numbers.
2. **Explicit comparators** — "almost twice", "markedly higher", "less steeply than", "by contrast", "consistently outnumbering".
3. **Vocabulary variety** — synonyms planned for the chart's main noun; varied trend verbs (climbed / fell / surged / dipped / plateaued).
4. **Strategic grouping** — data clustered into 2–3 themes, not listed item by item.
5. **Mostly accurate grammar** — the occasional minor error is allowed at Band 7; frequent low-level errors push down to 6.5 or lower.
6. **Tense consistency** — past for past data, present for current generic data, future for projections, with no slipping mid-sentence.
7. **160–190 words** — long enough to develop ideas but short enough to control errors.

**The single biggest jump from 6 to 7 is the overview + grouping move.** Band 6 candidates list; Band 7 candidates *select and group*. They look at the chart, decide on the 2 most important stories ("X dominated, Y rose fastest"), and write the response around those stories.

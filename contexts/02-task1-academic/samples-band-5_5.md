# IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 — Band 5.5 Samples

Band 5.5 is "modest" with weaknesses in vocabulary, grammar and sometimes coherence. These samples show the typical errors that prevent students from reaching Band 6. They are useful as **negative examples** for an LLM marker — what *not* to award higher than 6.

---

## Sample 1: Map — Hospital road access (2007 vs 2010)

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com (Report sample 3337 — Band 5.5)
**Task Type:** Map comparison
**Overall Band:** 5.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 5.5 | CC: 5 | LR: 5.5 | GRA: 5

### Prompt
The maps illustrate the changes in road access to a city hospital between 2007 and 2010.

### Original Answer Issues
- "City hospital in,2007" — punctuation error (rogue comma).
- "city hospital developed however, city hospital remained unchanged" — confused, contradictory, and lacks an overview.
- 2007 and 2010 used as adjectives instead of as time references with past tense verbs.
- Word count: 262 words (too long, increasing error rate).

### Word Count
262 words.

### Examiner Comments
The response attempts chronological organisation but the paragraphing is illogical. Numerous grammar errors cluster in every paragraph. The candidate confuses past simple with present perfect. There is no clear overview of the main change.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Some details covered but no overview; contradictory statements.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Paragraphing not logical; abrupt jumps.
- **Lexical Resource:** Limited change vocabulary ("developed", "remained" repeated).
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Tenses, articles, punctuation problems throughout.

### Improved Version (cited)
"The provided maps illustrate the changes in road access to a city hospital. In 2007, … By 2010, …" with consistent past simple, logical paragraphing per time period, and a clear overview.

## Sample 2: Bar chart — Fairtrade coffee and banana sales (5 European countries, 1999–2004)

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com (Report sample 3298 — Band 5.5)
**Task Type:** Bar chart (multi-country comparison)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt
The bar charts compare the sales of Fairtrade-labelled coffee and bananas in five European countries between 1999 and 2004.

### Original Answer Issues
- "the extent of money was paid" — awkward, should be "the amount spent" / "sales figures".
- "had been increased" — should be "had increased" (no passive form needed).
- "heist level" — spelling for "highest level".
- "staggering increased" — should be "staggering increase" (noun, not verb form).
- No clear overview statement.
- Word count: 249 words (over-long; concise version was 179).

### Examiner Comments
Despite adequate data coverage, the response lacks the overview that examiners expect. Lexical imprecisions and spelling mistakes dominate. The candidate attempts strong vocabulary ("staggering") but uses it grammatically wrongly. Comparative structures need work.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Misses an overview; some inaccuracies.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Adequate paragraphing but heavy on detail.
- **Lexical Resource:** Spelling, awkward phrasing ("the extent of money").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Past perfect misuse, wrong word forms.

### Improvement Notes
Replace "the extent of money was paid" with "sales reached / sales of X amounted to Y million euros". Always proofread spellings of high-value words ("highest", "increased", "fluctuated").

## Sample 3: Line graph — Meat and fish consumption in a European country (1979–2004)

**Source:** paramountielts.com (Band 5.5)
**Task Type:** Line graph (4 product types)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt
The line graph compares the consumption of fish and three different kinds of meat in a European country between 1979 and 2004 (grams per person per week).

### Original Answer Issues (typical at this band)
- Mixed tenses (past and present in same sentence).
- "Increase of" instead of "increase in".
- Repetition of "people", "country", "consumption", "graph".
- No clear overview; sometimes the response opens with detail.
- Sentences mostly simple S-V-O.

### Word Count
~150–170 words (often borderline).

### Examiner Comments
The candidate attempts to describe each line but does not group rising vs. falling categories. Tense inconsistency is a major issue (the data is past, but the candidate uses present forms). Comparison vocabulary is missing — listings instead of comparisons.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Lists; no grouping; no overview.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy.
- **Lexical Resource:** Limited; many basic-word repetitions.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Tense, prepositions, articles.

### Improvement Notes
Decide on past simple. Group: "the consumption of beef and lamb fell, whereas chicken consumption rose dramatically; fish remained relatively stable."

## Sample 4: Pie chart — Generic Band 5.5 typical answer

**Source:** Composite (typical writing9/engnovate Band 5.5 submissions)
**Task Type:** Pie chart (two periods)
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
The pie charts show the proportion of household income spent on various items in two countries.

### Typical Answer Pattern
"The pie chart show that food is the highest in country A. In country B food is also high. Country A spent on housing 25% and country B spent housing 35%. Other things are different. Overall, country A and country B spent different on items."

### Examiner Comments
- Subject-verb agreement: "the pie chart show" → "the pie charts show".
- Missing prepositions: "spent housing" → "spent on housing".
- Vague overview: "spent different on items" — uninformative and ungrammatical.
- No specific comparisons.
- Word count usually under 150.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-developed; weak overview.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** No clear paragraphs.
- **Lexical Resource:** Repetitive; basic.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Multiple basic errors.

### Improvement Notes
A genuine overview names the dominant share AND a clear contrast. Always proofread for plural and preposition.

## Sample 5: Process diagram — Typical Band 5.5

**Source:** Composite of typical Band 5.5 process responses
**Task Type:** Process diagram
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Prompt (typical)
The diagram below shows the process of making cement.

### Typical Answer Pattern
"The diagram show how to make cement. First we put limestone and clay. Then crusher. After that mixer mix them. The mixer make powder. Then we heat it. After heating we get cement."

### Examiner Comments
- Active voice instead of passive ("we put" / "the mixer mix" → "the limestone and clay are placed", "they are mixed").
- Missing articles throughout.
- Sequencing relies only on "first / then / after".
- No overview of total stages or start/end.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** No overview; vague stages.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Simple sequencing only.
- **Lexical Resource:** Inadequate process vocabulary.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Subject-verb, voice, articles.

### Improvement Notes
Use passive: "Limestone and clay are first crushed and then mixed in equal proportions. The mixture is heated in a rotary kiln, after which …". Add an overview: "The diagram illustrates a five-stage process by which raw materials are converted into cement."

## Sample 6: Line graph — Generic Band 5.5

**Source:** Composite Band 5.5 line-graph response
**Task Type:** Line graph
**Overall Band:** 5.5

### Typical Answer Pattern
"The graph show the number of cars in 3 countries between 2000 and 2010. In 2000 USA has 100 million cars, China 50 million, India 20 million. In 2005 USA has 120, China 80, India 30. In 2010 USA has 130, China 150, India 40. Overall China increase a lot."

### Examiner Comments
- Tense errors: "USA has" → "the USA had".
- Subject-verb: "the graph show" → "the graph shows".
- Missing units, articles.
- Listing without grouping or comparison.
- Overview is one sentence.
- Likely under 150 words.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Listing only; under-length; weak overview.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** No paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource:** Same verbs repeatedly.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Tense and agreement errors throughout.

### Improvement Notes
Compare: "China overtook the USA between 2005 and 2010, with car numbers tripling from 50 million to 150 million, whereas the US figure rose only modestly."

## Common Band 5.5 Profile

Across these samples, the consistent pattern is:

1. **Frequent low-level grammar errors** — articles, agreement, tense, prepositions, voice.
2. **Limited vocabulary** — same words repeated; sometimes incorrect word forms.
3. **Listing rather than comparing** — data is reported but not grouped.
4. **Missing or weak overview** — examiner expects "Overall, …"; without it, score caps below 6.
5. **Tense inconsistency** — switching between past and present mid-sentence.
6. **Sometimes under 150 words** or padded over 220 with errors.
7. **Spelling errors** on key high-frequency academic words.

**Path to Band 6:** Fix the **overview** (one explicit "Overall, …" sentence), pick **one consistent tense**, and **group** data into "rose / fell / stayed". Even imperfect grammar can score 6 if these three structural moves are in place.

# IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 — Band 6.5 Samples

Band 6.5 is the bridge between "competent" (6) and "good" (7). Candidates show clearer overviews and some lexical/grammatical reach, but errors and repetition still cap the score below 7.

---

## Sample 1: Bar chart — Brisk Café seasonal food sales

**Source:** ieltschamp.com (Band 6.5 sample)
**Task Type:** Bar chart (seasonal comparison)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
The graph shows the average daily sales of selected food items at Brisk Café by season. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

(Items: hot coffee, iced coffee, soup, salad, ice cream — across winter and summer.)

### Answer (extracted)
The candidate identifies hot coffee as the most popular item in winter (70 servings) and iced coffee as the most popular in summer (40 servings). Soup peaks in winter, salad and ice cream peak in summer.

### Word Count
~165 words.

### Examiner Comments
"Generally well-structured" with "logical progression". Vocabulary is "reasonable" but "lacks more varied and sophisticated lexical choices". Grammatical range is good with "minor errors in punctuation and article use". Comparisons could be more explicit and structured. Sentence beginnings are repetitive.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Effectively identifies popular/unpopular items per season; comparisons could be more explicit.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical, but transitions need smoothing; vary sentence beginnings.
- **Lexical Resource:** Use "significantly higher", "markedly lower", "in stark contrast" rather than basic "more / less".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Minor article and punctuation errors.

### Improved Version (suggested)
"Hot coffee was significantly more popular in winter, with daily sales of 70 servings, whereas iced coffee dominated summer demand at 40 servings — its winter equivalent was less than half that figure."

## Sample 2: Line graph — Wheat exports (3 areas, 1985–1990)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fdde28312e6c0011a3e3d1)
**Task Type:** Line graph
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 8 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The graph below shows the wheat export of three different areas from 1985 to 1990. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (key features)
The candidate covers all three regions, with appropriate task tone and a 202-word response.

### Word Count
202 words.

### Examiner Comments
18 grammar mistakes detected. "Fluctuated", "went up", "nearly" repeated. Over-reliance on "however" as the contrast linker. Limited vocabulary variety in describing data. The candidate needs more complex sentence structures.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Complete; suitable tone.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical but linkers thin.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Fluctuated" / "went up" / "nearly" repeated; word repetition score 1/3 (low).
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** 18 errors detected.

### Improvement Notes
Vary fluctuation language: "experienced minor variations", "oscillated between", "showed an unstable pattern". Vary contrast: "by contrast", "on the other hand", "whereas", "while".

## Sample 3: Mixed (line + table) — NZ imports from Australia and Japan

## Sample 4: Bar chart — Country export values (2015 vs 2016)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fe15c1312e6c0011a3e3fd)
**Task Type:** Bar chart + table (5 product categories)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 8 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The chart shows the export values of five products in 2015 and 2016, with a table showing percentage changes year-on-year.

### Answer (key features)
The candidate covers all five products and the percentage changes. Some good vocabulary attempts: "increment", "decrement", "fluctuation" — but accuracy is weak.

### Word Count
219 words across 4 paragraphs (slightly long).

### Examiner Comments
17 grammar mistakes. "Shows" appears 6 times; "products", "jewellery", "billion" used repeatedly. Conclusion is too long and should be condensed. Overview is present but buried.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Complete; conclusion overlong.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Generally logical.
- **Lexical Resource:** Attempts at sophistication ("increment / decrement") are awkward — prefer "rise / decline".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Subject-verb agreement, articles, punctuation.

### Improvement Notes
"Increment" is technical/uncommon; in IELTS Task 1 use "increase" or "growth". Trim conclusion (Task 1 needs an overview, not a conclusion).

## Sample 5: Line graph — Asian urbanisation (1970–2040)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fe50af312e6c0011a3e410)
**Task Type:** Line graph (4 countries, 70-year span with projection)
**Overall Band:** 7.5 listed (but with strong 6.5/7.0 features in lexical area worth studying)
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 8 | LR: 4 | GRA: 9

### Prompt
The graph shows the percentage of population living in urban areas in four Asian countries (Malaysia, Indonesia, Philippines, Thailand) from 1970 to 2020 with projections to 2040.

### Answer (key features)
Malaysia: dramatic increase. Indonesia: rapid catch-up growth. Philippines: gradual rise. Thailand: comparable patterns.

### Word Count
~210 words.

### Examiner Comments
Strong grammar (9.0) and structure. The persistent issue is **vocabulary repetition**: "percentage" 6×, "figure" 2×, "proportion" 2×. "However" repeated. "Nearly", "around", "gradually", "fluctuations" each used twice. This is the classic Band 6.5 (LR) profile despite high grammar.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Excellent.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Strong.
- **Lexical Resource:** Pervasive repetition is the bottleneck.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Strong.

### Improvement Notes
Plan synonyms before writing: percentage → proportion / share / figure / rate. Avoid using two of these in the same sentence; rotate them across paragraphs.

## Sample 6: Process diagram — Book publishing (9 stages)

**Source:** engnovate.com (https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-1-essays/the-diagram-below-shows-the-various-stages-involved-in-publishing-a-book-65dc6be2b0b7b/)
**Task Type:** Process diagram (man-made)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GR: 7

### Prompt
The diagram below shows the various stages involved in publishing a book.

### Answer (key features)
Describes nine sequential stages from writing the script to delivery to bookshops.

### Word Count
~170 words.

### Examiner Comments
The candidate mentions nine stages where the diagram shows eight — a factual error. "Script" used 3× — needs synonyms ("manuscript", "draft"). Linking words limited. Some grammatical errors and punctuation issues.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Factual inaccuracy in stage count.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Repetitive linkers ("then", "after that"); needs more variety.
- **Lexical Resource:** Imprecise publishing vocabulary; "manuscript / proofreading / typesetting / printing / binding / distribution" should be used.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mix of structures; punctuation issues.

### Improvement Notes
Use the passive consistently: "the manuscript is edited", "proofs are checked", "the book is then bound". Vary sequencing: "initially", "subsequently", "in the next stage", "finally".

## Sample 7: Mixed (table + line) — Household appliances and housework hours

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com Band 6.5 sample (report 3314)
**Task Type:** Bar chart + line graph (1920–2019)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 6 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
The charts below show electrical appliance ownership and the average number of housework hours per week from 1920 to 2019.

### Answer (key features)
The student identifies the inverse relationship: as appliance ownership rose, housework hours fell.

### Word Count
218 words.

### Examiner Comments
The main flag: "Each trend reported must be accompanied by details, i.e. numbers." The original uses vague language ("upward trend") without precise figures. Repetitive errors recur.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Good identification of relationship; lacks specific figures.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical.
- **Lexical Resource:** Reasonable but missing precise data hooks.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mostly accurate.

### Improved Version (cited)
"By 1950, ownership began to rise significantly, reaching approximately 50%" and "households spent an average of 40 hours per week on housework" — adding numbers elevates the score.

## Common Band 6.5 Profile

- Task Achievement and Coherence usually 7+ (sometimes 8–9 from the platform).
- Lexical Resource is the most common bottleneck (4–6) because of repetition.
- Grammatical Range varies widely (4–7); accuracy in articles, agreement, prepositions is the cap.
- Word counts are healthy (170–220) but sometimes overlong, leading to more errors.

**Upgrade lever to 7:** Synonyms + accuracy + grouping. The candidate already covers the task; they just need to (a) plan 3–4 synonyms for the chart's key noun, (b) proofread for articles and agreement, and (c) group data into trends rather than listing every bar/line.

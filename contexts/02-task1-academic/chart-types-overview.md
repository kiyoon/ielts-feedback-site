# IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 — Chart Types Overview

This guide summarises the language, structure, and tense conventions for each chart type tested in Task 1 Academic. Use it together with the band-specific samples in this folder.

---

## 1. Universal Structure (all chart types)

A reliable Band 6–7 framework:

1. **Introduction (1 sentence)** — paraphrase the prompt (do **not** copy verbatim). Mention chart type, what is shown, location, units, and time period.
2. **Overview (2–3 sentences)** — start with "Overall, …". Identify 2–3 key features (highest/lowest, biggest change, dominant trend). **No specific numbers.**
3. **Body Paragraph 1 (3–5 sentences)** — describe one logical group with specific data.
4. **Body Paragraph 2 (3–5 sentences)** — describe the remaining group with comparison.

Target word count: **160–190**. Going below 150 is penalised. Going far above usually causes more errors.

---

## 2. Line Graph

**Tense:** Past simple if dates are in the past; future (will / is projected to) for projections; present perfect for recent trends up to "now".

**Trend verbs (rise):** rose, climbed, increased, grew, surged, soared, picked up.
**Trend verbs (fall):** fell, declined, dropped, decreased, plummeted, dipped.
**Stability:** remained stable / steady / constant; levelled off; plateaued; held steady.
**Fluctuation:** fluctuated, oscillated, varied.
**Adverbs:** sharply, dramatically, significantly, gradually, slightly, marginally, steadily.
**Noun forms:** a sharp rise, a slight decline, a steady increase, a marginal drop.

**Sentence patterns:**
- "X rose sharply from 20 in 1990 to 50 in 2000."
- "There was a steady increase in X between 1990 and 2000."
- "X experienced a fluctuation, dropping to 15 before recovering to 35."

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Mixing tenses (past + present in same sentence).
- "Climax" / "summit" instead of "peak".
- "Increased of 5%" instead of "increased by 5%".
- "Started at 1990" instead of "started in 1990".

---

## 3. Bar Chart

**Tense:** Past simple for a single year in the past; present simple if the chart shows current/general data; future for projections.

**Comparison language:** more than, less than, twice as many as, three times higher than, the same as, similar to, in contrast, whereas, while, by comparison.
**Superlatives:** the highest, the lowest, the most popular, the least frequent, the largest proportion.
**Quantifiers:** roughly, approximately, just over, just under, slightly above, almost.

**Sentence patterns:**
- "Spending on housing was the highest at 35%, followed by food at 22%."
- "Men were nearly twice as likely as women to choose option A."
- "While 40% of younger respondents agreed, only 12% of older ones did."

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Listing every bar without grouping (no comparison).
- Confusing % with absolute numbers ("50% people" → "50% of people").
- "Higher than" without a comparator ("X was higher" — higher than what?).

---

## 4. Pie Chart

**Tense:** Match the time reference (past for old data, present for "current" generic data).

**Proportion language:** accounts for, makes up, comprises, represents, constitutes, takes up.
**Fraction & percentage:** a quarter (25%), a third (33%), half (50%), two-thirds (66%), three-quarters (75%), a fifth (20%), a tenth (10%), the largest share, the smallest segment, a minority, the majority.

**Sentence patterns:**
- "Coal accounted for the largest share at 38%, followed by gas at 28%."
- "A quarter of household income was spent on rent."
- "Other sources made up just 4% of the total."

**Comparing two pie charts:** Always group changes (what increased / decreased / stayed the same) rather than describing each pie in isolation.

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Repeating "percentage" 5+ times — vary with "proportion / share / segment".
- Forgetting that totals must add to 100% (don't invent numbers).
- Treating two pie charts as unrelated.

---

## 5. Table

**Tense:** Match the data's time reference.

**Strategy:** Tables often look intimidating. **Don't list every cell.** Pick highest, lowest, biggest change, and one or two interesting comparisons.

**Sentence patterns:**
- "The figures for X were considerably higher than those for Y in every category."
- "Among the five products, A had the largest export value at $12 billion, while E had the smallest at $1.2 billion."

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Describing rows one by one with no overview.
- Missing units (saying "20" when the table says "20 million").

---

## 6. Mixed Charts (e.g., line + bar, pie + table)

**Strategy:** Two body paragraphs — one per chart. Make at least one **cross-reference** in the overview ("Overall, while X was rising, Y showed a different pattern…").

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Treating the two charts as unrelated.
- Spending 80% of words on one chart and 20% on the other.

---

## 7. Process Diagram

**Tense:** Present simple **passive** for man-made/manufacturing processes ("the milk **is heated**"). Present simple **active** for natural processes/life cycles ("the larva **grows** into…").

**Sequencing language:** firstly, initially, at the first stage, next, after that, subsequently, following this, then, once X is done, before, prior to, finally, lastly, in the final stage.

**Cause language:** as a result, consequently, which causes, leading to, so that.

**Structure:**
- Introduction: paraphrase, state how many stages, identify start and end.
- Overview: number of stages, where it starts, where it ends, whether linear or cyclical.
- Body 1: stages 1 to (N/2).
- Body 2: stages (N/2)+1 to N.

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Wrong voice (using active for manufacturing).
- Skipping or merging stages.
- No overview of total stages and start/end points.

---

## 8. Map (Changes Over Time)

**Tense:** Past simple if both maps are in the past; present perfect or past simple comparing past → now; future (will be / is to be) for planned changes.

**Change verbs:** was demolished, was knocked down, was replaced by, was converted into, was relocated to, was extended, was constructed, was built, was developed, was modernised, was expanded, was renovated, has been added.

**Location language:** in the north / south / east / west, to the north of, on the eastern side, adjacent to, opposite, next to, beside, surrounded by, in the centre.

**Sentence patterns:**
- "The forest in the south was cleared and replaced by a residential area."
- "A new road was constructed connecting the village to the highway."

**Structure:**
- Introduction: paraphrase, give the location and the period.
- Overview: 2–3 main changes (e.g., "the area became significantly more developed / urbanised / commercial").
- Body 1: describe Map 1 (past) OR group of changes 1.
- Body 2: describe Map 2 (later) OR group of changes 2.

**Common errors at Band 6:**
- Using "left" and "right" instead of compass directions.
- Mixing tenses inconsistently.
- Listing changes with no grouping.

## 9. Quick Reference: Band 6 → Band 7 Upgrade Checklist

| Feature | Band 6 typical | Band 7 target |
|---|---|---|
| Overview | One vague sentence, or missing | 2–3 sentences with 2–3 key features clearly stated, signposted by "Overall, …" |
| Data selection | Lists most data points | Selects 4–6 *key* features and groups them logically |
| Vocabulary | Repeats "increase / percentage / shows" | Uses synonyms (rose, climbed, surged; proportion, share, figure) |
| Grammar | Frequent article/preposition errors | Mostly accurate; only minor errors |
| Comparison | Implicit; reader must infer | Explicit comparators ("compared with", "whereas", "more than twice") |
| Tense | Mixed within sentences | Consistent and correct throughout |
| Linking | Heavy "however / and / also" | Varied: "in contrast", "by comparison", "meanwhile" |

---

## 10. Don'ts (all chart types)

- Do **not** give opinions, recommendations or causes (unless shown in the chart).
- Do **not** copy more than 4 consecutive words from the prompt.
- Do **not** invent data or interpret beyond the chart.
- Do **not** use bullet points, contractions ("don't"), or first person ("I think") in Task 1.
- Do **not** end with a conclusion ("In conclusion…") — Task 1 has an *overview*, not a conclusion.

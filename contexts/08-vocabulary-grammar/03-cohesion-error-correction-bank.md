# Cohesion Error Correction Bank — Atomic Reference (Band 6 → 7)

> Atomic reference for Coherence and Cohesion (CC) errors. The most common path to band-7 in CC is **flexible** use of cohesive devices, **clear referencing**, and **logical paragraphing** — not adding more linkers. Pair with `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md` (strategy) and `contexts/09-instructor-tips/08-cohesion-upgrades-instructor.md` (instructor patterns).
>
> **Reason** tags: `[mech]` = mechanical overuse; `[wrong]` = wrong linker for the relation; `[ref]` = referencing; `[para]` = paragraphing; `[flow]` = sentence flow.

---

## 1. Mechanical linker overuse

> Band-6 essays start *every* paragraph with *Firstly / Secondly / Finally / Moreover / Furthermore*. Band 7 examiners explicitly call this **mechanical** and limit the score. Vary or omit.

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Firstly,** education is important. **Secondly,** health matters. **Thirdly,** … | One key consideration is education. **Equally important is** health. **A further factor is** … | [mech] Avoid 1-2-3 as paragraph starters. | H |
| 2 | **Moreover,** … **Furthermore,** … **In addition,** … *(every paragraph)* | **Beyond this,** … **What is more important, however, is …** **Closely related to this is …** | [mech] Vary addition. | H |
| 3 | **However,** … **However,** … *(repeated)* | **However,** … **That said,** … **By contrast,** … **Conversely,** … | [mech] Rotate contrast linkers. | H |
| 4 | **For example,** … *(every body paragraph)* | **A case in point is …**; **One clear illustration is …**; **Take, for instance, …** | [mech] Vary exemplification. | H |
| 5 | **In conclusion,** I think… **In conclusion,** … *(repeated in the same conclusion)* | **In conclusion,** … *(once)* + **On balance,** *(once if needed)*. | [mech] One conclusion linker. | M |
| 6 | **Finally,** the third reason is… *(in a 3-body essay where you've already had Firstly/Secondly)* | **A final consideration is …** / **One last factor worth noting is …** | [mech] As above. | M |
| 7 | A linker at the start of every sentence (*Therefore, Thus, Hence, So*) | Use linkers only where they signal a real logical relation; otherwise drop. | [mech] One per paragraph average. | H |
| 8 | **Besides,** technology helps us. *(BrE: this sounds informal)* | **In addition,** technology helps us. / **Beyond this,** … | [mech] *Besides* is conversational. | M |
| 9 | **What's more,** … | **Moreover,** / **Furthermore,** / **In addition,** | [reg] *What's more* is conversational. | M |
| 10 | **Plus,** technology saves time. | **Additionally,** technology saves time. | [reg] *Plus* is conversational. | M |
| 11 | **Last but not least,** … | **A further point is …** / **Finally, …** | [mech] Cliché. | H |
| 12 | **All in all,** technology is good. | **On balance,** technology offers more advantages than drawbacks. | [reg] *All in all* is informal. | M |
| 13 | **As we all know,** … | **It is widely acknowledged that …** *(or just delete)* | [reg] Padding. | H |
| 14 | **It's a fact that** technology helps. | **Technology demonstrably contributes to …** *(or delete)* | [reg] Padding. | M |
| 15 | **Needless to say,** … | *(Delete — if it's needless to say, don't say it.)* | [reg] Padding. | M |

## 2. Linker misuse (wrong relation)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pollution is rising. **However,** governments must act. | Pollution is rising. **Therefore,** governments must act. | [wrong] *However* = contrast; here we need cause→action. | H |
| 2 | He studied hard. **Therefore,** he failed. | He studied hard. **However,** he failed. | [wrong] Contrast required. | M |
| 3 | The car is expensive. **Moreover,** it is unsafe. *(if these are the same kind of objection, OK)* | The car is expensive **and** unsafe. *(or)* … **Moreover,** … *(only if adding a separate strong point)* | [wrong] *Moreover* implies stepping up; not for trivial additions. | M |
| 4 | She is talented. **In contrast,** she works hard. | She is talented. **Moreover,** she works hard. | [wrong] *In contrast* needs opposites. | M |
| 5 | He is rich. **Although,** he is humble. | He is rich. **However,** he is humble. *(or)* **Although** he is rich, he is humble. | [wrong] *Although* needs a clause it's joined to, not a sentence-initial conjunctive adverb. | H |
| 6 | The plan failed. **Because,** funding was cut. | The plan failed **because** funding was cut. *(one sentence)* | [rule] *Because* is subordinator, not sentence-initial linker (in formal writing). | H |
| 7 | The system works well. **As a result,** many people use it. *(if cause/effect is meant)* — correct. **As a result,** I disagree. | The system works well. **As a result,** many people use it. / **For this reason,** I disagree. | [wrong] *As a result* must follow a cause. | M |
| 8 | **On the other hand,** … *(used without a "first hand")* | Use **on the other hand** only after **on the one hand**. Otherwise: **However,** / **By contrast,** | [wrong] Pair structure. | H |
| 9 | He is poor. **In addition,** he is sick. *(implies the same kind of point)* — fine. He is poor. **In addition,** he is happy. | He is poor. **Nevertheless,** he is happy. | [wrong] Contrast required, not addition. | M |
| 10 | **Despite of** the rain, … | **Despite the rain,** … / **In spite of the rain,** … | [rule] *despite* (no *of*) / *in spite of*. | H |
| 11 | **Although** he is poor**, but** he is happy. | **Although** he is poor, he is happy. *(no *but*)* | [rule] Don't pair *although* with *but*. | H |
| 12 | **Because** of he is tired. | **Because** he is tired. *(clause)* / **Because of** his tiredness. *(noun)* | [rule] As above. | H |
| 13 | **Even though** the cost, … | **Even though the cost is high,** … *(needs a clause)* | [rule] *even though* + clause. | M |
| 14 | **Instead of** he came. | **Instead of coming, he sent his deputy.** *(or)* **Rather than coming,** … | [rule] *instead of / rather than* + gerund/noun. | M |
| 15 | **In addition to**, students need books. | **In addition to** [noun/-ing], students need books. *(or)* **Additionally,** students need books. | [rule] *in addition to* + noun; *additionally* alone. | H |

## 3. Reference errors (ambiguous *it / this / they*)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | This is a serious issue. | **This trend / This phenomenon** is a serious issue. | [ref] Bare *this* is vague at band 7. | H |
| 2 | It will help society. *(no antecedent)* | **This policy / This approach / Such a measure** will help society. | [ref] Bare *it* without antecedent. | H |
| 3 | Some people drive cars. They pollute. | Some people drive cars, **which pollute** the air. *(or)* … cars; **these vehicles** pollute the air. | [ref] *they* could refer to people or cars. | H |
| 4 | Teachers and students benefit. They learn. *(who?)* | Teachers and students benefit; **the latter** learn while **the former** guide. *(or specify the noun)* | [ref] Use *the latter / the former*. | M |
| 5 | The government promised funds, but **they** didn't deliver. | The government promised funds, but **it / the administration** did not deliver. *(institutional singular AmE)* | [ref] Collective noun pronoun. | M |
| 6 | The company expanded. This made employees nervous. | The company expanded. **This expansion** made employees nervous. | [ref] Specify what *this* refers to. | H |
| 7 | Cars and bikes are popular. **It is** affordable. | Cars and bikes are popular. **They are** affordable. | [rule] Plural antecedent → plural pronoun. | M |
| 8 | If a student studies, you will improve. | If a student studies, **he/she/they will** improve. | [rule] Pronoun consistency — don't shift to *you*. | H |
| 9 | One should be careful with **your** money. | One should be careful with **one's** money. | [rule] *one* … *one's*. | M |
| 10 | The book and pen are mine. **It** is new. | The book and pen are mine. **They are** new. | [rule] Plural. | M |
| 11 | This shows that pollution is rising. *(referring to a paragraph)* | **This evidence / These findings** show that pollution is rising. | [ref] Replace *this* with *this + noun*. | H |
| 12 | The new law was passed. People hated **them**. | The new law was passed. People hated **it**. | [rule] Singular antecedent. | M |
| 13 | Children watch TV. **It** affects them. | Children watch TV; **this exposure** affects them. | [ref] *it* ambiguous (TV or watching). | M |
| 14 | Seoul is bigger than Busan. **He** has more people. | Seoul is bigger than Busan; **the former / it** has more people. | [rule] Cities are *it*, not *he*. | M |
| 15 | She gave the report to the manager who showed **it** to the board. *(OK if clear)* | If multiple antecedents possible: **the report** | [ref] Restate noun if ambiguous. | M |

## 4. Substitution & ellipsis (band-7 cohesion features)

| # | Original (over-repetitive band 6) | Improved (band 7+ with substitution / ellipsis) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I like coffee, and my brother likes coffee too. | I like coffee, and so **does my brother**. | [flow] Substitution with *do*. | M |
| 2 | She studies hard. He studies hard. | She studies hard, and **so does he**. | [flow] Substitution. | M |
| 3 | I have a car. Tom has a car. | I have a car. Tom **does too**. *(or)* Tom has **one** too. | [flow] Substitution with *one / do*. | M |
| 4 | Some students passed and some students failed. | Some students passed; **others failed**. | [flow] Substitution. | M |
| 5 | The first option is good. The second option is good. The third option is bad. | The first **two options** are good; the third is **not**. | [flow] Ellipsis after *not*. | M |
| 6 | She is rich and he is rich. | She is rich and **so is he**. | [flow] Substitution. | M |
| 7 | I know the answer. He knows the answer. | I know the answer, **and so does he**. | [flow] Substitution. | M |
| 8 | They asked if I could help. I said I could help. | They asked if I could help. I said **I could**. | [flow] Ellipsis. | M |
| 9 | She has more friends than I have friends. | She has more friends than **I do** / **than I**. | [flow] Ellipsis. | M |
| 10 | This issue, that issue, every issue is important. | **Each of these issues** is important. | [flow] Substitution / nominalisation. | M |

## 5. Lexical chains (synonym substitution & repetition)

| # | Original (band 5–6 — repetition) | Improved (band 7+ — lexical chain) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pollution is rising. Pollution is bad. Pollution must stop. | Pollution is rising. **This phenomenon** has serious consequences, and **the trend** must be reversed. | [flow] Vary noun reference. | H |
| 2 | The government must act. The government should also fund schools. The government … | The government must act. **The administration** should also fund schools. **Policymakers** … | [flow] Lexical chain. | M |
| 3 | Children need education. Children also need health care. Children … | Children need education. **Young people** also need health care. **The next generation** … | [flow] Synonym chain. | M |
| 4 | Cars are dangerous. Cars cause accidents. Cars pollute. | Cars are dangerous: **these vehicles** cause accidents and **automobiles** also pollute. | [flow] Synonym chain. | M |
| 5 | Technology has advantages. Technology has disadvantages. | **Technological progress** offers clear advantages, yet **it / this development** also poses risks. | [flow] Lexical chain + pronoun. | M |
| 6 | Climate change is dangerous. Climate change must be stopped. | Climate change is dangerous; **this global threat** must be addressed urgently. | [flow] Lexical chain. | M |
| 7 | Money. Money. Money. *(repeated noun)* | Money / financial resources / income / earnings / capital | [flow] Vary by context. | M |
| 8 | Students learn. Students take exams. Students … | Students learn through coursework; **learners** also take exams; **pupils** … *(BrE)* | [flow] Lexical chain. | M |
| 9 | Smoking is bad. Smoking causes cancer. Smoking should be banned. | Smoking is harmful: **the habit** causes cancer, and **it** should be heavily restricted. | [flow] Synonym + pronoun. | M |
| 10 | The teacher said. The teacher explained. The teacher told. | **The instructor** observed that … **He / She** then explained that … and added that … | [flow] Lexical chain. | M |

## 6. Paragraphing errors

| # | Error | Improved structure | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | One giant paragraph for the whole 250-word body. | Split into **2 body paragraphs** of ~80–100 words each, one main idea per paragraph. | [para] Band 7 requires paragraphing. | H |
| 2 | A two-sentence paragraph as a body. | Each body paragraph needs ~4–6 sentences (topic + support + example + link). | [para] Underdeveloped. | H |
| 3 | No topic sentence — paragraph starts with an example. | Start with a **topic sentence** that states the paragraph's claim, then exemplify. | [para] Band 7 reader-friendly. | H |
| 4 | New idea introduced in conclusion. | Conclusion = synthesis only. Move new ideas to a body paragraph or cut. | [para] Conclusions don't introduce new content. | H |
| 5 | Body paragraph mixes two main ideas. | Split into two paragraphs — **one main idea per paragraph**. | [para] Band 7 organisation. | H |
| 6 | No introduction (jumps straight into argument). | Always include a brief intro: paraphrase + thesis/position. | [para] Required by structure. | H |
| 7 | No conclusion (essay just stops). | Include a 2–3 sentence conclusion: restate position + synthesise. | [para] Required. | H |
| 8 | Each sentence on a new line / no blank line between paragraphs. | Use a clear blank line / indent between paragraphs. | [para] Visual structure. | M |
| 9 | Paragraph ending with an example (no link back). | End with a sentence that **links back** to the thesis or the next paragraph. | [para] Band 7 flow. | M |
| 10 | Topic sentence too vague (*This is important.*). | **State a specific, debatable claim** in the topic sentence. | [para] Topic-sentence quality. | H |

## 7. Sentence-to-sentence flow problems

| # | Original (choppy band 6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pollution is rising. Cities are crowded. Cars cause smog. | **Rising pollution, driven largely by urban congestion and vehicle emissions, has become a pressing concern.** | [flow] Combine three short sentences → one complex. | H |
| 2 | I went home. I was tired. | I went home **because I was tired**. *(or)* **Tired**, I went home. | [flow] Subordinate / participial. | H |
| 3 | She is a student. She studies hard. She gets good marks. | She is **a hard-working student who consistently achieves good marks**. | [flow] Relative clause + reduced. | H |
| 4 | New idea every sentence with no connector. | Add a connector or use a relative/participial clause to link cause-and-effect. | [flow] Cohesion. | H |
| 5 | Same subject every sentence (*Government should… Government must… Government can…*) | Vary subjects: **Policymakers must …; the administration could also …; reforms might further …** | [flow] Subject variety. | H |
| 6 | Two sentences with same conjunction (*and… and…*) | Use one *and*, one *which*, one participial. | [flow] Variety. | M |
| 7 | All sentences in subject-first position (~80 %). | Vary openers: adverbial phrase, participial, *what*-cleft, subordinate clause first. | [flow] Sentence-opener variety. | H |
| 8 | New paragraph with no transition from previous. | Open paragraph 2 with a **link phrase** that nods to paragraph 1: *While these benefits are clear, …* / *Despite these advantages, …* | [flow] Inter-paragraph cohesion. | H |
| 9 | Long run-on sentence with three or four ideas, no punctuation. | Split into 2–3 sentences, or use semicolons and complex subordination. | [flow] Sentence-boundary control. | H |
| 10 | Sentence ends with example; next sentence repeats the example. | End example, **start next sentence with a generalisation or counter-claim**. | [flow] Avoid redundancy. | M |
| 11 | Repeats the same connector (*also… also… also…*) | Rotate: *also / additionally / moreover / equally / furthermore / what is more*. | [flow] Lexical variety. | M |
| 12 | Ideas in a list with no logical sequence. | Order by **importance** (most → least), or **chronology**, or **cause → effect**. | [flow] Logical organisation. | H |
| 13 | Topic sentence and last sentence say the same thing. | Last sentence = **synthesis or implication**, not restatement. | [flow] Don't loop. | M |
| 14 | Pronoun reference jumps across paragraphs. | At paragraph boundaries, **restate the noun** rather than relying on *it / this*. | [ref] Cross-paragraph reference. | H |
| 15 | Conclusion just lists the body topics again. | Conclusion = **judgement + implication**, not a list. | [flow] Conclusion quality. | H |

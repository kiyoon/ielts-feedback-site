# Grammar Error Correction Bank — Atomic Reference (Band 6 → 7)

> Atomic, machine-parseable error → correction reference. Every row is independent: an LLM marker can quote one row directly as a suggestion; a student can scan to find the exact swap they need. Pair with `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/grammar-upgrades.md` (which gives strategy and showcase) and `contexts/09-instructor-tips/10-common-korean-chinese-speaker-errors.md` (which gives L1-specific patterns).
>
> **Frequency** column: H = High (appears in most band-6 essays), M = Medium (appears in many), L = Low (appears occasionally but score-impacting when it does). **Reason** column: tagged `[rule]` = strict grammar, `[style]` = preference / register, `[reg]` = register/formality only.

## 1. Articles (a / an / the / zero article)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I have **a** car. **Car** is red. | I have a car. **The car** is red. | [rule] Second mention of a known noun takes *the*. | H |
| 2 | **Government** should help the poor. | **The government** should help the poor. | [rule] Specific institution → definite article. | H |
| 3 | Pollution is **a** big problem in **the** modern cities. | Pollution is a big problem in **modern cities**. | [rule] *modern cities* is a generic plural — zero article. | H |
| 4 | She is **engineer**. | She is **an engineer**. | [rule] Singular countable profession → indefinite article. | H |
| 5 | I want to study **the** medicine. | I want to study **medicine**. | [rule] Subject of study (uncountable / generic) → zero article. | H |
| 6 | **The** life is hard for many people. | **Life** is hard for many people. | [rule] Abstract / generic noun → zero article. | H |
| 7 | He plays **the** football every weekend. | He plays **football** every weekend. | [rule] Sports → zero article (BrE/AmE). | M |
| 8 | She plays **piano**. | She plays **the piano**. | [rule] Musical instruments → definite article. | M |
| 9 | I went to **the** school by bus. | I went to **school** by bus. *(meaning: as a pupil)* | [rule] Institution as function → zero article. | M |
| 10 | He is in **hospital** for **operation**. | He is in **hospital** for **an operation**. | [rule] Singular countable event → *an*. | M |
| 11 | **The** English is spoken worldwide. | **English** is spoken worldwide. | [rule] Languages → zero article. | M |
| 12 | I bought **a** rice and **a** bread. | I bought **rice and bread**. | [rule] Uncountables take no indefinite article. | H |
| 13 | She gave me **a** good advice. | She gave me **good advice** / **a piece of advice**. | [rule] *advice* is uncountable. | H |
| 14 | He has **an** university degree. | He has **a university** degree. | [rule] *a/an* depends on **sound**, not letter; *university* starts with /j/. | M |
| 15 | I waited for **a** hour. | I waited for **an hour**. | [rule] *hour* starts with vowel sound /a/. | M |
| 16 | **The** people in my country are friendly. | **People** in my country are friendly. *(generic)* | [rule] Generic plural → zero article. | H |
| 17 | I love **the** classical music. | I love **classical music**. | [rule] Generic abstract → zero article. | M |
| 18 | He is **the** best student in **the** class. | He is **the best student in the class**. *(correct)* | [rule] Superlatives + specific group → *the*. | — |
| 19 | He is **best** student in our class. | He is **the best** student in our class. | [rule] Superlatives → *the*. | M |
| 20 | I will go to **the** Japan next year. | I will go to **Japan** next year. | [rule] Most country names → zero article. | M |
| 21 | He visited **United States** last summer. | He visited **the United States** last summer. | [rule] Country names with *of* / *Kingdom* / *States* / plural → *the*. | M |
| 22 | I climbed **Mount Everest** in **the** 2018. | I climbed Mount Everest in **2018**. | [rule] Years → zero article. | M |
| 23 | Sun rises in east. | **The sun** rises in **the east**. | [rule] Unique objects / cardinal directions → *the*. | M |
| 24 | She is **a** honest person. | She is **an honest** person. | [rule] *honest* — silent *h*, vowel sound. | M |
| 25 | The number of **the students** has risen. | The number of **students** has risen. | [rule] Generic group after *number of* → zero article. | H |
| 26 | **An** unemployment is rising. | **Unemployment** is rising. | [rule] Abstract uncountable → zero article. | M |
| 27 | I want to be **the** doctor. | I want to be **a doctor**. | [rule] Profession (one of many) → indefinite. | M |
| 28 | She has **a** beautiful long black hair. | She has **beautiful long black hair**. | [rule] *hair* (mass) → zero article. | M |
| 29 | Children should learn **the** history. | Children should learn **history**. | [rule] Subject of study → zero article. | M |
| 30 | I read **a** interesting article. | I read **an interesting** article. | [rule] Vowel sound determines *a/an*. | M |

## 2. Subject–verb agreement

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Many people **thinks** that… | Many people **think** that… | [rule] Plural subject → plural verb. | H |
| 2 | The number of cars **have** increased. | The number of cars **has** increased. | [rule] *the number of* takes singular verb. | H |
| 3 | A number of students **was** absent. | A number of students **were** absent. | [rule] *a number of* takes plural verb. | H |
| 4 | Everyone **have** their own opinion. | Everyone **has** their own opinion. | [rule] *everyone / everybody / nobody* are singular. | H |
| 5 | News **are** changing fast. | News **is** changing fast. | [rule] *news* is uncountable / singular. | M |
| 6 | The information **are** useful. | The information **is** useful. | [rule] *information* is uncountable. | H |
| 7 | Mathematics **are** my favourite subject. | Mathematics **is** my favourite subject. | [rule] *-ics* subjects (math, physics, economics) are singular. | M |
| 8 | Either the teacher or the students **is** wrong. | Either the teacher or the students **are** wrong. | [rule] *either…or* — verb agrees with the **nearer** noun. | M |
| 9 | Neither of the options **are** acceptable. | Neither of the options **is** acceptable. | [rule] *neither / each / every* take singular. | M |
| 10 | The staffs **is** trained. | The staff **are** trained. *(BrE)* / The staff **is** trained. *(AmE)* | [rule] *staff* is collective; never *staffs*. | H |
| 11 | The police **is** investigating. | The police **are** investigating. | [rule] *police* is plural collective. | M |
| 12 | One of the most important factor **are**… | One of the most important **factors is**… | [rule] *one of the* + plural noun + singular verb. | H |
| 13 | There **is** many problems. | There **are** many problems. | [rule] *there is/are* — agree with the real subject. | H |
| 14 | My family **are** small. | My family **is** small. *(AmE)* / My family **are** small. *(BrE, when emphasising members)* | [style] AmE prefers singular; BrE allows both. | M |
| 15 | The data **is** clear. | The data **are** clear. *(strict)* / The data **is** clear. *(modern accepted)* | [style] *data* — both accepted in modern academic writing. | L |
| 16 | Each of the students **have** completed the test. | Each of the students **has** completed the test. | [rule] *each of* takes singular verb. | M |
| 17 | The committee **have** decided. | The committee **has** decided. *(AmE / unified)* / The committee **have** decided. *(BrE / individuals)* | [style] Collective nouns vary by variety. | L |
| 18 | The majority of citizens **agrees**. | The majority of citizens **agree**. | [rule] *majority of* + plural → plural verb. | H |
| 19 | My friend along with his parents **are** coming. | My friend along with his parents **is** coming. | [rule] *along with / together with* don't change subject number. | L |
| 20 | Twenty per cent of the population **have** access. | Twenty per cent of the population **has** access. | [rule] *X per cent of* + uncountable/singular → singular. | M |

## 3. Tense (present / past / perfect / future / mixed)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | People **are believing** that education is important. | People **believe** that education is important. | [rule] *believe / know / understand* are stative — no continuous form. | H |
| 2 | The chart shows the sales of cars in 2000, and it **increases** until 2010. | The chart shows car sales, **which increased** steadily until 2010. | [rule] Past timeframe → past tense. | H |
| 3 | Since 2000, pollution **increased** sharply. | Since 2000, pollution **has increased** sharply. | [rule] *since / for* with present perfect. | H |
| 4 | I **am living** here for five years. | I **have been living** here for five years. | [rule] Continuing action → present perfect continuous. | H |
| 5 | Yesterday I **have gone** to the library. | Yesterday I **went** to the library. | [rule] Specific past time → simple past, not present perfect. | H |
| 6 | If technology **will improve**, our lives **become** easier. | If technology **improves**, our lives **will become** easier. | [rule] First conditional: *if* + present, *will* + verb. | H |
| 7 | When I **will arrive**, I **will call** you. | When I **arrive**, I **will call** you. | [rule] Future time clauses use present, not future. | M |
| 8 | He **didn't went** to school. | He **didn't go** to school. | [rule] *didn't* + base form. | H |
| 9 | She **was study** at the library. | She **was studying** at the library. | [rule] Past continuous: *was/were* + V-ing. | M |
| 10 | I **have been knowing** her for ten years. | I **have known** her for ten years. | [rule] *know* is stative. | M |
| 11 | The data shows that population **increases** between 1990 and 2000. | The data show that population **increased** between 1990 and 2000. | [rule] Past dates → past tense. | H |
| 12 | By 2050, the population **will reach** 10 billion. | By 2050, the population **will have reached** 10 billion. | [rule] *by* + future → future perfect. | M |
| 13 | I **am studying** English since 2020. | I **have been studying** English since 2020. | [rule] *since* needs perfect, not progressive present. | H |
| 14 | She **have** finished her homework. | She **has** finished her homework. | [rule] Third person singular → *has*. | H |
| 15 | The trend **was started** in the 1990s. | The trend **started** in the 1990s. | [rule] *start* is intransitive — no passive needed. | M |
| 16 | He **is born** in Korea. | He **was born** in Korea. | [rule] *be born* — fixed past. | H |
| 17 | If I **would have** money, I **would** travel. | If I **had** money, I **would** travel. | [rule] Second conditional: *if* + past, *would* + verb. | H |
| 18 | I **will** call you when I **will get** home. | I will call you when I **get** home. | [rule] Time clause uses present after *when / before / until / as soon as*. | M |
| 19 | They **are working** here since January. | They **have been working** here since January. | [rule] *since* + perfect continuous. | H |
| 20 | This problem **is exist** for decades. | This problem **has existed** for decades. | [rule] Duration to present → present perfect. | M |
| 21 | The graph **had shown** an upward trend. | The graph **shows / showed** an upward trend. | [rule] Past perfect requires a later past reference point. | M |
| 22 | I **didn't saw** him yesterday. | I **didn't see** him yesterday. | [rule] *did not* + base form. | M |
| 23 | She **goes** to Paris last week. | She **went** to Paris last week. | [rule] *last week* → past tense. | H |
| 24 | I **wrote** three essays this morning. *(if morning is ongoing)* | I **have written** three essays this morning. | [rule] If the time period is unfinished → present perfect. | M |
| 25 | When he arrived, the meeting **already started**. | When he arrived, the meeting **had already started**. | [rule] Earlier past event → past perfect. | M |
| 26 | He **is going** to school every day. | He **goes** to school every day. | [rule] Habits → simple present. | H |
| 27 | Last year, the company **has launched** a new product. | Last year, the company **launched** a new product. | [rule] Specific past time → simple past. | H |
| 28 | The phenomenon **was started** to appear in the 1990s. | The phenomenon **began to appear** in the 1990s. | [rule] *begin / start* are unaccusative — no passive. | M |
| 29 | I **have visited** Paris in 2019. | I **visited** Paris in 2019. | [rule] Specific past date → simple past. | H |
| 30 | While I **was studying**, my phone **was ringing**. | While I **was studying**, my phone **rang**. | [rule] Background = continuous; interrupting event = simple past. | M |

## 4. Prepositions (in / on / at, dependent prepositions)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| 2 | We discussed **about** the issue. | We discussed the issue. | [rule] *discuss* takes a direct object — no preposition. | H |

| 4 | I depend **of** my parents. | I depend **on** my parents. | [rule] *depend on*. | H |
| 5 | The test consists **to** two parts. | The test consists **of** two parts. | [rule] *consist of*. | H |

| 8 | I am interested **on** music. | I am interested **in** music. | [rule] *interested in*. | H |
| 9 | She is keen **of** swimming. | She is keen **on** swimming. | [rule] *keen on*. | M |
| 10 | He is similar **with** his father. | He is similar **to** his father. | [rule] *similar to*. | M |

| 14 | I listen music. | I listen **to** music. | [rule] *listen to*. | H |

| 17 | He is responsible **of** the project. | He is responsible **for** the project. | [rule] *responsible for*. | H |
| 18 | This is the reason **of** the problem. | This is the reason **for** the problem. | [rule] *reason for*. | H |

| 25 | I'll meet you **on** the morning. | I'll meet you **in** the morning. | [rule] *in the morning / afternoon / evening*. | H |
| 26 | Suddenly **on** night, … | Suddenly **at** night, … | [rule] *at night* (fixed). | M |
| 27 | He lives **at** Seoul. | He lives **in** Seoul. | [rule] *in* + city. | H |
| 28 | I will see you **in** the weekend. | I will see you **at** the weekend. *(BrE)* / **on** the weekend *(AmE)*. | [rule] BrE *at* / AmE *on*. | M |

| 31 | He is suffering **from** stress. *(correct)* / suffering **with** stress. | He is suffering **from** stress. | [rule] *suffer from*. | M |
| 32 | A solution **of** the problem | A solution **to** the problem | [rule] *solution to*. | H |

| 34 | He is capable **to do** it. | He is capable **of doing** it. | [rule] *capable of* + gerund. | M |
| 35 | I insist **to go**. | I insist **on going**. | [rule] *insist on* + gerund. | M |

## 5. Conditionals (zero / first / second / third / mixed)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | If you **will heat** water, it boils. | If you **heat** water, it boils. | [rule] Zero conditional: *if* + present, present. | H |
| 2 | If pollution **will continue**, public health **will deteriorate**. | If pollution **continues**, public health **will deteriorate**. | [rule] First conditional: *if* + present, *will* + verb. | H |
| 3 | If I **could live** anywhere, I **will live** in Tokyo. | If I **could live** anywhere, I **would live** in Tokyo. | [rule] Second conditional: *if* + past, *would* + verb. | H |
| 4 | If governments **will invest** more, emissions **could be** reduced. | If governments **invested** more, emissions **could be** reduced. | [rule] Hypothetical → past simple in *if*-clause. | H |
| 5 | If stricter laws **were introduced** earlier, the crisis **would be** avoided. | If stricter laws **had been introduced** earlier, the crisis **would have been** avoided. | [rule] Third conditional: past perfect + would have V3. | H |
| 6 | I **would have say** hello if I had seen you. | I **would have said** hello if I had seen you. | [rule] *would have* + past participle. | M |
| 7 | If we **acted** twenty years ago, we **would not face** this crisis today. | If we **had acted** twenty years ago, we **would not be facing** this crisis today. | [rule] Mixed conditional: past perfect + would + base. | M |
| 8 | If governments **would invest** more, … | If governments **invested** more, … *(or)* **Were governments to invest** more, … | [rule] *would* in *if*-clause is non-standard in academic writing. | H |
| 9 | If anyone **objects**, they **would speak** now. | If anyone **objects**, they **should speak** now. *(or)* **Should anyone object**, they should speak now. | [style] Inverted *should* — band 7 flair. | L |
| 10 | If she **would have** money, she **would buy** a house. | If she **had** money, she **would buy** a house. | [rule] No *would have* in *if*-clause. | H |
| 11 | If the policy was implemented, results would improve. | If the policy **were** implemented, results would improve. *(formal)* | [style] Subjunctive *were* in formal hypothetical. | L |
| 12 | Unless you **don't** study, you will fail. | Unless you study, you will fail. | [rule] *unless* already negative — no double negative. | M |
| 13 | If only I **knew** the answer yesterday. | If only I **had known** the answer yesterday. | [rule] Past unreal regret → past perfect. | M |
| 14 | Provided that the data **will be** accurate, results are reliable. | Provided that the data **are** accurate, results are reliable. | [rule] *provided that* + present (real condition). | M |
| 15 | If the temperature **would rise**, ice **will melt**. | If the temperature **rises**, ice **will melt**. *(or zero)* If the temperature **rises**, ice **melts**. | [rule] Match conditional types consistently. | M |

## 6. Modals (can / could / should / would / might)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | He **can to** swim. | He **can** swim. | [rule] Modals + base form (no *to*). | H |
| 2 | She **musts** finish. | She **must** finish. | [rule] Modals don't take *-s*. | H |
| 3 | We **should to** invest more. | We **should** invest more. | [rule] No *to* after modals. | H |
| 4 | He **can be able to** swim. | He **can** swim. *(or)* He **is able to** swim. | [rule] Don't double up *can* + *be able to*. | M |
| 5 | I **must to** go now. | I **must** go now. *(or)* I **have to** go now. | [rule] Modal + base form. | H |
| 6 | You **don't must** smoke here. | You **must not / mustn't** smoke here. | [rule] *must not* (prohibition) ≠ *don't have to* (no obligation). | M |
| 7 | You **mustn't** come — it's optional. | You **don't have to** come — it's optional. | [rule] *don't have to* = no obligation; *mustn't* = forbidden. | M |
| 8 | He **could** finish the marathon yesterday. | He **was able to** finish the marathon yesterday. | [rule] Past one-off achievement → *was able to*; *could* = general past ability. | M |
| 9 | She **may has** left. | She **may have** left. | [rule] Modal + *have* + V3 (perfect modal). | M |
| 10 | This **may to be** the cause. | This **may be** the cause. | [rule] No *to* after modal. | H |
| 11 | I **would like** that you come. | I **would like you to come**. | [rule] *would like* + object + *to*-infinitive. | M |
| 12 | We **should had** acted sooner. | We **should have** acted sooner. | [rule] *should have* + V3 = past regret. | M |
| 13 | He **can** be a doctor when he grows up. | He **could / may / might** be a doctor when he grows up. | [rule] *can* = current ability; future possibility → *could/may/might*. | M |
| 14 | I **will can** help you tomorrow. | I **will be able to** help you tomorrow. | [rule] No double modal — *will be able to*. | H |
| 15 | Governments **must** to act. | Governments **must** act. | [rule] No *to* after modal. | H |
| 16 | This problem **may to occur** again. | This problem **may occur** again. | [rule] No *to* after modal. | H |
| 17 | She **maybe** the best candidate. | She **may be** the best candidate. | [rule] *maybe* (adverb) ≠ *may be* (modal + verb). | M |
| 18 | Children **should not to watch** violent films. | Children **should not watch** violent films. | [rule] *should not* + base form. | M |

## 7. Passive voice (formation, when to use, when not)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | English is **speak** in many countries. | English **is spoken** in many countries. | [rule] Passive = *be* + V3. | H |
| 2 | The internet **is using** by people. | The internet **is used** by people. | [rule] Passive form: *is/are* + V3. | H |
| 3 | It is **believe** that… | It **is believed** that… | [rule] V3, not base form. | H |
| 4 | The problem **was discussed** by us yesterday. | We **discussed** the problem yesterday. *(or keep passive if agent is unimportant)* | [style] Avoid unnecessary passive when agent matters. | M |
| 5 | A new policy **has been** introduce last year. | A new policy **was introduced** last year. | [rule] Specific past → simple past passive *was/were* + V3. | M |
| 6 | The chart **is showed** the data. | The chart **shows** the data. | [rule] *show* here is active, not passive. | H |
| 7 | The trend **was happened** in 2010. | The trend **happened** in 2010. | [rule] *happen / occur / appear* are intransitive — no passive. | H |
| 8 | The phenomenon **was occurred** twice. | The phenomenon **occurred** twice. | [rule] *occur* is intransitive. | H |
| 9 | Researches **have been done** on this topic. | Research **has been carried out / conducted** on this topic. | [rule] *research* uncountable; *do research* is informal — *carry out / conduct*. | M |
| 10 | The largest population **had** São Paulo. | The largest population **was taken up by** São Paulo. | [style] Task 1 passive with figure verbs is band-7. | H |
| 11 | The cake **was eaten** by **me**. | I **ate** the cake. | [style] Avoid clumsy *by me / by us* — use active. | M |
| 12 | These laws should **introduce** quickly. | These laws should **be introduced** quickly. | [rule] Passive needs *be*. | H |
| 13 | This issue **needs to address**. | This issue **needs to be addressed**. *(or)* This issue **needs addressing**. | [rule] *need* + to-infinitive passive, or *need* + gerund. | M |
| 14 | The system was **designed for help** users. | The system **was designed to help** users. | [rule] *to*-infinitive of purpose. | M |
| 15 | A solution must **find** soon. | A solution must **be found** soon. | [rule] Modal + *be* + V3. | H |
| 16 | The data **is collected** every year. *(correct)* / The data **collect** every year. | The data **are collected** every year. | [rule] *data* often plural; passive = *be* + V3. | M |
| 17 | The bridge **was build** in 1990. | The bridge **was built** in 1990. | [rule] V3 of *build* is *built*. | M |

## 8. Reported speech / indirect speech

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | He said **"I am tired."** *(in essay context)* | He said **(that) he was tired.** | [rule] Reported speech uses backshift. | M |
| 2 | She told **that** she was leaving. | She **said that** she was leaving. *(or)* She **told me that**… | [rule] *tell* needs an object; *say* doesn't. | H |
| 3 | He asked **what was the problem**. | He asked **what the problem was**. | [rule] No question word order in reported questions. | M |
| 4 | She asked **do I know** the answer. | She asked **whether/if I knew** the answer. | [rule] Yes/no question reported with *if/whether*. | M |
| 5 | He said me that he was busy. | He **told me** that he was busy. *(or)* He **said (to me)** that he was busy. | [rule] *say to* / *tell* + object. | H |
| 6 | She said yesterday she will go today. | She said **the previous day** she **would go** that day. | [rule] Time/place shift in reported speech. | M |
| 7 | He told **to me** that… | He **told me** that… | [rule] *tell* + direct object — no *to*. | M |
| 8 | She suggested **me to leave**. | She suggested **(that) I leave** / suggested **leaving**. | [rule] *suggest* doesn't take object + to-infinitive. | H |
| 9 | He recommended **me to read** it. | He recommended **(that) I read** it / recommended **reading** it. | [rule] *recommend* — same pattern as *suggest*. | M |
| 10 | He explained me how to do it. | He explained **to me** how to do it. *(or)* He explained **how to do** it. | [rule] *explain* + *to* + person + thing. | M |

## 9. Relative clauses (defining / non-defining)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The man **which** lives next door is friendly. | The man **who** lives next door is friendly. | [rule] *who* for people. | H |
| 2 | This is the book **who** I read. | This is the book **which / that** I read. | [rule] *which / that* for things. | H |
| 3 | My father **who is a teacher** lives in Seoul. *(meaning: I have one father)* | My father, **who is a teacher**, lives in Seoul. | [rule] Non-defining → commas. | M |
| 4 | Students who study hard, get good marks. | Students **who study hard** get good marks. | [rule] Defining clause — no commas. | M |
| 5 | London, **that** is the capital of England, is busy. | London, **which** is the capital of England, is busy. | [rule] Never *that* in non-defining clauses. | M |
| 6 | I met a man **his** name was John. | I met a man **whose** name was John. | [rule] *whose* = possessive relative. | M |
| 7 | The house **where I lived in it** was small. | The house **where I lived** was small. *(or)* The house **in which I lived**. | [rule] Don't double the preposition with *where*. | M |
| 8 | The reason **why because** I came is… | The reason **(why)** I came is… *(or)* The reason **(that)** I came is… | [rule] *why* alone is enough. | M |
| 9 | The day **when** I arrived was sunny. *(correct)* / **on which** I arrived. | The day **on which I arrived** was sunny. *(formal)* | [style] *on which* — formal alternative to *when*. | L |
| 10 | This is the city **where I visited**. | This is the city **(that) I visited** / **which I visited**. | [rule] *visit* takes a direct object — no *where*. | M |
| 11 | The student that I met him was clever. | The student **(that/whom) I met** was clever. | [rule] Don't double the object. | M |
| 12 | He is the person **whom** is responsible. | He is the person **who** is responsible. | [rule] *who* is subject; *whom* is object. | M |
| 13 | Climate change **what** is a global crisis… | Climate change, **which** is a global crisis,… | [rule] *what* is not a relative pronoun in this slot. | M |
| 14 | The book what I bought | The book **(that / which) I bought** | [rule] Same as above. | M |
| 15 | My friend, who lives in Paris **she is** a doctor. | My friend, **who lives in Paris**, **is** a doctor. | [rule] No double subject — relative pronoun replaces it. | M |

## 10. Participle clauses & reduced relatives

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The students **who are studying** abroad face challenges. | Students **studying abroad** face challenges. | [style] Reduced relative — *V-ing*. | M |
| 2 | The houses **which were damaged** by fire will be demolished. | The houses **damaged by fire** will be demolished. | [style] Reduced relative — V3. | M |
| 3 | Because she had completed her studies, she applied for jobs. | **Having completed her studies**, she applied for jobs. | [style] Perfect participle clause. | M |
| 4 | When he saw the problem, he reacted quickly. | **Seeing the problem**, he reacted quickly. | [style] Present participle clause. | M |
| 5 | **Walking to school, the rain started.** | **Walking to school, I was caught in the rain.** | [rule] Dangling participle — subjects must match. | H |
| 6 | **Influenced by global trends, the strategy changed.** *(if "the company" was the agent)* | **Influenced by global trends, the company changed its strategy.** | [rule] Subject of participle = subject of main clause. | M |
| 7 | If the laws are enforced, the environment will be protected. | **Enforcing the laws**, governments would protect the environment. | [style] Conditional → participle clause. | L |
| 8 | When you live in a big city, you face many challenges. | **Living in a big city**, you face many challenges. | [style] Time clause → participle. | M |
| 9 | Because **was tired**, he went home. | **Being tired**, he went home. *(or)* **Tired**, he went home. | [rule] Adjective participle drops *being* possible. | M |
| 10 | Before he left, he locked the door. | **Before leaving**, he locked the door. | [style] Preposition + gerund — concise alternative. | M |

## 11. Conjunctions & sentence-boundary errors

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I went home, I was tired. | I went home **because** I was tired. *(or)* I went home; I was tired. | [rule] Comma splice — fix with subordinator or semicolon. | H |
| 2 | Pollution affects everyone, the wealthy can no longer escape it. | Pollution affects everyone**;** the wealthy can no longer escape it. | [rule] Two independent clauses → semicolon. | H |
| 3 | **Although** technology is useful, **but** it has drawbacks. | **Although** technology is useful, it has drawbacks. *(or)* Technology is useful, **but** it has drawbacks. | [rule] Don't pair *although* with *but*. | H |
| 4 | **Because** I was tired, **so** I went home. | **Because** I was tired, I went home. *(or)* I was tired, **so** I went home. | [rule] Don't pair *because* with *so*. | H |
| 5 | **Even though** he tried, **however** he failed. | **Even though** he tried, he failed. *(or)* He tried; **however**, he failed. | [rule] Don't pair *even though* with *however*. | M |
| 6 | Because **of** he was tired. | **Because** he was tired. *(or)* **Because of** his tiredness. | [rule] *because* + clause; *because of* + noun phrase. | H |
| 7 | **Despite** the cost is high, demand grows. | **Despite the high cost**, demand grows. *(or)* **Although the cost is high**, demand grows. | [rule] *despite* + noun/-ing; *although* + clause. | H |
| 8 | In spite **of** he is tired,… | **Although** he is tired,… *(or)* **In spite of** his tiredness,… | [rule] Same pattern as *despite*. | M |
| 9 | He is poor. **But also** kind. | He is poor **but also** kind. *(or)* He is **not only poor but also kind**. | [rule] *but also* needs a clause / paired structure. | M |
| 10 | I like coffee. **And** tea. | I like coffee **and** tea. | [rule] Sentence fragment. | H |
| 11 | The reason is **because** the system failed. | The reason is **that** the system failed. | [rule] Avoid *reason is because*. | H |
| 12 | He is famous because of he sings well. | He is famous **because** he sings well. *(or)* **because of** his singing. | [rule] *because* (clause) vs *because of* (noun). | H |
| 13 | **As long as** the issue, the policy will fail. | **As long as the issue persists**, the policy will fail. | [rule] *as long as* needs a clause. | M |
| 14 | He is intelligent. **Moreover** is hard-working. | He is intelligent**;** moreover, **he** is hard-working. | [rule] Conjunctive adverb still needs subject. | M |
| 15 | I went to bed early **for** I was tired. *(literary, archaic)* | I went to bed early **because** I was tired. | [style] *for* = "because" is archaic — avoid in academic IELTS. | L |

## 12. Word order (especially adverb placement)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I **always am** late. | I **am always** late. | [rule] Adverbs of frequency go after *be*. | H |
| 2 | He **drinks always** coffee. | He **always drinks** coffee. | [rule] Adverbs of frequency go before main verb. | H |
| 3 | She speaks **fluent English very**. | She speaks English **very fluently**. | [rule] Adverb modifies verb; ordering. | M |
| 4 | He has **probably** come. *(correct)* / He **probably has** come. | He **has probably** come. | [rule] Mid-position adverb after auxiliary. | M |
| 5 | I went **last week to Paris**. | **Last week I went to Paris.** *(or)* I went **to Paris last week**. | [rule] Place before time. | M |
| 6 | She **slowly carefully** read. | She read **slowly and carefully**. | [rule] Two adverbs in same slot — coordinate them. | M |
| 7 | I read the book **interesting** yesterday. | Yesterday I read **the interesting book**. | [rule] Adjective before noun. | H |
| 8 | He bought **a red beautiful big** car. | He bought **a beautiful big red** car. | [rule] Adjective order: opinion → size → age → shape → colour. | M |
| 9 | **Yesterday at the library I studied.** *(awkward)* | **Yesterday I studied at the library.** | [style] Standard order: S-V-O-place-time. | M |
| 10 | She hardly works. *(if you mean the opposite)* | She works hard. | [rule] *hardly* = barely; *hard* (adv) = with effort. | M |

## 13. Inversion / cleft / fronting (band 7+ structures)

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Technology not only changes how we live but also reshapes work. | **Not only does technology change** how we live, **but it also reshapes** work. | [style] *Not only* fronting — band 7 flair. | L |
| 2 | We rarely see such a transformation. | **Rarely do we see** such a transformation. | [style] Negative-adverb inversion. | L |
| 3 | We can solve this only by working together. | **Only by working together can we solve** this. | [style] *Only by* fronting + inversion. | L |
| 4 | Children should not be exposed to such content. *(emphatic)* | **Under no circumstances should children be exposed** to such content. | [style] Emphatic negative inversion. | L |
| 5 | We seldom see this level of cooperation. | **Seldom do we see** this level of cooperation. | [style] *Seldom* + inversion. | L |
| 6 | Education matters most. | **It is education that matters most.** | [style] *It*-cleft for emphasis. | L |
| 7 | Many people don't realise that pollution affects mental health. | **What many people don't realise is that** pollution affects mental health. | [style] *What*-cleft. | L |
| 8 | Government action is needed urgently. | **What is needed urgently is** government action. | [style] *What*-cleft. | L |
| 9 | Pollution is the most serious issue. | **The most serious issue is pollution.** *(or, fronted)* **Most serious of all is pollution.** | [style] Fronted complement. | L |
| 10 | I have never seen such a problem. | **Never have I seen** such a problem. | [style] *Never* fronting. | L |

## 14. Conditional / comparative / parallel connectors

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | She is **not only smart but is also** kind. | She is **not only smart but also** kind. | [rule] Parallel structure — same word class either side. | M |
| 2 | He likes **both reading and to write**. | He likes **both reading and writing**. *(or)* He likes **both to read and to write**. | [rule] *both…and* parallel. | M |
| 3 | **Either you study, or fail.** | **Either you study, or you fail.** | [rule] Full clause both sides. | M |
| 4 | The more you practise, **the most** you improve. | **The more** you practise, **the more** you improve. | [rule] *the more X, the more Y* — comparative both sides. | M |
| 5 | **As more** people learn English, **as more** they travel. | **The more** people learn English, **the more** they travel. | [rule] Pattern is *the more…the more*, not *as more*. | M |
| 6 | Bigger cities, bigger problems. *(headline-style)* | **The bigger the city, the bigger the problem.** | [style] Full grammatical pattern in essays. | L |
| 7 | She is **so** intelligent **that anyone**. | She is **more intelligent than anyone (else)**. | [rule] Comparative *more…than*. | M |
| 8 | This city is **as big than** that. | This city is **as big as** that. | [rule] *as…as* (equal) vs *more than* (unequal). | M |
| 9 | **Not only** does he write well, **but** he speaks fluently. | **Not only** does he write well, **but he also** speaks fluently. | [style] *but also* preferred. | M |
| 10 | The plan is good **as much as** practical. | The plan is **as good as it is practical**. | [rule] *as X as Y*. | L |

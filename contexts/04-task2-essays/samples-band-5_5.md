# IELTS Task 2 — Band 5.0 / 5.5 Sample Essays

These essays sit just below the typical "Band 6" target. They are useful as **negative training examples** showing the patterns an LLM marker should detect: fragmented sentences, frequent tense errors, comma splices, redundant phrasing, and partial task fulfilment.

---

## Sample 1: Solution of Overcrowded Traffic (Band 5)

**Source:** British Council Sample Scripts — Academic Writing Sample Task 2A, Script A
**Question Type:** Problem/Solution
**Overall Band:** 5
**Per-criterion (if available):** N/A (overall only)

### Prompt
The number of cars on British roads has increased dramatically. Discuss the causes of overcrowded traffic and propose solutions.

### Essay
Nowaday, there are alot of cars on British road and they have increased day to day. By the year 2000 there may be as many as 29 million vehicles on British roads. In this essay, I intend to examine about the solutions of these problems.

Firstly, the people living in Britain need to think about themselves. If they used the bus and train instead of their car, this problem would resolve a little. Because of this, the British Government should introduce, to control car ownership and use. For example, the government can ban to enter the road by car in the someday all family from a house.

Secondly, the buses and trains of government should be free for public population. Thus, the people would use these transport vehicles instead of their own car. After that, the roads in Britain would be more safer and more comfortable.

Lastly, the number of cars that are exported from another country should decrease and the prices of car should increased incase they aren't overcrowded. For example, the prices of cigarettes increased and the consumption of cigarettes went down.

In conclusion, If these measures put into action the problem of traffic can be decreased in the British roads.

### Word Count
~210 words (under 250 — penalised)

### Examiner Comments (verbatim from British Council)
"The answer is short at just over 200 words and thus loses marks for content. There are some relevant arguments but these are not very well developed and become unclear in places. The organisation of the answer is evident through the use of fairly simple connectives but there are problems for the reader in that there are many missing words and word order is often incorrect. The structures are quite ambitious but often faulty and vocabulary is kept quite simple."

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response:** Under-length; arguments under-developed; "unclear in places."
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Simple connectives (firstly/secondly/lastly) used mechanically.
- **Lexical Resource:** Kept simple; no academic register; "alot" misspelling, "Nowaday" non-standard.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** "missing words" frequent; word order errors; "more safer" double comparative; ambitious complex structures fail.

## Sample 2: Nuclear Power (Band 5)

**Source:** British Council Sample Scripts — Academic Writing Sample Task 2B, Script A

Arguments in favour nuclear power: The nuclear energy produces by chemical materials. It is comparatively cheaps than other energy. To produce the power it only involve some expert people and energy plant. Where to produce other energy it needs large involvement like workers, machineries, etc. And also takes more time. The nuclear power plants are well protected and monitor. That is why there is less possibilities. The threat of nuclear weapons maintains world peace because the developed countries like UK, USA, Canada, France etc have nuclear weapons (warhead). Each country do not give threat to other country. Because they know if one country disturb others, then other will create problems from them. So it is well balanced and world peace maintains peacefully.

Though there are sometimes creates problems by the nuclear technology but sometimes it also help the mankind in the field of medical and engineering sectors. On the other hand in the field of Engineering by the nuclear power engineers can do lot of things like operate engine instead of electricity. ~245

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response:** Position present, but supporting arguments not adequately linked.

## Sample 4: Brain Drain — Young People Leaving (Band 5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-young-people-are-leaving-their-country-and-leave-only-old-people-in-the-country-6658ef7f45dc4/)
**Question Type:** Two-part question (problems + solutions)
**Overall Band:** 5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 6 | CC: 5.5 | LR: 4.5 | GRA: 4.5

### Prompt
Some young people are leaving their country and leave only old people in the country. What problems are caused by this issue? What can be done to solve this situation?

### Essay
The phenomenon of young people leaving their home countries in search of better opportunities or lifestyles abroad has become increasingly prevalent in many parts of the world. However, this leads a country to abnormal age category because old people are being left behind in the country and this problem can be posed by the help of a government.

First of all, one of the primary issue that raises from this problem is the decrease of a need for new, young labor demand. Each passing day a new technology is emerging and old generation are having difficulties to understand and manage it and without the help of new generation this can degrade the status of a country. Therefore, many factors can face with difficulties with force labor and these would lead to economic depression. Another factor to mention is that many old people live in suburb eras by their children but after migration of their children old people are left alone with no one to take care of them. Thus, goverment should take of old generation and set aside money for their health problems and sending nurses to take care of them and improve infrastructures like health centres and hospitals. Finally, older people may suffer from psychiatric disorders. This population usually tend to spend time with their family and rely on them and in fact, they are tied to younger people. If young members of their family move to cities, they would not cope with the feelings of loneliness and get mentally ill.

To address these challenges, government should improve the conditions of the government in order to pursue youth to stay in their own country. This should involve better job opportunities, high salary and good environment. In addition, there should be some ground rules for students who should return to their homeland after student load programs. The government should take responsibility and start funding projects and encouraging investments in those areas so young adults won't have to leave to secure a job and elderly people would be close to their children that take care of them.

In conclusion, while youth migration poses significant challenges for countries of origin, by addressing the root causes of migration, creating opportunities, improving living conditions governments can harness the potential of their youth populations and foster sustainable development for generations to come.

### Word Count
387

### Examiner Comments
- *Task Response:* Addresses prompt by discussing problems caused and providing solutions.
- *Coherence & Cohesion:* Logical paragraphs; transition words could be more varied.
- *Lexical Resource:* Range present but awkward phrasing and inaccuracies.
- *Grammatical Range:* Noticeable grammar errors — subject-verb agreement, word choice.

### Specific Issues
- "old generation **are**" SVA error (collective noun mishandled).
- "this problem can be posed by the help of a government" — wrong preposition / wrong verb (should be "addressed").
- "suburb eras" → "suburban areas".
- "student load programs" → "study-loan programmes" or "scholarship".
- Mechanical introductions to body paragraphs ("First of all", "Another factor").

## Sample 5: Older vs Young Workers (Band 5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/nowadays-more-and-more-people-have-to-compete-with-young-people-for-the-same-jobs-3f3d50f9c3046a8b/)
**Question Type:** Problem/Solution
**Overall Band:** 5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 5.5 | CC: 5 | LR: 5.5 | GRA: 3.5

### Prompt
Nowadays more and more people have to compete with young people for the same jobs. What problems does this cause? What are some possible solutions?

### Essay
In recent days, among older workers and young people competition occurs for the same position. This trend leads to several issues, such as difficulties for young people who search jobs, hardship for seniors to continuing their jobs and there are several solutions to addressed.

On the one hand, people who are old strive to compete with young workers. Because juvenile are inclined to utilize advanced technology rather than olders. As in eras of seniors's technology was not well developed as today and now related their ages they can not evolve their computers skills. That's the reason why they are not able to work with computers. In addition to this young job seekers experience difficulties to land a job because they have not enough experiences. Therefore, this problem makes it harder for them to enter company.

Turning to the other side of the argument, to tackle these issues, companies should take drastic measures. Firstly, companies can provide courses for older workers to evolve their skills and learn new updates about their profession. Furthermore, companies also can ensures internships for young talents. These incentives serve them to prove themselves that can work this workplace and they can achieve novel information about their occupation. These implements foster people to do their best.

In conclusion, there might competition occur between older and young job seekers, however company incentives might help people who want to find a job.

### Word Count
223 (under 250 — penalised)

### Examiner Comments
- Vocabulary repetition: "their" 7×, "young" 6×, "older" 3×.
- "Numerous grammatical errors significantly affect clarity and fluency."
- "Flow of ideas is inconsistent."
- Falls short of 250-word minimum.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response:** Underdeveloped; problem paragraph confuses old workers' problems with young workers' problems.
- **Lexical Resource:** "juvenile" mis-collocated; "olders" non-word; "eras of seniors's technology" garbled.
- **Grammar:** "to continuing", "are inclined to utilize advanced technology rather than olders" — fragment structure; "they can not evolve their computers skills" — possessive missing; "ensures internships" SVA.

**Question Type:** Opinion (Agree/Disagree)
**Overall Band:** 5.5
**Per-criterion:** Not individually specified

### Prompt
The prevention of health problems and illness is more important than treatment and medicine. Government funding should reflect this. To what extent do you agree?

### Essay (key features summarised)
The student takes a balanced position, claiming prevention is cheaper than treatment but acknowledging some diseases cannot be prevented. Structured intro / 2 body paragraphs / conclusion (~270 words).

### Examiner Comments
- **Weak thesis statement** — uncommitted stance.
- **Poor paraphrasing** — opening uses informal "paying money", weak vocabulary.
- **Excessive repetition** — "you" and "paying money" overused.
- **Wrong essay type** — handles an opinion essay as advantage/disadvantage format.
- **Limited cohesion** — few transitional phrases.
- **Vocabulary gaps** — "use money" instead of "spend money".
- **Informal tone** — idioms inappropriate for academic.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response:** Mismatched essay format (adv/disadv when opinion required).
- **Coherence:** Few transitional phrases; weak sentence variety.
- **Lexical Resource:** Collocation errors; informal idioms; pronoun "you" overused.
- **Grammar:** Repetitive sentence patterns.

### Improved Version (Band 7+ teacher rewrite — described)
The model essay takes a clear *disagreement* stance, supporting treatment-and-medicine funding. It includes specific examples (an uncle with lung cancer, USA's Parkinson's research), maintains formal academic tone, and uses cohesive devices throughout (~296 words).

Many people believe that being compititive on our daily basis creates a healthy working and studying environment while others, on the other hand, think that we should interpret more coopration in those activities. I personally think that both state valid points and its not really beneficial to choose or pick between them. It is mostly a combination of both that leads to a healthy productive environment.

Compitition on one hand, is very important between any productive group members. This is mainly because the people tend to push eachothers' limits as it always leave the idea of needing to improve in their mind. This concept is reinforced by the fact that most people tend to make comparisons between themselves and their colleages which can really help in building a good productive workspace. Although, overdoing this might lead to what some people call toxic compititiveness that will do more harm than good.

On the other hand, cooperating in any kind of work really helps in achieving goals as fast as possible. Moreover, it creates an environment that allow students for instance to share the knowledge between the class. Furthermore, the idea of coopration can really help neutralize the situation if it ever gets over compititive. - **Lexical Resource (6):** Repetitive spelling errors significantly hurt LR.

## Common Patterns at Band 5.0 – 5.5

1. **Word count under 250** (≈ 200–230 words): automatic penalty.
2. **Frequent spelling errors** (e.g. "alot", "compitition", "coopration") — normally easy with proofreading.
3. **Grammatical fragments**: clauses starting with "Because…" punctuated as full sentences; missing subjects or verbs.
4. **Subject-verb agreement** errors with collective/plural nouns ("old generation are…", "an environment that allow…").
5. **Mechanical signposting**: "Firstly, Secondly, Lastly" without genuine progression.
6. **Vocabulary repetition** (≥7× of the same content word).
7. **Mismatched essay type**: writing adv/disadv when prompt asks for opinion, or vice versa.
8. **Generic body content** without specific examples — no statistics, no named entities.
9. **Comma splices** and missing words ("they have not enough experiences" — missing "got" or rephrase).
10. **Informal register**: "I think", "really", "lots of", contractions ("can't"), use of "you".

# IELTS Task 2 — Band 7.0 Sample Essays

Band 7.0 is the threshold of "good user". Examiners look for: a clear position throughout the response, well-developed main ideas with relevant support, logically organised paragraphs with a range of cohesive devices, less common vocabulary used with awareness of style/collocation, and a variety of complex structures with the majority of sentences error-free. Errors and slips are still tolerated, but they should not impede communication.

The samples below are reproduced verbatim from publicly available IELTS preparation sites. Each is graded Band 7.0 (overall) by the source. Where a per-criterion breakdown is given, individual scores may vary between 6.0 and 8.0 — the overall is 7.0 because the weighted average lands there.

## Sample 1: Migration to Cities (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [ieltsbuddy.com — IELTS Band 7 Essay Samples](https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-band-7-essay-samples.html)
**Question Type:** Two-part question (causes + adv/disadv)
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Young people are leaving their homes in rural areas to work or study in cities. What are the reasons? Do the advantages of this development outweigh the drawbacks?

### Essay
The comparison of standards of the cities and small town or villages has been always a debate. Recently, teenagers choose to live in the cities rather than their home villages because of school or job opportunities. This essay will discuss multiple reasons behind this trend and explain why the advantages of being in a city do indeed outweigh its drawbacks.

There are several reasons to desire living in urban areas. Firstly, it gives people an opportunity to study in better schools which cannot be found in rural areas. Since in the modern world education means very much for people's future, it is crucial to have higher education degrees for those individuals to find well-paid jobs. In addition to that, city life provides people with completely different experiences than their home villages. Thanks to the schools, work or social gathering places, they get to meet a greater number of people from all around the country compared to their rural towns which is crucial for one's personal development. Lastly, in the cities, not only they get bigger number of job options, but also they can earn larger amount of money. It is very well know that job market is significantly limited in the villages also the current jobs barely pay enough.

It is clearly seen that benefits of leaving villages outweigh its few number of deficits. It is worth to mention that people face some issues, such as being away from their extended family, more competitive and challenging job market, and substantially more expensive living cost, when they move to the cities. Advantages like learning and exploring new experiences, getting a better education leading to a better paid job and having an interesting career, however, surpass the number of the drawbacks of this development.

To conclude, there are various reasons for young generation to leave their homes to live in the cities and this movement's benefits easily outweigh its disadvantages.

### Word Count
~340 words

### Examiner Comments
The question receives adequate answers with supported ideas. However, drawbacks require expanded discussion to reach Band 8. The essay demonstrates good organization with transition signals, though some devices appear overused in initial positions. Vocabulary shows appropriate topic-related terms and collocation awareness, though uncommon lexical items are limited. Grammar includes solid complex structures but contains errors, particularly toward the essay's conclusion.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **Task Response:** Both parts of the prompt addressed (reasons + outweigh). A clear position is taken and held throughout. Where it falls short of 8: the drawbacks paragraph is compressed into one sentence rather than fully developed.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Four-paragraph structure with clear topic sentences. "Firstly / In addition to that / Lastly" carry the body paragraph. Cohesion within the longer sentence on "city life" is slightly mechanical.
- **Lexical Resource:** Reasonable range — "well-paid jobs", "personal development", "deficits", "outweigh" — used with general accuracy. Some collocations clumsy ("standards of the cities", "current jobs barely pay enough").
- **GRA:** Mix of complex and compound sentences; relative clauses used. Errors present (article omission "have higher education degrees", "It is very well know") but do not impede meaning.

## Sample 2: Dangerous Sports (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [ieltsbuddy.com — IELTS Band 7 Essay Samples](https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-band-7-essay-samples.html)
**Question Type:** Two-part question (why + how to minimise)
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Large numbers of people participate in sports that are extremely dangerous. Why do you think people do this? How can the risks of participation be minimised?

### Essay
Today many games are being played all over the world. Some of them are extremely risky and life threatening. Despite of these facts their popularity is growing and many people are playing such games on a professional level. This essay will identify the reasons why people are playing such life threatening games and provide some practical approaches to minimize the risk associated with these games.

First of all we need to find out the reason why such dangerous games are becoming so popular. To start with, the major attraction for players is good money that could be earned from a single game in just one day. This type of quick money increases stimulation among many players to adopt games such as boxing, wrestling, bull riding etc. Perhaps, fame and popularity is another compelling reason that encourages people to choose certain games. Furthermore, its proven that it is human nature to accept challenges and do things differently. Many players love to accept challenges and they want to astonish the world by their unique talent and skills.

As these games are now being introduced in the world and many people are joining them it is important to reduce risks by introducing some policies and procedures. For instance, anyone who wants to participate in particular games would not be allowed to play it until he/she deemed hundred per cent competence in required skills and knowledge. Secondly, it is important to endorse high quality personal protection equipment and training to combat risk associated with games.

In conclusion, excellent level of policies, procedures and training are integral part of treating the risks associated with such games.

### Word Count
~280 words

### Examiner Comments
All task components receive coverage with a clear position presented. Main ideas require greater specificity to advance to Band 8. The essay demonstrates logical progression with diverse cohesive devices and clear paragraph organization. Vocabulary range suffices with some less common terms, though word choice errors prevent higher scores. Grammar is generally strong but multiple sentence errors appear, with some particularly noticeable; accuracy improvements are necessary to maintain this level.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **Task Response:** Both parts of the two-part question receive at least one body paragraph. Reasons are concrete (money, fame, challenge); solutions are concrete (training, equipment).
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Standard four-paragraph layout. Topic sentences signpost each paragraph. "First of all / Furthermore / Secondly" structure works.
- **Lexical Resource:** Some less common items: "compelling reason", "endorse high quality protection equipment", "integral part", "combat risk". Mis-collocations: "stimulation among many players", "deemed hundred per cent competence".
- **GRA:** Variety of structures (passives, infinitive of purpose, relative clauses). Errors visible — "Despite of these facts", "its proven", missing article "the world by their unique talent" — but they do not block understanding.

## Sample 3: Live/Work Anywhere via Technology (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/the-impact-of-technology-on-society-6606c5eea1739/)
**Question Type:** Discuss positive and negative effects
**Overall Band:** 7.0 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 6 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
The impact of technology on society. Discuss the positive and negative effects of technology.

### Essay
Technology, a ubiquitous presence in our daily lives, has revolutionized everything from communication to transportation, entertainment, and healthcare. Its pervasive influence has ushered in significant advancements and conveniences, a testament to its positive impact. However, it is crucial to acknowledge that it is not all sunshine and rainbows.

Technology, particularly social networks, encompassing Facebook and Twitter, has made the task of reconnecting with old friends and forging new connections a breeze. This ease of communication is just one of the many ways technology has enriched our lives. For instance, the use of educational video games has sparked a newfound interest in learning among students. Moreover, research shows that students enjoy learning with technology, leading many schools to provide free Internet on campus. Consequently, this empowerment allows students to conduct research and learn independently, a truly transformative aspect of technology.

With the increased addiction to social networks and Internet games, people are spending more time on computers and rejecting their everyday offline lives. Therefore, it has increased isolation and socialisation. For example, students no longer take time to solve equations and research topics; all they do is query Google or use a calculator to prove the answer. Furthermore, without these devices, they would not know how to achieve the same results in a library or on paper. As a result, technology has replaced many positions humans used to occupy.

In conclusion, technology has brought significant positive and negative changes to our society. We must use technology responsibly, avoiding harm to our wellbeing and preventing social inequalities.

### Word Count
255 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay adequately discusses both the positive and negative effects but could benefit from more specific examples and statistics. Transitions between ideas need strengthening for improved cohesion. Vocabulary variety is encouraged, particularly reducing repetition of terms like "technology" (6 uses) and "students" (4 uses).

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** Both sides covered; clear position implied (responsible use). Slightly under-developed.
- **CC:** Logical, but transition words concentrated in body 2 ("For instance / Moreover / Consequently"). The contrast pivot at start of body 3 needs more work.
- **LR:** Good less-common items — "ubiquitous", "pervasive influence", "ushered in", "a testament to", "not all sunshine and rainbows". Some imprecise: "increased isolation and socialisation" (probably meant "and reduced socialisation").
- **GRA:** Complex sentences with apposition (commas), conditionals ("without these devices, they would not know"). Largely accurate.

## Sample 4: Socialising With Work Colleagues (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-that-its-a-good-idea-to-socialise-with-work-colleagues-during-evenings-and-weekends-c5d529fcf9590be0/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
Some people think that it's a good idea to socialise with work colleagues during evenings and weekends. Other people think it's important to keep working life completely separate from social life. Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

### Essay
Being a part of a working community is essential in our modern life. We spend time with our colleagues during meetings, discussions as well as lunch breaks. Moreover, I know some employees who are in favour of socialising with each other even in their time off.

Personally, I think it is a good idea as humans can get to know one another much better, which will definitely contribute to building a strong and a close-knit community at work. I firmly believe, that it is far more effective to work in a healthy working environment where everyone listens to the others, provides support and ready to help whatever happens. Also, I would like to admit that these working conditions will definitely reduce stress and make you feel fullfilled and satisfied with what you do.

However, some people prefer not keeping in touch with their colleagues at the weekend. Regarding my experience the first reason for that is the inevitability of the conversations about work. For instance, I used to go out with two women from my former job who always gossiped about others in an office, discussed career prospects or a current project with tight deadlines. Undoubtedly, speaking about work in your downtime is likely to lead to burnout and doesn't give you an opportunity to recuperate and recharge your social battery.

To conclude, I would rather highlight that everyone is different. Some of us have introverted types of personalities, whereas others are extraverts, so we all need different things to unwind. Therefore, the decision whether to spend your free time with your colleagues or not should never be judges.

### Word Count
269 words

### Examiner Comments
Strengths: appropriately addresses both viewpoints with clear position-taking. Ideas are supported with relevant examples, particularly the personal anecdote about office gossip. Areas for improvement: repeated vocabulary ("working", "colleagues", "others", "about") requires synonyms. Grammatical accuracy needs refinement — "should never be judges" contains an error. The conclusion could develop ideas more thoroughly for a higher overall band score.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR (8):** Both views addressed; opinion clear and held throughout. Personal example concrete.
- **CC (7):** Clear paragraph roles. Pivots between paragraphs are explicit ("However").
- **LR (7):** Useful collocations: "close-knit community", "tight deadlines", "recharge your social battery", "lead to burnout", "downtime", "to unwind".
- **GRA (6):** This is the criterion holding it back from 7.5: "should never be judges" (= "judged"); "ready to help whatever happens" (article + structure); "fullfilled" misspelt.

## Sample 5: Government Funding of Healthcare and Education (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-the-government-should-pay-for-healthcare-and-education-but-other-people-claim-that-it-is-the-individuals-65c35f6bcef71/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.0–7.5 (engnovate marks 7.5; included here as a high 7.0 / low 7.5 reference)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Some people think the government should pay for healthcare and education but other people claim that it is the individual's responsibility.

### Essay
It is true that the demand for healthcare and educational services has significantly increased worldwide in recent years. While there is a widespread belief that individuals should be responsible for paying for their own healthcare and education, the government would still be accountable on this issue.

On the one hand, some people believe that health and education are personal matters, and it is the responsibility of each individual to maintain their own health and education. The fact that different segments of the population, such as employed and unemployed citizens, contribute varied levels of taxes to the government, creates disparities in the amount of government funding for health care and educational services. Therefore, it would be unfair if all citizens received the same amount of government funding for these services. Furthermore, by paying their own fees, people would be more serious about taking care of themselves.

On the other hand, some people believe that the responsibility of paying for healthcare and educational expenses belongs to the government. This can be achieved through income taxes, property taxes, and value-added taxes, which can strongly support the government in developing their army and technology. Therefore, government budgets should be allocated to fund medical activities and educational programs for their citizens, especially for the benefit of the poor who do not have access to good education and decent healthcare. By covering these expenses, governments would gain several advantages, such as training productive laborers who can contribute to economic development.

In conclusion, the responsibility for paying fees for healthcare and educational services falls on both individuals and governments. Individuals should be accountable for their own education and healthcare, while governments should take appropriate actions to relieve the heavy burden of expenses.

### Word Count
285 words

### Examiner Comments
Strengths: balanced argument addressing both perspectives with appropriate organization. Areas for improvement: incorporate more specific examples, enhance transitional phrases, reduce vocabulary repetition (particularly "government", "healthcare", "education").

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** Both perspectives developed with reasoning. Opinion is the synthesis ("both").
- **CC:** Classic four-paragraph "On the one hand / On the other hand" structure works.
- **LR:** "fiscal stability"-adjacent terms ("disparities", "allocated", "decent healthcare", "value-added taxes") give it lexical credibility.
- **GRA:** Mostly accurate complex structures; some heavy noun phrases ("the responsibility of paying for healthcare and educational expenses belongs to the government") that could be tightened.

## Sample 6: Urbanisation — Positive or Negative? (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-that-urbanisation-is-increasing-day-by-day-65891cc4208b7/)
**Question Type:** Positive/Negative development
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Some people think that urbanisation is increasing day by day. What do you think — is it a positive or negative development?

### Essay
Statistics show that the world's population is increasing rapidly. It is expected that most of us will be living in cities within the next few decades. The question of whether urbanisation is a positive or negative development remains controversial.

A rapid influx of people moving from rural to urban areas is bound to cause problems. Firstly, pressure on resources such as housing and transportation intensifies. It is becoming difficult for many people to afford adequate housing in cities. A by-product of this is the creation of slums causing low-income families to group together in neglected parts of the city. These people often become trapped in a cycle of poverty that is difficult to escape.

As mentioned above, the growth of urban areas can also lead to severe traffic congestion because more and more vehicles travel into the city from the suburbs. This has many knock-on effects, such as problems with air quality. It also leads many city dwellers to experience mental health issues because travelling across large cities is tiring and stressful.

All of this being said, I don't believe that urbanisation is a wholly negative development. There are advantages to living in large cities that are well managed. For example, there are more schools which means more education opportunities. Access to higher-quality health care is often better in cities. Some cities have also introduced ride-sharing, e-bikes and part and ride services that reduce environmental problems.

My opinion is that many of the problems associated with urbanisation are avoidable but dealing with increasing populations in cities is a formidable challenge. How governments, businesses and society respond to this challenge will dramatically affect the future of our world.

### Word Count
277 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay addresses both perspectives on urbanization effectively. However, the opinion could be more explicitly stated in the introduction. Vocabulary shows strength but contains repetition — particularly "cities" (7 instances), "urbanisation" (3), "people" (3) and "problems" (3) — suggesting opportunities for synonym variation.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** Both negative and positive sides developed; opinion stated in conclusion (slightly late but acceptable).
- **CC:** Topic sentences strong: "A rapid influx…", "As mentioned above…", "All of this being said…".
- **LR:** "knock-on effects", "by-product", "city dwellers", "formidable challenge", "ride-sharing, e-bikes and part-and-ride services" — exam-friendly less-common items.
- **GRA:** Mix of structures, including non-finite clauses ("causing low-income families to group…"), passive voice, future expectations.

## Sample 7: Media Bias — Why and What Can Be Done (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/many-people-feel-that-media-coverage-is-becoming-increasingly-biased-today-e21a804f21263567/)
**Question Type:** Two-part (why + solutions / problem & solution)
**Overall Band:** 7.0 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 6.5 | LR: 6 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Many people feel that media coverage is becoming increasingly biased today. Why is this? What can be done to fix this problem?

### Essay
People often rely on media houses and news channels for global information and updates, with the hope of it being neutral in many ways. However, media coverage these days is becoming biased and focusing on promoting news that is targeted to a specific audience. This essay portrays various reasons as to why this happens and what can be done to fix these issues.

A major reason why media coverage is becoming biased is due to overall viewership. Viewers prefer a certain type of content, which media channels promote through their products. In doing so, media houses tend to amend facts and opinions to suit their target audience. Often, many viewers of these specific media channels with hidden coverage tend to believe this bias and formulate strong opinions based on limited facts. A solution to this problem would be to establish a statutory board that creates a framework of operation, stating rules and policies for relaying information. For example, in Norway, all news channels are managed by the Censor Board of Norway, which sets a benchmark and working policy within which all media houses function.

In addition to viewership, media houses are also controlled by many political parties, who tend to promote their agenda and political ideas through media channels. Major media houses are financially backed by political parties, leading to information being either omitted or exaggerated depending on the alignment of the news with their ideologies. This is one of the major reasons why media coverage tends to become biased. For instance, in India, news channels like Republic Inc and India Today focus mostly on new political developments of the ruling party instead of covering issues such as illiteracy and poverty. To overcome this issue, there must be a regulation that does not allow media channels to be owned entirely by political parties and instead promotes part ownership through public investment, giving people the right to share their opinions about media coverage.

In conclusion, media coverage and information are largely becoming biased for viewers. To overcome this challenge, there should be more regulations and policies to manage how information is portrayed to viewers.

### Word Count
353 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay adequately addresses both parts of the prompt, providing relevant reasons. Further development of the solutions and a more in-depth exploration of the causes could elevate the response. Vocabulary repetition noted (particularly "media", "channels", "houses").

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** Both parts (why + solutions) covered; named examples (Norway, India) elevate the answer.
- **CC:** Each body paragraph follows cause→solution; logical, but the cause and solution are stitched together rather than separated.
- **LR (6):** Heavy repetition of "media houses / channels". Some good items: "statutory board", "benchmark", "promote their agenda", "alignment of the news".
- **GRA (7.5):** This is the strongest criterion — present participle modifiers, embedded clauses, conditional structures.

## Sample 8: Pollution and Housing vs Disease Prevention (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-governments-should-focus-on-reducing-environmental-pollution-and-housing-problems-to-help-people-prevent-illness-and-disease-656c4acfbcfd0/)

**Per-criterion:** TR: 6 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
Some people think governments should focus on reducing environmental pollution and housing problems to help people prevent illness and disease. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay
It is often argued that focusing on decreasing environmental pollution and housing issues, officials are able to aid people in the prevention of illness and disease. Looking on it as an insupportable argument, I aim to challenge this view in this essay.

Of course, it is an undeniable fact that environmental pollution and housing problems can face people with a substantial number of diseases that can be avoided by adopting viable solutions by governments. For instance, the elders who are exposed to air pollution are more likely to have respiratory disease; likewise, housing problems in slums like the lack of unfiltered water can lead to poisoning. However, it should not be ignored that illnesses can stem from other factors as well. Thus, governments are better to broaden their horizon in this field.

On the other hand, from my perspective, the government had better adopt appropriate measures in the field of disease prevention rather than addressing house problems or pollution. To illustrate, providing individuals with vital information regarding all kinds of diseases, governments are able to minimize the likelihood of becoming ill. For instance, according to the results of a research conducted in Japan, having informed Japanese how to treat a person with contagious disease, officials were able to manipulate the situation well.

In conclusion, although alleviating the problems of housing and environmental pollution by government can be beneficial to minimize the number of diseases, it seems to that this this issue can be addressed with more practical measure like raising the public awareness regarding all sorts of illnesses.

### Word Count
258 words

### Examiner Comments
The composition addresses the prompt with a defined stance. While supporting details and illustrations appear, the argument could demonstrate improved equilibrium by recognizing advantages of the proposed governmental interventions. Organization follows traditional structure, though expanded cohesive markers would strengthen connections. Vocabulary selection is sophisticated yet exhibits repetition reducible through synonyms.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR (6):** This is what stops 7.5 — the stance ("insupportable argument") is dismissive, undermining the discussion. The example of Japan is a fact-substitute rather than evidence.
- **CC (7):** Paragraph roles clear: concession → counterargument → conclusion.
- **LR (7):** "viable solutions", "stem from other factors", "broaden their horizon", "alleviating the problems", "raising public awareness" — all good Band 7+ collocations.
- **GRA (7):** Adverbial participles, comparative + better-as-adverb constructions, complex conditionals.

## Sample 9: Job Hopping — Positive or Negative Development? (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/the-recent-time-has-witnessed-a-widespead-of-changing-job-or-career-of-the-youth-66750c6dd140d/)
**Question Type:** Positive/Negative development
**Overall Band:** 7.0–7.5 (engnovate gives 7.5; sits squarely on the 7.0/7.5 boundary)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 7

### Prompt
The recent time has witnessed a widespread change of job or career amongst the youth. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

### Essay
In recent years, those just entering the workforce have been alternating between occupations more frequently. Whether this practice has been beneficial to workers or not has been a subject of fierce debate. While some believe that changing jobs equips young people with a more expansive skill set, I contend that it is more likely to impair one's career due to a hesitation on the part of companies to hire employees who change jobs constantly.

One major reason behind the rise of this phenomenon is that young people are still seeking their passions. As many are unclear about their career path, they try out jobs to discover what they would enjoy pursuing professionally. If they are dissatisfied with a given position, young people will find another job that better suits them. The other key reason is young people want to be equipped with a diverse skill set. As different jobs utilize different knowledge bases, young people often find it advantageous to acquire a wide-ranging variety of professional skills. Therefore, in the future when a new job is offered to them, youths will be better prepared for the work entailed in this position.

However, this phenomenon has negative effects on workers as companies are reluctant to hire for uncertainty. Employers often hold a prejudice against those who frequently alternate between occupations as they think a person who does so will not be able to contribute to their company long-term. To cite an example, according to a survey conducted by Forbes in 2011, 70% of American employers would reject hiring so-called "job hoppers" on the basis that they want prospective employees to devote themselves to a company in the long run. These employers have also expressed that if someone cannot promise to remain loyal to their company, then it would be counterproductive to hire and commit resources to them. Based on this study, it is justifiable to claim that constantly changing jobs can hinder a person's career.

In conclusion, young people change jobs to acquire multiple skills and find out what they desire to do in their careers. However, I contend that the drawbacks are much more substantial since this might impede one's career opportunities. Nonetheless, it can be predicted that young people's careers will continue to change in their search for true passions.

### Word Count
381 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay demonstrates solid understanding with well-developed and balanced discussion, supported by relevant evidence including a Forbes survey reference. Strengths include logical organization and smooth transitions. However, assessors note vocabulary repetition (e.g., "young" appears 7 times) and recommend incorporating synonyms for more sophisticated expression.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** Position clear in introduction and reaffirmed in conclusion. The Forbes statistic is a strong piece of evidence.
- **CC:** "However", "To cite an example", "Based on this study", "Nonetheless" — varied connectors.
- **LR:** "alternating between occupations", "expansive skill set", "knowledge bases", "counterproductive", "remain loyal", "hinder a person's career" — strong collocations.
- **GRA:** Reduced relatives, complex noun phrases, conditional sentences. Largely error-free.

## Sample 10: Big Companies vs Small Businesses (Band 7.0–7.5)

## Sample 11: Career Importance — Agree/Disagree (Band 7.0)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/the-most-important-element-in-a-persons-life-is-their-work-66b87e2a02290/)
**Question Type:** Agree/Disagree (To what extent…)
**Overall Band:** 7.0
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 6.5

### Prompt
The most important element in a person's life is their work. Without a satisfying career, life is meaningless. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay
The notion that the most crucial facet in an individual's life is their career is believed by a number of individuals. Without a desirable job, life is meaningless. From my perspective, I partly disagree with this statement for the following reasons.

There are some legitimate reasons why some people agree with this idea. To begin with, a career will help people achieve financial stability. With enough money, people can acquire amenities and their favorable items, for instance a bag, thereby satisfying themselves and having a fulfilled life. Another reason is that the working environment can leave significant impacts on a person. If one is in a good and promoting working condition, with sufficient competition from colleges, their career prospects can be promising, encouraging them to work harder for a more lucrative income.

However, I believe that career is not the main factor that contributes to a happy life. First and foremost, a person's social life can help them to be more satisfied. Communities can hold emotional values, which is difficult to find in a working environment, one of them is sense of belonging. Individuals in a friendly neighborhood will have more chances to socialize, therefore enhancing their bonds and protecting their mental health; while on the other hand, not having a strong connection outside working areas and family, can be depressing and could lead to mental health issues. Furthermore, some relationships can contribute positively to a person's emotion. For instance, people can share some of their deepest thoughts or problems they are facing to their bestfriend, seeking advice or simply just finding a person to relieve themselves. This would lead to a more fulfilled life, rather than just life with a promising career.

To conclude, while a desirable job is admittedly crucial, I stand my viewpoint that other facets, namely social life, is just as necessary as a favorable job for the above-mentioned reasons.

### Word Count
314 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay presents a clear and well-developed argument, exploring both sides of the issue with effective examples. However, elaboration on social life's importance could strengthen the response. Organization is logical with smooth transitions, though minor grammatical errors affect flow. Vocabulary demonstrates sophistication, yet complexity could improve. Grammar is generally sound but shows minor issues affecting clarity; more complex structures would enhance the response.

### Why this is band 7 (not 6)
- **TR:** "Partly disagree" is held; both agreement and disagreement addressed with reasons.
- **CC:** Paragraph topic sentences signpost each role.
- **LR (7.5):** "facet", "lucrative income", "sense of belonging", "fulfilled life", "career prospects" — solid Band 7+.
- **GRA (6.5):** Slips holding it back from 7.5: "competition from colleges" (= "colleagues"), "share … to their bestfriend" (= "with"), "stand my viewpoint" (collocation), "is just as necessary as a favorable job" (subject-verb agreement with "facets").

## Common Patterns at Band 7.0

1. **Clear position** stated in the introduction and reaffirmed in the conclusion.
2. **Both/all parts of the prompt addressed** with at least one developed body paragraph each.
3. **A discernible range of cohesive devices**, sometimes mechanical but consistently used.
4. **Topic-related vocabulary with awareness of collocation**: 8–12 distinct less-common items per essay.
5. **Mix of complex and simple sentence structures** — relative clauses, conditionals, participle clauses, passive voice.
6. **Errors are noticeable but not impeding**: typos, article issues, occasional SVA, occasional word-form slip.
7. **Examples are concrete** — country names, named entities, statistics (Forbes, Singapore, Norway, India, Japan).
8. **Word count comfortably above 250** — typically 270–380.

## What Stops a Band 7.0 from Reaching 7.5

- A weak criterion (often CC or GRA) at 6 or 6.5 dragging down the average.
- Lexical repetition (e.g., "media houses" 7×, "young people" 7×, "technology" 6×).
- Mechanical cohesion — same connector phrases ("Firstly / Secondly / In conclusion") instead of varied linking.
- Examples present but generic — the Band 7.5 essays cite Forbes 2011, COVID-19 ICU overcrowding, Earth Hour, etc.
- Word-choice errors at the rate of one per paragraph rather than one per essay.

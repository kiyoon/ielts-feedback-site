# IELTS Task 2 — Band 7.5 Sample Essays

Band 7.5 sits between Band 7 ("good user") and Band 8 ("very good user"). Examiners look for: a fully developed position with extended ideas, logical paragraphing with skilful use of cohesive devices and clear referencing, sufficient flexibility and precision in vocabulary including some natural and sophisticated less-common items, and a wide range of structures with the majority of sentences error-free (errors and inappropriacies rare).

Most band 7.5 essays in this file have at least one criterion sub-score at Band 8 (often Task Response or Lexical Resource).

The samples are reproduced verbatim from publicly available IELTS preparation sites.

## Sample 1: Try New Things vs Familiar Routines (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-like-to-try-new-things-65b21eb93d5df/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Some people like to try new things, for example, places to visit and types of food. Other people prefer to keep doing things they are familiar with. Discuss both these attitudes and give your own opinion.

### Essay
The allure of novelty attracts some individuals, while others find comfort in familiar routines. This essay examines both perspectives and provides a personal viewpoint. I personally advocate for embracing new experiences, as I believe they offer opportunities for personal growth and fulfillment.

Many people derive contentment from adhering to familiar practices and environments. Those with this disposition find security in established habits and knowledge. They contend that familiarity fosters predictability and stability in life. While this perspective holds merit for those who seek comfort in the status quo, it might also prevent them from experiencing the diverse pleasures that new experiences can offer. For instance, an individual who consistently sticks to the same cuisine and avoids exploring new flavors may miss out on enriching gustatory experiences. However, venturing beyond their comfort zone may expose them to new culinary delights and broaden their cultural understanding.

Conversely, some individuals actively seek out challenges and new experiences to expand their knowledge and skills. These individuals demonstrate a willingness to explore different locales, cuisines, and activities, as well as embark on varied career paths. Their openness to change and willingness to confront challenges contribute to personal growth and resilience. For example, my husband and I recently ventured to a new country and encountered a situation where we lost our way to the hotel. Initially, it was a disconcerting experience, leading us to consider avoiding such adventures in the future. However, the assistance we received from locals not only guided us to our destination but also instilled in us the confidence to navigate unfamiliar terrain. This encounter served as a transformative learning experience, empowering us to embrace future challenges without fear.

In my view, both attitudes have their respective merits and relevance based on individual inclinations and circumstances. Nevertheless, I am inclined towards pursuing new experiences, as I believe they provide avenues for personal and intellectual growth. Embracing change not only opens doors to new opportunities but also adds depth and richness to life.

In conclusion, adhering to familiar routines offers a sense of stability with minimal risks but may limit the potential for enriching experiences. Conversely, confronting challenges and embracing new experiences may entail risks, yet the rewards often translate to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.

### Word Count
373 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay appropriately addresses the prompt with relevant, fully extended ideas. Organization is coherent with effective cohesive devices. Vocabulary demonstrates precision and natural control, though more sophisticated lexical choices could enhance resource. Grammar shows flexibility and control, though minor punctuation refinements and more complex structures would strengthen range.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)
- **Task Response:** Five-paragraph structure (intro / view 1 / view 2 / personal preference / synthesis) — far more developed than typical Band 7. Both views are extended with concrete examples (the personal anecdote in body 2 is specific and integrated).
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Skilful paragraph roles; cohesion is achieved through reference ("Those with this disposition", "These individuals", "this encounter") rather than only connectors.
- **Lexical Resource:** Sophisticated lexical chunks: "the allure of novelty", "adhering to familiar practices", "the status quo", "enriching gustatory experiences", "venturing beyond their comfort zone", "disconcerting experience", "instilled in us the confidence", "transformative learning experience".
- **GRA:** Wide range — non-finite clauses ("leading us to consider…"), passive constructions, fronted adverbials, complex noun phrases. Errors are rare.

## Sample 2: Technology and Environmental Problems (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/development-in-technology-has-brought-various-enviornmental-problems-65bccc93a053c/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Development in technology has brought various environmental problems. Some believe that people need to live simpler lives to solve environmental problems. Others, however, believe technology is the way to solve these problems. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

### Essay
The environment is currently being negatively impacted by technological advancements. While some think that living simpler lives is the only way to solve these environmental issues, I believe that technological advancements may help in their solution.

On the one hand, people think that individuals should lead simpler lives and use less technology. The first recommendation is that instead of using cars, individuals should walk or ride bicycles. This is due to the fact that there are currently more automobiles being produced and driven, which results in high exhaust fume levels and air pollution being a major problem. In order to limit the exploitation of natural resources like nuclear power and fossil fuels, people should also make an effort to use less electricity. The yearly Earth Hour event, in which everyone is asked to turn off their lights for sixty minutes, serves as an illustration of this concept.

On the other hand, I think that technological advancements can make a significant difference in environmental preservation. First off, such advancements have made it possible for individuals to find methods of recycling items like glass and paper, which lessens the issue of disposing of waste. Second, technology can contribute to the use of renewable resources in the production of power. For example, numerous nations across the globe have constructed solar-powered power plants. Because of this, producing electricity does not require using non-renewable resources, nor does it cause grave environmental harm.

In conclusion, while some believe that living a simpler life can help prevent environmental difficulties, I believe that technical advancements can help address these challenges.

### Word Count
262 words

### Examiner Comments
The response demonstrates solid understanding of both perspectives but suffers from minor coherence lapses, particularly the repetition of the same point in the second paragraph. Vocabulary is generally appropriate but contains repetition.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)
- **Task Response:** Both views fully presented in their own paragraph; opinion stated in introduction and reaffirmed in conclusion.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** "On the one hand / On the other hand" is balanced with mid-paragraph connectors ("First off / Second / Because of this").
- **Lexical Resource:** "exploitation of natural resources", "exhaust fume levels", "renewable resources", "non-renewable resources", "grave environmental harm" — Band 7.5 collocations.
- **GRA:** Passives ("being negatively impacted", "are produced and driven"), reduced relatives, parallel structures.

## Sample 3: Free Government-Funded Education and Healthcare (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-believe-that-education-and-healthcare-should-be-free-of-charge-and-funded-by-the-government-65febc0db6b94/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Some people believe that education and healthcare should be free of charge and funded by the government. Others think that it should be the responsibility of the people to pay for these services. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

### Essay
The matter of whether the government should bear the cost of education and healthcare services or if individuals should be responsible for it remains a contentious topic. While both perspectives hold certain merit, my stance is that prioritizing government investment in these sectors is crucial for national development and prosperity.

On one hand, there are justifiable arguments supporting the notion that residents should cover the cost of their own education and healthcare. Chief among these is the preservation of fiscal stability, as self-financing ensures that the public budget and national reserve fund are not unduly strained, thereby mitigating overall financial risks. Additionally, proponents of individual responsibility argue that personal investment in these services fosters a sense of accountability and encourages efficient resource allocation. However, this perspective overlooks the fact that not everyone has the financial capability to manage such expenses effectively, leading to deeper-rooted issues of inequality and social exclusion.

On the other hand, there are more compelling reasons why education and healthcare services should be provided free of charge. By offering education and healthcare at no cost, the financial pressures on citizens are relieved, enabling individuals, particularly the youth, to reach their fullest potential and contribute to the nation's advancement. Furthermore, eliminating financial barriers in essential areas like education and healthcare empowers individuals to focus on personal growth and societal contributions, rather than being consumed by the pursuit of monetary gain. This, in turn, leads to a more equitable society where opportunities are accessible to all, regardless of socio-economic background.

In conclusion, I firmly perceive that governments should bear the primary responsibility for financing education and healthcare services. By doing so, they not only enhance societal welfare and individual empowerment but also establish a solid foundation for sustainable national development.

### Word Count
291 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay effectively presents both viewpoints with clear opinion stated in the conclusion. Recommendations include incorporating specific examples to strengthen arguments and using synonyms to reduce vocabulary repetition.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)
- **TR:** Both views developed at length; the writer's view is clear from intro through conclusion. Two-pronged arguments per side.
- **CC:** Mid-paragraph connectors ("Chief among these / Additionally / However") manage internal flow without becoming repetitive.
- **LR:** "contentious topic", "fiscal stability", "unduly strained", "mitigating overall financial risks", "deeper-rooted issues", "compelling reasons", "equitable society", "regardless of socio-economic background" — high-end Band 7.5 collocations.
- **GRA:** Long, controlled sentences; participial phrases; conditional and passive structures all accurate.

## Sample 4: Globalisation Has Benefited the World (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/there-is-no-doubt-about-that-globalisation-has-benefited-the-world-by-bringing-together-people-6602942a8b025/)
**Question Type:** Agree/Disagree (To what extent…)
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
There is no doubt about that globalisation has benefited the world by bringing together people, business and nations. People who criticise it stand in the way of progress. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay
Globalization has positively impacted the world in numerous ways. Even though it has helped many sectors and countries flourish, some people condemn it thereby obstructing global development. I believe that globalization has mostly been beneficial but there are two sides to this change.

Bringing the world more close has many advantages for the advancement of humans. The ability to travel quickly from one country to another has made it possible for citizens of any country to seek employment elsewhere to earn more money and live a better life. For example, an underpaid nurse in Africa can choose to work in the UK where the salary is higher and the working conditions are superior. Furthermore, through the internet transfer of knowledge and technology has become instant. Hence, any information can be sent immediately anywhere across the globe. For instance, a doctor in the UK can guide a surgery via video call in a war-stricken country and save lives without compromising her safety.

On the hand, increased connectedness can also pose some problems. When international companies enter a country, they disrupt local businesses. If importing onions is cheaper than producing them locally, the domestic farmers will lose income and suffer as the consumers will opt to buy inexpensive products. Moreover, global growth has led to swift spread of diseases worldwide. A recent example of this is Covid, which spread around the globw within weeks causing catastrophe.

In summary, global proliferation has brought people and nations closer than ever before. While accelerating human progress, it has resulted in minor flaws as well. In my view, globalization has been largely favourable.

### Word Count
267 words

### Examiner Comments
The response successfully addresses the question with a balanced position. Strengths include pertinent illustrations and organized structure. Areas for development involve expanding counterarguments and reducing vocabulary repetition (particularly "country", "globalization", "global"). Minor grammatical refinements would support advancement to higher bands.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)
- **TR:** Both sides covered (mostly favourable / minor flaws). Concrete examples: African nurse, UK doctor video-call surgery, COVID, importing onions.
- **CC:** Paragraph functions explicit. Mid-body connectors varied ("Furthermore / Hence / For instance / Moreover").
- **LR:** "war-stricken country", "without compromising her safety", "global proliferation", "swift spread of diseases", "minor flaws".
- **GRA:** Conditional ("If importing onions is cheaper… the domestic farmers will lose"), past participle clauses ("causing catastrophe"), passive constructions.

## Sample 5: Health Awareness vs Hospital Construction (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-that-the-government-should-spend-money-on-health-awareness-while-others-argue-it-is-better-to-spend-66196c1e0761f/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Some people think that the government should spend money on health awareness while others argue it is better to spend on building hospitals rather than health awareness. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

### Essay
The allocation of government funds in the healthcare sector is a topic of debate, with some advocating investment in health awareness campaigns, whereas others prioritize the construction of hospitals. Although funding health awareness initiatives has its advantages, I argue that hospital growth needs to take precedence.

Supporters of health awareness initiatives argue for their long-term advantages in disease prevention and reducing the burden on healthcare systems. For instance, successful anti-smoking campaigns and obesity prevention programs have demonstrated how education can positively influence public health. By educating individuals on healthy lifestyle choices, such campaigns contribute to lowering healthcare costs and improving overall well-being. Statistics reveal that populations with higher health literacy tend to adopt healthier behaviors, resulting in decreased rates of chronic conditions and a more efficient use of healthcare resources.

Conversely, proponents of building hospitals argue that immediate healthcare needs require urgent attention, necessitating the growth of more medical facilities. In situations such as public health emergencies or rapid population growth, the availability of hospitals and treatment centers becomes paramount. Insufficient healthcare infrastructure can lead to serious consequences, as evidenced during the recent COVID-19 pandemic. Specifically, emergency departments and intensive care units were pushed to their limits as they struggled to accommodate the sheer volume of critically ill individuals, leading to instances of overcrowding and disorder within medical facilities. Therefore, investing in hospital buildings ensures timely access to medical care and enhances the capacity to handle healthcare crises effectively.

In conclusion, while both health awareness campaigns and hospital construction are essential components of a comprehensive healthcare system, it seems to me that, overall, it is advantageous to invest more in hospital infrastructure in order to ensure adequate medical care during emergencies.

### Word Count
281 words

### Examiner Comments
The essay addresses the prompt by discussing both views and presents a clear opinion. Strengths include well-developed arguments with relevant examples. Suggested improvement: provide more balanced discussion by giving equal weight to both perspectives.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)
- **TR:** Both views in their own paragraph; opinion held throughout. The COVID-19 example is concrete and elaborated (overcrowding, ICU capacity).
- **CC:** "Whereas / For instance / By educating / Conversely / Specifically / Therefore" — wide range without redundancy.
- **LR:** "anti-smoking campaigns and obesity prevention programs", "public health emergencies", "necessitating the growth", "paramount", "sheer volume of critically ill individuals", "comprehensive healthcare system" — sophisticated and natural.
- **GRA:** Participial complements ("necessitating the growth…"), passives, complex prepositional phrases. Largely error-free.

## Sample 6: Environmental Problems — Individuals vs Society (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-that-environmental-problems-are-too-big-for-individuals-to-solve-66802b6f8e097/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 7.5 | LR: 7.5 | GRA: 7.5

### Prompt
Some people think that environmental problems are too big for individuals to solve. Others believe that individual actions can help improve the environment. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

### Essay
There is a growing debate regarding the role of individuals in addressing environmental issues. Some individuals argue that environmental problems are too vast and complex for individual efforts to make a significant impact. They contend that issues such as climate change and pollution require large-scale governmental and corporate interventions to bring about meaningful change. From their perspective, individual actions like recycling or reducing personal carbon footprint may seem insignificant in the face of industrial-scale pollution and deforestation.

On the other hand, proponents of the view that individuals can make a real difference emphasize the power of collective action. They believe that small, individual actions, when multiplied across communities and societies, can lead to substantial environmental improvements. Advocates of this perspective often point to grassroots movements, community initiatives, and consumer pressure as examples of how individual choices can influence corporate practices and government policies.

In my opinion, while it is true that some environmental challenges require systemic changes at a higher level, individuals still play a crucial role in fostering a culture of sustainability. Small actions like reducing waste, conserving energy, and supporting environmentally friendly products can collectively contribute to a healthier planet. Moreover, individual efforts can serve as a catalyst for broader change by raising awareness, influencing social norms, and holding larger entities accountable for their environmental impact.

In conclusion, while it is undeniable that addressing environmental problems requires coordinated efforts at various levels, individuals should not underestimate the power of their actions. By making conscious choices in their daily lives and advocating for sustainable practices, individuals can indeed make a real difference in mitigating environmental degradation and promoting a more sustainable future for all.

The essay presents a well-balanced discussion of both views with a clearly articulated personal opinion. Vocabulary is precise and varied; grammar is largely accurate.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)

- **CC:** Paragraph topic sentences are full clauses; cohesion via reference ("They contend / Advocates of this perspective / Moreover, individual efforts").

## Sample 7: News Media — Good News vs Reality (Band 7.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/the-media-should-include-more-stories-which-report-good-news-65d9be8591973/)
**Question Type:** Agree/Disagree (To what extent…)
**Overall Band:** 7.5 (±0.5)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 8 | GRA: 8

### Prompt
The media should include more stories which report good news. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay
There is a prevalent debate that the news broadcast from government or private channels should be accurate. While others agree with this notion, I am inclined to broadly agree with the argument for several multifaceted reasons.

The editors of the news should consider that the purpose of the news is to provide actual updates, not entertainment. News is a form of reporting that interprets real-life events for the public. Thus, various types of news offer myriad benefits to the community. For instance, weather forecast programs warn individuals to be aware of taking preventive measures for upcoming hazards like storms, earthquakes, and flooding. The news of vehicle accidents in urban areas reminds people to control their bad driving behavior, such as crossing the red light and driving beyond the speed limit. In certain circumstances, there may be some intensity to the cases that are necessary to report, coupled with the news, such as the rate of fatalities at the scene, to maintain the reality of the news.

In contrast, most people enjoy watching news involving good content rather than violent ones. For instance, the number of viewers is greater for news about celebrity marriages and their daily activities compared to severe crime news, such as cases of robbery and murder. They tend not to see the disgusting pictures of injuries at the scene, like blood and crushed body organs in car accident cases. Additionally, for students who have faced sorrowful events in their academic results and adults who have to overcome intense difficulties in their workplaces, watching bad news disappoints them repeatedly. Therefore, it is imperative that the amount of news broadcast be balanced between good and bad.

In conclusion, while broadcasting good news seems to be instrumental in maintaining the positive emotions of the viewers, it is crucial to prioritize the reality and quality of the news. The effective strategy is to separate the news into distinct categories of good and bad before broadcasting.

### Examiner Comments
The essay addresses the prompt with relevant reasoning but contains some focus inconsistencies regarding news accuracy versus good news inclusion. Organization is generally clear, though cohesive transitions could strengthen the argument. Vocabulary demonstrates sophistication with minor repetition issues ("their", "cases"). Grammar is largely accurate with only minor punctuation lapses.

### Why this is band 7.5 (not 7)

- **CC (7):** "In contrast / Therefore / Additionally" manage flow.
- **LR (8):** "myriad benefits", "preventive measures", "the rate of fatalities at the scene", "imperative", "instrumental in maintaining" — clearly Band 8 lexical sophistication.

## Common Patterns at Band 7.5

1. **A fully developed personal opinion paragraph** — not a token concession but a substantive synthesis with reasoning.
2. **Specific, named examples**: Earth Hour, COVID-19 ICU overcrowding, Forbes 2011 survey, Singapore medical insurance, Norway Censor Board, an underpaid African nurse moving to the UK.
3. **Internal cohesion via reference**, not just connectors — pronouns and demonstratives ("They contend", "These individuals", "this encounter") manage flow.
4. **Sophisticated lexical chunks** integrated naturally: "the allure of novelty", "fiscal stability", "fostering a culture of sustainability", "war-stricken country", "transformative learning experience".
5. **Variety of complex sentence types** — non-finite clauses, participial complements, conditionals, passives, embedded relative clauses, subjunctive.
6. **Errors are noticeable on close reading** but rare and never impede understanding (typos, occasional missing article, occasional mis-collocation).
7. **Word count comfortably 270–380** with paragraphs of 4–6 well-developed sentences.

## What Stops a Band 7.5 from Reaching 8.0

- Vocabulary repetition of one or two key topic words (typically 6–8 occurrences) instead of synonymising.
- Examples that are concrete but not always *integrated* into the argument — they sit in apposition rather than driving the point.
- Cohesion that is consistent but occasionally mechanical at paragraph boundaries ("On the one hand / On the other hand").
- One or two minor grammar slips per essay (article omission, mis-spelling, awkward verb form).

## Reference: A Band 8.0+ Reach

For comparison with Band 7.5 work, here is the kind of feature seen in Band 8 essays:
- Lexical resource at near-native sophistication with idiomatic and figurative usage ("not all sunshine and rainbows", "in the face of industrial-scale pollution").
- Cohesion managed entirely through reference and substitution; connectors are sparing and varied.
- Examples integrated into the argument rather than appended ("…as evidenced during the recent COVID-19 pandemic. Specifically, emergency departments and intensive care units were pushed to their limits…").
- Errors are rare exceptions — perhaps one minor slip per 300 words.

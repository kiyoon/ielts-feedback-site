# IELTS Task 2 — Band 6.5 Sample Essays

Band 6.5 sits exactly between the "competent user" (Band 6) and "good user" (Band 7) tiers. These essays are usually well organised with a clear position; the gap to Band 7 is typically (i) lexical sophistication, (ii) grammatical range, or (iii) depth of supporting examples.

---

## Sample 1: Streaming Services and Inequality (Band 6.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-believe-that-streaming-services-have-made-television-more-accessible-and-diverse-b22245f171bc13da/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 8 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GRA: 5

### Prompt
Some people believe that streaming services have made television more accessible and diverse. Others argue that they have created new forms of inequality in media consumption. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

### Essay
In recent years, streaming platforms have reshaped the way citizens consume television content, enabling it to be more various and accessible than in the past. While some argue that these services democratize the content and entertainment, other people believe that new forms of imbalances in media consumption emerge due to streaming platforms. In this essay, I will discuss both perspectives before stating my opinion.

On the one hand, streaming services such as Netflix, Disney have transformed television to be more accessible and diverse. This is because they offer a wide range of contents that would otherwise be unavailable through conventional broadcasting, which allows users to watch contents anytime and anywhere when they have Internet connection. This versatile means has got rid of geographical barriers, allowing audiences to explore the shows from different countries, which boosts the cultural insights. Moreover, streaming services also provide diverse genres and languages, expanding the audiences' choices. Consequenty, many people are no longer restricted ta local channels and can be exposed to better viewing experiences.

On the other hand, streaming platforms can also create inequality in media consumption. To begin with, not everyone are capable of accessing to a reliable Internet connect or even afford-subscription fees, especially low-income individuals in less developed areas. This means that the increasing groups of peopte are excluded from the advantages of expeniencing streaming platforms. Furthermore, some dominant industries, including Netflix, Disney, attract a huge numbers of people by mass appeal of different choices, which reflects dominant content, particularly Western values. This leads to the harshly competitive market where the local businesses attempt to survive. As a result, many have access to a rich diversity of entertainment when others encounter not only technological barriers but also economic problems.

In conclusion, although Streaming platforms offer the more accessible and diverse way to television, they also introduce new forms of disparities. In my opinion, the benefits of streaming services are significant, greater efforts should be implemented to ensure the equal access so that their merits can be shared on global scale.

### Word Count
~330

### Examiner Comments
"Essay addresses prompt adequately but shows organizational weaknesses, grammatical errors (subject-verb agreement, article misuse), and vocabulary repetition requiring attention to improvement."

### Specific Issues
- **TR (8):** Both sides explored at length; opinion clear.
- **CC (6):** Long body paragraphs without sub-structure.
- **LR (6):** "various" used to mean "varied"; "Consequenty" / "ta" / "peopte" / "expeniencing" — typos; "got rid of" informal.
- **GRA (5):** "not everyone are" SVA; "afford-subscription fees" hyphen mistake; "a huge numbers of" article+number disagreement.

## Sample 2: University vs Work Experience (Band 6.5)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-that-studying-at-university-is-the-best-way-to-prepare-for-a-successful-career-6093f0ffb8bb30d0/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
Some people think that studying at university is the best way to prepare for a successful career, while others believe that gaining work experience is more important. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

### Essay [244 words; partial summary]
Argues that pursuing higher education provides superior career preparation. Presents arguments favouring university study (knowledge acquisition, skill development, networking) while acknowledging early work experience offers practical benefits. Concludes that university is preferable but suggests combining both approaches via part-time employment during studies.

### Examiner Comments
- "Falls short of the required 250 words" (244 words).
- Vocabulary repetition: "university" 6×.
- "Good command of grammar" with minor SVA / article / tense errors.

### Specific Issues
- **TR (7):** Both views addressed; opinion stated.
- **CC (7):** Logical organisation.
- **LR (6):** Repetition; needs *higher education, tertiary studies, undergraduate programme*.
- **GRA (6):** SVA, article, tense — minor but consistent.

### Why This Is Band 6.5 (not 7)
Sub-250 word penalty + LR repetition. Adding 30 well-chosen words and synonymising "university" twice would push to Band 7.

## Sample 6: Marriage and Childbirth in Thirties (Band 6.5)

**Source:** [ieltspodcast.com](https://www.ieltspodcast.com/writing-task-2/writing-band-6-5/)
**Question Type:** Pos/Neg development (+ extent)
**Overall Band:** 6.5

### Prompt
Today, most people get married and give birth in their thirties rather than when they are younger. Is it a positive or negative development in your opinion? To what extent do you support this development?

### Essay [summarised by source]
Argues this is predominantly negative, citing economic pressures (job availability, housing costs) and biological concerns (maternal-age health risks). Opening states the writer's position; subsequent paragraphs develop economic and biological arguments.

### Examiner Comments
- **Lexical Resource:** Unnatural collocations ("constitute a family", "tendency for our reality"); awkward word choices.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Generally sound; "before decide" (should be "before deciding"); possessive issues.
- **Cohesion & Coherence:** Adequate paragraph organisation; occasional awkward linking ("However, this scenario is…").
- **Task Response:** Inconsistent focus; essay drifts toward explaining causes rather than evaluating positive/negative.

### Specific Issues
- **TR:** Drifts from evaluation into causation — common Band 6.5 pitfall.
- **LR:** Unnatural collocations distinguish 6.5 from 7.
- **GRA:** Gerund/infinitive confusion; possessive errors.

## Common Patterns at Band 6.5

1. **Strong Task Response (often Band 7+ on TR alone)** — both views/parts are addressed.
2. **Weakness clusters in one criterion** — usually GRA or LR, dragging the overall to 6.5.
3. **Length issues** — under 250 words is a common Band 6.5 cap (TR penalty).
4. **Repetition**: 1–2 keywords used 6–9 times instead of synonymised.
5. **Awkward collocations**: "constitute a family", "boost cultural insights", "got rid of geographical barriers".
6. **Article and preposition errors** — "afford-subscription fees", "afford to subscription fees".
7. **Long, well-structured paragraphs** but with grammatical wobbles inside.
8. **Examples used but generic** — "for example, Netflix, Disney" without elaboration.

## What Stops a Band 6.5 Essay from Reaching Band 7
- One or two SVA / article / preposition errors per paragraph.
- 1–2 mis-collocated phrases per paragraph.
- Generic examples (no proper nouns with elaboration, no data, no named places/dates).
- Conclusion is paraphrase of intro rather than synthesis.

## What a Band 6.5 Already Does Well
- Clear thesis and position.
- Both sides covered in discuss-both-views prompts.
- 4-paragraph or 5-paragraph essay structure.
- Reasonable range of complex sentences (relative clauses, conditionals).
- Topic-specific vocabulary used at least once per body paragraph.

# Model Feedback Examples (Band 6 / 6.5)

Real, sourced examples of feedback given to band-6 (and adjacent) IELTS Writing essays. All quoted feedback is verbatim from the cited source where possible; where the source did not surface the candidate's full essay text, the topic and examiner commentary are quoted.

---

## Example 1 — IELTS Advantage band 6 student answer (Task 2)

**Source:** https://www.ieltsadvantage.com/2015/09/09/band-6-student-answer-with-examiners-report/

**Topic:** Discuss both views — were children happier in the past than today, and were adults?

### Examiner's report (verbatim quotes)

- **Task Response — Band 6:**
  > "You address all parts of the task and cover both arguments. However, the question asked you to discuss both views and you spend much more time saying why children were happy and not adults."

- **Coherence and Cohesion — Band 7:**
  > "You logically organise information and ideas; there is clear progression throughout. The structure that you have used really helps in this area and makes everything easy to follow and flows very nicely."
  > "You do have a tendency to overuse them. You don't get more marks for every cohesive device you use."

- **Lexical Resource — Band 6:**
  > "You use an adequate range of vocabulary for the task such as 'financial responsibilities', 'academic environment' and 'scientific competitions'."
  > "It is better to use simple words and make no mistakes than use advanced words, but in error."

- **Grammar — Band 5:**
  > "You attempt complex sentences but these tend to be less accurate than simple sentences. You make frequent grammatical errors and some errors can cause some difficulty for the reader."
  > "It is better to write in a simple way and make no mistakes, than use complex grammar and structures with frequent errors."

**Take-aways:** This is the canonical examiner-report style — one strong opening line, one concession-pivot ("However…"), exact quotation of the candidate's vocabulary, and a closing principle ("It is better to…").

## Example 2 — IELTS Podcast band 6.5 (Task 2)

**Source:** https://www.ieltspodcast.com/writing-task-2/writing-band-6-5/

**Topic:** Today, most people get married and give birth in their thirties rather than when they are younger. Is it a positive or negative development? To what extent do you support this development?

### Examiner commentary (verbatim quotes)

- **Lexical Resource:**
  > "The phrase 'early weddings followed by pregnancy' sounds unnatural."
  > "'This is a common dream between many women' lacks clarity."
  > Word choice problems: "'contributing for that' (should be 'contributing to'), 'convicts of their situation' (should be 'convinced'), 'dreams of constitute a family' (should be 'dreams of having'), 'a tendency for our reality' (unnatural collocation)."

- **Grammatical Range and Accuracy:**
  > Strengths: "'has grown in importance over the past few years'" and "well-constructed relative clauses".
  > Errors: "'before decide' (should be 'before deciding'), 'for the couple' (should be 'by'), 'the couple limits' (should use possessive form)."

- **Coherence and Cohesion:**
  > "The essay demonstrates 'accurate use of "although"' and organises ideas with 'Firstly' and 'Secondly'. However, 'However' is misused when 'Although' would be appropriate."

- **Task Response:**
  > "The writer needs to focus more on whether it is a positive development rather than why it has happened."

**Overall: 6.5 / 7.0**

**Take-aways:** Errors itemised in the (original → suggested) format; strengths and weaknesses interleaved; explicit "needs to focus more on…" directive at the end.

## Example 3 — IELTS Buddy band 6 (Water shortages, Task 2)

**Source:** https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-writing-task-2-band-6-examples.html

### Per-criterion examiner-style comments (verbatim)

- **Task Response (6/10):**
  > "You have discussed the issues quite well but not all are fully explained."
- **Coherence and Cohesion (6/10):**
  > "You introduce 'global warming', a 'cause' near the end. Surely this should be with the other 'causes' at the start."
- **Lexical Resource (6/10):** Vocabulary adequate but awkwardly applied — e.g., "solutions to solve" is redundant.
- **Grammatical Range and Accuracy (6/10):**
  > "This is probably just a 6 but it's borderline 5. You do have quite a few small errors, especially nearer the start."

**Take-away:** Notice the candid tone ("borderline 5") — examiner-grade frankness paired with specific text references.

## Example 4 — IELTS Buddy band 5–6 (Reading & Writing skills)

**Source:** https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-writing-task-2-band-6-examples.html

- **Task Response (5/10):**
  > "Much of your answer does not actually answer the question — how are reading and writing skills affected."
- **Coherence and Cohesion (6/10):**
  > "Each body paragraph should have a central idea but your essay does not appear to do this. It gets confusing as to what the main idea of each is."
- **Recommendation:**
  > "Reduce the amount you write and focus on brainstorming (making sure your ideas answer the question), planning, organising and checking grammar."

**Take-away:** Off-topic content punished hard; closing recommendation is procedural ("brainstorm-plan-organise-check").

## Example 5 — IELTS-Blog band 6 (Rural students at university)

**Source:** https://www.ielts-blog.com/ielts-writing-samples/ielts-essay-samples-of-band-6/

**Topic:** Should it become easier for students from rural areas to study at universities? To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Teacher's feedback (verbatim quotes)

**Strengths:**
- "The writer's position is relevant to task prompt, the main ideas are also relevant."
- "The information is presented coherently, it is evident that the writer progresses from one idea to another."
- "The linking words and phrases are used in a correct way."
- "The range of vocabulary is sufficient here."

**Weaknesses:**
- "Not all the parts of the task were covered."
- "Not all of them are developed well enough."
- "There are some attempts to use more sophisticated words but many of them are inaccurate."
- "There are some errors in grammar and punctuation, they don't make the meaning much harder to understand."

**Overall:** "This essay seems worthy of IELTS Band 6."

**Take-away:** This is a clean strengths-list / weaknesses-list format — bulletable, terse, and ideal as an LLM target structure.

## Example 8 — IELTS Online Tests sample examiner evaluation

**Source:** https://ieltsonlinetests.com/ielts-writing-examiner-evaluation

### Per-criterion comments (verbatim)

- **Task Response:**
  - "Length is appropriate, at 269 words."
  - "Ideas are connected to topic, but could be better supported with more details."
  - "The opinion is clear."
- **Coherence/Cohesion:**
  - "There appear to be 6 paragraphs. There should only be 4 paragraphs."
  - "The introduction only has 2 sentences and the conclusion only has 1 sentence. There should be at least 3 sentences in a paragraph."
  - "There are few linking words / transition words used."
- **Vocabulary:**
  - "Some informal words are used, like 'kids'. All words should be formal and academic."
  - "Incorrect collocation, such as 'do games'. That should be 'play games'."
  - "Easy words are used, such as 'unhappy'. More advanced vocabulary should be used, such as 'ominous'."
  - "Vocabulary repetition – such as 'rule'. There should be more paraphrasing."
- **Grammar:**
  - "'Research' is uncountable, so 'researches' is wrong."
  - "More than one sentence is begun with a conjunction, 'but'. Conjunctions should only be used in the middle, to link two independent clauses."

**Take-away:** Bullet-point granular feedback — each bullet has the issue, an example from the essay, and the correction.

## Example 9 — IELTS Luminary band 5 (smartphone use, Task 2)

**Source:** https://www.ieltsluminary.com/ielts-essay-correction-by-an-examiner

### Sample feedback excerpts (verbatim)

- **Task Achievement (Band 5.0):** "The essay addresses the task and provides some reasons for children's smartphone use and an opinion on its impact."
- **Coherence and Cohesion (Band 5.0):** "The essay uses basic linking devices like 'on the other hand' and 'to sum up.'"
- **Lexical Resource (Band 4.5):** "Some topic-specific vocabulary is used, such as 'online classes,' 'WhatsApp,' and 'Zoom.'"
- **Grammatical Range and Accuracy (Band 4.0):** "Some correct sentence structures are present, especially in simpler sentences."

**Take-away:** Lower-band feedback follows the same structure as band 6 — only the descriptors and examples shift.

## Example 10 — IELTS-470 / RSSing band 6.5 (essay rewriting commentary)

**Source:** https://ielts470.rssing.com/chan-58146017/article90.html

### Examiner commentary (verbatim quotes)

- **Probable score:** 6.5 / 7.0.
- **Task Achievement & Coherence:** "Good to organize your ideas into appropriate paragraphs." / "Good organisation – introducing your conclusion clearly and appropriately."
- **Lexical Resource:** Strong collocations: "pursue a course of study" and "launch a career". Flags: "The collocation is 'dream of sthg'." Informal: "loads".
- **Grammatical Range and Accuracy:** "The weakest area." Errors include subject-verb agreement, missing pronouns, article misuse — e.g., "a small numbers of people believes".
- **Coherence and Cohesion (linking devices):** Approves "In contrast"; critiques "nevertheless" used out of context.
- **Closing advice:** "Self-proofreading before submission could easily boost your score by at least 10%."

## Patterns across all 11 examples

| Pattern | Frequency | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Quote candidate's exact phrase before commenting | ~100% | "You X… such as 'Y'." |
| Concession-pivot ("However…") | ~90% | Examples 1, 2, 5, 7 |
| Explicit per-criterion band score | ~90% | Examples 1, 2, 6, 7, 9 |
| "It is better to…" principle | recurring | Example 1 |
| Numeric thresholds (paragraph count, word count, repetitions) | only in automated graders | Examples 6, 7, 8 |
| Closing forward-looking line | ~80% | Examples 4, 10, 11 |
| Strengths-then-weaknesses bullets | ~70% | Example 5 |

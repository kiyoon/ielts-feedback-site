# Trend Language Bank — Verbs / Nouns / Adverbs with Intensity Calibration

> **Compiled from:** Korean (scorefactory, 해커스), Chinese (新通教育, 沪江, 可可英语, 环球教育, 老烤鸭), Vietnamese (ZIM, Vietop), Japanese (solo-ielts).
>
> The single most-tested area of Task 1: how to vary `increase / decrease / stay the same`. Drop-in tables below.

---

## 1. The intensity calibration master table

🇨🇳 cram-school canon (新通教育, 沪江): **never use a verb without calibrating intensity**. The verb must agree with the slope on the chart.

| Slope direction | Slight | Moderate | Sharp | Extreme |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Up** | edge up · inch up · creep up · rise slightly | rise · grow · climb · go up | surge · jump · shoot up · soar | rocket · skyrocket · spike · leap |
| **Down** | dip · ease · slip · edge down | fall · decline · drop · decrease | plunge · plummet · sink · tumble | crash · collapse · nosedive |
| **Flat** | barely change · remain largely unchanged | hold steady · level off · stabilise | plateau · remain constant · stay flat | — |
| **Volatile** | show minor fluctuation | fluctuate · oscillate · vacillate | swing wildly · vary considerably | seesaw · whipsaw |

⚠ instructor-flagged error (CN gedu.org): do not pair `slightly` with `rocket`. Hedging adverbs and intensity verbs **must agree**. "The figure rocketed slightly" is wrong; it should be "the figure edged up" OR "the figure rocketed dramatically".

## 4. Verb-only bank — flat / steady

| Verb / phrase | Note |
|---|---|
| level off | flatten after movement |
| level out | same as above |
| flatten out | gentler tone |
| plateau | stay flat at a high or steady value |
| stabilise | reach equilibrium |
| remain stable | most common formal version |
| remain constant | strongest "no change" |
| remain steady | "no change", neutral |
| remain unchanged | strongest "literally identical" |
| stay flat | informal |
| hold steady | active flavour |
| level off at X% | always pair with the value |
| reach a plateau | nominal version |

```
- The figure for energy use levelled off at around 50% from 2005 onwards.
- After 2010, electricity consumption plateaued at approximately 30 TWh.
- The proportion of car owners remained largely unchanged throughout the period.
```

## 5. Verb-only bank — fluctuation / volatility

| Verb / phrase | Note |
|---|---|
| fluctuate | most common; pair with "wildly" / "moderately" / "slightly" |
| oscillate | sounds more academic, but rare in Task 1 |
| vacillate | very formal; use sparingly |
| swing | informal-active |
| vary | broad-purpose |
| seesaw | imagery — up and down repeatedly |
| undulate | wave-like movement (less common) |
| zigzag | visual; use only for genuinely jagged lines |
| show fluctuation | nominalised version |
| be volatile | for prices/stocks contexts |

```
- The price of crude oil fluctuated wildly between 2008 and 2012.
- Interest rates oscillated within a narrow band during the period.
- The figure showed considerable fluctuation before stabilising in 2015.
```

## 6. Adverb bank — by intensity

🇨🇳 (gedu.org / 新通) "verb + adverb" structure:

| Intensity | Adverbs |
|---|---|
| very slight | slightly · marginally · gently · gradually (gentle sense) · mildly · negligibly |
| moderate | steadily · gradually (true sense) · moderately · progressively |
| considerable | considerably · substantially · markedly · significantly · noticeably · appreciably |
| sharp | sharply · steeply · rapidly · drastically |
| dramatic | dramatically · drastically · radically · precipitously |
| sudden | suddenly · abruptly · without warning |

**Pairing rules (cross-source consensus):**
- `rose / fell + slightly / marginally / gradually / steadily / sharply / dramatically`
- `surged / plummeted + dramatically / drastically` (do **not** pair with `slightly`)
- `levelled off / plateaued / stabilised + with no further change / at around X%`

## 7. Noun-form bank (the "There was a + adj + noun + in" pattern)

🇨🇳 (老烤鸭, 沪江) Pattern 1:

```
There was + a/an + [adjective] + [trend noun] + in + [data]
```

| Trend | Noun forms |
|---|---|
| Up | a rise / an increase / a growth / a surge / a jump / an upturn / an upswing / an upward trend / an upward tendency |
| Down | a fall / a decline / a decrease / a drop / a slump / a downturn / a downswing / a downward trend / a contraction / a reduction |
| Flat | stability / a plateau / no change / a stable trend / equilibrium |
| Volatile | a fluctuation / volatility / oscillation / variability |

```
- There was a substantial rise in the value of stocks on March 15th.
- There was a sharp drop in industrial production after 2008.
- There was a marked fluctuation in the price of raw materials between 2010 and 2012.
- There was a noticeable upward trend in renewable energy consumption.
```

## 8. The "five sentence patterns" for trend description (CN canon)

🇨🇳 source: 新通教育 / 沪江 / 老烤鸭 (consensus across three).

### Pattern 1 — Existential
```
There was + a/an + [adjective] + [trend noun] + in + [data]
```
> "There was a steady increase in the number of car owners between 2000 and 2015."

### Pattern 2 — Subject + change verb
```
[Subject] + [change verb] + [adverb / by + figure / from X to Y]
```
> "The number of households grew gradually from 5 million to 8 million."

### Pattern 3 — Time period as subject (witness/see/experience)
```
[Time period] + saw / witnessed / experienced + a + [adjective] + [trend noun] + in + [data]
```
> "The years from 1986 to 1990 witnessed a steady rise in the number of subscribers."
> "The decade from 2000 to 2010 saw a substantial decline in coal consumption."

### Pattern 4 — Location as subject
```
[Location/Country] + experienced / underwent + a + [adjective] + [trend noun] + in + [data] + [time]
```
> "Australia experienced a slight growth in immigrant numbers from 1966 to 1976."
> "Japan underwent a sharp contraction in birth rates between 1990 and 2010."

### Pattern 5 — Passive
```
A + [adjective] + [trend noun] + was witnessed / observed / recorded + in + [data]
```
> "A pronounced upward trend was observed in renewable energy consumption."
> "A steady decline was recorded in print media circulation."

## 9. Specific reference points — peak / trough / cross-over

| Concept | Drop-in phrasing |
|---|---|
| reached the highest | peaked at X / hit a peak of X / reached its zenith / hit the highest point at X / culminated at X |
| reached the lowest | bottomed out at X / hit a trough of X / reached its lowest point / fell to a low of X / hit rock bottom |
| crossed over (lines met) | the figures intersected / the two lines converged / the lines crossed over / overtook one another |
| overtook | surpassed / outstripped / overtook / eclipsed |
| was overtaken by | was surpassed by / was eclipsed by / was outpaced by |
| stayed at the same level as | maintained parity with / remained on a par with / kept pace with |

```
- Coal consumption peaked at 2,500 tonnes in 2005 before declining sharply.
- The unemployment rate hit a trough of 3.2% in 2018.
- In 2010, the figures for solar and wind energy converged at roughly 15%.
- After 2015, China's emissions overtook those of the United States.
```

## 10. Quantified change — "by X" vs "to X" vs "from X to Y"

🇨🇳 (xhd 新航道) common-error: `rose to 10%` ≠ `rose by 10%`. Always pre-check the candidate's preposition.

| Concept | Preposition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| End point | to | "rose to 80%" |
| Magnitude of change | by | "rose by 30 percentage points" |
| Range / span | from … to … | "rose from 50% to 80%" |
| Rate (per year) | at a rate of | "grew at a rate of 5% per annum" |
| Multiple | by a factor of / threefold / fourfold | "grew threefold" |

⚠ percentage vs percentage point: a change from 20% to 25% = "rose **by 5 percentage points**", **not** "by 5%" (which would mean 21%).

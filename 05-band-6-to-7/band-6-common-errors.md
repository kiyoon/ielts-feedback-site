# Band 6 — Exhaustive Catalog of Common Errors

> If a student is sitting on 6.0, the same handful of error types are almost certainly recurring across every essay. This file lists them so they can be hunted down systematically. Each entry follows the pattern: **what the error looks like → why it caps the score → the band-7 fix.**

---

## A. Grammar errors

### A1. Article errors (a / an / the / zero article)

The single biggest leak of marks for Band 6 writers, especially those whose first language has no article system (Korean, Japanese, Chinese, Russian).

| Wrong | Right | Note |
|---|---|---|
| *She wants to become teacher.* | *She wants to become **a** teacher.* | Singular countable, first mention |
| *The education is important for the society.* | *Education is important for society.* | Abstract / general nouns take zero article |
| *Government should help poor people.* | ***The** government should help poor people.* | Specific institution → *the* |
| *Internet has changed our lives.* | ***The** internet has changed our lives.* | Unique entity → *the* |
| *Most of students fail this part.* | *Most students fail this part.* / *Most of **the** students…* | *most of* requires *the* |
| *In the United Kingdom is rainy.* | *In **the** United Kingdom **it** is rainy.* | Country names with "Kingdom/States/Republic" take *the*; subject required |

### A2. Subject–verb agreement

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *The students enjoys the lecture.* | *The students enjoy the lecture.* |
| *One of my co-workers are here.* | *One of my co-workers is here.* |
| *Their team captain are retiring.* | *Their team captain is retiring.* |
| *Everyone have their own opinion.* | *Everyone has his or her own opinion.* |
| *The number of cars are increasing.* | *The number of cars is increasing.* |
| *News are spreading quickly.* | *News is spreading quickly.* (uncountable) |

### A3. Tense errors

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *People are believing that…* | *People believe that…* | Stative verbs don't take continuous |
| *Since 2010, many people moved to cities.* | *Since 2010, many people **have moved** to cities.* | *since* + present perfect |
| *I am living here for five years.* | *I have been living here for five years.* | Duration → present perfect / perfect continuous |
| *Yesterday I have visited my friend.* | *Yesterday I visited my friend.* | Finished time → simple past |
| *If I will have time, I will help.* | *If I have time, I will help.* | First conditional: no *will* in *if*-clause |

### A4. Conditional sentence errors

| Wrong | Right | Type |
|---|---|---|
| *If I could live anywhere, I will live in Paris.* | *If I could live anywhere, I **would** live in Paris.* | 2nd conditional |
| *If governments would invest more, pollution can be reduced.* | *If governments **invested** more, pollution **could** be reduced.* | 2nd conditional |
| *If she had studied, she will pass.* | *If she had studied, she **would have passed**.* | 3rd conditional |
| *If water reaches 100°C, it will boil.* | *If water reaches 100°C, it boils.* | 0 conditional (general truth) |

### A5. Preposition errors

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *Discuss about the issue* | *Discuss the issue* |
| *Different than* | *Different from* |
| *In Monday* | *On Monday* |
| *In the morning of Tuesday* | *On Tuesday morning* |
| *Depend of* | *Depend on* |
| *Consist of / consisted by* | *Consist of / be made up of* |
| *Married with* | *Married to* |
| *Listen the music* | *Listen **to** the music* |
| *Suffer with depression* | *Suffer **from** depression* |
| *Reason of the problem* | *Reason **for** the problem* |

### A6. Countable vs uncountable

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *She gave me many informations.* | *She gave me a lot of information.* |
| *I have many homeworks.* | *I have a lot of homework.* |
| *Researches show that…* | *Research shows that…* |
| *Furnitures, advices, equipments, knowledges* | *Furniture, advice, equipment, knowledge* |
| *Less people, less cars* | *Fewer people, fewer cars* (countable → fewer) |

### A7. Word order

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *I have played for six years basketball.* | *I have played basketball for six years.* |
| *Why you think this is important?* | *Why do you think this is important?* |
| *I don't know what is the answer.* | *I don't know what the answer is.* (embedded question) |
| *Always I drink coffee.* | *I always drink coffee.* |

### A8. Run-on sentences and comma splices

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *People think the government should provide free healthcare it would help many poor people.* | *People think the government should provide free healthcare **because** it would help many poor people.* |
| *I go shopping every Saturday, I buy clothes.* | *I go shopping every Saturday**.** I buy clothes.* |
| *Pollution is rising, governments must act.* | *Pollution is rising**;** governments must act.* |

### A9. Sentence fragments

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *Was a beautiful day.* | *It was a beautiful day.* |
| *Because of the high cost.* | *This happens because of the high cost.* |
| *Although there are many advantages.* | *Although there are many advantages, there are also drawbacks.* |

### A10. Modal verb errors

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *You must to finish the task.* | *You must finish the task.* |
| *People should to recycle.* | *People should recycle.* |
| *He can to swim.* | *He can swim.* |
| *Government must to provide…* | *Government must provide…* |

### A11. Passive voice errors

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *The internet is using by people every day.* | *The internet is used by people every day.* |
| *English is speak in many countries.* | *English is spoken in many countries.* |
| *The problem can solved easily.* | *The problem can be solved easily.* |
| *It is believe that…* | *It is believed that…* |

### A12. Dangling and misplaced participles

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *Walking to school, the rain started.* | *As I was walking to school, the rain started.* |
| *Being a busy person, my work suffered.* | *Being a busy person, I let my work suffer.* |

## B. Vocabulary errors

### B1. Overuse of basic words

Reaching only for *important*, *good*, *bad*, *big*, *very*, *thing*, *people*, *many* throughout an essay caps the lexical resource score at 6.

### B2. Wrong collocations

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *make damage* | *cause damage* |
| *make a homework* | *do homework* |
| *do a mistake* | *make a mistake* |
| *do crime* | *commit a crime* |
| *say a lie* | *tell a lie* |
| *take a decision* | *make a decision* (in most varieties) |
| *strong rain* | *heavy rain* |
| *high knowledge* | *deep knowledge / extensive knowledge* |
| *fast food is delicious but unhealth* | *fast food is delicious but unhealthy* |

### B3. Thesaurus syndrome

Replacing simple, accurate words with complex words the writer doesn't fully understand.

| Mis-attempt | What was intended | Better |
|---|---|---|
| *This is a **perilous** matter.* | serious problem | *This is a serious problem.* |
| *Government should **defend** pollution.* | combat | *Government should combat pollution.* |
| *People are **utilising** their time **leisurely**.* | spending free time | *People are spending more of their leisure time…* |
| *Children **acquire** computer at young age.* | get / start using | *Children start using computers at a young age.* |

### B4. Register / formality clashes

Mixing slangy or chatty language into an academic essay.

| Wrong (too informal) | Right (academic) |
|---|---|
| *kids* | children / young people / minors |
| *a lot of stuff* | a range of issues / various factors |
| *guys* | individuals / men |
| *gonna / wanna / kinda* | going to / want to / kind of (avoid altogether) |
| *Don't, can't, won't* | do not, cannot, will not (no contractions) |
| *get* (overused) | obtain / receive / acquire / become |
| *thing(s)* | factor / aspect / issue / element |

### B5. Repetition

Using the same word 6+ times in 250 words signals limited range. Build a synonym chain instead:
*pollution → contamination → emissions → environmental damage → such harm.*

### B6. Spelling errors

Common ones flagged repeatedly: *enviroment* (environment), *goverment* (government), *recieve* (receive), *seperate* (separate), *thier* (their), *teh / adn / tehm* (typos), *occured* (occurred), *begining* (beginning), *neccessary* (necessary), *responsable* (responsible), *succesful* (successful).

### B7. Word-form errors

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| *the development of country is depend on education* | *the development of a country **depends** on education* |
| *poverty cause to crime* | *poverty causes crime* / *poverty leads to crime* |
| *unhealth food* | *unhealthy food* |
| *the increase of pollution affect to health* | *the increase in pollution affects health* |

## C. Cohesion errors

### C1. Mechanical linker overuse

Rigid use of *Firstly... Secondly... Finally... In conclusion* at the start of every paragraph or sentence is the single clearest Band 6 marker.

| Band 6 (mechanical) | Band 7 (flexible) |
|---|---|
| *Firstly, technology is useful.* | *One clear advantage of technology is its usefulness.* |
| *Secondly, technology saves time.* | *A further benefit lies in the time it saves.* |
| *Finally, technology is fun.* | *Equally important is the entertainment value it offers.* |
| *In conclusion, technology has pros and cons.* | *On balance, while technology brings undeniable benefits, its drawbacks deserve serious attention.* |

### C2. Linker per sentence ("stamping")

Starting almost every sentence with a discourse marker (*Moreover, Furthermore, In addition, Besides, Additionally*) within the same paragraph.

### C3. Wrong linker for the function

| Wrong | Why | Right |
|---|---|---|
| *Pollution is rising. **Moreover**, it harms health.* | *Moreover* adds a separate point; harm is consequence | *Pollution is rising; **as a result**, it harms health.* |
| *I agree. **However**, education is important.* | *However* signals contrast that isn't there | Just delete *However* |
| *He is rich. **Therefore**, he is unhappy.* | *Therefore* signals logical consequence; this is contrast | *He is rich; **nevertheless**, he is unhappy.* |

### C4. Weak referencing

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| *Computers are useful. **Computers** help students. **Computers** are everywhere.* | *Computers are useful; **they** help students and **such devices** are now ubiquitous.* |
| *This is a problem.* (which? unclear) | *This widespread littering is a problem.* |
| *They use them every day.* (who? what?) | *Adolescents use these platforms every day.* |

### C5. No topic sentences

Body paragraphs that start with an example or a statistic instead of a clear claim. The reader has to guess the paragraph's purpose.

### C6. Random paragraphing

One-sentence paragraphs, or 12-sentence walls of text. Band 7 expects clear paragraphing of ~5–7 sentences with one central idea per paragraph.

## D. Task response errors

### D1. Partial task answer

If the prompt asks two questions (e.g. *causes* AND *solutions*) and the essay covers only causes, the maximum TR score drops to 5.

### D2. Unclear or shifting position

| Symptom | Effect |
|---|---|
| *"I agree"* in intro, *"on the other hand"* heavy body, *"both views are valid"* in conclusion | TR cannot exceed 6 |
| Hedging so much that no view is identifiable: *"It depends on many factors."* | TR cannot exceed 6 |

### D3. Underdeveloped ideas

A paragraph of: claim + restated claim + vague closing line.
Band 7 requires: claim + reasoning + concrete example + implication.

### D4. Off-topic drift

Writing about a related but different topic. Common when memorised paragraphs are forced into the response.
**Example:** Prompt asks about *online learning for young children*. Writer discusses *online learning at university level*. → Off-topic → TR ≤ 5.

### D5. Memorised templates

Recognisable phrases that examiners are trained to detect and penalise:
- *"It is a controversial topic in modern society."*
- *"This essay will discuss both views in detail."*
- *"It is a two-edged sword."*
- *"In a nutshell..."*
- *"There are pros and cons."*
- *"Time is no doubt of the essence."*

### D6. Fake statistics and surveys

Inventing percentages and "studies show that…" hurts credibility. Examiners cannot verify and recognise the pattern.

### D7. No examples or vague examples

| Vague (Band 6) | Concrete (Band 7) |
|---|---|
| *In many countries, this is a problem.* | *In Seoul, for instance, the cost of housing has tripled in two decades.* |
| *Some people think so.* | *Older generations, in particular, often hold this view.* |
| *Studies show…* | *A recent OECD report suggests…* (only if true) — otherwise *historically, evidence suggests…* |

### D8. Conclusion that only repeats

Band 6 conclusion = "In conclusion, [paraphrased intro]." Band 7 conclusion synthesises: it shows how the arguments fit together, perhaps suggests an implication, and re-states the position once.

### D9. Unbalanced coverage

In a "discuss both views" essay: 200 words on view A, 50 words on view B → not "addressing all parts equally."

### D10. Personal pronouns misuse

Task 1 (Academic) should not use *I*, *we*, *you*. Task 2 *can* use *I think / in my opinion* in the position statement, but overusing personal pronouns throughout drops formality.

## E. Time-management symptoms that produce Band 6

- **Under length** (<250 words for Task 2) → automatic TR penalty.
- **Over-running** so the conclusion is rushed or missing.
- **No planning** → repetition, contradictions, paragraph imbalance.
- **No proofreading** → preventable spelling/article errors that would have been caught.

## Quick self-audit checklist (mark each "yes" or "no")

| # | Question | Yes/No |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Did I answer **every** part of the prompt? | |
| 2 | Is my position the **same** in introduction, body, and conclusion? | |
| 3 | Does each body paragraph have a topic sentence? | |
| 4 | Did I include at least one concrete example per body paragraph? | |
| 5 | Did I avoid starting every paragraph/sentence with *Firstly/Secondly/Moreover*? | |
| 6 | Did I check articles (a/an/the/zero) on every noun? | |
| 7 | Did I check subject–verb agreement? | |
| 8 | Did I use at least one relative clause and one conditional or participle clause correctly? | |
| 9 | Did I avoid *very/big/good/bad/important* (or replace each with a precise word)? | |
| 10 | Did I avoid memorised phrases like *two-edged sword* and *in a nutshell*? | |

Any "no" → that's a band-7 leak.

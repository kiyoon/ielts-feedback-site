**Coherence and Cohesion:** Paragraphing is logical: introduction, overview, USA/Germany, then India. Progression is easy to follow, but cohesion relies on basic signals: “After this,” “However,” “after that,” and “In contrast.” The phrase “In contrast, India is different” is clear but too blunt; a Band 7 version would use reference and comparison more naturally, such as “By contrast, India followed a different pattern.” This is Band 6 because cohesion works, but linking and referencing are somewhat mechanical (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `09-instructor-tips/08-cohesion-upgrades-instructor.md`).

**Lexical Resource:** There is enough task vocabulary: “consumed,” “reached a peak,” “decline,” “similar pattern,” and “increased rapidly.” However, the essay repeats “consumption,” “figure,” and “level,” and several collocations are unnatural: “had a growth,” “the figure of USA,” “all over the period,” and “had reach to.” Band 7 requires more precise and flexible chart language, for example “remained by far the largest consumer,” “peaked at,” and “rose gradually.” This is a solid Band 6 LR profile: adequate range, but restricted precision and several word-choice errors (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `05-band-6-to-7/vocabulary-upgrades.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** The essay uses some complex structures, such as “which is much higher than Germany…” and a comparative clause in the final sentence. Errors are frequent but meaning remains clear: “USA’s consumption increase,” “it slightly decrease,” “grew slow,” and “had reach to.” Because the graph covers 1990–2020, most reporting should use past-tense forms consistently; present-tense phrases like “India is different” weaken control. This remains Band 6 because communication is not seriously impeded, but error-free sentences are not frequent enough for Band 7 (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `08-vocabulary-grammar/01-grammar-error-correction-bank.md`; `09-instructor-tips/10-common-korean-chinese-speaker-errors.md`).

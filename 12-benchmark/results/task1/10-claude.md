## Per-criterion justification

**Task Achievement — 6.0.** The essay covers all three countries and reports key data points (1990 start values, USA peak, India's late surge), satisfying the Band 6 requirement to "present and adequately highlight key features" (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6). However, the overview is thin: "had a growth during this time period" is vague, and the second overview sentence mixes overview with detail. The candidate also omits a clear statement of the *gap* between countries, which Band 7 overviews typically include (`05-band-6-to-7/task-response-upgrades.md`). The data point "four times higher than 1990" is a comparison error (1,200 vs 300 = 4×, fine in magnitude, but phrased as "four times higher than" rather than "four times as high as"). Coverage is adequate but not "clearly highlighted" (Band 7 threshold).

**Coherence and Cohesion — 6.0.** Paragraphing is logical (intro → overview → USA/Germany detail → India detail), matching the Band 6 description of "arranges information coherently" (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`). Linkers are mechanical and sometimes faulty: "However, then it started to decline" misuses *however* (no real contrast within the same trend description), and "In contrast, India is different" is redundant — *in contrast* already signals difference (`05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md`, `08-vocabulary-grammar/03-cohesion-error-correction-bank.md`). Reference is occasionally unclear ("the figure of USA" — should be "the figure for the USA").

**Lexical Resource — 6.0.** Range is adequate but repetitive: *consumption / consumed* appears five times, *figure* twice, *grew/growth* three times, with no synonyms like *usage*, *demand*, *levels of*, or trend verbs *climbed / surged / dipped* from `09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`. Collocations are mostly correct ("reached a peak", "grew … steadily") but "grew slow" is a word-form error (adverb needed). No precise comparative phrasing such as *roughly double*, *nearly four-fold*, *the steepest rise* (`09-instructor-tips/05-superlative-and-comparative-upgrades.md`). This profile matches Band 6: "adequate range … some inappropriate word choice" (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy — 5.5.** Errors are frequent and intrude on meaning: tense slips ("USA's consumption increase" → *increased*; "had reach to" → *had reached*; "slightly decrease" → *decreased*), article errors ("the figure of USA" → *for the USA*), and a comparison-form error ("which is four times higher than 1990" → *as high as the 1990 figure*). Sentence variety is limited; almost every clause is SVO with simple coordinators. Per `05-band-6-to-7/grammar-upgrades.md` and `09-instructor-tips/07-band7-grammar-showcases.md`, Band 7 requires "a mix of simple and complex sentences" with "good control" — the present density of basic verb-form errors pushes this criterion below 6.

However, the overview overgeneralises: “electricity consumption in all three countries had a growth” ignores the later decline in the USA and Germany. A Band 7 overview needs the main trends and contrasts to be clearly selected, while Band 6 allows some inaccurate or less effective highlighting (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6; `05-band-6-to-7/task-response-upgrades.md`).

Referencing is repetitive and sometimes awkward, especially “the figure of USA” and “the figure of India.” This matches Band 6, where cohesion works but may be mechanical or faulty (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6; `05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md`).

The range is limited and repetitive, with “consumption” and “figure” doing too much work. Several collocations are unnatural: “had a growth,” “the figure of USA,” “all over the period,” and “became around.” This fits Band 6 LR: adequate range, but noticeable word-choice and collocation errors (`01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6; `08-vocabulary-grammar/02-vocabulary-error-correction-bank.md`).

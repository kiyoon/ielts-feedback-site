## Per-criterion justification

**Task Achievement — 6.0.** The candidate covers all three countries and identifies the broad trend, but the overview is weak: "electricity consumption in all three countries had a growth" is vague and grammatically off, and the second overview sentence repeats the same point ("USA stayed the highest, while India had the lowest") without naming a second key feature such as the divergence in trajectories (USA peaks then falls; India accelerates). Per `01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6 ("presents an overview with information appropriately selected") and `05-band-6-to-7/task-response-upgrades.md`, a band-7 overview needs **two** distinct big-picture features. Data is mostly accurate but the claim "four times higher than 1990" is a comparison error — 1,200 vs. 300 is **four times as high / a fourfold increase**, not "four times higher" (which strictly means 5×); see `08-vocabulary-grammar/02-vocabulary-error-correction-bank.md`.

**Coherence and Cohesion — 6.0.** Paragraphing is logical (intro → overview → USA+Germany → India), but cohesion is mechanical. "After this," "However, then it started to decline," and "In contrast, India is different" are clunky and partially redundant ("In contrast" + "is different" doubles the contrast). Reference is sometimes unclear: "it" in "after that it slightly decrease" could refer to Germany's figure or its growth. Per `05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md` and `09-instructor-tips/08-cohesion-upgrades-instructor.md`, band 7 needs varied connectors (whereas, by contrast, having + p.p., subsequently) and pronoun clarity.

**Lexical Resource — 6.0.** Range is adequate but repetitive: "consumption" / "figure" / "kWh" recur with little paraphrase, and trend verbs are limited to *grew, increase, decline, decrease, reached a peak*. The corpus offers far richer trend language (`09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`: *climbed, surged, levelled off, dipped, plateaued, edged up, rose sharply*) and paraphrase chains for "consumption" → *usage, expenditure, demand* (`09-instructor-tips/01-paraphrase-banks-task1.md`). "Had a growth" is an awkward noun-of-verb construction flagged in `08-vocabulary-grammar/02-vocabulary-error-correction-bank.md`.

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy — 5.5.** Errors are frequent enough to push GRA below 6: "consumption increase steadily" (missing -d), "it slightly decrease" (subject-verb agreement / tense), "had reach to 1,200" (past perfect form + redundant *to*), "grew slow" (adverb missing → *slowly* / *slowly grew*), "reached a peak about 13,500" (missing *of*), and the article slip "the figure of USA" (→ *the US figure* / *the figure for the US*). Per `01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6 vs. 5 ("makes some errors" vs. "frequent errors which... cause some difficulty"), the cluster of past-tense and article errors warrants 5.5, not 6.0.

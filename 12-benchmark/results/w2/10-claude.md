## Structural feedback
The essay's headline structural failure is **prompt coverage, not paragraph shape**. Two of four required questions are unanswered. Part 1 ("scientific basis of resource depletion") needs a mechanism paragraph — e.g. *finite stock minus extraction rate exceeding regeneration rate*; *peak-oil curve*; *EROI decline*; *biogeochemical cycling limits*. Part 3 ("why international cooperation is necessary") needs a body paragraph in its own right — fish stocks, atmospheric carbon, lithium and rare-earth supply chains, and trans-boundary aquifers do not respect borders, so unilateral action is undermined by free-riders (Tragedy-of-the-Commons logic). The "one example" requirement is also unmet: there is **zero** named example anywhere (no Montreal Protocol, no Paris Agreement, no specific technology, no country, no figure). The 4-paragraph shape is fine in principle, but it now needs to become a **5-paragraph structure**: intro → scientific basis → science's solutions + role of cooperation → limitation → conclusion, *or* a 4-paragraph version where Body 1 fuses basis+solution and Body 2 fuses cooperation+limitation. **The single highest-impact fix here is adding two missing prompt-part paragraphs, not the lexical upgrades in the table below — without them, TR remains capped at Band 5 regardless of how polished the language becomes.** The Body-2 self-cancellation flagged in iter 04 still stands ("the scientific method could handle several errors … it is still up to humans" concedes the limitation it is meant to assert).

## Structural feedback
**The single highest-impact fix is structural, not sentence-level.** The essay does not answer the prompt. The prompt asks the candidate to **discuss the influence of mathematics on the development of computer science**, and ideally to engage with the claim that mathematics is "the most important" driver. Instead, the essay drifts into a history of human communication (Mesopotamia, Egypt, Morse code) before arriving at computers in the final paragraph — and even then it describes how computers work (binary switches) without linking that back to *mathematics specifically*. There is no thesis statement that takes a position, no topic sentences that signal a discussion structure, and the essay is truncated with no conclusion. Body 1 is a chronological tour of communication tools that is only loosely connected to mathematics; Body 2 is a fragment about binary that never names the mathematical concepts (Boolean algebra, binary number system, Turing's computability theory, Shannon's information theory) that would make the argument. To reach band 7, restructure as: (intro) paraphrase + clear thesis "mathematics has been the foundational driver" + outline; (body 1) one mathematical foundation, e.g. Boolean algebra → logic gates, with a named example; (body 2) a second mathematical foundation, e.g. Turing's theory of computation, with a named example; (conclusion) synthesis. See `05-band-6-to-7/paragraph-structure-band7.md` for the PEEL template and `04-task2-essays/samples-band-7_0.md` for paragraph shapes.

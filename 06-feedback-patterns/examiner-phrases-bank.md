# Examiner Phrases Bank

A catalogue of stock phrases that examiners, teachers, and graders re-use across IELTS Writing feedback. Phrases are grouped by criterion and band context. All quoted strings are sourced from the URLs in the rightmost column or in the "Sources" footer.

## 5. Cross-criterion / generic stock phrases

### 5a. Opening/framing lines

- "Overall, this is a competent answer that addresses the prompt."
- "Your essay demonstrates a clear position with well-developed main ideas." (cathoven.com)
- "Let's look at this essay against the four IELTS criteria."
- "There are some real strengths here, and some specific things to work on."

### 5b. Concession-pivot ("Yes, but…")

- "You X… **However,** Y."
- "While the use of cohesive devices may be good, students have a tendency to overuse them." (howtodoielts.com)
- "Your X is good but could benefit from Y." (cathoven.com)
- "These are good attempts; the next step is to make them consistent."

### 5c. Closing / forward-looking lines

- "To move toward Band 7.5+, focus on…" (cathoven.com)
- "In your next essay, prioritise (1) …, (2) …, (3) …."
- "If you applied these changes, this essay would likely move into Band 7."
- "Most students at Band 6–6.5 aiming for Band 7 do reach it with focused practice on idea development."

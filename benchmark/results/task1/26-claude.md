Reference is weak: "it" in "after that it slightly decrease" is fine, but "Its consumption grew slow" creates ambiguity. No clear topic sentences in body paragraphs.

**Lexical Resource (6.0).** Repetition is high: "consumption" (×5), "figure" (×3), "kWh per person" (×4). Trend verbs are basic — "grew", "increase", "decline", "decrease", "slightly decrease" — with no nominalised alternatives ("a steady rise", "a gradual decline") from `contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`. "Slow" used as adverb ("grew slow") is a band-5 feature.

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy (5.5).** Multiple tense errors: "USA's consumption increase steadily" (missing -d), "after that it slightly decrease" (missing -d), "had reach to" (wrong participle + redundant "to"). Article problems: "the figure of USA" should be "the figure for the USA"; "figure of India" same. "had a growth" is unidiomatic (use "saw growth" / "rose"). Comparative structure "four times higher than 1990" is incomplete ("than the 1990 figure"). Per `contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/01-grammar-error-correction-bank.md`, these are classic band-5/6 slip patterns.

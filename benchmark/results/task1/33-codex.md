However, the overview is only partly successful because it misses the clearest grouped pattern: the USA and Germany peaked then fell, while India accelerated after 2000. This fits Band 6: “a relevant overview is attempted” and selected features are “adequately highlighted,” rather than the Band 7 requirement for a clear overview and categorisation (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6–7).

**Coherence and Cohesion:** Organisation is clear: introduction, overview, USA/Germany details, then India. The grouping of “Germany had a similar pattern” and “In contrast, India…” is logical, so progression is easy to follow. Cohesion remains mechanical, relying on “However,” “After this,” “then,” “after that,” and “In contrast,” with limited reference/substitution. Band 7 requires flexible cohesion including reference and substitution, while Band 6 allows cohesion that is useful but “faulty or mechanical” (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6; `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md`).

**Lexical Resource:** Vocabulary is adequate for the task: “illustrates,” “consumption,” “peak,” “decline,” and “rapidly” are relevant. The range is restricted and sometimes imprecise: “had a growth,” “the figure of USA,” “all over the period,” and “India is different” are understandable but not natural academic Task 1 phrasing. Repetition of “consumption,” “figure,” and country names also limits flexibility. This matches Band 6 LR, where meaning is clear despite restricted range and lack of precision (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md` §Band 6; `contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`).

## Per-criterion justification
**Task Response / Achievement:** The response covers all three countries and reports relevant figures, such as “approximately 11,000 kWh,” “7,500 kWh in 2010,” and “1,200 kWh per person.” However, the overview is only partially successful: “all three countries had a growth” is too broad because the USA and Germany rose and then declined. A Band 7 overview needs clear main trends and differences, while Band 6 allows an attempted overview with some missing or inaccurate detail (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/task-response-upgrades.md`). The essay also misses the strongest comparative feature: India had the fastest relative rise despite remaining far below the others.

**Coherence and Cohesion:** The essay has a logical paragraph structure: introduction, overview, main comparison of the USA/Germany, then India. Progression is clear, but cohesion is mechanical in places: “After this,” “However, then,” “after that,” and “In contrast” are used as simple signposts. Referencing is repetitive, especially “the figure of USA” and “the figure of India,” rather than using substitution such as “the US figure,” “this trend,” or “the latter.” This fits Band 6, where cohesion works but is not flexible enough for Band 7 (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/band-6-vs-7-comparison.md`; `contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/03-cohesion-error-correction-bank.md`).

**Lexical Resource:** The essay uses adequate Task 1 vocabulary: “illustrates,” “approximately,” “reached a peak,” “decline,” and “similar pattern.” Still, several choices are awkward or imprecise: “had a growth,” “the figure of USA,” “all over the period,” and “India is different.” Repetition of “consumption,” “figure,” and “kWh per person” also limits flexibility. Band 6 is appropriate because meaning is clear, but word choice and collocation are not consistently precise (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** There is a mix of simple and complex sentences, including “which is much higher than Germany,” but accuracy is uneven. Errors include “USA’s consumption increase,” “it slightly decrease,” “grew slow,” and “had reach to 1,200.” These are mostly tense, subject-verb agreement, adverb form, and preposition errors; they do not usually block understanding, which supports Band 6 rather than Band 5. Band 7 would require more frequent error-free sentences and better control of complex structures (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/01-grammar-error-correction-bank.md`).

## Per-criterion justification
**Task Response / Achievement:** The response selects relevant figures: “11,000 kWh,” “6,000 kWh,” “300 kWh,” and “1,200 kWh per person.” It also gives an overview: “the figure of USA stayed the highest, while India had the lowest level.” However, “all three countries had a growth” is too broad because the USA and Germany rose, peaked, and then declined. Band 7 requires a clear overview with key trends and comparisons, while Band 6 allows an overview that is present but less fully developed (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`; `contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/04-task-response-error-correction-bank.md`).

**Coherence and Cohesion:** The four-paragraph structure is logical: introduction, overview, USA/Germany, then India. There is some useful grouping when Germany is linked to the USA with “a similar pattern,” and India is separated with “In contrast.” Still, cohesion is mechanical: “After this,” “However,” “after that,” and “In contrast” do most of the linking work. Band 7 needs more flexible reference and substitution, such as “this downward trend” or “the latter country,” not repeated “consumption” and “figure” (`contexts/05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md`; `contexts/09-instructor-tips/08-cohesion-upgrades-instructor.md`).

**Lexical Resource:** Vocabulary is adequate for the task: “illustrates,” “approximately,” “peak,” “decline,” and “rapidly” are all relevant. The Band 6 limit is the repeated and sometimes unnatural wording: “had a growth,” “the figure of USA,” “all over the period,” “grew slow,” and “had reach to.” A Band 7 answer would use more precise trend collocations such as “rose steadily,” “peaked at,” “declined slightly,” and “climbed to” (`contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`; `contexts/09-instructor-tips/01-paraphrase-banks-task1.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** The essay has some complex forms, for example “which is much higher than Germany” and “which is four times higher than 1990.” However, recurring errors reduce control: “USA’s consumption increase,” “it slightly decrease,” “grew slow,” and “had reach to.” Since the graph covers 1990–2020, past-tense control is important; the essay often drops past forms. Meaning remains clear, so GRA is Band 6 rather than Band 5, but error-free sentences are not frequent enough for Band 7 (`contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/01-grammar-error-correction-bank.md`; `contexts/02-task1-academic/chart-types-overview.md`).

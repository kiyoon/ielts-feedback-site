**Coherence and Cohesion — 6.0.** Paragraphing is logical (intro / overview / USA-Germany / India), and linkers are used ("However", "In contrast", "After this"). But cohesion is mechanical and over-relies on simple connectors — "After this", "However, then" — exactly the pattern flagged in `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md` as a Band-6 ceiling. Reference is occasionally faulty: "India is different" (vague pronoun-style reference). No range of cohesive devices in the Band-7 sense (e.g., "whereas", "by contrast", "having peaked at…").

**Lexical Resource — 6.0.** Topic vocabulary is adequate ("electricity consumption", "per person", "steadily", "rapidly") but the candidate repeats "consumption", "figure", "kWh", and "grew/increase" without paraphrase. `contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md` lists at least eight upgrades the candidate could have used (climbed, surged, levelled off, plateaued, dipped). "The figure of USA / figure of India" is a collocation error — should be "the figure for" (`contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/02-vocabulary-error-correction-bank.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy — 5.5.** Errors are frequent and impede precision: "USA's consumption increase" (subject–verb), "after that it slightly decrease" (subject–verb), "had reach to" (wrong participle + redundant preposition), "had a growth" (awkward nominalisation), "is much higher" (tense — should be past). `contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/01-grammar-error-correction-bank.md` and the Korean-speaker error patterns in `contexts/09-instructor-tips/10-common-korean-chinese-speaker-errors.md` (article omission, S–V agreement) match exactly. Sentence range is limited — almost all clauses are SVO with "and"/"but". This is the Band-5/6 boundary per `contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`.

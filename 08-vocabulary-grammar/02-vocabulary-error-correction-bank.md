# Vocabulary Error Correction Bank — Atomic Reference (Band 6 → 7)

> Atomic table reference for word-choice, collocation, register, and L1-transfer errors that cap students at band 6 in Lexical Resource. Pair with `05-band-6-to-7/vocabulary-upgrades.md` (strategy + topic clusters), `09-instructor-tips/00-user-shared-feedback*.md` (real instructor patterns), and `06-paraphrase-quick-lookup.md` (panic lookup).
>
> **Reason** column tags: `[lex]` = wrong word; `[coll]` = collocation; `[reg]` = register/formality; `[count]` = countability; `[L1]` = L1-transfer error; `[form]` = word-form. **Frequency:** H/M/L.

## 1. Wrong word choice / word form

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The **economic** crisis hurt poor families. *(if you mean "thrifty/cheap")* | The **economical** option saves money. | [form] *economic* = of the economy; *economical* = thrifty. | M |
| 2 | The **historic** building has 50 floors. *(if you mean "of history")* | The **historical** building has 50 floors. | [form] *historic* = important; *historical* = of/about history. | M |
| 3 | He gave a very **sensible** speech *(emotionally fragile)*. | He gave a very **sensitive** speech. *(if emotionally aware)* | [form] *sensible* = wise; *sensitive* = emotionally aware. | M |
| 4 | I had a **classical** lesson today. *(meaning ordinary)* | I had a **classic** lesson today. *(meaning typical/iconic)* | [form] *classic* = iconic; *classical* = of antiquity / formal style. | M |
| 5 | He's a very **economic** person. | He's a very **economical** person. *(thrifty)* | [form] As above. | M |
| 6 | She is a **politic** leader. | She is a **political** leader. | [form] *politic* (adj. = prudent, archaic) ≠ *political*. | L |
| 7 | The medicine is **affecting**. | The medicine is **effective**. | [form] *affect* (v.) ≠ *effective* (adj.). | M |
| 8 | The food was **delicious** good. | The food was **delicious**. | [lex] *delicious* already strong — drop *good*. | M |
| 9 | The information is very **useful** for the people. | The information is highly **valuable** to the public. | [reg] Upgrade *useful* + *people*. | M |
| 10 | The **price** of the population is rising. | The **size** / **figure** of the population is rising. | [coll] *price* doesn't collocate with *population*. | M |
| 11 | We need to **rise** awareness. | We need to **raise** awareness. | [form] *raise* (transitive) vs *rise* (intransitive). | H |
| 12 | The **amount** of students is increasing. | The **number** of students is increasing. | [count] *amount* + uncountable; *number* + countable. | H |
| 13 | He gave me an **advise**. | He gave me **advice** / **a piece of advice**. | [form] *advise* (v.) / *advice* (n.). | H |
| 14 | The new policy will **effect** millions. | The new policy will **affect** millions. | [form] *affect* (v.) / *effect* (n.). | H |
| 15 | He **lays** on the bed all day. | He **lies** on the bed all day. | [form] *lie* (intrans.) / *lay* (trans.). | M |
| 16 | The ratio **rose by** 10 % to 30 %. *(check meaning)* | The ratio **rose to** 30 % *(end point)* / **rose by** 10 % *(amount)*. | [coll] *by* = amount; *to* = end point. | H |
| 17 | The price **decreased of** 10 %. | The price **decreased by** 10 %. | [coll] *decrease/increase by*. | H |
| 18 | The **quantity** of pollution is high. | The **level** of pollution is high. | [coll] *level of pollution* preferred. | M |
| 19 | She has a **strong** knowledge of physics. | She has a **deep / extensive / thorough** knowledge of physics. | [coll] *strong knowledge* is non-native. | M |
| 20 | The information **made** me confused. | The information **confused me** / **left me confused**. | [coll] *make + adjective* often awkward — use the verb directly. | M |
| 21 | He **maked** a mistake. | He **made** a mistake. | [form] Irregular past of *make*. | H |
| 22 | He **goed** home. | He **went** home. | [form] Irregular past of *go*. | H |
| 23 | They **catched** the bus. | They **caught** the bus. | [form] Irregular past of *catch*. | M |
| 24 | This is **more better**. | This is **better** / **far better** / **considerably better**. | [form] Don't double comparatives. | H |
| 25 | The **most easiest** task | The **easiest** task | [form] No double superlative. | H |
| 26 | He is a **success** student. | He is a **successful** student. | [form] *success* (n.) → *successful* (adj.). | M |
| 27 | This problem is very **importance**. | This problem is very **important**. | [form] *importance* (n.) → *important* (adj.). | M |
| 28 | I was **boring** in the lecture. | I was **bored** in the lecture. | [form] *bored* (feeling) vs *boring* (causing). | H |
| 29 | The film was very **interested**. | The film was very **interesting**. | [form] *-ing* describes the cause; *-ed* describes the experiencer. | H |

| 5 | The data **is** important. **They** show… | The data **are** important. They show… | [rule] Pronoun must agree. | M |
| 6 | This is good. *(no antecedent)* | **This idea / This approach** is good. | [rule] *this* alone is too vague at band 7. | H |
| 7 | They say money is everything. *(generic they)* | **It is widely believed that** money matters / **Some argue that** money matters. | [reg] Avoid generic *they*. | M |
| 8 | Everybody loves **their** mother. | Everybody loves **his or her** mother *(formal)* / **their** mother. | [style] As above. | L |
| 9 | The committee announced **its** decision. *(AmE/unified)* / **their** decision *(BrE/individuals)* | Choose one; stay consistent. | [style] Collective pronoun. | L |
| 10 | One should keep **their** word. | **One should keep one's word.** *(formal)* | [reg] *one*… *one's* in formal writing. | L |

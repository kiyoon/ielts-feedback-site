# Spelling and Mechanics — Atomic Reference

> Atomic reference for spelling traps, punctuation errors, capitalisation, number formatting, and hyphenation. Spelling errors in the Writing test do not deduct as severely as in Reading/Listening, but **clusters of spelling/punctuation errors lower the GRA score** because examiners flag the essay as not "frequently error-free."
>
> **Reason** tags: `[sp]` = spelling; `[punct]` = punctuation; `[cap]` = capitalisation; `[num]` = number formatting; `[hyph]` = hyphenation; `[var]` = variety (BrE/AmE).

## 2. Punctuation errors

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I went home, I was tired. | I went home **because** I was tired. *(or)* I went home; I was tired. | [punct] Comma splice — fix with conjunction or semicolon. | H |
| 2 | Education is important however many ignore it. | Education is important**;** however, many ignore it. *(or)* Education is important. **However,** many ignore it. | [punct] *however* needs sentence break or semicolon. | H |
| 3 | In the modern world technology is essential. | In the modern world**,** technology is essential. | [punct] Comma after introductory phrase. | H |
| 4 | Although it was raining, but we went out. | Although it was raining**, we went out.** *(no *but*)* | [punct] Don't pair *although* with *but*. | H |
| 5 | The 1990's saw rapid change. | The **1990s** saw rapid change. | [punct] Decade plurals — no apostrophe. | M |
| 6 | Government's are responsible. | **Governments** are responsible. *(plural, no apostrophe)* | [punct] Plural ≠ possessive. | H |
| 7 | The students's books | The **students'** books *(plural possessive)* | [punct] Plural possessive — apostrophe after *s*. | H |
| 8 | It's effects are clear. | **Its** effects are clear. | [punct] *its* (possessive) ≠ *it's* (it is). | H |
| 9 | Whose going to the meeting? | **Who's** going? *(who is)* | [punct] *whose* (possessive) ≠ *who's*. | M |
| 10 | The teacher said that, students must work. | The teacher said that students must work. *(no comma after *that*)* | [punct] No comma after *that*-clause start. | M |
| 11 | I bought apples oranges and pears. | I bought apples**, oranges and pears.** *(BrE)* / **apples, oranges, and pears.** *(Oxford comma — AmE)* | [punct] List comma. | H |
| 12 | He said "I am tired." | He said**,** "I am tired." | [punct] Comma before direct speech. | M |
| 13 | The reason; is unclear. | The reason **is** unclear. *(no semicolon)* | [punct] Semicolon misuse. | M |
| 14 | The list contains; apples, oranges, pears. | The list contains**: apples, oranges, pears.** | [punct] Colon introduces a list. | M |
| 15 | One factor stands out, government inaction. | One factor stands out**: government inaction.** | [punct] Colon for explanation. | M |

## 3. Capitalisation errors

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | i went to london last summer. | **I** went to **London** last summer. | [cap] *I* and proper nouns. | H |
| 2 | He speaks english fluently. | He speaks **English** fluently. | [cap] Languages capitalised. | H |
| 3 | She is studying french and history. | She is studying **French** and history. | [cap] Languages capitalised; subjects (*history*) usually not. | M |
| 4 | The president visited Korea. | **The President / president** visited Korea. *(capitalise when referring to a specific titled person; lowercase as a generic role)* | [cap] Title rule. | M |
| 5 | I love Christmas, easter and new year. | I love Christmas, **Easter** and **New Year**. | [cap] Holidays. | M |
| 6 | He is a Christian and a Buddhist. | He is a **Christian** and a **Buddhist**. *(correct)* | [cap] Religions/adjectives of religion. | M |
| 7 | the United states of america | the **United States of America** | [cap] Country names — capitalise main words. | M |
| 8 | summer is the hottest season. | **Summer** is the hottest season. *(Note: seasons are NOT capitalised)* — **summer** is the hottest season. | [cap] **Seasons are lowercase.** | M |
| 9 | January, february, march | **January, February, March** | [cap] Months always capitalised. | H |
| 10 | He works at apple inc. | He works at **Apple Inc.** | [cap] Company names. | M |

## 4. Number / figure formatting

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 5 percent of people | **5 % / 5 per cent** of people *(BrE)* / **5 percent** *(AmE, one word)* | [num] BrE: *per cent* (two words) / *%* . AmE: *percent*. Be consistent. | M |
| 2 | five % | **five per cent** *(prefer the word form when starting a sentence)* | [num] Don't mix written word + symbol. | M |
| 3 | $5 million | **$5 million** *(correct)* / **5 million dollars** | [num] Acceptable; symbol attaches to number. | L |
| 4 | 1,000,000 | **one million** / **1 million** | [num] Prefer compact form in essays. | L |
| 5 | 2 of 5 | **two out of five** / **2/5** | [num] Words for small counts in prose. | L |
| 6 | 19th century | **the 19th century** / **the nineteenth century** | [num] Add *the*. | M |
| 7 | 1990 to 2000 | **between 1990 and 2000** / **from 1990 to 2000** | [num] *between X and Y* (not *to*). | M |
| 8 | 50% | **50 %** *(BrE space)* / **50%** *(AmE no space)* | [num] BrE: space; AmE: no space. Pick one. | L |
| 9 | He earned 50000 USD. | He earned **$50,000** / **50,000 USD**. | [num] Use comma in 4+ digit figures. | M |
| 10 | The result was 5,32 metres. | The result was **5.32 metres**. | [num] English uses period for decimals (BrE/AmE both). | M |

## 5. Hyphenation

| # | Original (band 5–6) | Improved (band 7+) | Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | well known author | a **well-known** author *(before noun)* / The author is **well known**. *(after verb)* | [hyph] Hyphenate compound modifier before noun. | H |
| 2 | long term effect | **long-term** effect | [hyph] Same. | H |
| 3 | high quality work | **high-quality** work | [hyph] Same. | H |
| 4 | five year old child | a **five-year-old** child | [hyph] Compound modifier with number. | M |
| 5 | up to date information | **up-to-date** information *(before noun)* | [hyph] Compound modifier. | M |
| 6 | non profit organisation | **non-profit** organisation *(BrE)* / **nonprofit** *(AmE)* | [hyph] Variety. | M |
| 7 | second hand car | **second-hand** car | [hyph] Compound. | M |
| 8 | self confidence | **self-confidence** | [hyph] Always hyphenated with *self-*. | M |
| 9 | re evaluate | **re-evaluate** *(BrE prefers hyphen for *re-* + *e*)* / **reevaluate** *(AmE)* | [hyph] Variety. | L |
| 10 | co operation | **co-operation** *(BrE)* / **cooperation** *(AmE)* | [hyph] Variety. | L |
| 11 | full time job | **full-time** job *(modifier)* / works **full time** *(adverb)* | [hyph] Modifier vs adverb. | M |
| 12 | so called expert | **so-called** expert | [hyph] Compound modifier. | M |
| 13 | open minded | **open-minded** | [hyph] Compound adjective. | M |
| 14 | state of the art technology | **state-of-the-art** technology | [hyph] Compound modifier (3+ words). | M |
| 15 | low income families | **low-income** families | [hyph] Compound modifier. | M |

## 6. Quick BrE / AmE spelling reference

| Concept | BrE | AmE |
|---|---|---|
| -ise / -ize | organise, recognise | organize, recognize |
| -our / -or | colour, behaviour, favour | color, behavior, favor |
| -re / -er | centre, theatre, metre | center, theater, meter |
| -ce / -se *(noun/verb)* | licence (n.) / license (v.) | license (both) |
| -ogue / -og | catalogue, dialogue | catalog, dialog |
| double l | travelling, modelling | traveling, modeling |
| -ll- before suffix | enrolment | enrollment |
| Past tense | learnt, dreamt, burnt *(both accepted)* | learned, dreamed, burned |
| Specific | aluminium, programme *(broadcast)*, cheque | aluminum, program, check |
| Aeroplane / airplane | aeroplane | airplane |

> **Tip:** Pick **one variety and stay consistent**. IELTS accepts both, but mixing within an essay (e.g., *colour* + *organize*) signals carelessness.

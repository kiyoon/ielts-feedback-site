# IELTS GT Task 1 — Band 6.0 Letter Samples

Band 6 letters address all three bullet points and use generally clear language, but suffer from tone slips, repetitive vocabulary, and minor grammar errors. The tone issue is often the limiting factor — once that is fixed, candidates often jump straight to 7.

---

## Sample 1: Semi-formal — Job application to a restaurant

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69f89d017bb8dd0012bc9d9c)
**Letter Type:** Semi-formal (job application)
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 5 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
You are interested in working part-time at a restaurant in your area. Write a letter to the restaurant manager. In your letter:
- Explain why you are writing
- Describe your relevant experience
- Say when you are available to work

### Answer (key features)
The candidate clearly states intent to apply, mentions previous experience as a waiter, but says they are "available whenever you need" rather than naming specific days/times.

### Word Count
~155 words.

### Examiner Comments
"You clearly say you want the job" — task addressed. However, "the time is not clear enough" — a concrete day and time should be stated rather than vague flexibility. Sentences could be more concise. Vocabulary is adequate but lacks variety. Minor grammar errors throughout.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (5):** Bullet 3 is too vague — examiners want specific availability.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (6):** Sentences run on; needs trimming.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** Adequate but no register-specific vocabulary ("position", "available from", "prior experience").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (6):** Some article/preposition errors.

### Improved Version
"I am available to begin work from Monday, 13 May, and can work weekday evenings from 5 pm onwards as well as full days on Saturdays and Sundays. I would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss my application at your convenience."

## Sample 2: Formal — Reception area improvement (workplace)

**Source:** ieltsjuice.com (Letter sample 3184 — Band 6.0)
**Letter Type:** Formal (suggestion to manager)
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 6 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
You work for a company. You have noticed problems in the reception area of your office. Write a letter to your manager. In your letter:
- Describe the complaints you have heard
- Explain why the reception area is important
- Suggest improvements

### Answer (key features)
The candidate covers all three bullets in 218 words, mentioning lack of seating and understaffing, explaining first impressions, and proposing additional furniture and staff.

### Word Count
218 words (slightly long; tone slips creep in with extra words).

### Examiner Comments
"There may be inconsistencies in tone." Cohesion within and between sentences "may be faulty or mechanical." Vocabulary is adequate but contains some inaccuracies in less common word choices. Grammar errors are present but rarely impede communication. The candidate should "adopt a formal tone and maintain it throughout."

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (6):** All bullets covered; tone inconsistencies.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (6):** Mechanical use of "Firstly / Secondly / Thirdly" without smoother transitions.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** Mix of formal and informal; "things" / "stuff" type vocabulary shouldn't appear.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (6):** Some errors in punctuation and articles.

### Improvement Notes
Maintain "I would suggest / it would be advisable / it might be worth considering" throughout instead of slipping into "we should just".

## Sample 3: Formal — Complaint about online purchase

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fb6e907bb8dd0012bc9fe7)
**Letter Type:** Formal (complaint)
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 5 | GRA: 5

### Prompt
You bought a clothing item online but it arrived in poor condition. Write a letter to the company. In your letter:
- Give details of the purchase
- Describe the problem
- Explain why you need a replacement urgently

### Answer (key features)
Asma writes about a pink kurta with a hole in the trousers and a colour mismatch. Order #345678. Wedding next Saturday creates urgency.

### Word Count
~170 words.

### Examiner Comments
"Add one more small detail about the order, like size, date, or price." Could be more explicit about requesting specific action and timeline. Grammar errors with verbs and prepositions are flagged. The complaint vocabulary is mild — should be sharper for a formal complaint.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (7):** All three bullets covered.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Logical paragraphs.
- **Lexical Resource (5):** Mild language ("not nice", "I am sad") instead of "unsatisfactory", "deeply disappointed", "unacceptable".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (5):** Multiple verb-tense and preposition errors.

### Improvement Notes
Use complaint-strength vocabulary: "I was deeply disappointed to discover…", "the item is clearly unfit for purpose…", "I would expect a full refund or replacement within seven working days."

## Sample 4: Informal — Letter to a friend (typical Band 6)

**Source:** Composite (typical writing9 / engnovate Band 6 informal letters)
**Letter Type:** Informal
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt (typical)
A friend is going to visit your country soon. Write to your friend. In your letter:
- Suggest places to visit
- Recommend what to bring
- Offer help during their stay

### Typical Band-6 Answer
"Dear John, I am writing this letter to suggest places you visit when you come to my country. There are many beautiful places in my country. You should visit the beach and the mountain. You can bring warm clothes because it is cold in mountain. I will help you when you come. We can go shopping and eat food. Please tell me when you arrive. Best regards, Ali."

### Word Count
~95 words (well under length — would automatically score below 6).

### Examiner Comments
- **Tone slip:** "I am writing this letter to" is too formal for a friend; should be "I'm writing to" or just plunge in casually.
- "Best regards" is semi-formal — should be "Take care" / "Lots of love" / "Cheers".
- Repetitive: "country" 3×, "you" 5+ times in basic position.
- No specific recommendations — vague.
- Word count well below 150.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length; vague bullets.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy.
- **Lexical Resource:** Limited.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** "in mountain" → "in the mountains".

### Improvement Notes
Open warmly: "Hi John! It's brilliant news that you're finally coming to visit — I can't wait!" Use contractions, idioms, and specific places ("Petra in May is unmissable").

## Sample 5: Formal — Complaint about faulty equipment (workplace)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69f810fb7bb8dd0012bc9d3e) — Band 7.0 listed but a typical Band 6 has these issues
**Letter Type:** Formal (complaint)
**Overall Band:** 6.0 (representative pattern)

### Prompt
You bought a piece of equipment from a company online and it does not work. Write a letter to the company. In your letter:
- Describe the problem with the equipment
- Explain how this problem is affecting your work
- Say what you want the shop or company to do

### Answer (Band 6 version — pattern)
The candidate describes a faulty desk, mentions back pain due to fixed height, and asks for a replacement.

### Word Count
~145 words (under length).

### Examiner Comments
- Could elaborate on **when** the desk was purchased and what troubleshooting steps were tried.
- Could use more polite phrasing for the replacement request — "I would like" is mild; "I would appreciate a full replacement at no additional cost" is stronger.
- Should use stronger linking words: First, Also, As a result, Therefore.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Limited linkers.
- **Lexical Resource:** Lacks complaint-strength vocabulary.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Generally clear with minor errors.

### Improvement Notes
Add purchase date, order reference, prior contact attempts. Be assertive but polite: "I trust this matter will be resolved within seven working days."

## Sample 6: Semi-formal — Late rent payment to landlord (Band 6 version)

**Source:** Pattern from writing9 / various Band 6 submissions
**Letter Type:** Semi-formal (request)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt
You are unable to pay your rent on time this month. Write a letter to your landlord. In your letter:
- Explain why you cannot pay on time
- State when you can pay
- Apologise

### Typical Band-6 Answer
"Dear Sir, I am writing to inform you that I cannot pay rent this month on time. The reason is that my mother is sick and I have to pay for hospital. I will pay rent on the 15th of next month. I am very sorry for the inconvenience. Please understand my situation. I hope you accept my request. Thank you. Yours faithfully, Ali."

### Word Count
~75 words (severely under-length).

### Examiner Comments
- Word count below 150 — Task Achievement capped.
- Bullets covered but each underdeveloped.
- "Inform" and "request" are semi-formal-appropriate, but "please understand my situation" is awkward.
- No specifics: how much rent, what hospital costs, why a 15-day delay specifically.
- "Yours faithfully" appropriate (no name given).

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length; under-developed.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Each bullet is a single sentence.
- **Lexical Resource:** Basic.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mostly accurate.

### Improvement Notes
Add detail: "An unforeseen medical emergency has arisen — my mother was admitted to hospital last week with a serious condition, and I have had to cover substantial care costs. Once my next salary is paid on the 14th, I will transfer the full rent on the 15th of next month."

## Sample 7: Informal — Apology to friend for missed event

**Source:** Pattern from various Band 6 informal letters
**Letter Type:** Informal (apology)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt
You missed your friend's birthday party last week. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Apologise for missing the party
- Explain why you couldn't attend
- Suggest a way to make it up

### Typical Band-6 Answer
"Dear Sarah, I am writing this letter to apologize for missing your birthday party last week. I am very sorry. I could not come because I was sick. I had a fever and I had to stay in bed. To make it up, I would like to invite you for dinner next weekend at a restaurant. Please let me know if you are free. I am sorry once again. Yours sincerely, Maria."

### Word Count
~90 words (under-length).

### Examiner Comments
- **Tone slip:** "I am writing this letter to apologize" is too formal for a friend.
- "Yours sincerely" is semi-formal — wrong sign-off for a friend.
- "I am very sorry" / "I am sorry once again" — repetition of basic apology.
- Under-length.
- No personal warmth ("I missed seeing you on your big day!").

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Under-length; tone wrong.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Adequate.
- **Lexical Resource:** Basic.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Accurate.

### Improvement Notes
Open: "Hi Sarah! I'm so, so sorry I couldn't make it to your birthday last week — I felt awful missing such a big day." Sign off: "Lots of love, Maria xx".

## Sample 8: Formal — Inquiry about a course

**Source:** Pattern from typical Band 6 inquiry letters
**Letter Type:** Formal (inquiry)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt
You have seen an advertisement for an English course at a college. Write a letter to the course director. In your letter:
- Ask for information about the course content
- Inquire about fees and starting dates
- Ask about accommodation

### Typical Band-6 Answer
"Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to ask about the English course in your college. I want to know what is the content of the course. Also, I want to know the fees and when it starts. Please tell me about accommodation also. Looking forward to your reply. Thank you. Yours faithfully, Ahmed."

### Word Count
~70 words (severely under-length).

### Examiner Comments
- Word count caps Task Achievement.
- "I want to know" 2× — should be "I would be grateful if you could let me know" / "Could you please advise…".
- "Tell me" too direct — "could you provide details about".
- No specifics about which course, prior experience, target start date.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Drastically under-length.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy.
- **Lexical Resource:** Direct demands instead of polite questions.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Generally accurate.

### Improvement Notes
"I would be grateful if you could send me detailed information about the course curriculum, including the level (CEFR equivalent), modules covered, and class size. I would also appreciate clarification on the tuition fees, available start dates over the next six months, and whether the college offers accommodation in residential halls or assists with private rentals."

## Common Band 6.0 Profile (letters)

Across all samples, Band 6 letter writers:

1. **Cover all three bullet points** — but at least one is under-developed.
2. **Often write under 150 words** — automatic Task Achievement cap.
3. **Mix registers** — formal opening with informal closing, or vice versa.
4. **Repeat basic verbs** — "ask", "tell", "want", "say" instead of register-specific alternatives.
5. **Use generic openings** — "I am writing this letter to…" with no purpose specificity.
6. **Use wrong sign-offs** — "Yours sincerely" to a friend, "Best wishes" to a manager.
7. **Have minor but recurring grammar errors** — articles, prepositions, agreement.

**The single biggest upgrade lever from 6 to 7 in letters: tone consistency + specific details.** Pick the register at the start and sustain it; replace generic statements with concrete details (date, amount, place, time).

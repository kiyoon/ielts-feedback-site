# W4 (Task 2) — Band 7 model answer (international travel)

**Type:** Model rewrite of `W4-T2-international-travel.md`, targeting Band 7.
**Approach:** One-sided — both body paragraphs argue the advantages; disadvantages
are conceded briefly and rebutted, so the "advantages outweigh" position is

## Prompt

International travel has many advantages to both travellers and the country visited. Do you think the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?

## Model answer (Band 7)

Crossing borders for leisure or work has become far more common than it once was, and it clearly affects both the people who travel and the nations that receive them. Although international travel is not without its problems, I firmly believe that its benefits outweigh its drawbacks, because it broadens travellers’ horizons and brings valuable income to host countries.

The most obvious advantage for travellers themselves is the chance to experience cultures that cannot be found at home. By living among local people, even briefly, visitors encounter different languages, cuisines and customs first-hand, which tends to make them more open-minded and adaptable. A student who spends a summer abroad, for instance, often returns not only with improved language skills but also with a broader perspective on how others live. Such personal growth is difficult to gain from books or films alone, and it stays with travellers long after the trip ends.

International tourism is, in addition, a powerful source of revenue for the destinations involved. Money spent by visitors on accommodation, transport and food flows directly into local businesses, creating jobs and encouraging governments to invest in roads, airports and public services. In many regions, tourism is in fact the single largest employer, supporting families who would otherwise struggle to find stable work. This economic boost benefits not just the tourism sector but the wider community.

Admittedly, international travel can create difficulties, such as overtourism in popular cities or the vulnerability of visitors who do not speak the local language. These concerns are real, yet they can largely be managed through sensible regulation and better traveller awareness, whereas the cultural and economic gains are lasting.

In conclusion, while the disadvantages of international travel should not be ignored, I am convinced that the advantages are considerably greater, since travellers grow as individuals and host nations prosper economically.

## Why this is Band 7 (and how it fixes the original)

- **Clear position throughout** — the intro states "advantages outweigh", both
  bodies argue advantages, the conclusion restates it. No 50/50 "pros and cons"
  drift (the original's main weakness).
- **Intro follows a template** — (1) topic paraphrase, (2) position + outline of
  the two reasons that the bodies then deliver. Roadmap matches the body.
- **PEEL development** — each body has Point → Explanation → Example → Link,
  instead of listing undeveloped claims.
- **Weighing, not just listing** — the concession paragraph admits disadvantages
  but explicitly rebuts them ("…whereas the cultural and economic gains are
  lasting"), which is what an *outweigh* question demands.
- **Lexical resource** — "broadens travellers' horizons", "open-minded and
  adaptable", "single largest employer", "sensible regulation" — a wider range
  than the original, with natural collocations.
- **Grammar** — a mix of complex structures (participle/relative clauses, "By
  living…", "which tends to…") kept mostly error-free.

# Band 6.0 → Band 7.0 — Index

This directory is a comprehensive guide to lifting an IELTS Writing score from band 6 to band 7. The guide is built around the four official criteria (Task Response, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range and Accuracy) and is designed to be read by a student or used as reference by a feedback agent.

## The Band 7 mental model in one paragraph

A Band 7 essay differs from a Band 6 essay along five axes simultaneously: it answers **all** parts of the prompt (not just some); it holds **one** clear position from start to finish; every main idea is **explained and illustrated** with a concrete example; cohesion is **flexible**, built into sentences via referencing and lexical chains rather than stamped in via *Firstly/Secondly/Moreover*; and grammar shows a **variety** of complex structures (relative clauses, conditionals, participles, complex noun phrases) used **accurately**, with the majority of sentences error-free. Vocabulary is **precise** (not necessarily rare), and the writer hedges absolute claims with modals. The motto — repeated across nearly every IELTS source — is *control beats complexity*: a clear, accurate, well-developed simple essay scores higher than a complex, error-strewn one.

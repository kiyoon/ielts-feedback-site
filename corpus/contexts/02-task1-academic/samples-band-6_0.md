# IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 — Band 6.0 Samples

This file collects Task 1 Academic responses scored around Band 6.0 (overall) with examiner feedback. These represent the typical "stuck at 6" profile: the candidate completes the task and achieves logical organisation, but lexical resource and grammatical accuracy hold them back. Use these to study the gap between Band 6 and Band 7.

---

## Sample 1: Line graph — US household technology adoption (1995–1999)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fdda73312e6c0011a3e3c9)
**Task Type:** Line graph
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 9 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The graph below shows the percentage of households with different kinds of technology in the US from 1995 to 1999. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

(Categories shown: washing machine, telephone, computer, internet, mobile phone — all measured as % of households.)

### Answer (extracted summary)
The candidate writes that "all 5 categories of technology had a growth for this entire period." Washing machines consistently showed the highest adoption rates, while phones experienced dramatic increases (from approximately 15% to 80%). The remaining technologies (computer, internet, mobile phone) showed steady but smaller increases.

### Word Count
142 words (below the 150-word minimum — this would be penalised).

### Examiner Comments
The response covers the task and is organised in a logical sequence with paragraph breaks. However, lexical resource is the weak point: words like "percentage", "figures", "growth" and "increased" are repeated multiple times across the response. Sentences mostly use a single basic structure — "X had a growth from … to …" — limiting the grammatical range score. Falling under 150 words also costs marks on Task Achievement.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Word count under 150. Overview is present but undeveloped (one short sentence).
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical, but linkers are repetitive ("also", "in addition", "moreover") with weak variety.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Percentage" used 6+ times; "increase" / "growth" used interchangeably without adding precision (e.g. "rose sharply", "climbed steadily", "surged").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mostly simple sentences. Minor article and preposition errors. Few complex structures.

### Improvement Notes
Replace "had a growth" with the verb "grew" or "rose" + adverb (sharply, steadily, gradually). Use "telephones surged from 15% to 80%, the steepest rise of any technology". Pad to 165 words by adding 1–2 specific data points and an extra comparison.

## Sample 2: Map comparison — Canterbury school location planning

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fda8cf7bb8dd0012bca1e4)
**Task Type:** Map (two proposed sites)
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 6 | CC: 9 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The maps below show two proposed sites (S1 and S2) for a new school in Canterbury. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (key features)
The candidate compares S1 (in the countryside, 6 km from the nearest town, accessible only by a small road) with S2 (closer to a residential area, near a main road and bus stop). They note S2's better accessibility but lack of green space.

### Word Count
~190 words.

### Examiner Comments
Coherence is strong (well-paragraphed comparison). However, Task Achievement is held to 6 because the response does not include a clear overview that highlights the most important contrast. Lexical resource and grammar are the main weaknesses: 23 grammar mistakes were flagged, mostly articles ("new school" should be "a new school", "in the countryside"), prepositions ("near to" → "near"), and basic agreement errors.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Adequate response, but no clear overview comparing the two sites' overall character. Reorganising paragraphs around themes (accessibility vs. environment) would help.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical; one of the strongest aspects.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Area" / "side" repeated 5+ times. Could use "vicinity", "surroundings", "neighbourhood".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Frequent article omission ("near park" → "near a park"), preposition errors ("located at countryside" → "located in the countryside"), and some subject–verb agreement issues.

### Improvement Notes
Add an overview: "Overall, S2 is more accessible and closer to residents, whereas S1 is more isolated but offers a greener environment." Group changes by theme rather than walking through each map.

## Sample 3: Bar chart — Secondary education enrolment (4 regions, 2000)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69ff19dd312e6c0011a3e4ce)
**Task Type:** Bar chart (cross-region comparison)
**Overall Band:** 6.0
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 7 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The bar chart below shows the percentage of school-aged girls and boys enrolled in secondary school in four world regions in the year 2000. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (key features)
The candidate notes the highest enrolment in North America/Europe (~95% boys, ~95% girls), with sub-Saharan Africa lowest (~30% / ~25%). Gender gaps were small in developed regions and wider in developing regions.

### Word Count
~170 words.

### Examiner Comments
The response addresses the task fully. The major weakness flagged: "Don't use the same linking words: also, overall — excessive repetition." Word repetition is high: "percentage" used 6 times, "shows" used twice. Lexical Resource scored only 4. Thirteen grammar mistakes were flagged.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Complete response, suitable tone, all four regions covered.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Repetitive linkers ("also" appears multiple times). Vary with "in addition", "by comparison", "in contrast".
- **Lexical Resource:** Suggested replacements for "girls / boys / education / percentage" — e.g. "female students / male students / school enrolment / proportion".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Limited complex sentence structures. Errors in articles and number agreement.

### Improvement Notes
Group regions by enrolment level (high/middle/low). Use one comparative complex sentence per group, e.g. "Whereas almost all children in North America were enrolled, fewer than one in three attended secondary school in sub-Saharan Africa."

## Sample 4: Line graph — Carbon dioxide emissions (4 European countries, 1967–2007)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fd1d307bb8dd0012bca14f)
**Task Type:** Line graph
**Overall Band:** 6.0–6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 9 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The graph below shows average carbon dioxide emissions per person in the United Kingdom, Sweden, Italy and Portugal between 1967 and 2007. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (key features)
UK had highest emissions throughout, declining from ~11 to ~9 tonnes. Sweden peaked around 1980 then declined sharply. Italy and Portugal both rose, with Portugal showing the largest relative increase.

### Word Count
172 words across 3 paragraphs.

### Examiner Comments
Strong on task and coherence. Lexical resource is the weakness — "diagram" and "graph" are repeated as is "information". Grammar issues include: "the Portugal is" (should be "Portugal"), subject–verb agreement errors, article omission/insertion errors. The introduction could be rephrased to avoid copying from the prompt.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Good selection of key data; clear overview.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Strong paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Information / graph / diagram" used 4+ times. Country names often need no article ("Portugal", not "the Portugal").
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Subject–verb agreement, articles. The candidate avoids complex structures.

### Improvement Notes
"Carbon dioxide emissions per capita" is the technical term. Replace "the graph shows" with "the line chart illustrates / depicts / compares". Drop articles before country names.

## Sample 5: Bar chart — British charity donation by age (1990 vs 2010)

**Source:** engnovate.com (Band 6 user-written sample)
**Task Type:** Bar chart (two-period comparison)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt
The bar graph compares the percentage of British people who gave money to charity by age range (18–25, 26–35, 36–50, 51–65, over 65) in 1990 and 2010.

### Answer (extracted)
"The bar chart shows the percentage of British people that gave money to charity in different age ranges, comparing the year 1990 and 2010. Overall, in the year 1990, charity donation increased by age, with the highest percentage in the 36–50 group. In 2010, however, donation grew until the age of 65 and then decreased. People aged over 65 donated less in 2010 than in 1990."

### Word Count
~150 words.

### Examiner Comments
The response identifies the main shift (peak shifted from 36–50 in 1990 to 51–65 in 2010), which is the key feature. However, the language is repetitive ("percentage", "donation", "age"). Several grammatical issues: "comparing the year 1990 and 2010" → "between 1990 and 2010"; "donation increased by age" is unclear. The overview is too short.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Identifies main change, but overview is one sentence.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Reasonably linked.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Donation" / "gave money" / "charity" repeated.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Several preposition and article errors.

### Improvement Notes
Add a stronger overview: "Overall, the peak donation age group shifted upwards by 15 years over the two decades, while overall participation rose for all but the oldest group."

## Sample 6: Pie chart — Energy production by source (Band 6 typical)

**Source:** Pattern based on common writing9 / engnovate pie-chart submissions
**Task Type:** Pie chart (often paired)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt (typical)
The pie charts show the proportion of electricity generated from different sources in a country in 1980 and 2010. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (Band-6 typical features)
"In 1980, coal was the largest source of electricity at 45%, followed by oil (25%) and natural gas (20%). Nuclear was only 5% and renewables 5%. In 2010, the percentage of coal decreased to 30%, while gas increased to 35%. Nuclear became 15% and renewables 10%. Oil decreased to 10%."

### Word Count
~155 words.

### Examiner Comments
Adequate task achievement; the response identifies the largest sources and correctly lists the changes. However, "percentage" is overused, the overview merely lists 1980 data without summarising change, and comparisons are implicit rather than explicit. Many candidates at this band give every percentage but forget to group: which sources rose, which fell, which dominated overall.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Lists data; overview is missing the trend ("overall, the energy mix became more diversified, with coal losing dominance").
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy — one sentence per source.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Percentage" 6+ times. Use "proportion / share / segment / accounted for".
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Mostly simple sentences with comma splices.

### Improvement Notes
Group: "Fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) dominated in both years, but their combined share fell from 90% to 75%." This is one complex sentence covering three data points.

## Sample 7: Bar chart — Tourist leisure activities in Greece (4 nationalities)

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69ff1292312e6c0011a3e4ca)
**Task Type:** Bar chart (multi-category comparison)
**Overall Band:** 6.0–6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 9 | CC: 9 | LR: 4 | GRA: 4

### Prompt
The chart below shows the average hours per day Chinese, American, Turkish and Brazilian tourists spent on different leisure activities while on holiday in Greece in August 2019. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Answer (key features)
Chinese tourists spent the most time on shopping (~5 hrs); Americans spent most time on beach activities; Turkish tourists were most balanced across categories; Brazilians spent the most on socialising.

### Word Count
~180 words.

### Examiner Comments
Strong organisation and task fulfilment. 26 grammar mistakes flagged — predominantly subject–verb agreement, prepositions, and article use. "Tourists / hours / books / places / amount" used multiple times. Introduction matches only 85% of expected paraphrasing.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Comprehensive, four nationalities covered.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Logical paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource:** Heavy repetition of "hours" and nationality names. Could use pronouns ("the former / the latter"), demonyms ("visitors from China"), and synonyms.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Frequent agreement errors ("Chinese tourists spends" → "spend"); preposition mistakes ("on the beach" vs. "at the beach").

### Improvement Notes
Use phrases like "Chinese visitors devoted the most time to …" and "by contrast, American holidaymakers favoured …".

## Sample 8: Line graph — Jam consumption in an Asian country (1991–2014)

**Source:** engnovate.com Band 6.5 sample
**Task Type:** Line graph (3 product types)
**Overall Band:** 6.0–6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 6 | LR: 7 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
The line graph illustrates the consumption of three types of jam (strawberry, lemon and cherry) in an Asian country between 1991 and 2014, measured in tonnes.

### Answer (vocabulary used)
The candidate uses some good phrases: "incredible downfall", "considerable growth", "fluctuations", "insignificant drop". However, "tonnes" appears 10 times.

### Word Count
247 words (over-long).

### Examiner Comments
Adequate trend coverage but factual inaccuracies in the lemon jam description. Vocabulary has some good moments but is undermined by repetition. "Incredible downfall" is dramatic but slightly informal. Cohesion is hampered by run-on sentences.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Inaccuracies in reading the chart.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Run-on sentences; transitions between trend descriptions are abrupt.
- **Lexical Resource:** "Tonnes" 10× — should use "the figure", "this amount", "demand for it", or simply omit on subsequent mentions.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Article and preposition errors; some agreement issues.

### Improvement Notes
Aim for 170–180 words. Quality over quantity. "Incredible" is too strong/subjective for academic Task 1 — prefer "dramatic / sharp / steep".

## Sample 11: Pie chart — Household income spending (typical Band 6)

**Source:** Composite (multiple writing9 / engnovate band 6 submissions)
**Task Type:** Pie chart (one or two)
**Overall Band:** 6.0

### Prompt (representative)
The pie chart shows the percentage of monthly household income spent on different categories by an average family in a country.

### Answer (representative)
"The pie chart shows different categories of monthly spending by a family. Housing is the highest at 35%. Food is 22%. Transport is 15%. Entertainment is 10%. Bills are 10%. Other is 8%. - **Coherence & Cohesion:** Choppy; one-fact sentences.
- **Lexical Resource:** Heavy repetition of "is" and "percentage".

Group: "Essential expenses (housing, food, transport, bills) accounted for 82% of income, dwarfing discretionary categories such as entertainment (10%)."

## Common Band 6.0 Profile (synthesis)

Across all the samples above, Band 6.0 candidates consistently show:

1. **A complete response that addresses the task** — Task Achievement is rarely the limiting factor on its own.
2. **Logical paragraph structure** — but with a weak or missing overview.
3. **Repetitive vocabulary** — especially basic chart-description words ("percentage", "shows", "increase").
4. **Frequent low-level grammar errors** — articles, prepositions, subject-verb agreement, occasional spelling.
5. **Limited complex sentences** — most are simple S-V-O with one or two subordinate clauses.
6. **Implicit rather than explicit comparisons** — listing data rather than comparing it.

**The single most common upgrade lever:** Strengthen the **overview** (2–3 sentences signposted with "Overall, …" describing 2–3 grouped key features without numbers) and use **explicit comparators** ("whereas", "by contrast", "more than twice as", "in contrast to").

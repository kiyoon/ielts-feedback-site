## 1. Vietop — 6 Cách Paraphrasing trong IELTS Writing

🔗 https://vietop.edu.vn/blog/cach-paraphrasing-trong-ielts-writing/

**The 6 paraphrase methods (definitive Vietnamese guide):**

### Method 1 — Reorder words in the sentence
> Original: "People eat more food produced in other regions than local food."
> Paraphrased: "The consumption of imported food has gained popularity in recent years."

### Method 2 — Synonym / antonym substitution
> Original: "My car needs petrol."
> Paraphrased: "My vehicle requires fuel."

### Method 3 — Active ↔ passive voice
> Original: "People say that global warming is caused by fossil fuel burning."
> Paraphrased: "Global warming is said to be caused by fossil fuel burning."

### Method 4 — Word-family forms (noun ↔ verb ↔ adjective)
> Original: "Education is obviously a much better measure for raising awareness."
> Paraphrased: "People who are properly educated have stronger heightened awareness."

### Method 5 — Enumeration (list specifics instead of general term)
> Original: "Advertising is all around us."
> Paraphrased: "Pop-ups, flyers and billboards are virtually everywhere."

### Method 6 — Dummy "It" subject
> Original: "Children should obey rules their parents and teachers set."
> Paraphrased: "It is claimed that offspring should follow all rules their parents and teachers set."

**Takeaway:** Vietop's 6-method framework is the **most teachable, exam-driven** paraphrase rubric across all three regions. Rotation of methods is what differentiates band 7 from band 6.

## 2. ZIM — Cách paraphrase Writing Task 1

🔗 https://zim.vn/cach-paraphrase-writing-task-1-trong-ielts

**Excerpt:** "Cách paraphrase câu mở đầu của bài Writing Task 1 thường tuân theo 3 phần: Mở đầu - Thay thế động từ; Nội dung - Sử dụng từ đồng nghĩa, thay đổi từ loại, thay đổi cấu trúc ngữ pháp; Thời gian - 'from 2000 to 2010' thành 'between 2000 and 2010' hoặc 'over a 10-year period'."

**Translation:** "Paraphrasing the opening sentence of Task 1 follows three parts: Opening — replace the verb; Content — use synonyms, change word forms, change grammatical structures; Time — `from 2000 to 2010` becomes `between 2000 and 2010` or `over a 10-year period`."

**Three components MUST change:**

1. **The verb** (`shows` → `illustrates / depicts / outlines / sets out`)
2. **The time** (`from X to Y` → `between X and Y` / `over the X-year span`)
3. **The subject noun** (`population` → `demographic figure`; `people` → `citizens`)

**Takeaway:** ZIM's "three-component rule" is one of the cleanest mnemonic structures available — every band-7 candidate's introduction should reflect changes in all three.

## 3. ZIM — Subject paraphrase bank (most-used Task 1 subjects)

🔗 https://www.ieltstutor.me/blog/cac-cach-paraphrase-chu-ngu-thuong-gap-ielts-writing-task-1

**Subject paraphrase patterns:**

| Original subject | Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| spending on X | the expenditure on X / the amount allocated to X / the budget devoted to X |
| sales of X | the turnover of X / the volume of X sold |
| production of X | the output of X / the manufacture of X |
| means of transport | modes / forms of transport / vehicles |
| car owners | car users / motorists |
| Internet users | those connected to the Internet / online users |
| how people in X spent time | how time was spent by people in X / the manner in which X allocated time |

**Takeaway:** Vietnamese cram schools have the deepest bank of **subject-noun paraphrase**, more nuanced than Korean or Chinese sources. The "subject is where students get stuck" rule is most clearly articulated in Vietnamese resources.

## 4. Vietop — Cách viết câu dài (long sentences) cho Task 2

🔗 https://vietop.edu.vn/blog/cach-viet-cau-dai-ielts-writing-task-2/

**5 techniques for extending sentences:**

### 1. Explanation & Specification
> "Fossil fuels are bad" → "Fossil fuels, such as coal, oil and natural gas, can have detrimental effects on the environment, especially the air quality."

### 2. Paraphrasing with supporting words
> "Smoking is bad" → "Smoking has negative impacts on people's health."

### 3. Relative clauses (Mệnh đề quan hệ)
> "Fossil fuels are bad for the environment" → "Fossil fuels, **which include coal, oil and natural gas**, can have detrimental effects on the environment."

### 4. Adding noun modifiers
> "Playing sports is good" → "Playing sports can bring benefits **to children's health**."

### 5. Coordinating & subordinating conjunctions
> "Gasoline and oil have been primary power sources, **but** nuclear and renewable energy have been least popular."

**Takeaway:** Vietop's "build out from short" approach is the **most beginner-friendly** sentence-extension method. For learners who write only short sentences, this 5-step ladder is the recommended remediation path.

## 6. ZIM — 7 phrases to replace "good"

🔗 https://zim.vn/cac-cum-tu-thay-the-cho-good

**The seven replacements with collocations:**

| Word | Collocation type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Decent | Decent + noun | "decent neighborhood / decent living conditions" |
| Satisfactory | Satisfactory + noun | "satisfactory result / satisfactory explanation" |
| Acceptable | Acceptable + noun | "acceptable behavior / acceptable level" |
| Passable | Passable + noun | "passable performance / passable attempt" |
| Competent | Competent + noun | "competent professional / competent leader" |
| Fine | Fine + noun | "fine art / fine cuisine" |
| Qualified | Qualified + noun | "qualified candidate / qualified professional" |

**Takeaway:** ZIM's emphasis on **collocational fit, not just synonym matching** — only `decent` collocates with neighborhood; only `competent` collocates with professional. Same Vietnamese translation does NOT mean interchangeable.

## 7. IELTS Fighter — Lộ trình luyện IELTS Writing 5.0 → 7.0 trong 3 tháng

🔗 https://ielts-fighter.com/tin-tuc/lo-trinh-luyen-ielts-writing-tu-5-0-len-7-0-trong-3-thang_mt1561296479.html

**Synthesised key principles:**

> "Nguyên tắc ở band 6 là không lặp lại keyword quan trọng quá 2 lần, còn band 7 nghĩa là không lặp lại lần nào. Đa số các từ này là động từ và tính từ."

**Translation:** "The rule at band 6 is to not repeat important keywords more than twice; band 7 means not repeating them at all. Most of these words are verbs and adjectives."

**Takeaway:** IELTS Fighter's specific quantification — **band 6 allows ≤2 repetitions of keywords; band 7 allows zero repetitions of content keywords (verbs/adjectives)**.

## 8. IELTS Fighter — Unit 7 Writing Task 2 paraphrase

🔗 https://ielts-fighter.com/writing/Unit-7-Writing-task-2-Cach-paraphrase-lai-de-bai_mt1472611990.html

**Synthesised:** "Có 2 cách paraphrase đề bài: Một là paraphrase từng từ một, có nghĩa là tìm một từ khác có nghĩa giống hoặc tương đương cho từng từ trong đề bài. Hai là paraphrase ý, có nghĩa là viết lại toàn bộ ý của đề bài bằng cách diễn đạt khác."

**Translation:** "There are 2 ways to paraphrase a topic: (1) word-by-word paraphrase, finding a synonym for each word in the topic; (2) idea paraphrase, rewriting the entire idea using different expressions."

**Takeaway:** Word-by-word paraphrase is the band-6 approach (often produces awkward results). Idea-level paraphrase is the band-7+ approach. **Mix both** — paraphrase 60% by idea, 40% by synonym.

## 9. IELTS Fighter — Linking words for Band 7

🔗 https://ielts-fighter.com/writing/ielts-writing-tips-band-7_mt1540845653.html (search-confirmed)

**Synthesised:** "Linking words là yếu tố quan trọng giúp tăng điểm Coherence & Cohesion. Các từ nối này cần đa dạng, chính xác và logic. Tương tự Task 1, từ nối trong Task 2 cũng đóng vai trò quan trọng giúp đưa band điểm lên 7.0."

**Takeaway:** IELTS Fighter aligns with the Korean/Chinese consensus — **diversity, accuracy, logic** are the three criteria for band-7 cohesion.

## 10. DOL English — Sample Band 7 essays

🔗 https://www.dolenglish.vn/blog/ielts-writing-band-7

**Synthesised approach (from search summary):** DOL focuses on **structural integrity over flashy vocabulary** — every body paragraph must have:

1. **Topic sentence** (one main argument)
2. **Explanation** (why is this argument true?)
3. **Example** (a concrete instance)
4. **Link/Evaluation** (back to thesis)

**Takeaway:** DOL's TEEL structure is identical to the Korean PEEL (Point/Evidence/Explanation/Link) — Vietnamese and Korean cram schools both teach this rigorous paragraph anatomy.

## 11. IELTS Nguyễn Huyền — Cách paraphrase chi tiết

🔗 https://ielts-nguyenhuyen.com/cach-paraphrase-trong-ielts-writing-task-1/

**Synthesised:** Vietnamese popular blogger Nguyễn Huyền's translation-based paraphrase approach: paraphrase **phrase by phrase** rather than word by word.

> Original: "the percentage of households with a computer"
> Phrase 1: "the percentage of" → "the proportion of"
> Phrase 2: "households with a computer" → "homes that own a computer / computer-owning households"
> Combined: "the proportion of computer-owning households"

**Takeaway:** Phrase-level paraphrase is the **band-7 sweet spot** — preserves accuracy while restructuring the noun phrase.

## What Vietnamese sources offer that Korean / Chinese do NOT

1. **Per-topic collocation files** — ZIM has 8+ standalone topic-collocation pages (Cities, Education, Technology, Business, Traffic, Language, Art, Health). Chinese and Korean sources tend to have one consolidated 200-word list rather than topic-deep dives.

2. **6-method paraphrase framework** — Vietop's explicit 6-method numbered system (file 01 §15) is more teachable than the Korean "do all of these" approach or the Chinese "memorise 50 synonym groups" approach.

3. **Phrase-level paraphrase** as a distinct strategy — Vietnamese sources clearly distinguish word-level / phrase-level / idea-level paraphrase, while Korean and Chinese sources tend to lump them together.

4. **Collocation accuracy emphasis** — ZIM's "good" replacement bank explicitly teaches that `decent / passable / competent / fine / qualified` are NOT interchangeable despite same translation. This nuance is rarer in CN sources (which tend to list synonym groups without collocational restrictions).

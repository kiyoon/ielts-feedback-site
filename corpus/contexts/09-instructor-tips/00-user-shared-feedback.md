# User-shared Feedback (Task 1 Academic — Population/City Charts)

> Source: feedback the repo owner personally received from a Korean IELTS instructor and found very useful. This is **gold content** — high-density paraphrase + structural upgrades that move a Task 1 answer from band 6 to band 7. LLM agents marking essays should use these as concrete suggestion patterns.

**Context:** Task 1 Academic, describing a chart about population across cities over time (e.g., bar/line chart with city populations, possibly with a forecast year).

---

## 1. Paraphrasing the prompt vocabulary

| Common (band 6) | Upgrade (band 7+) |
|---|---|
| Population | demographic structure / demographic balance / demographic figure |
| City | urban area / cosmopolitan area / metropolitan area |
| Measured in 1 million people | measured in millions |
| Predicted | foreseen / forecasted / anticipated / expected |
| People | citizens / residents |
| Living (in) | dwell in / reside in |
| "the number of people who live in X" | the number of people who dwell/reside in X |

---

## 2. Comparative upgrades — "higher"

| Common | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| higher | higher / more significant / more prominent |

**Tip — always pair the comparative with a hedging adverb:**

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| Still higher | Still **relatively / considerably / slightly** higher |

This shows precise quantitative comparison rather than a vague "more."

---

## 3. "had a large population" → use possession/proportion verbs in PASSIVE

| Active (band 6) | Use these verbs | Passive (band 7) |
|---|---|---|
| had a large population | consist of / account for / make up / take up / allocate | "The largest population **was taken up by** São Paulo among the cities." |

**Why passive?** It varies grammatical structure and shifts focus to the figure being described, which is a hallmark of academic register in Task 1.

**Pattern:** `the [superlative] [noun] was [verb-past-participle] by [subject]`

---

## 4. "the largest" — superlative synonym bank

### Synonyms (high → highest)
- the most prominent
- the most overwhelming
- the most dominant
- the most significant

### Antonyms (low → lowest)
- the most marginal
- the most negligible
- the most minimal
- insignificant

**Example use:**
> "The figure of population seems to be **insignificant**."

---

## 5. Describing a wrong forecast / mis-estimation

| Plain (band 6) | Upgrade (band 7) |
|---|---|
| The estimation went in a completely wrong direction. | The figure of population in 2000 was **overestimated**. |

Other useful verbs in this family: `underestimated`, `was projected to … but actually …`, `the actual figure fell short of the forecast`, `exceeded the forecast`.

---

## Quick checklist when describing a population/city chart

1. Paraphrase "population" → demographic figure/structure/balance.
2. Paraphrase "city" → urban / metropolitan / cosmopolitan area.
3. Use **passive** with `make up / take up / account for / consist of / allocate`.
4. Vary your superlative — not just "the largest" but "the most dominant / prominent / overwhelming / significant."
5. Add a hedging adverb to every comparative: "considerably / slightly / relatively / significantly higher."
6. Use `dwell in / reside in` instead of "live in."
7. Use `forecast / anticipate / project / expect` instead of "predict."
8. For inaccurate forecasts, say `overestimated / underestimated`.

---

## Pattern bank (drop-in phrases)

```
- the largest [figure/share/proportion] was taken up by ___ among the [cities/countries/groups]
- the figure of [X] in [year] was overestimated / underestimated
- ___ accounted for the most dominant share of ___
- the number of citizens who dwell in ___ was forecasted to reach ___
- the demographic figure of ___ was anticipated to rise considerably
- ___ made up a relatively marginal proportion of the total
- the demographic balance shifted slightly in favour of ___
- the figure appears to be insignificant compared to ___
```

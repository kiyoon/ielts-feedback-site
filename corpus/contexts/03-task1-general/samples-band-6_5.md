# IELTS GT Task 1 — Band 6.5 Letter Samples

Band 6.5 letters cover all bullets and maintain mostly appropriate tone, but lack the consistent register, varied vocabulary, or specific detail of a Band 7. They typically have 2–4 minor errors and one or two register slips.

---

## Sample 1: Semi-formal — Mountain walking tour inquiry

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69fe0923312e6c0011a3e3f5)
**Letter Type:** Semi-formal (inquiry to tour operator)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
You have seen a brochure advertising a one-week mountain walking tour. Write a letter to the tour operator. In your letter:
- Explain why you are interested in the tour
- Ask for details about prices and dates
- Mention any concerns about your fitness

### Answer (extracted features)
The candidate (Krithi Santosh) writes about a "seven-day walking tour in the mountains near the river Yemena". They express enthusiasm for camping and bonfires. The letter has clear paragraphing and appropriate formal tone.

### Word Count
~165 words.

### Examiner Comments
"Your tone is polite and right for a formal letter." All three bullets covered. The letter "could add a little more about why you want this tour" and "ask more clear questions about price, dates, and what help is there for less fit people." Some phrases are unnatural; needs simpler, clearer linking words like "also", "however", "because". 3 grammar mistakes. 4 linking words used (under the 7+ benchmark).

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (7):** All bullets covered; questions in bullet 2 are not specific enough.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Logical paragraphing; not enough linkers.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** Some unnatural phrases.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (6):** A few minor errors.

### Improvement Notes
Make bullet 2 explicit: "Could you confirm the total cost, including any single-occupancy supplement, and let me know which tour dates between June and September still have availability?"

## Sample 2: Formal — Restaurant celebration thank-you

**Source:** writing9.com (https://writing9.com/text/69f205ca75fa8600118da251)
**Letter Type:** Formal (compliment / feedback)
**Overall Band:** 6.5
**Per-criterion:** TA: 6 | CC: 6 | LR: 7 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
You recently celebrated a special occasion at a restaurant with friends. Write a letter to the restaurant manager. In your letter:
- Give details of your visit
- Explain the reason for the celebration
- Mention what you particularly enjoyed about the food and service

### Answer (extracted features)
Kazuya Nakagawa describes celebrating a friend's 20th birthday at a sushi restaurant, with a complimentary birthday cake and staff singing happy birthday.

### Word Count
~160 words.

### Examiner Comments
"The letter has a clear opening and ending." Covers all three points. Areas for improvement: "Answer all three parts more fully" with clearer details. Adopt "a more natural formal tone" (avoid "I would appreciate you for"). "Link ideas in a smoother way" rather than starting sentences with "And" or "Because". Maintain "one main idea in each paragraph". Employ varied complex and simple sentence structures.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (6):** Bullets covered but under-developed.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (6):** Sentence-initial "And" / "Because" — informal mistakes in formal context.
- **Lexical Resource (7):** Reasonable variety.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (6):** "I would appreciate you for" is awkward — should be "I would like to express my appreciation for".

### Improvement Notes
Replace "And the staff sang happy birthday" with "Furthermore, the staff went out of their way to sing happy birthday to my friend" or "What particularly impressed me was the staff's spontaneous rendition of 'Happy Birthday' for the celebrant."

## Sample 3: Formal — Camping holiday complaint (Band 6.5 with upgrade)

**Overall Band:** 6.5

### Prompt
You recently went on a camping holiday but the experience was disappointing. Write a letter to the company. In your letter:
- Describe the holiday
- Explain what went wrong
- Say what you want the company to do

### Original Answer Issues
- "Deprive from having place for rest imdividually" — awkward and misspelled.

- "Convinient" → "convenient" (spelling).

### Word Count
237 words (over-long).

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement:** Bullets covered.
- **Coherence & Cohesion:** Adequate paragraphing.
- **Lexical Resource:** Spelling errors; awkward register.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Preposition and word-form errors.

### Improved Version (provided)
The 7.0+ rewrite uses sophisticated vocabulary ("immersing yourself in nature's beauty", "invigorating") and more polished phrasing — and is shorter (164 words), demonstrating that quality beats quantity.

## Sample 5: Informal — Letter to a friend (Band 6.5 typical)

**Source:** Pattern from writing9 / engnovate Band 6.5 informal letters
**Letter Type:** Informal
**Overall Band:** 6.5

### Prompt (typical)
A friend has written asking for advice about taking up a new sport. Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:
- Suggest a sport
- Explain its benefits
- Recommend how to get started

### Answer (Band 6.5 features)
"Hi Mark, Hope you're doing well! I was so pleased to get your message about wanting to try a new sport. I'd really recommend taking up swimming. It's great because it works your whole body and is easy on your joints. To get started, you could join the local pool — they have classes for beginners on Tuesday evenings. Looking forward to hearing how you get on! Take care, Sam."

### Word Count
~100 words (short, but pattern shown).

### Examiner Comments
- Tone consistent — informal throughout. Good.
- Contractions used naturally.
- Sign-off appropriate ("Take care").
- Recommendations are specific.
- However, word count would need to reach 160+ for Band 6.5; develop bullets further.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (6):** Tone good; needs more development per bullet.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Smooth transitions.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** Adequate informal range.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (7):** Accurate.

### Improvement Notes
For Band 7, add an idiom ("you'll be hooked in no time"), an anecdote ("I started two years ago and haven't looked back"), and more specific advice ("don't forget your goggles!").

## Sample 6: Formal — Noise complaint to landlord (Band 6.5 typical)

**Source:** Pattern from writing9 / various Band 6.5 complaints
**Letter Type:** Formal/semi-formal (complaint to landlord)
**Overall Band:** 6.5

### Prompt
The flat above yours is causing noise problems. Write a letter to your landlord. In your letter:
- Describe the noise problem
- Explain how it is affecting you
- Suggest what should be done

### Answer (Band 6.5 features)
"Dear Mr. Thompson, I am writing to bring to your attention a serious noise issue from the flat above mine, number 4B. For the last three weeks, loud music and shouting have been audible until well past midnight on most weeknights. As a result, I have not been able to sleep properly and my work performance has begun to suffer. I would be grateful if you could speak to the tenants in 4B and remind them of the building's quiet-hours policy. If the situation does not improve, I would request that more formal action be considered. Thank you for your attention to this matter. Yours sincerely, James Wright."

### Word Count
~115 words (still under length).

### Examiner Comments
- Tone consistent and appropriately formal.
- Specific details: flat number, time period, frequency.
- Polite but firm requests.
- Issue: under-length (would need ~50 more words); could add health impact details, prior conversations attempted, deadline for action.

### Specific Issues
- **Task Achievement (7):** Tone strong, but length insufficient.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Logical.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** Adequate.
- **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (7):** Accurate.

### Improvement Notes
Develop the impact paragraph: "The lack of sleep has begun to affect my health — I have suffered headaches and difficulty concentrating at work, and last week I was forced to take a day's sick leave. I have also raised the matter directly with the tenants twice, but the disturbance has continued unabated."

- **Coherence & Cohesion (7):** Smooth.

## Common Band 6.5 Profile (letters)

Band 6.5 letter writers:

1. **Address all three bullets** with reasonable balance.
2. **Maintain register** — though slip occasionally (one or two awkward phrases).
3. **Have a clear structure** — opening, body, closing.
4. **Show some lexical variety** but still repeat basic words.
5. **Have minor recurring grammar errors** — usually 3–5 in 170 words.
6. **Sometimes write under 150 words** — limiting Task Achievement.
7. **Use limited cohesive devices** — relying on "first / also / however".

**Path to Band 7:**
- Add **specific concrete detail** to each bullet (date, name, number, place).
- **Vary cohesive devices** — for formal: "furthermore, in addition, consequently, moreover"; for informal: "anyway, plus, by the way, on top of that".
- **Include one register-marker per paragraph** — formal: "I would be grateful", "I trust", "at your earliest convenience"; informal: "can't wait", "give me a shout", "you'd be a star".
- **Proofread for the obvious errors:** articles, agreement, prepositions, spelling.

## Per-criterion justification

**Task Achievement — 6.0.** The essay covers all three countries and selects defensible main features, but the overview is thin and partly inaccurate. "electricity consumption in all three countries had a growth" misrepresents the USA (which peaked then fell) and Germany (which also declined after 2010); only India grew throughout. A band-7 overview must capture this divergence (`contexts/05-band-6-to-7/task-response-upgrades.md`). Data is partially supported but key figures are missing (no 2010 USA value, no India 2000/2010 anchors), and "four times higher than 1990" is mathematically wrong (1,200/300 = 4×, so it is "four times **as high as** in 1990", not "higher"). This is the classic band-6 "presents an overview but…tendency to overgeneralise" descriptor (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`).

**Coherence and Cohesion — 6.0.** Paragraphing is logical (intro / overview / USA-Germany / India) and the reader can follow. However, linkers are mechanical — "However," "After this," "In contrast" — and reference is repetitive: "the figure of USA", "the figure of India", "USA's consumption" instead of pronouns or substitution (`contexts/05-band-6-to-7/cohesion-upgrades.md`). "In contrast, India is different" is tautological. This matches band-6: "arranges information…coherently…uses cohesive devices effectively, but cohesion within and/or between sentences may be faulty or mechanical" (`contexts/01-band-descriptors/task1-academic-band-descriptors.md`).

**Lexical Resource — 6.0.** Range is adequate ("illustrates", "approximately", "steadily", "peak", "in contrast") but trend verbs are repeated ("grew" ×2, "increase/increased" ×3, "decline/decrease" ×2) where the corpus offers "rose/climbed/surged/levelled off/dipped" (`contexts/09-instructor-tips/03-trend-language-bank.md`). One collocation error: "had a growth" (use "saw growth" / "experienced growth"). "consumption" is repeated four times where "usage", "demand", "the figure" could substitute (`contexts/08-vocabulary-grammar/06-paraphrase-quick-lookup.md`).

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy — 5.5.** Four errors in 178 words: (1) "USA's consumption increase" (missing -d), (2) "it slightly decrease" (missing -d), (3) "had reach to 1,200" (wrong participle + redundant "to"), (4) "is much higher than Germany" (tense slip into present in a past-data sentence). Sentence structures are almost entirely simple/compound; no relative clauses, no participial reductions, no inversion. Per `contexts/05-band-6-to-7/grammar-upgrades.md`, band 7 requires "a variety of complex structures" with "frequent error-free sentences" — this script delivers neither.

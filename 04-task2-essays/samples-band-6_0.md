# IELTS Task 2 — Band 6.0 Sample Essays

Band 6.0 is the **target band** for many IELTS candidates entering this dataset. These essays "address the task" and have a "clear position" with adequate (sometimes generic) support, but contain noticeable errors in grammar/vocabulary, occasional cohesion misfires, and lack the precision of Band 7.

---

## Sample 1: Wages of Entertainers (Band 6)

**Source:** [ieltsbuddy.com](https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-band-6-essay-samples.html)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 6
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
Some people believe that entertainers are paid too much and their impact on society is negative, while others disagree and believe that they deserve the money that they make because of their positive effects on society. Discuss both opinions and give your own opinion.

### Essay
The entertainment industry is one of the largest sectors in all around the world. Some think that the people who work in that industry earn too much money considering their bad influence on society, and I agree. Others, however, believe that their positive impact on others is worth the money that they are paid.

On the one hand, there is no doubt that show business is an enormous and unfairly well paid sector. In addition to that, members of it do not add real value, compared to others like, for instance, education workers. Although in some countries teachers live with unreasonable wages, their responsibility, is extremely valuable for next generations become better people. Whereas a singer can earn double their yearly salary from one concert. The other important point is, for a balanced and equal society, the difference between income levels must not be very high. Regardless than their contribution, no one should make billions of dollars that easily, because that imbalance does have a significant negative impact on societies.

On the other hand, some people think that entertainers' contribution to the modern life is worth the money they earn. It can be understood that for many people, watching a movie or going to a concert is irreplaceable with other activities; therefore, they think that their positive impact is crucial for a significant proportion of people. In addition to that, celebrities do compromise their privacy and freedom with being known by many others. In exchange of that, they do deserve a comfortable life with significantly better paychecks.

In conclusion, despite their minimal contribution with their work to the people and sacrifice from their private life; I believe that their impact is far from being positive and they are not paid fairly or balanced with others.

### Word Count
~290

### Examiner Comments
"Task fully addressed with clear position, though some generalizations lack focus. Organization is adequate but sequencers like 'in addition' appear too early before topic development. Vocabulary adequate but contains inaccuracies. Multiple grammatical errors noted."

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response (7):** Both sides covered, position clear; "minimal contribution" generalisation undermines TR.
- **Coherence & Cohesion (6):** "In addition to that" appears at sentence-start of a new idea — premature signposting; comma splice patterns.
- **Lexical Resource (6):** "irreplaceable with other activities" — wrong preposition; "Regardless than" — wrong construction (should be "Regardless of").
- **Grammar (6):** Sentence fragment ("Whereas a singer can earn..."); inconsistent comma usage; "their responsibility, is extremely valuable for next generations become better people" — comma + missing "to".

## Sample 2: Computers in the Classroom (Band 6)

**Source:** [ieltsbuddy.com](https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-band-6-essay-samples.html)
**Question Type:** Opinion (Agree/Disagree)
**Overall Band:** 6
**Per-criterion:** TR: 6 | CC: 6 | LR: 7 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
School children are becoming far too dependent on computers and this is having an alarming effect on reading and writing skills. Teachers need to avoid using computers in the classroom at all costs and go back to teaching basic study skills. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay
Children are born into the digital world. From young age, they know how to operate computers, iPad, and TV. It is part of their daily life. School age children is no exception to the use of computers. They are confident users of computers and very dependent on them which can lead to decline in reading and writing skills. Some teachers utilise the computers well in their lessons, while others avoid the use of computers in their classrooms. I believe good balance of both is needed to help students' reading and writing skills to improve.

Computers can help students with reading. For example, if students come across unknown words, they can search the unknow words and hear the pronunciation. If it was not for the computers, they have to find someone who knows how to pronounce the words for them. Therefore, computers can play positive role in students' reading skills.

On the other hands, writing skills need to be improved by lots of handwritten works. If students are using computers all the time and getting the help of autocorrection, they will not improve their writing skills. They will not know how to edit as autocorrect is doing the job for them.

In conclusion, I believe that teachers should not allow students to do all the work on the computers especially writing tasks. However, teacher should not avoid the use of computer as computers can be a great help if they use it effectively. Rather than avoiding computers that students are so used to, teachers need to come up with how to use it effectively to enhance students' reading and writing skills.

### Word Count
~285

### Examiner Comments
"Question adequately addressed with relevant ideas but lacks sufficient support in body paragraphs. Introduction should be shorter (2–3 sentences). Linking devices are overly mechanical with transition words in initial position. Referencing issues noted. Grammar generally good with minor errors throughout. Vocabulary range sufficient with some less common words but errors in word choice prevent Band 7."

### Specific Issues
- **Task Response (6):** Position clear (balance) but body paragraphs short and unsupported with examples.
- **Coherence (6):** Long introduction; "On the other hands" wrong; mechanical "Therefore", "However".
- **Lexical Resource (7):** "utilise", "autocorrection", "come up with" — Band 7 features. Errors: "the unknow words" (typo), "From young age" (missing article).
- **Grammar (6):** "School age children is" SVA; "they have to find" should be "they would have to find" (conditional).

## Sample 3: Improved Medical Care / Longevity (Band 6 borderline 7)

**Source:** [ieltsbuddy.com](https://www.ieltsbuddy.com/ielts-band-6-essay-samples.html)
**Question Type:** Adv/Disadv (do advantages outweigh)
**Overall Band:** 6 (borderline 7 — see GRA only at 6)
**Per-criterion:** TR: 7 | CC: 7 | LR: 7 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
One of the consequences of improved medical care is that people are living longer and life expectancy is increasing. Do you think the advantages of this development outweigh the disadvantages?

### Essay
Since the medical care system has made significant progress in the last decades, people nowadays get older. While this can be seen as a clear benefit on a personal view, it causes huge problems on a global view. That's why I think that the benefits of a higher life expectancy do not outweigh the drawbacks.

On the one hand, an advantage of a longer life and better medical care is that people can spend more time with their loved ones without being in pain. As it is possible to treat illnesses the way, that they don't affect the patient's life anymore. For example, even a few years ago a lot of people died having a heart attack. With the increased medical possibilities this isn't the case anymore. As a result, people can overcome illnesses that were deadly.

On the other hand, the global impact a longer life expectancy has is huge. This can be seen by the fact that a longer life of individuals means a higher population on planet earth. For instance, we already have about 8 billion inhabitants and this number is increasing steadily. Consequently, we'll not have enough resources to gain food and water for such a high number of individuals.

Furthermore, a longer life period also causes higher costs for the medical care system. Reason for that is that more resources are needed to keep people healthy and alive. To illustrate, for the prevention of a heart attack the patient gets a variety of pills to decrease his blood pressure. This medication is expensive. Accordingly, we will need more and more young people to finance the medical system, or the system must be changed.

All in all, it is understandable that people wish to have a healthy and very long life. But in my opinion the global disadvantages clearly outweigh the personal advantages of a longer life and better medical care.

Grammatical range acceptable with mix of simple and complex sentences, though some fragments and comma splice errors noted, particularly in the first body paragraph. Score approaches Band 7 but grammatical issues prevent it."

### Specific Issues
- **TR (7):** Position clear; both sides addressed; advantages slightly underdeveloped.
- **CC (7):** Adequate sequencing; redundant transitions.
- **LR (7):** "life expectancy", "deadly illnesses", "blood pressure" — competent.
- **GRA (6):** Sentence fragment: "As it is possible to treat illnesses the way, that they don't affect…" — comma + that-clause wrongly punctuated; "we'll not" awkward.

## Sample 5: "People Are Never Satisfied" (Band 6)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/people-are-never-satisfied-with-what-they-have-26069d2692eba13f/)
**Question Type:** Discuss / opinion on a statement
**Overall Band:** 6
**Per-criterion:** TR: 6.5 | CC: 6 | LR: 6 | GRA: 6

### Prompt
"People are never satisfied with what they have; they always want something more or something different." Discuss.

### Essay
Many people believe that humans are never fully satisfied with what they have. They often want more or something different. This is a natural part of human nature. In some ways, it can be helpful, but it can also cause problems if not controlled.

On the positive side, wanting more can help people improve their lives. When someone is not happy with their situation, they may work harder to reach a better one. For example, a person might want a better job or a nicer home. To achieve this, they may study, learn new skills, or take on new responsibilities. This kind of motivation can lead to success. It can also lead to progress in the world. Many scientific discoveries and new technologies exist because people were not satisfied with old ways and wanted something better.

However, this desire for more can also make people feel unhappy. If we always focus on what we do not have, we might forget to enjoy what we already do have. Today, social media and advertisements often show perfect lives, which can make people feel like their own lives are not good enough. This can lead to stress, jealousy, and low self-esteem. People may start to believe they need more money, better clothes, or more exciting experiences to be happy.

Also, always wanting something different can affect relationships and personal stability. A person who is always chasing something new might not stay in one place or keep strong friendships. They might get bored easily or feel unsatisfied even when things are going well. In the end, constantly wanting more doesn't always lead to true happiness.

In conclusion, it is normal to want more or something new. This can help us improve ourselves and our world. But if we always focus on what we don't have, we may never feel happy. The best choice is to find a balance—work for a better future, but also learn to appreciate what we already have.

### Word Count
~340

### Examiner Comments
"Balanced discussion with relevant examples; clear organization and easy-to-follow structure. More sophisticated vocabulary needed; vocabulary repetition (particularly: people, something, always, better, might)."

### Specific Issues
- **TR (6.5):** Balanced and supported; lacks a clear personal stance which prevents 7.
- **CC (6):** Logical; some choppy sentence flow ("Also, always wanting something different…").
- **LR (6):** Vocabulary very plain — "people", "things", "good", "better"; no idiomatic expressions or topic-specific lexis.
- **GRA (6):** Mostly accurate but limited to simple/compound sentences. Few subordinate clauses; no inversion; few participle constructions.

### Why This Is Band 6 (not 7)
The essay is grammatically clean and logically organised — a B2 student. To reach 7, it needs:
- Less repetition of "people / always / things"; introduce *human nature, ambition, discontent, contentment, gratitude, materialism*.
- More complex sentences (e.g. "Driven by social-media comparison, individuals frequently overlook…"); use of relative clauses and participle phrases.
- Stronger thesis taking a clear side rather than middle ground.

## Sample 8: Government vs Individual Lifestyle (Band 6)

**Source:** [engnovate.com](https://engnovate.com/ugc-ielts-writing-task-2-essays/some-people-think-the-governments-should-act-to-decide-how-people-live-in-order-to-have-a-healthier-life-6645eda93ccd7/)
**Question Type:** Discuss both views + opinion
**Overall Band:** 6
**Per-criterion:** TR: 6.5 | CC: 5.5 | LR: 5.5 | GRA: 5.5

### Prompt
Some people think the governments should act to decide how people live in order to have a healthier life. Others think individuals should decide their own lifestyle. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

### Essay
In contemporary society, it is generally believed that governments should play a crucial part in promoting public health while others argue that each individual should have freedom to make choices for themselves in terms of lifestyle. This essay will discuss both perspectives and present my opinions.

On the one hand, one of the potential benefits of government intervention in lifestyle choices is releasing regulations against unhealthy products. It is believed that in an attempt to motivate citizens to establish healthier lifestyles, governments ought to strictly regulate the marketing and distribution of harmful substances including alcohol, tobacco, and junk food. For example, one of realistic practices can be considered is implementing higher taxes on sugary beverages or banning advertising of cigarettes which can discourage consumption and reduce associated health risks. This would drive to a reduction in unhealthy behaviors and contribute to improved public health outcomes. Furthermore, the government's concerns can provide a motivation for public health through campaigns and education. In particular, governments can play a role in educating the public about healthy lifestyle choices through public health campaigns, school programs. Launching campaigns designed to promote regular exercise, balanced diets, and smoking cessation can raise awareness and empower individuals to make healthier decisions. Therefore, the population's state of wellness can be benefited and lead to positive behavioral changes resulting from enhanced knowledge and awareness.

On the other hand, advocates for individual autonomy argue that people ought to have the right to make their own choices regarding their lifestyle without government interference. To be specific, individuals should possess the freedom to select their dietary preferences, exercise routines, and recreational activities based on personal beliefs and preferences. Thus, respecting individual autonomy fosters a sense of personal responsibility for one's own health and well-being. In addition, different cultures and individuals have diverse values, traditions, and beliefs regarding health and lifestyle, which may not align with government mandates. For instance, dietary practices, physical activity preferences, and attitudes towards risk-taking vary among populations and should be respected as part of cultural diversity. As a consequence, allowing individuals to make their own lifestyle choices promotes cultural autonomy and respects the diversity of human experiences.

Taking everything into consideration, although there are some potential benefits to be gained from government's playing a role in deciding citizens' lifestyle, I firmly believe that it is necessary for individuals to have their freedom to pursue the living style that they believe is best for themselves.

- Minor lapses; needs specific examples.
- Issues with paragraph transitions.
- Errors: subject-verb agreement, article usage, prepositions.

### Specific Issues
- **TR (6.5):** Both views thoroughly discussed; opinion appears only at end.
- **CC (5.5):** Body 1 is one giant block; needs to break into 2 supporting points with paragraph break.

## Common Patterns at Band 6.0

1. **Position clear and task addressed**, but with generalisations.
2. **Mechanical signposting** ("Firstly, Secondly, Lastly", "On the one hand … On the other hand").
3. **Adequate vocabulary** but repeated content words (≥ 6× of *people / education / students / health*).
4. **Subject-verb agreement** errors with abstract or collective nouns.
5. **Comma splice / fused sentences** in body paragraphs.
6. **Wordy, indirect introductions** ("In contemporary society, it is generally believed that…").
7. **Body paragraphs not balanced** — one side gets more space.
8. **Conclusion paraphrases introduction** rather than synthesising new insight.
9. **Typos** ("compitition", "loosing", "unknow", "rayes") — easy fixes.
10. **No idiomatic / collocational vocabulary** — "deepen knowledge" never "broaden horizons", "seek answers" never "scratch the surface".

## What Stops a Band 6 Essay from Reaching Band 7
- LR repetition; 1–2 wrong-word/spelling errors per paragraph.
- GRA simple structures; few subordinate clauses; SVA errors.
- TR over-generalisation; lack of specific examples (no proper nouns, no data).
- CC overuse of mechanical connectives; weak referencing.
